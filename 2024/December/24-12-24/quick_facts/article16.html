Kashmir Artisans Revive Dodo Craft

Key Highlights

  • Introduction: Kashmir artisans revived the dodo craft introduced by Mauritius tourists 20 years ago.
  • Artisans' Contribution: Detailed papier mâché models symbolize deforestation and extinction.
  • Economic Impact: Over 50,000 dodo models exported in 2023, with high demand in Europe and Mauritius.
  • Production Process: Models crafted in 5-10 days, with up to 3,000 models produced per season.
  • Cultural and Historical Significance: Papier mâché dodos preserve memory of the extinct bird and integrate global symbols.
  • Handicrafts Promotion: Increased demand boosts local economies and promotes eco-conscious themes.

The Dodo Bird

  • Extinction: The dodo, native to Mauritius, went extinct in 1681 due to human activities.
  • Symbolism: The dodo represents human-induced extinction and environmental neglect.

Assam Bird Sanctuary: Declining Species

  • Decline in Bird Species: 72% drop in species count over 27 years at Bordoibam-Bilmukh Bird Sanctuary.
  • Causes: Overfishing, poaching, habitat degradation, and excessive human activity.
  • Conservation Needs: Urgent conservation efforts needed to protect Assam's biodiversity.
