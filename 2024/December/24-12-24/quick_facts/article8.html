Eklavya Model Residential Schools and PVTG Sub-Quota

Overview

The government is struggling to meet the 5% sub-quota for Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs) in admissions to Eklavya Model Residential Schools (EMRS), aiming to improve education for tribal students in remote areas.

Eklavya Model Residential Schools (EMRS)

Launched in 1997-98, EMRS aims to provide quality education to Scheduled Tribe (ST) children in remote regions. These co-educational schools offer free education from grades VI to XII and follow the CBSE curriculum. The initiative is managed by the National Education Society for Tribal Students (NESTS).

5% Sub-Quota for PVTG Students

In 2019, the government set a 5% sub-quota for PVTG students, but only 3.4% of students in EMRS come from these communities. Despite the sub-quota, challenges like infrastructure gaps, teacher shortages, and dropout rates persist, particularly in states like Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, and Gujarat.
