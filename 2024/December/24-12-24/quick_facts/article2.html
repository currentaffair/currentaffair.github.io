US Eradicated Northern Giant Hornet

Introduction

The Northern Giant Hornet, also known as the "murder hornet," has been successfully eradicated in the United States.

About the Northern Giant Hornet

  • Scientific Name: Vespa Mandarinia
  • Size: Largest hornet in the world, measuring up to 2 inches long.
  • Native to Asia and first spotted in Washington state in 2019.
  • Has a large yellow/orange head and black eyes.
  • Threat to native pollinators, capable of killing a beehive in 90 minutes.
  • Venomous: Delivers seven times more venom than a honeybee and can sting multiple times.

Behavior and Nesting

  • Builds nests in forested areas, often in underground cavities.
