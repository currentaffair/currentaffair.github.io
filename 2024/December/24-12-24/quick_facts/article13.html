India's Gold Imports and Economic Impact

The Union government launched a detailed examination of gold import data after a record high of $14.8 billion imports in November 2023. This surge is attributed to increased demand for gold investments, tariff reductions, and trade agreement loopholes.

Key Factors Behind Gold Import Surge

  • Reduction of import duties from 15% to 6% in the Budget 2024-25, making gold more attractive.
  • Impact on trade balance, weakening the rupee and increasing the current account deficit.

About DGCIS

The Directorate General of Commercial Intelligence and Statistics (DGCIS), founded in 1862, handles commercial data and trade statistics. It's headquartered in Kolkata and is a key resource for policymakers and researchers.
