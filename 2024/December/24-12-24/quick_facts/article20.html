National Programme for Prevention and Control of NCD

What is NP-NCD?

The NP-NCD addresses the rising burden of non-communicable diseases (NCDs) such as cardiovascular diseases, cancer, and diabetes, contributing to 63% of deaths in India.

Objectives and Implementation

The program aims to prevent and control NCDs through infrastructure strengthening, human resource development, and health promotion. It is implemented at the national, state, and district levels, with financial support through the NCD Flexi-Pool under the National Health Mission.

Key Components

The NP-NCD includes screening for diabetes, hypertension, and cancers. It also supports NCD clinics, cardiac care units, cancer care infrastructure, and training of health workers.

Impact

As of now, the program has established 770 District NCD Clinics, 372 Day Care Centres, 233 CCUs, and over 6,400 CHC NCD Clinics, facilitating accessible diagnosis and management.

Non-Communicable Diseases

NCDs are chronic diseases influenced by genetic, environmental, and behavioral factors. Key risk factors include tobacco use, alcohol, inactivity, and air pollution.

Global Impact and Prevention

NCDs account for 74% of global deaths, with 85% occurring in low- and middle-income countries. Prevention includes reducing tobacco use, promoting exercise, and improving air quality. WHO aims to reduce premature NCD deaths by one-third by 2030.
