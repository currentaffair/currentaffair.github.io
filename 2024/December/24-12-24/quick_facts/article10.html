Odisha's Fiscal Evolution & Charan Singh's Legacy

Odisha's Fiscal Journey

Over the last two decades, Odisha has evolved from one of India's most indebted states to the least indebted. The state's debt-to-GSDP ratio peaked at 57.3% in 2002-03 and now stands at just 16.0% in 2023-24.

Fiscal reforms, such as the Odisha Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Act (OFRBMA), have set benchmarks like a revenue surplus, fiscal deficit cap, and debt limit, contributing to consistent revenue growth.

Despite constraints, Odisha has prioritized infrastructure development, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic, posting a revenue surplus.

Chaudhary Charan Singh: A Visionary Leader

Born in 1902, Chaudhary Charan Singh was an influential leader who played a key role in shaping India's agriculture and rural development policies. Known as the 'Kisan Leader,' he drafted the Debt Redemption Bill and worked to support farmers through initiatives like the Minimum Support Price (MSP).
