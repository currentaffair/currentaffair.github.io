Key Government Policies and Bills

Interception Rules (2024)

The Union Government introduced new interception rules empowering agencies to intercept phone messages under specific conditions. The Union Home Secretary, State Home Secretaries, or Joint Secretary can issue orders for interceptions in unavoidable circumstances. Interception records must be destroyed within six months unless required for legal purposes.

Protected Area Regime (2023)

The Maharashtra government has reimposed the Protected Area Regime (PAR) in Manipur, Mizoram, and Nagaland due to security concerns, including ethnic violence and cross-border influx. Foreigners must obtain Protected Area Permits (PAP) to enter these areas. The reimposition impacts tourism but strengthens security measures along India's border regions.

Maharashtra Special Public Security Bill (2024)

The Maharashtra Special Public Security Bill aims to combat Naxalism. It allows the government to declare organizations as unlawful and criminalizes membership, fund-raising, and related activities. Offenders can face up to seven years in prison and fines up to Rs 5 lakh. The bill differs from the UAPA by providing a faster legal process and more inclusive definitions of unlawful activity.

Assam Bird Sanctuary Crisis

A study conducted between 2022-2024 found a 72% decline in bird species at Bordoibam-Bilmukh Bird Sanctuary. Causes include overfishing, poaching, and habitat destruction. Immediate conservation efforts are needed to restore the wetland ecosystem and prevent further loss of biodiversity.
