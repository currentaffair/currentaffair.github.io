Starlink Devices in Manipur & Assam Bird Sanctuary Decline

Starlink Technology in Manipur

Recently, the Indian Army seized Starlink-branded devices along with weapons in Manipur, despite the service being unauthorized in India. Starlink provides high-speed, low-latency internet using low Earth orbit satellites, particularly popular in remote areas and disaster zones.

Controversy and Legal Issues

Starlink devices with extremist group markings were found during raids. Elon Musk responded by claiming Starlink is disabled in India. The technology is pending approval in the country, but unauthorized devices may still function due to satellite internet's technical limitations.

Regulation Challenges

In India, satellite communication devices are strictly regulated under the Indian Wireless Act and Indian Telegraph Act. Unauthorized use, including Starlink devices, is illegal, though identifying illegal buyers is complicated due to shadow companies. Despite this, the Starlink app remains available for download in India.

Assam Bird Sanctuary Decline

The Bordoibam-Bilmukh Bird Sanctuary in Assam has seen a 72% decline in bird species over the past 27 years. A study conducted between October 2022 and March 2024 recorded only 47 species compared to 167 in 1997. Overfishing, poaching, and habitat destruction are major causes of the decline.
