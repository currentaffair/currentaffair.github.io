Khalistan Terror Attack and Encounter

Introduction

Three pro–Khalistan operatives involved in a grenade attack at Bakshiwala police post (Punjab) were killed in an encounter in Pilibhit, Uttar Pradesh. The operatives were linked to the Khalistan Zindabad Force (KZF), a terror group with ties to Pakistan’s ISI.

Details of the Encounter

  • The encounter was a breakthrough against the KZF terror module by Punjab Police.
  • Two AK-47 rifles and two 9mm Glock pistols were recovered from the deceased operatives.

Cross-Border Terrorism and International Links

  • Mastermind Jagjeet Singh, allegedly a British Army personnel, used the alias Fateh Singh Baghi, indicating international involvement.
  • The terror module is controlled by Pakistan-based Rajeet Singh Nita and Greece-based Jaswinder Singh Mannu.
  • The UK-based Jagjeet Singh’s involvement suggests the role of the diaspora in funding, radicalizing, and supporting terror activities.
  • The KZF operates with cross-border support from Pakistan, Greece, and the UK, showing a well-coordinated transnational operation.

Radicalization and Recruitment

  • The operatives came from lower socioeconomic backgrounds, with at least one having a criminal history, indicating recruitment of marginalized youth.
  • Radical groups tend to recruit disillusioned individuals, including those with criminal backgrounds.

Investigations and Future Actions

  • Ongoing investigation to uncover the broader terror network, focusing on logistics, financing, and collaborators.
  • The operation highlights persistent threats from both domestic and international terror groups with foreign backing.
