Gaganyaan Mission - HLVM3 Assembly

Introduction

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has started the assembly of the Human Rated Launch Vehicle Mark-3 (HLVM3) at Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), Sriharikota, for the Gaganyaan mission.

HLVM3 Assembly

The assembly began on December 18, marking the start of the HLVM3-G1/OM-1 mission. The nozzle end segment of the S200 motor was stacked as part of the formal launch campaign.

Gaganyaan Mission Objective

  • To demonstrate human spaceflight by launching a three-member crew into an orbit of 400 km for a three-day mission.
  • Ensure the crew's safe return by landing in sea waters.

Mission Structure

  • Three uncrewed missions to validate technologies.
  • One crewed mission following successful tests.

Safety and Redundancy

  • Advanced crew escape systems and safety measures for astronaut protection during all phases of the mission.

10th Anniversary of LVM3-X/CARE Mission

The assembly coincides with the December 18, 2014, launch of LVM3-X, which successfully tested re-entry, deceleration, and recovery technologies essential for the Gaganyaan program.

Key Achievements of the 2014 Mission

  • Controlled re-entry using thrusters.
  • Recovery of the Crew Module from the Bay of Bengal, 1600 km from SDSC.

Mission Details

Launch Vehicle:LVM3-X (Experimental Variant of GSLV Mark-III)
Launch Site:Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), Sriharikota
Payload:Crew Module Atmospheric Re-entry Experiment (CARE)
Mission Type:Suborbital Test Flight
Primary Objective:Test re-entry and recovery capabilities of the Crew Module
Payload Mass:3,735 kg
Altitude Achieved:126 km
Mission Duration:20 minutes
Technologies Demonstrated:Re-entry Systems, Deceleration Systems, Crew Module Recovery

Mission Milestones

  • Smooth liftoff and ascent.
  • Successful Crew Module separation.
  • Thruster-oriented module for safe re-entry.
  • Successful recovery by Indian Coast Guard from Bay of Bengal.
