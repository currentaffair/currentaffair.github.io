One Nation, One Election - JPC Scrutiny

What is a Joint Parliamentary Committee (JPC)?

A JPC is a special committee set up by Parliament to investigate a specific issue or Bill. It is formed when both Houses of Parliament agree on the motion. The JPC members represent both ruling and opposition parties, with the number of members varying based on the issue at hand.

Current JPC on "One Nation, One Election"

The current JPC for the "One Nation, One Election" bills has 31 members: 21 from the Lok Sabha and 10 from the Rajya Sabha. The committee's role is to conduct a thorough investigation and scrutiny of the bills.

Functions of a JPC

JPCs investigate financial irregularities, legislative proposals, and public interest issues. They review documents, summon individuals for questioning, and submit a report to Parliament with their findings and recommendations.

JPC's Recommendations

While the JPC's recommendations are not legally binding, the government must provide an "Action Taken Report" to Parliament, detailing the steps it has taken based on the committee's suggestions.
