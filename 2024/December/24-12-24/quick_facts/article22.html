Monarch Butterfly Decline

About Monarch Butterflies

Monarch butterflies belong to the brush-footed butterflies family (Nymphalidae), with about 300 species. They are large, colorful butterflies with long, orange wings marked with black and white. Known for migrating long distances, they feed mainly on milkweed.

Population Decline

The population of Monarch butterflies has declined significantly over the past decade, with a 23-72% drop and an 84% decline in eastern monarchs between 1996 and 2014. They breed exclusively on milkweed plants, which have been increasingly removed due to farming practices and weedicides.

Contributing Issues

Habitat destruction caused by legal and illegal logging, as well as deforestation for agriculture and urban development, worsens the Monarch's population decline. Extreme weather events like storms and droughts disrupt flowering cycles, leading to mass mortality among the butterflies.
