Flowers Pollination by Large Carnivore

Overview

The Ethiopian wolf, a rare and endangered species, aids in pollination of Ethiopian red hot poker flowers. This challenges traditional plant-pollinator relationships by transferring pollen through its muzzle.

Ethiopian Wolf

The Ethiopian wolf is a specialized rodent hunter found in the Afroalpine ecosystem of Ethiopia. It has a reddish coat, white markings on its throat and chest, and a black bushy tail. With fewer than 500 left, it is Africa's most endangered carnivore.

Other Carnivore Pollinators

Other carnivores, like bats, civets, mongooses, and even sun bears, also play a role in pollination by consuming nectar.
