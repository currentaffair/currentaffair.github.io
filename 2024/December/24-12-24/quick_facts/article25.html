Polar Sundial at VSSC

Innovative Design at VSSC

The Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC) unveiled a polar sundial at its Space Museum. The sundial provides Indian Standard Time (IST) using advanced design and scientific precision.

Features of the Polar Sundial

  • IST Display: Integrates analemmatic correction for precise time conversion.
  • Polar Configuration: Aligned with Earth's polar axis and Thiruvananthapuram’s latitude.
  • Space Themes: Features a 3D-printed miniature of the Rohini RH200 sounding rocket.

History and Functioning of Sundials

Sundials are ancient timekeeping devices that use the Sun’s shadow. Their designs evolved from ancient Egypt to modern times. Sundials work based on Earth’s rotation, with the shadow’s position indicating time.

Rohini RH200 Rocket

The Rohini RH200 rocket is a key component in India’s space research, supporting over 1,600 missions. It holds the record for 200 consecutive successful launches as of 2022.

About VSSC

The Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC) is a prominent ISRO facility in Thiruvananthapuram, specializing in rocket development for India’s satellite program.
