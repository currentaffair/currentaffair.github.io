Crime and Criminal Tracking Network, Labour Migration & Undersea Cable Systems

CCTNS - Crime and Criminal Tracking Network

  • Launched: 2009 with a ₹2,000 crore budget.
  • Goal: Enhance police efficiency, transparency, and citizen-oriented services.
  • Features: 17,130 police stations connected, crime data analytics, citizen services, and integration with criminal justice systems.
  • New Laws: Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (replacing outdated laws).
  • Updates: Mandatory video recording, forensic investigations, arrest transparency, case follow-up.

Labour Migration and Skill Development

  • Global Trends: Countries seek skilled labor to address demographic challenges.
  • India’s Policy Gaps: No national policy on international labor mobility, skill gaps, and lack of return migration strategies.
  • Recommendations: National policy framework, skill gap analysis, integrating skills with global standards, return migrant reintegration.

Undersea Cable Systems

  • Systems: India Asia Xpress (IAX) and India Europe Xpress (IEX) for global connectivity.
  • Technology: High-capacity optical fiber cables, providing unlimited bandwidth and low latency.
  • India’s Capacity: 17 international subsea cables with a capacity of 138.55 Tbps.
