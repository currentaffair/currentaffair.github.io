Energy Challenges in Extreme Weather

Key Issues

  • Chillai Kalan: A 40-day harsh winter period in Kashmir with sub-zero temperatures, including the coldest night in 33 years at -8.5°C.
  • Energy Crisis: Long power cuts (12-16 hours) made electric heating ineffective, forcing reliance on traditional heating methods like kangris and bukharis.
  • Economic Impact: Increased demand for firewood and charcoal boosted local businesses, while modern heating devices went underused.
  • Challenges for KPDCL: Power infrastructure overloading during winter damaged transformers and circuits, leading to prolonged outages.
  • Safety Concerns: Ice-covered roads posed risks for motorists, requiring extra caution.
  • Environmental Concerns: The return to wood for heating raised deforestation and air quality concerns.

Way Forward

  • Infrastructure Development: Modernize energy distribution to withstand winter demands.
  • Renewable Energy: Promote solar and wind energy solutions tailored to Kashmir’s climate.
  • Community Initiatives: Encourage energy conservation and the adoption of hybrid heating systems.
