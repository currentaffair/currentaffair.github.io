Milkweed Fibre - A Natural Resource

Introduction

The Union Ministry of Textiles is promoting the research and development of new natural fibres, including milkweed fibre.

What is Milkweed Fibre?

  • Derived from the seeds of the milkweed plant (Asclepias syriaca L.), a flowering plant native to Southern Canada and the United States.
  • Found growing wild in India, particularly in Rajasthan, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu.
  • The plant can grow up to 1.8 meters tall and produces white latex when cut.

Properties of Milkweed Fibre

  • Contains oily material and lignin, making it difficult to spin.
  • Hydrophobic and oleophobic due to natural waxes, which repel water and oil.
  • The plantâ€™s latex contains toxic compounds, but young shoots, leaves, flower buds, and immature fruits are edible when properly prepared.

Applications of Milkweed Fibre

  • Used in the paper industry and as an insulating filler material.
  • Incorporated into water safety equipment like life jackets and belts.
  • Effective in oil spill cleanup as it absorbs oil while repelling water.
