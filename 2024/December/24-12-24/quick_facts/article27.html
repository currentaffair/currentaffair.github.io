Spot-billed Pelicans & Assam Bird Sanctuary Decline

Spot-billed Pelicans

The breeding season for spot-billed pelicans has started at Uppalapadu Bird Protection Centre, home to around 3,000 of these birds. Spot-billed pelicans (scientific name: Pelecanus philippensis) are relatively small pelicans found in Southeast Asia, especially India and Sri Lanka.

Physical Description & Behavior

These pelicans have gray dorsal feathers, white ventral feathers, and an orange-yellow eye ring with a purple patch near each eye. They are social birds, usually living in flocks. They are strong swimmers and good fliers, although they are not graceful on land.

Food & Habitat

Spot-billed pelicans are carnivorous, feeding mostly on fish, but sometimes small reptiles and aquatic crustaceans. They hunt in both freshwater and marine environments, sometimes diving just below the surface. They prefer wetlands and coastal areas.

Conservation Status

Listed as "Near Threatened" on the IUCN Red List, the spot-billed pelican is also protected under India's Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972 (Schedule IV).

Assam Bird Sanctuary Decline

Over a period of 27 years, Bordoibam-Bilmukh Bird Sanctuary in Assam has experienced a 72% decline in bird species count. A study conducted between October 2022 and March 2024 recorded only 47 species compared to 167 species in 1997. Major causes of the decline include overfishing, poaching, and habitat degradation.
