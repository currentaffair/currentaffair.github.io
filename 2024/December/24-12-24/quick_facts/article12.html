Endangered Species & Maharashtra Special Public Security Bill

Grey Junglefowl: Endangered Species

The Grey Junglefowl, also known as Sonnerat's junglefowl, is native to India and is the wild ancestor of the domestic chicken. It is listed in Schedule I of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972, and the Wildlife (Protection) Amendment Act 2022.

Significance & Habitat

This species is found primarily in peninsular India, including Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, and Rajasthan. It lives in primary forests, dry scrub, and secondary growth forests.

Threats & Conservation

Hybridization with domestic chickens and lack of awareness threaten the species' genetic purity. It is listed as "Least Concern" by the IUCN due to its wide geographical distribution and slow population decline.

Maharashtra Special Public Security Bill 2024

The Maharashtra government introduced the MSPC Bill to combat Naxalism. It criminalizes activities related to unlawful organizations, with penalties ranging from two to seven years in prison.

Key Provisions

  • Criminalizes membership, fund-raising, and management of unlawful organizations.
  • Offenses are cognizable and non-bailable.

Differences with UAPA

The Maharashtra Bill uses a panel of judges for confirmation and empowers district officials to expedite prosecution, whereas UAPA requires higher authority approval.

Unlawful Activity Definition

The Bill defines "unlawful activity" broadly, including acts of violence, vandalism, and promoting disobedience to law.
