Cephalopod Intelligence and Animal Welfare

Key Highlights

  • Cephalopod Intelligence: Comparable to vertebrates in learning, memory, and problem-solving.
  • Example: Octopus vulgaris has 500 million neurons, 300 million in arm "mini-brains".
  • Unique Behaviors: Advanced camouflage, using chromatophores to create intricate patterns.
  • Learning and Decision-Making: Capable of delayed gratification (e.g., waiting for preferred prey like live shrimp).
  • Animal Welfare Implications: Current welfare standards exclude invertebrates, but ethical guidelines are being advocated.
  • Ethical Considerations: U.S. states are banning octopus farming in 2024 due to growing concern for cephalopods' welfare.

Way Forward

  • Broaden animal welfare frameworks to include intelligent invertebrates.
  • Support research on cephalopod cognition and its implications for biodiversity conservation.
