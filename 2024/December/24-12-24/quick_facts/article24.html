Key Highlights of Internal Displacement Report & Maharashtra Special Public Security Bill

Internal Displacement Report (PROGRESS 2024)

Published By: International Organization for Migration (IOM) Global Data Institute (GDI) and Georgetown University

Purpose: Analyze solutions to internal displacement and understand displaced persons' preferences and needs.

Key Findings: 60% of displaced persons prefer staying in their current location, with safety being a major factor. Economic opportunities become more critical after five years of displacement.

Data Sources: 116,000 household surveys across 12 countries.

About IOM

Founded: 1951, part of the UN system. Objective: Focus on migration solutions and humanitarian support for displaced populations.

Maharashtra Special Public Security Bill 2024

Purpose: Combat Naxalism's growing influence in urban areas by granting power to declare organizations unlawful.

Key Provisions: Criminalizes membership, fundraising, management, or involvement with unlawful organizations. Punishments include 2-7 years imprisonment and fines up to Rs 5 lakh.

Differences from UAPA: The Bill has a quicker process for declaring organizations unlawful, empowering local authorities to approve prosecutions.
