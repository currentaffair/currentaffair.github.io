PM's Visit to Kuwait & India-France Cooperation

PM's Visit to Kuwait

PM Modi's visit to Kuwait marked the first visit by an Indian Prime Minister in 43 years. The focus was on defense and trade, with engagements including talks with Kuwaiti leadership, a visit to an Indian labor camp, and participation in the Gulf Cup football tournament opening.

Bilateral Agreements

India and Kuwait signed a defense cooperation pact and a bilateral investment treaty, strengthening their relationship. The total trade between India and the GCC reached $184.46 billion in 2022-23, with emphasis on the welfare of Indian workers abroad.

India-France Cooperation

India and France signed an MoU to develop the world's largest National Museum, Yuga Yugeen Bharat, located in New Delhi. The museum will span 117,000 square meters and include 950 rooms, housed in the North and South Blocks.

Key Pillars of India-France Cooperation

  • Defense: Strong defense ties with agreements for the exchange of personnel and joint exercises.
  • Space: Collaboration on satellite launches, including GSAT-24.
  • Civil Nuclear: France's involvement in India's nuclear projects like the Jaitapur Nuclear Power Project.
  • Economic: Bilateral trade of $13.4 billion in 2022-23, with France being India's 11th largest investor.
  • Marine and Maritime: Partnership on the Blue Economy and Ocean Governance.
