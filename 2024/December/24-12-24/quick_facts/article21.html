Tiger Straying and Maharashtra Bill

Tiger Straying from Similipal to Purulia

A 3-year-old female tiger from Similipal Reserve Forest, Odisha, has strayed into Purulia, West Bengal, crossing Jhargram. The tiger, fitted with a radio collar, was originally translocated from Maharashtra. Purulia's fragmented forests lack prey, making it unsuitable for tigers.

Mitigation Efforts

The West Bengal Forest Department is working to drive the tiger back to Similipal or relocate it to a more suitable habitat. A public advisory has been issued to avoid forest areas for safety. The region saw a similar incident in 2018 when a tiger was hunted after straying.

Maharashtra Special Public Security Bill 2024

The Maharashtra government introduced the MSPC Bill to tackle Naxalism in urban areas. It criminalizes membership and support for unlawful organizations, with punishments ranging from 2 to 7 years in prison. The bill allows district authorities to expedite prosecution, distinguishing it from the UAPA, which requires approval from higher authorities.

Unlawful Activity Under the Bill

The bill broadly defines "unlawful activity" to include actions that disrupt public order, challenge legal authority, or encourage violence, such as vandalism and promoting disobedience to law.
