Birhor Tribe Movement Against Child Marriage

Context

The Birhor tribe, a particularly vulnerable group in Jharkhand, has joined a movement in Giridih against child marriage for the first time.

About the Birhor Tribe

  • Primary Region: Jharkhand, with presence in Chhattisgarh, Orissa, and West Bengal.
  • Name Meaning: "Birhor" means 'jungle people'; "bir" means 'jungle', "hor" means 'men'.
  • Language: Birhor language belongs to the Munda group of languages within the Austroasiatic family, with similarities to Santali, Mundari, and Ho languages.
  • Ethnology: Short stature, long head, wavy hair, and broad nose. The Birhor claim descent from the Sun and regard Kharwars as their brothers.
  • Economy: A nomadic subsistence economy focused on hunting, gathering, and rope-making from vine species.
  • Socio-economic Classification: Includes wandering "Uthlus" and settled "Janghis".

Tribes of Jharkhand

The Birhor tribe is part of a rich cultural heritage in Jharkhand, alongside other tribes such as the Munda, Oraon (Kurukh), Santhal, Ho, and many others, each with distinct cultural practices.
