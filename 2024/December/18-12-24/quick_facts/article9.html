World's Youngest Chess Champion: Gukesh

Gukesh Becomes the Youngest Chess Champion

At 18 years old, Gukesh has officially become the youngest World Chess Champion, surpassing Garry Kasparov's record. He won the title by defeating the previous champion, Ding Liren, in the final match in Singapore.

Controversy Over the Title

While Gukesh holds the record for the youngest universally recognized World Chess Champion, there is a dispute with Ukrainian Grandmaster Ruslan Ponomariov. Ponomariov won the FIDE World Chess Championship at the same age in 2002, but his victory was through a knockout tournament, not by defeating the reigning champion directly.

About the Candidates Tournament

The Candidates Tournament is the final stage of the World Chess Championship cycle. It determines the challenger for the current World Champion and has been organized by FIDE since 1950.

About FIDE

The International Chess Federation (FIDE), founded in 1924 and based in Switzerland, is the governing body for international chess competitions. It oversees nearly 200 national federations and has been recognized by the IOC since 1999. Their motto, "We are one family," reflects their mission to unite chess players worldwide.
