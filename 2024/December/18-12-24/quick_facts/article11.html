India's GDP Growth Slows Temporarily

Overview of Growth Slowdown

India's GDP growth slowed to 5.4% in Q2 2024-25, attributed to temporary factors such as the general elections and festival timing. Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman described the slowdown as a 'blip'.

Growth Context

India remains the fastest-growing major economy, with an average growth rate of 8.3% over the past three years. Despite the slowdown, the economy is still showing resilience.

Manufacturing Resilience

50% of Industrial Production Index (IIP) sectors remain strong, and capital expenditure increased by 6.4% during this period.

Inflation Control Measures

The government managed to control food inflation through buffer stocks and subsidies, which helped maintain stability in the economy.

Festival Impact on Consumer Spending

The alignment of festivals led to uneven consumer spending, contributing to the growth slowdown during the quarter.
