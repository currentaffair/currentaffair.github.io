Failing PDS Beneficiaries in Odisha

Key Issues

Three women in Odisha died after consuming toxic mango kernel gruel, highlighting issues with the Public Distribution System (PDS) in the state.

Relevance

This issue is relevant to GS 1 (Society, Poverty).

Challenges

  • PDS Delays: Digitization and e-KYC mandates have caused delays in food distribution.
  • Hunger Deaths: Hunger-related deaths, particularly in marginalized Adivasi communities, are recurring.
  • Impact on Women: High anemia rates (49%) due to poor nutrition.
  • Accessibility: Remote areas lack proper PDS infrastructure.
  • Policy Issues: Welfare schemes depend on universal mobile and Aadhaar access, which is unfeasible in rural areas.

PDS Scheme Overview

  • Purpose: To provide food grains and essential commodities at affordable prices to the poor.
  • Operational Responsibility: Joint responsibility of the Central and State Governments.
  • Central Government Role: Procurement, storage, transportation, and bulk allocation through the Food Corporation of India (FCI).
  • State Government Role: Allocation within the state, identification of eligible families, issue of ration cards, and supervision of Fair Price Shops (FPSs).
