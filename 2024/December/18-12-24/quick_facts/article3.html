Added Sugar in Nestlé Baby Products - Compliance

Context

The Union Health Minister clarified that the added sugar in Nestlé baby products complies with the Food Safety and Standards (Foods for Infant Nutrition) Regulations, 2020. It adheres to global sugar limits.

Details

The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) took action based on a Swiss NGO's report, conducting inspections in April 2024. The inspections confirmed compliance with the 2020 regulations.

FSSAI & Food Safety Regulations

  • FSSAI Role: Developed under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, FSSAI ensures food safety across manufacturing, storage, and distribution.
  • Infant Nutrition Regulations: These guidelines ensure safety and nutritional standards for food intended for infants under two years old, prohibiting harmful additives.

Key Regulations & Safeguards

  • Approval and Monitoring: Manufacturers must obtain FSSAI approval; products undergo batch-wise sampling.
  • Labeling & Advertising: Clear preparation instructions and warnings must be on labels, and misleading advertisements are prohibited.

Importance of Food Safety

Food safety regulations safeguard public health, ensuring infants receive adequate nutrition, and help reduce malnutrition and health risks.
