Temple Bodies Challenge HC Directive on Use of Elephants

Context

The Kerala High Court ruled that using elephants in festivals like Thrissur Pooram is not an “essential religious practice,” challenging the tradition of elephant parades.

Details

Elephant parades have long been a cultural and religious symbol at Thrissur Pooram, central to devotion and heritage for over 200 years.

Ethical & Legal Concerns

  • Ethical Issues: Critics point to mistreatment of elephants and safety risks for both animals and the public.
  • Legal Implications: Animal rights laws call for a reevaluation of such traditional practices, raising the question of ethical standards in public festivals.

Conclusion

The case highlights the need to balance cultural preservation with animal welfare and public safety. A compromise is possible, allowing both traditions and ethical standards to coexist.
