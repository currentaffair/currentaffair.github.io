Composite License for Insurer

The central government proposed introducing composite licenses through an amendment to the Insurance Act, 1938, to boost insurance penetration in India and aim for "Insurance for All by 2047".

What is a Composite License?

A composite license allows insurers to offer life and non-life insurance under a single registration, reducing the need for separate licenses for each line of business.

Benefits of Composite License

  • Reduces costs and compliance, enhancing operational efficiency.
  • Life insurers can offer indemnity-based health insurance, improving underwriting.
  • Agents can sell both life and non-life products to meet customer needs better.

Regulatory Changes Needed

PSU insurers must amend the Life Insurance Corporation (LIC) Act of 1956 and the General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act (GIBNA) of 1972 to qualify for composite licenses.
