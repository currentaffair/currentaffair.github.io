White-Winged Wood Duck and Conservation

About the White-Winged Wood Duck

The White-Winged Wood Duck (Asarcornis scutulata), also known as 'Deo Hans' or Spirit Duck in Assamese, is the state bird of Assam, India. It is found in protected areas like Nameri Tiger Reserve and Dibru-Saikhowa National Park.

Features of the White-Winged Wood Duck

  • Appearance: Black body, white head with black spots, white-spotted wings, and red/orange eyes.
  • Size: Average length of 81 cm, males larger with more sheen.
  • Diet: Omnivorous, feeding on a variety of plant and animal matter.
  • Activity Pattern: Crepuscular (active at dusk and dawn).

Major Threats

  • Habitat Loss: Destruction of wetlands and forests.
  • Nesting Habitat Loss: Loss of swampy areas with large trees.
  • Poaching: Direct threats from poaching of birds and eggs.

Conservation Status

The White-Winged Wood Duck is listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List, with around 800 individuals remaining globally, mostly in Assam, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.

National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries of Assam

  • Kaziranga National Park: UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to the Great Indian One-Horned Rhinoceros.
  • Manas National Park: Known for tigers and elephants, also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Orang National Park: Known for rhino conservation.
  • Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary: High density of the Indian One-Horned Rhinoceros.
  • Nameri National Park: Important bird-watching site.
  • Dibru-Saikhowa National Park: Wetland ecosystem, home to the White-Winged Duck.
  • Dehing Patkai Wildlife Sanctuary: Part of the Eastern Himalaya biodiversity hotspot.
  • Raimona National Park: Recently declared, focused on tiger and elephant conservation.
