MGNREGA Wages and Inflation

Key Issues with MGNREGA Wages

The House panel has recommended revising MGNREGA wages to reflect rising inflation. Current wages are inadequate to meet the increasing cost of living, which has discouraged participation in the scheme.

Regional Disparity in Wages

Wages vary significantly, ranging from ₹234 in Nagaland to ₹374 in Haryana. These discrepancies are partly due to outdated base rates, which are based on 2019 values or ₹100 minimum, whichever is higher.

Worker Concerns

Workers have raised concerns over the fairness and timeliness of wages, demanding better pay to sustain the objectives of MGNREGA.

About MGNREGA

MGNREGA is a 2005 law aimed at providing 100 days of unskilled employment to rural households, ensuring a minimum wage. The Act includes provisions for unemployment allowance, social audits, and decentralized planning through Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs).
