New Species of Darwin Wasp Discovered

Introduction

Researchers from the Ashoka Trust for Research in Ecology and the Environment (ATREE) and collaborators have identified five new species of Darwin wasp subfamily Microleptinae from India and Thailand.

About the Darwin Wasp

The Darwin wasp is a parasitoid that lays its eggs on or in the bodies of other arthropods, such as moths, butterflies, beetles, ants, bees, and wasps. These wasps have a characteristic structure with six legs, large compound eyes, three ocelli, and longer antennae with multiple segments. Adults typically feed on nectar and pollen and are solitary, except for mating.

Ecological Significance

Darwin wasps play a critical role in controlling populations of crop-damaging pests like aphids and caterpillars, offering a natural form of pest management without causing environmental contamination.

Newly Discovered Species

  • Microleptes gowrishankari - Found in Biligiri Ranganathaswamy Temple Tiger Reserve, Karnataka.
  • Microleptes sandeshkaduri - Found in Siang Valley, Arunachal Pradesh.
  • Microleptes chiani - Found in Kalakad Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve, Tamil Nadu.
  • Microleptes tehriensis - Found in Tehri, Uttarakhand.

About ATREE

The Ashoka Trust for Research in Ecology and the Environment (ATREE) is a non-profit organization focused on environmental protection and sustainable development. ATREE emphasizes the creation and dissemination of interdisciplinary knowledge to support local communities, policymakers, and the public, while also building capacity for future scholars and leaders.
