Jalvahak Scheme

Objective

The Jalvahak scheme aims to promote inland waterways and cargo movement by unlocking the trade potential of waterways, reducing logistics costs, and alleviating congestion in road and rail networks.

Incentives

  • Reimburses up to 35% of operating expenses for cargo movement on National Waterways 1 (Ganga), 2 (Brahmaputra), and 16 (Barak) via the Indo-Bangladesh Protocol Route.
  • Encourages the hiring of vessels owned by private operators, promoting competition and efficiency.

Economic and Environmental Impact

The scheme aims to shift 800 million tonne-kilometres of cargo by 2027 and increase cargo movement from 132.89 million tonnes (2023-24) to 200 million tonnes by 2030, and 500 million tonnes by 2047, supporting the Blue Economy and Atma Nirbhar Bharat initiatives.

Inland Waterways Authority of India (IWAI)

The IWAI was established in 1986 to regulate and develop inland waterways. India has 14,500 km of navigable waterways, including rivers, canals, and backwaters. The National Waterways Act, 2016 declared 111 waterways as National Waterways.
