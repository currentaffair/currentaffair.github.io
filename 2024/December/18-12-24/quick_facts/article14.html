DRI Authority and MFN Status Revocation

Delhi High Court and DRI Officer's Authority

The Delhi High Court noted a "flux" in the legal position regarding DRI officers' authority to issue show-cause notices and recover duties under the Customs Act of 1962. This poses a challenge for enforcement of the Customs Act, particularly in preventing duty evasion and smuggling.

About the Directorate of Revenue Intelligence (DRI)

Founded in 1957, DRI is India's principal anti-smuggling and intelligence agency. It is responsible for enforcing various acts, including the Customs Act of 1962, to prevent smuggling, fraud, and tax evasion.

Key Functions of DRI

  • Detecting illegal smuggling (e.g., drugs and wildlife trafficking)
  • Enforcing the Customs Act and related laws
  • Investigating international trade fraud and duty evasion
  • Sharing intelligence on smuggling trends and seizures

Recent Issue on DRI Officer's Powers

The Delhi High Court's observation on the DRI's authority raises concerns about its enforcement actions and the ability of officers to combat smuggling and duty evasion effectively.

Switzerland's Revocation of India's MFN Status

Switzerland revoked India's "Most Favoured Nation" (MFN) status following an Indian Supreme Court ruling in 2023. This decision affects India's tax treaties and will lead to higher withholding taxes for dividends from Swiss entities starting January 2025.

OECD and the MFN Clause

The MFN clause ensures trade benefits extended by one country are granted to all others in similar agreements. Switzerland's move complicates India-Switzerland relations and introduces challenges for Indian companies operating in Switzerland.
