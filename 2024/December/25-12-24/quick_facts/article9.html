Murder Hornets (Vespa mandarinia)

About the Murder Hornet

The Murder Hornet is the largest hornet in the world, reaching up to 2 inches in length. It poses a threat to native pollinators, especially honeybees.

Key Facts

  • Habitat: Prefers lower altitude forests, avoiding large plains and high mountains.
  • Distribution: Native to China, Korea, Japan, and India, but invasive in North America.
  • Venom: Delivers venom almost 7 times more potent than a honeybee's sting.
  • Diet: Feeds on native insects, including bees, butterflies, and wasps.

Why "Murder Hornets"?

They were first reported in Washington in 2019, and their ability to destroy honeybee colonies raised concerns about the impact on agriculture and the economy.

Threat to Humans

Murder Hornets usually don't attack unless threatened but can sting repeatedly. The sting is highly toxic, potentially causing anaphylaxis and severe pain. They are responsible for several fatalities in Asia each year.
