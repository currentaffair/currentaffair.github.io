Never Events and Opinion Trading in India

Never Events: A Healthcare Concern

Never events refer to preventable incidents in healthcare, such as wrong-site surgeries or mismatched blood transfusions. These events are serious and can result in severe harm, disability, or death.

Characteristics of Never Events

  • Serious, preventable incidents
  • Common examples include wrong-site surgeries and lethal medication doses
  • Often linked with "sentinel events" or "serious reportable events"

Organizations Addressing Never Events

  • NHS (UK) lists 16 never events
  • United States recognizes 29 events
  • Cigna and Leapfrog Group emphasize transparency and accountability

ALARP Principle

The ALARP (As Low As Reasonably Practicable) principle helps minimize risks of never events, acknowledging that absolute prevention may not be feasible due to socio-technical complexities.

The Indian Context: Medical Negligence

In India, "Never Events" is not explicitly adopted. Instead, medical issues are categorized under medical negligence, including acts of commission (e.g., incorrect medication) and omission (e.g., neglect of surgical material).

Opinion Trading: A Growing Industry in India

Opinion trading platforms in India, like Probo and MPL, allow users to bet on future outcomes (e.g., sports scores, elections). The sector is growing with over 5 crore users and large investments.

Legal Challenges for Opinion Trading Platforms

Opinion trading platforms face legal ambiguity, particularly around whether they should be classified as skill-based or chance-based games. This uncertainty complicates regulation and user protection.

Way Forward for Opinion Trading

India can learn from the regulatory frameworks in countries like the U.S. and Australia. A comprehensive law governing opinion trading platforms is needed to ensure user safety and industry transparency.
