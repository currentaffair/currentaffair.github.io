Helmeted Water Toad & US Territorial Acquisitions

Helmeted Water Toad (Calyptocephalella gayi)

Known as the "living fossil", this large frog has survived since the time of the dinosaurs. It's one of the largest frogs, growing over 30 cm in length and weighing up to 1 kg.

Key Facts

  • Appearance: Broad head, big mouth, color changes from yellow, green, brown to gray.
  • Behavior: Aggressive towards predators.
  • Habitat: Found in cool ponds and wetlands in central Chile.
  • Protection Status: Vulnerable due to climate change and habitat destruction.

What is a Living Fossil?

A "living fossil" is a species that has survived with minimal change over millions of years, resembling ancient species.

US Territorial Acquisitions

US President-elect Donald Trump expressed interest in purchasing Greenland, continuing US ambitions in strategic regions. Past acquisitions include:

  • Louisiana Purchase (1803): Doubled US size.
  • Alaska Purchase (1867): Increased strategic importance post-Klondike Gold Rush.

Greenland Facts

  • World's largest island, part of Denmark.
  • Rich in natural resources: coal, gold, copper, etc.
