Opinion Trading and Protected Area Permits (PAP) in India

What is Opinion Trading?

Opinion trading platforms allow users to bet real money on future events like sports scores, elections, or cryptocurrency trends. Popular platforms such as Probo and MPL offer these services, with Probo covering various industries and MPL focusing on cricket predictions.

Growth of Opinion Trading

Opinion trading has gained traction in India, with over 5 crore users and transaction volumes exceeding Rs 50,000 crore. Major investors include Sequoia Capital and Y Combinator. Platforms leverage advertising on Google and Meta for user acquisition, with expected revenues surpassing Rs 1,000 crore in 2024-25.

Legal Challenges in India

The main legal challenge for opinion trading platforms is the lack of clear regulations. The ambiguity between skill and chance games raises concerns about user safety and fairness. There is no dedicated law for these platforms, creating a legal grey area.

PAP (Protected Area Permit) in India

The PAP is a special permit for foreign nationals to visit sensitive areas near India's international borders. Areas like Manipur, Mizoram, and Nagaland require a PAP due to security concerns, reintroduced in 2024 after being relaxed in 2010.

Legal Framework and Implementation of PAP

The PAP is governed by the Foreigners (Protected Areas) Order, 1958. Foreign nationals must apply for a PAP through the Ministry of Home Affairs. Certain exemptions exist for countries like Bhutan and India citizens.
