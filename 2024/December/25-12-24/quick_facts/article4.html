Fiscal Consolidation and Tobacco Taxation in India

State Fiscal Health and Progress

The RBI report indicates state governments have made good progress in fiscal consolidation, maintaining gross fiscal deficits under 3% of GDP for three consecutive years (2021-2024). Revenue deficits have also remained low, allowing increased capital spending and improved expenditure quality.

Case Study: Delhi Government's Welfare vs. Fiscal Health

  • Delhi's budget has grown from ₹30,940 crores (2014-15) to ₹76,000 crores (2024-25).
  • GSDP increased from ₹4.95 lakh crores to ₹11.8 lakh crores during the same period.
  • Debt-to-GDP ratio dropped from 6.4% (2013-14) to 3.9% (2023), one of India's lowest.
  • Per capita income in Delhi grew from ₹2.47 lakh to ₹4.62 lakh between 2014-2024.

Subsidy Expenditure and Fiscal Health

The RBI report stresses the importance of rationalizing subsidy expenditures. Excessive subsidies may limit resources for vital sectors like infrastructure, education, and healthcare, which are key for long-term growth.

State Debt Status

State debt as a percentage of GDP has decreased from 31% (2021) to 28.5% (2024), but it remains above pre-pandemic levels. The RBI emphasizes fiscal consolidation to ensure long-term financial stability.

Recommendations for Improving State Finances

  • Prioritize sectors like education, healthcare, and infrastructure over subsidies.
  • Adopt clear strategies to reduce debt levels in line with FRBM guidelines.
  • Implement outcome budgeting for better accountability and impact measurement.
  • Include climate action in fiscal plans for sustainable growth.
  • Improve fiscal data transparency to enhance risk assessment and reduce borrowing costs.

Tobacco Taxation for Public Health

The government is considering reducing GST rates on essentials and raising taxes on tobacco to address public health concerns, given tobacco's significant harm in India.

Benefits of Higher Tobacco Taxes

  • Higher taxes discourage new users and encourage quitting, especially among young people and low-income groups.
  • Generate revenue for public welfare programs like healthcare and education.

Challenges in Tobacco Taxation

  • Different tax rates for tobacco products create substitution opportunities, particularly with bidis.
  • Taxes must be adjusted regularly to keep pace with inflation and rising incomes.
  • Bidis and smokeless tobacco should face the same tax rates as other tobacco products.

Proposed Reforms

  • Implement uniform tax rates across all tobacco products, including bidis and smokeless tobacco.
  • Regularly adjust taxes to reflect inflation and rising incomes.
  • Raise tobacco taxes to reduce consumption and improve public health.
