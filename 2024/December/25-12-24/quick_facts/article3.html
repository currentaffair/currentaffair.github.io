Lokayukta: Structure, Jurisdiction, and Investigations

Context: Karnataka High Court and the MUDA Scam

The Karnataka High Court stayed the Lokayukta investigation into the alleged MUDA scam.

About Lokayukta

Lokayukta, like Lokpal, is an anti-corruption body investigating public-sector corruption. Lokpal handles national matters, and Lokayukta deals with state-level corruption.

Established through the Lokpal and Lokayukta Act of 2013, it has been operational in 21 states and one union territory (Delhi).

State Variations and Structure

  • Lokayukta has different structures across states, with some states also having an Upalokayukta.
  • States like Rajasthan, Karnataka, and Maharashtra have both Lokayukta and Upalokayukta, while others only have one.

Appointment and Qualifications

  • The state governor appoints Lokayukta and Upalokayukta, often consulting the chief justice and opposition leader.
  • In states like Uttar Pradesh and Gujarat, judicial qualifications are required for Lokayukta appointments.

Tenure and Jurisdiction

  • Lokayuktas usually serve for 5 years or until they reach 65 years old. Reappointment is not allowed.
  • The jurisdiction varies: In some states, Lokayukta oversees ministers and public servants, including chief ministers in certain states.

Investigations and Powers

  • Lokayuktas investigate complaints or take action on their own initiative in most states.
  • In some states, such as Uttar Pradesh and Assam, Lokayukta lacks independent investigation powers.

Key Points on Lokayukta's Role

  • The Lokayukta reports annually to the governor, who presents the findings to the state legislature.
  • Recommendations by Lokayukta are advisory and non-binding.
