Siliguri Corridor and Extradition Request for Sheikh Hasina

Siliguri Corridor: Geopolitical Importance

The Siliguri Corridor, also known as the Chicken's Neck, is a 22 km narrow stretch connecting Northeast India with the rest of the country. It borders Nepal, Bhutan, and Bangladesh, making it strategically and economically significant.

Strategic Significance

  • Vital land link for defense and mobility of armed forces
  • Economic importance for trade and connectivity with neighboring countries
  • Vulnerable to blockades, making it critical for national security

Need for Security

  • Strategic connectivity could be disrupted, isolating the northeastern states
  • Close to sensitive borders with China, Nepal, and Bangladesh
  • Ensures the free movement of Indian forces and supplies in case of conflict

Enhancing the Corridor's Potential

  • Improved connectivity with neighboring countries could make the region an economic and transport hub
  • Focus on industrialization, medical hubs, and educational growth in the region
  • Ongoing development projects, such as Bagdogra Airport upgrade, will boost tourism

Extradition Request for Sheikh Hasina

India received a note verbale from Bangladesh to extradite former Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina under the 2013 extradition treaty. The treaty was amended in 2016 to simplify the process.

Key Articles of the Extradition Treaty

  • Article 6: Extradition may be refused if the offence is political in nature.
  • Article 8: A person cannot be extradited if the accusation isn't made in good faith.
  • Article 10 (3): Simplified extradition process, requiring only an arrest warrant.

Conclusion

The Siliguri Corridor is vital for India's national security, economic growth, and regional connectivity. Monitoring and enhancing this corridor is essential. Additionally, the extradition request for Sheikh Hasina highlights the importance of international legal frameworks in addressing issues between countries.
