India-China Relations: A Path Toward Cooperation

Introduction

The hallmark of mature leaders is their ability to resolve disputes peacefully and avoid escalation into conflict. Even when long-standing disputes trigger tensions, it is essential that dialogue, not warfare, takes precedence. This was seen in the military standoff between India and China, which began in June 2020 following the violent confrontation in the Galwan Valley. Both leaders, Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Chinese President Xi Jinping, demonstrated political maturity by facilitating a disengagement agreement, resulting from patient diplomatic and military talks.

Strategic Determination: The Path Forward

Shun Hostility, Pursue Cooperation

India and China face a strategic crossroads. Will they choose to continue their relationship in the direction of comprehensive cooperation, or will the weight of mutual distrust push them toward increased rivalry and minimal cooperation? A choice to follow the path of confrontation could risk future military clashes, especially as the unresolved boundary dispute remains a source of tension along the Line of Actual Control (LAC).

Strategic choice: The decision between cooperation or confrontation lies in the hands of Mr. Modi and Mr. Xi. Bold new thinking is required from both sides.

China's Role in Trust-Building

Addressing India’s Security Concerns

China must reassure India of its peaceful intentions by taking steps to demonstrate that it does not pose a national security threat, either independently or in alliance with Pakistan. China’s past reluctance to condemn Pakistan-sponsored terrorism has led many Indians to perceive China as unfriendly. Additionally, China should advocate for India’s permanent membership in the United Nations Security Council and treat India as an equal partner in a multipolar world.

India’s Role in Trust-Building

Avoiding the China Containment Strategy

India must resist the temptation to exaggerate the power asymmetry between the two nations. While partnerships like the Quad (India, Japan, Australia, and the United States) signal India’s growing alliance with the U.S., it is essential that India does not let this shift its policies into direct opposition to China. Furthermore, India’s continued commitment to the "One China" policy is paramount, as is refraining from inflating anti-China sentiments within the media.

Historical and Civilizational Context

A Long-Standing Relationship

India and China share a long history of civilization, with cultural, philosophical, and economic exchanges that have contributed to their mutual growth. The wisdom of these two ancient civilizations points toward higher ideals, such as peace, global development, and climate action. Realpolitik must not obscure these ideals, nor should it dictate the future of India-China relations.

Trust-Building Ideas for Future Cooperation

Economic and Technological Synergies

The future of India-China relations lies in partnership, especially in the context of their economic strengths. China’s market offers immense opportunities for India, particularly in areas such as infrastructure and green energy. Conversely, China could benefit from India’s rapidly expanding market and potential in sectors like digital technology and clean energy.

Global South Cooperation

Collaborating for Global Stability

As leading nations of the Global South, India and China can cooperate on a range of issues affecting developing nations in Asia, Africa, and Latin America. Their collaboration could significantly impact global governance and contribute to resolving regional conflicts like the Russia-Ukraine war and instability in Myanmar.

Addressing Common Challenges

Youth Employment and Mobility

Both nations face challenges in creating opportunities for their youth. One initiative, the Bangladesh-China-India-Myanmar (BCIM) Corridor, could provide new prospects for employment and development in India’s northeastern states and support the Act East Policy. Such cooperative efforts, though long-term, would lay the groundwork for a better future.

Five Low-Hanging Fruits for Immediate Action

Immediate Actions for Better Bilateral Relations

  • Restart direct flights suspended due to COVID-19.
  • Ease visa restrictions for businesspeople, students, and tourists.
  • Re-establish journalist exchanges between the two countries.
  • Lifting the ban on Chinese apps in India.
  • Work on balancing the trade deficit by increasing exports from India to China.

Conclusion

The path ahead for India-China relations requires a cautious yet optimistic approach. The two countries must work together to overcome mutual distrust, expand cooperation, and focus on shared challenges. By taking steps toward mutual respect, transparency, and economic collaboration, India and China can build a more peaceful and prosperous future, not just for themselves, but for the entire global community.
