Women Electors Turned Out More Than Men in LS Polls

Overview

The 2024 Lok Sabha elections revealed significant gender differences in voter turnout, with women outnumbering men in several states. The gender gap in turnout has widened from 0.16 points in 2019 to 0.23 points in 2024.

Key Findings

  • Female Turnout Surpassing Male: In 15 out of 28 major states, female turnout was higher than male turnout.
  • States with Higher Female Turnout: Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttarakhand, Arunachal Pradesh, Meghalaya, Manipur, and West Bengal.
  • States with Higher Male Turnout: Gujarat, Maharashtra, Jammu & Kashmir, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka.

Electorate and Migration Influence

  • Impact of Migration: Migration patterns, especially male-dominated migration from Bihar and Jharkhand, contributed to the higher female turnout in these states.
  • Political Factors: Political engagement and mobilization efforts targeting women may have also influenced the higher female turnout.

Implications

These trends highlight the need for further research on migration data and gender-based turnout gaps. Future strategies may focus on increasing voter participation among migrant workers and addressing the gender gap in voter turnout.
