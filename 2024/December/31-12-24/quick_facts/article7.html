Mission SpaDeX: Space Docking Experiment

Mission Overview

Mission SpaDeX aims to demonstrate space docking technology essential for future space missions like satellite servicing, lunar exploration, and interplanetary missions.

Key Details

  • Launch Vehicle: PSLV-C60
  • Launch Date: December 30, 2024, 9:58 p.m.
  • Location: Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota
  • Spacecraft Involved: Chaser (SDX01) and Target (SDX02), both 220 kg each.
  • Orbit: 470 km circular orbit at 55-degree inclination

Mission Phases

  • Initial Phase: Separation of SDX02, followed by SDX01.
  • Far Rendezvous: SDX02 adjusts velocity to match SDX01's orbit.
  • Final Phase: Chaser spacecraft docks with Target in January 2025.

Mission Significance

This mission is crucial for the future of space exploration, enabling lunar, interplanetary, and space station missions, including:

  • Lunar Missions: Sending Indian astronauts to the Moon.
  • Interplanetary Missions: Sample returns from the Moon and Mars.
  • Space Station: Operation of an Indian space station.
