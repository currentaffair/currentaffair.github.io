Women Electors Turned Out More Than Men in LS Polls

Overview

The 2024 Lok Sabha elections saw a notable gender gap in voter turnout, with women voters surpassing men in several states, according to the Election Commission's data.

Key Trends

  • Gender Gap: Female turnout surpassed male turnout by 0.23 points in 2024, up from 0.16 in 2019.
  • Higher Female Turnout: Seen in states like Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttarakhand, Arunachal Pradesh, and West Bengal.
  • Male Dominance: Higher male turnout observed in Gujarat, Maharashtra, Jammu & Kashmir, and Rajasthan.
  • Electoral Composition: States like Bihar saw more registered male electors, but women had a higher turnout.
  • Migration Impact: Male migration from Bihar and Jharkhand affected the turnout, as many males couldn't return to vote.
  • Political Factors: Political mobilization targeting women contributed to higher female voter turnout.

Conclusion

The growing gender gap in voter turnout highlights the influence of migration and political engagement efforts, suggesting areas for future policy focus to improve electoral participation.
