Fighter Jet Generations

Fighter jets are classified into generations based on technological advancements.

What is a "Generation"?

The term refers to a significant leap in fighter technology. It emerged in the 1990s and guides comparisons of fighter capabilities.

Generations of Fighter Jets

First Generation (1943-1955)

Early jet fighters, subsonic, with basic avionics and armed with machine guns. Examples: Messerschmitt Me 262, MiG-15.

Second Generation (1955-1970)

Transonic and supersonic fighters with afterburners, radar, and guided missiles. Examples: MiG-21, F-104 Starfighter.

Third Generation (1960s-1970s)

Multi-role capabilities, advanced radar, and electronic warfare. Examples: F-4 Phantom, MiG-23.

Fourth Generation (1970-2000)

Fly-by-Wire systems, stealth, and advanced avionics. Examples: F-16, Su-30, Rafale.

Fifth Generation (2000-Present)

Stealth, integrated systems, and advanced automation. Examples: F-22, Su-57, J-20.

Sixth Generation (Under Development)

Potential features include hypersonic speeds, AI, and directed energy weapons. Examples: Tempest, FCAS.
