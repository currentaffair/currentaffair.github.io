Bald Eagle: U.S. National Bird

Scientific Information

Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus

Common Name: Bald Eagle

Native Range: North America, from Mexico to Canada, with a significant population in Alaska.

Appearance & Behavior

Appearance: Brown body, white head and tail, yellow beak and feet.

Juvenile Appearance: Mostly brown with white markings on wings and chest.

Sexual Dimorphism: Females are larger but share the same coloration.

Habitat & Diet

Habitat: Found year-round in Alaska, the coasts, Rocky Mountains, and Mississippi River.

Diet: Primarily fish, small birds, rodents, carrion, and opportunistically steals food.

Conservation Status

Current Status: Delisted from Endangered Species Act in 2007. IUCN: Least Concern.

Population Decline

Once abundant, the bald eagle population declined due to hunting, DDT usage, and habitat destruction. The population reached near-extinction levels by the early 20th century.

Efforts for Recovery

Important efforts included banning DDT in 1972, enacting the Endangered Species Act in 1973, and launching captive breeding programs with a method called "hacking."
