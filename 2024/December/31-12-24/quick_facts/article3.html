Kaveri Engine Development

Context

India's Kaveri engine marks a major step in indigenous aerospace development, approved for in-flight testing.

Kaveri Engine Features

  • Thrust: Produces 49-51 kN thrust, with plans for 73-75 kN with afterburner.
  • Engine Type: Turbofan engine designed for fighter jets (Tejas) and UAVs (Ghatak).
  • Development Timeline: Over three decades of research by DRDO and GTRE.

Testing and Development

  • Ground and high-altitude tests conducted in India and Russia, showing reliability and performance.
  • Ghatak UAV will use the engine for stealth operations and internal weaponry.

Challenges and Significance

  • Delayed due to complex jet engine technology.
  • Enhances India's aerospace autonomy, reducing dependence on foreign engines.
  • Boosts the domestic aerospace industry, supporting local manufacturers and export potential.

Way Forward

  • Focus on rigorous testing, expand operational scenarios, and leverage AI optimization.
  • Prepare for mass production and establish manufacturing hubs in India.

Types of Engines

Engines convert energy into mechanical work and are classified based on combustion location, design, fuel type, ignition method, and cylinder arrangement.
