Needle-Free Shock Syringe

Introduction

Researchers at IIT Bombay have developed a shockwave-based, needle-free syringe that ensures painless drug delivery, reducing skin damage and infection risks.

How It Works

The shock syringe uses high-energy shockwaves to deliver drugs through the skin, offering a painless alternative to traditional injections. The device consists of three parts: driver, driven, and drug holder, which create a microjet for drug delivery.

Technology Behind It

The syringe uses a micro shock tube and pressurized nitrogen gas to generate a liquid microjet that travels faster than sound to penetrate the skin.

Test Results

The shock syringe was tested on rats, showing deeper tissue penetration and minimal skin damage compared to regular needles.

Potential Benefits

This technology could speed up immunization efforts like Mission Indradhanush and reduce the risks of bloodborne diseases from needle-stick injuries.

Cost and Durability

The shock syringe is cost-effective, designed for over 1000 uses with only nozzle replacements needed.
