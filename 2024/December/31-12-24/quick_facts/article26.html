Kashmir's Papier Mache Revives the Dodo

In Srinagar, papier mache artisans are crafting and exporting large quantities of dodo figurines, primarily to Mauritius and Europe around Christmas 2024.

Dodo

The dodo (Raphus cucullatus) is an extinct bird that was native to the island of Mauritius, which became extinct in 1681 due to human activity and invasive species.

Papier Mache Art

Papier mache, a 600-year-old Kashmiri handicraft, uses paper pulp to create intricate objects. It originated under King Zain-ul-Abidin in the 15th century and has been influenced by Persian motifs. Recently, dodo figures were introduced into the craft.

Other Crafts of Kashmir

Kashmir is also known for other crafts such as carpets, pashmina, sozni, and shawls, many of which have received Geographical Indication (GI) tags.
