India’s Obligations Towards the Rohingya

The plight of Rohingya refugees in India highlights major violations of constitutional and human rights. The refugees, fleeing persecution in Myanmar, face detention and mistreatment under India's domestic laws.

International Protections

Though India is not a signatory to the 1951 Refugee Convention, it is bound by international obligations such as non-refoulement, preventing the deportation of individuals to harm. India’s adherence to the ICCPR (International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights) remains crucial.

India’s Legal Position

Rohingya refugees are classified as “illegal migrants” under Indian law, facing obstacles like the Citizenship Amendment Act (2019) and lack of a standard refugee policy.

Living Conditions and Policy Gaps

Many Rohingya refugees live in detention centers under inhumane conditions. India’s handling of the refugees underscores the need for a comprehensive refugee law to ensure fair treatment and adherence to international standards.
