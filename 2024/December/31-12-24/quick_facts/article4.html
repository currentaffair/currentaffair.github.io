Needle-Free Shock Syringe

Overview

Researchers at IIT Bombay developed a shockwave-based, needle-free syringe that delivers drugs painlessly, reducing skin damage and infection risks.

How It Works

  • Shockwave Technology: Uses high-energy shockwaves to penetrate the skin without needles.
  • Shockwave: A pressure wave moving faster than sound through air or solids.
  • Components: Driver, driven, and drug holder create a shockwave-driven microjet for drug delivery.
  • Micro Shock Tube: Uses pressurized nitrogen gas to create a liquid drug jet that penetrates the skin.

Benefits

  • Painless: No needles, reducing skin damage and pain.
  • Effective: Demonstrated deeper tissue penetration with minimal damage in rats.
  • Cost-effective: Designed for 1000+ uses with only nozzle replacements needed.
  • Improves Immunization: Can accelerate immunization drives like Mission Indradhanush (MI) and reduce needle-stick injury risks.
