Judicial Accountability in India

The recent controversy surrounding Justice Shekhar Kumar Yadav's speech highlights issues of judicial accountability in India.

Judicial Review Mechanism

The Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968 outlines the process for holding judges accountable for misbehavior or incapacity, involving a committee with a Supreme Court judge, Chief Justice, and an eminent jurist.

Impeachment Process

A two-thirds majority vote in Parliament is required for a judge to be removed, but this process has had limited success in ensuring accountability.

Past Cases

Past cases such as Justice V. Ramaswami and Justice Soumitra Sen show failures in holding judges accountable, as judges can resign to avoid consequences.

Resignation Loophole

Judges' ability to resign before impeachment prevents accountability, unlike elected officials who cannot escape accountability through resignation.

Forum for Judicial Accountability (FJA) Stance

The FJA advocates for investigations to continue even if a judge resigns, arguing that investigations are vital for maintaining public trust.

Institutional Challenges

Despite calls for reform, the Rajya Sabha Chairperson, Hamid Ansari, rejected continuing investigations after a judge's resignation, reflecting institutional challenges in reforming judicial accountability.
