Private Members' Bills in Parliament: Minimal Engagement

Overview

An analysis by PRS Legislative Research reveals that minimal time is allocated to Private Members' Bills in both the 17th and 18th Lok Sabha sessions, reflecting a lack of attention to this parliamentary instrument.

Time Spent on Private Members' Bills (2019–2024)

  • 17th Lok Sabha: 9.08 hours in Lok Sabha, 27.01 hours in Rajya Sabha.
  • 18th Lok Sabha: Only 0.15 hours in Lok Sabha, 0.62 hours in Rajya Sabha.

Significance

Private Members' Bills allow individual MPs to propose legislation and express their views. They have historically played a role in political messaging and reform, such as the 1966 Bill proposing eligibility criteria for the Prime Minister’s post.

Reasons for Decline

  • Lack of seriousness and attendance by MPs.
  • Indifference towards the tool, as pointed out by Jairam Ramesh, Chief Whip of the Congress in the Rajya Sabha.

Implications

The decline in attention to Private Members' Bills reflects a weakening of parliamentary tools, limiting opportunities for political innovation and legislative reform.
