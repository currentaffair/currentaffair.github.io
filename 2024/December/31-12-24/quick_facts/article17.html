Parker Solar Probe's Historic Flyby

The Parker Solar Probe flew closer to the Sun than any spacecraft, reaching 6.1 million km on December 24, 2023.

Scientific Mission

Launch Date: August 2018 (7-year mission)

Objective: Study the Sun's corona and improve space-weather predictions.

Flyby Details

Flyby Date: December 24, 5:23 AM IST

Temperature: Over 930°C, spacecraft protected by advanced heat shield, instruments stayed at ~29°C.

Unique Capabilities

Speed: 690,000 km/h, covering New Delhi to Chennai in 10 seconds.

Orbit Adjustment: Uses Venus flybys to tighten orbit around the Sun.

Scientific Goals

Study Topics: Origin of solar winds, Sun's corona temperature, and formation of CMEs.

Future Plans

Upcoming Flybys: March 22, 2025 and June 19, 2025.

Significance for Space Science

The Parker Solar Probe advances understanding of solar phenomena and their impact on Earth.
