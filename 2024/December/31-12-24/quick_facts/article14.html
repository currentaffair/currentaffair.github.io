Financial System Stability - RBI Report 2024

Key Highlights

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) reports a stable financial system in 2024, supported by improved asset quality, strong capital buffers, and shifting deposit preferences due to rising interest rates.

Asset Quality

  • GNPA Ratio: Declined to a 12-year low of 2.6% (Sept 2024).
  • NNPA Ratio: Stable at 0.6%.
  • Provisioning Coverage Ratio (PCR): Increased to 77%, driven by proactive provisioning, especially by Public Sector Banks (PSBs).

Profitability Trends

  • Positive Trends: Improved Return on Assets (ROA), Earnings before provisions and taxes increased.
  • Challenges: Decline in Net Interest Margin (NIM), shift in deposits to higher interest rate categories.

Banking Resilience

Resilience is supported by strong capital buffers, robust earnings, and improved asset quality.

Deposit Profile Shift

  • Decline in CASA deposits in favor of higher-interest term deposits.
  • Moderate growth in loans and deposits during H1 2024.

Implications & Outlook

  • Banking Sector Stability: Strong fundamentals enhance resilience against economic shocks.
  • Asset Quality: Reduced NPAs and proactive provisioning improve investor confidence.
  • Deposit Behavior: Rising interest rates alter deposit preferences, affecting bank margins.
  • Outlook: Focus on maintaining capital buffers and adapting to evolving deposit dynamics.
