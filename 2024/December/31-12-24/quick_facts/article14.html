ISRO’s SpaDeX Mission

ISRO's Space Docking Experiment (SpaDeX) mission is set to launch on December 30, 2024, with the goal of demonstrating satellite docking capabilities, a key technology for future space missions.

About SpaDeX

SpaDeX (Space Docking Experiment) is a technology demonstrator mission to show in-space docking technology. The mission involves two spacecraft: SDX01 (Chaser) and SDX02 (Target), which will dock autonomously in low Earth orbit.

Mission Details

  • Objective: To develop docking technologies for SDX01 and SDX02 in low Earth orbit, using advanced sensors and propulsion systems.
  • Duration: 2 years.
  • Docking Mechanism: The spacecraft will use the Bharatiya Docking System (BDS), allowing flexible and precise docking for future space operations.
  • Docking Challenge: The spacecraft must reduce their relative speed to just 0.036 km/h to dock.

Why Space Docking Matters

Space docking is crucial for assembling space stations, transferring supplies, and supporting future missions like lunar exploration and Mars missions. It also plays a vital role in satellite servicing and the planned Bharatiya Antariksh Station (BAS).

Significance for India

  • Modular Infrastructure: Docking is essential for building modular space stations and interplanetary missions.
  • Human Spaceflight: Space docking is key for crew transfers during missions like Gaganyaan.
  • Global Standing: Successful docking will position India alongside major space powers like Russia, the US, and China.
