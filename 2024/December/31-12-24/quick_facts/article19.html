Judicial Accountability and Controversy

The recent controversy over Justice Shekhar Kumar Yadav's speech has sparked debate over judicial accountability in India.

Judicial Review and Accountability

The Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968 outlines the process for holding judges accountable for misbehavior or incapacity. A committee investigates the issue after a successful impeachment motion in Parliament.

Impeachment Process

A judge can only be removed with a two-thirds majority vote in Parliament, but the process has been unsuccessful in many cases.

Past Cases

Justice V. Ramaswami

Found guilty of extravagant spending but remained in office due to the failure of impeachment in the Lok Sabha.

Justice Soumitra Sen

Resigned before the impeachment motion was introduced after being found guilty of misappropriating funds.

Resignation Loophole

Judges can resign before impeachment, avoiding accountability, unlike elected officials who cannot evade responsibility by resigning.

Forum for Judicial Accountability (FJA) Stance

The FJA advocates for continuing investigations even after a judge's resignation to maintain public trust in the judiciary.
