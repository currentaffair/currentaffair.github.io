Parker Solar Probe - Closest Flyby to the Sun

Scientific Mission Overview

Launch Date: August 2018

Objective: Explore the Sun’s corona and improve space-weather predictions impacting Earth.

Recent Flyby Details

Flyby Date: December 24, 5:23 AM IST

Closest Approach: 6.1 million km from the Sun, facing temperatures over 930°C.

Temperature Inside: ~29°C, thanks to an advanced heat shield.

Unique Capabilities

Speed: 690,000 km/h, capable of covering the distance from New Delhi to Chennai in 10 seconds.

Orbit Adjustment: Uses Venus flybys to tighten its orbit around the Sun.

Scientific Goals

  • Study the origin of solar winds.
  • Understand why the Sun’s corona is hotter than its surface.
  • Explore the formation of coronal mass ejections (CMEs).

Future Plans

Upcoming Flybys: March 22, 2025 and June 19, 2025.

Significance for Space Science

The Parker Solar Probe represents bold exploration, advancing our understanding of solar phenomena and their effects on Earth.
