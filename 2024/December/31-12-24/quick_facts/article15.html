Private Members’ Bills Get Short Shrift in Parliament

An analysis by PRS Legislative Research reveals minimal time spent on private members’ bills in the 17th and early 18th Lok Sabha sessions, showing a lack of engagement with this legislative tool.

Time Spent on Private Members’ Bills (2019–2024)

17th Lok Sabha: 9.08 hours in the Lok Sabha, 27.01 hours in the Rajya Sabha.

18th Lok Sabha: Significant decline—0.15 hours in the Lok Sabha and 0.62 hours in the Rajya Sabha.

Significance of Private Members’ Bills and Resolutions

Private Members' Bills offer MPs a chance to propose legislation and express views independently. They have historically been used for political messaging (e.g., 1966 Bill on Prime Minister’s eligibility criteria). However, only 14 Bills have passed since independence, with none since 1970.

Reasons for Decline

Lack of seriousness: MPs often neglect sessions where their Bills are listed.

Indifference: The decline is further emphasized by Jairam Ramesh, Congress’ Chief Whip in Rajya Sabha.

Implications

The decline of Private Members' Bills signifies a weakening of parliamentary tools for individual expression and a missed opportunity for political and legislative innovation.
