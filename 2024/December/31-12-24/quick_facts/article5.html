Tiger Translocation and Conservation Efforts

Context

Chief Minister Mohan Yadav has ordered the translocation of 15 tigers from Madhya Pradesh to Rajasthan, Chhattisgarh, and Odisha to enhance tiger conservation efforts.

Interstate Tiger Translocation

Translocation aims to relocate tigers to prevent overpopulation, reduce human-wildlife conflict, and ensure genetic diversity across tiger populations. It also focuses on restoring habitats and boosting the ecological balance.

Role of NTCA

The National Tiger Conservation Authority (NTCA) coordinates and supervises translocation projects, ensuring adherence to scientific methods for success.

Challenges Faced

The first interstate tiger translocation project in Satkosia Tiger Reserve (Odisha) faced failure due to poor community engagement, unsuitable habitat, and project management issues.

Lessons Learned

  • Conduct thorough habitat assessments.
  • Engage local communities and offer compensation schemes.
  • Implement rigorous monitoring and adapt strategies dynamically.
  • Enhance national coordination for smoother project execution.

Benefits of Tiger Relocation

Restores ecological balance, increases biodiversity, and mitigates human-tiger conflicts by reducing overcrowding in reserves and promoting regeneration of landscapes.

Tiger Reserves in Madhya Pradesh

  • Kanha Tiger Reserve: Known for Bengal tigers and Barasingha conservation.
  • Bandhavgarh Tiger Reserve: Home to the highest density of Bengal tigers.
  • Pench Tiger Reserve: Famous for diverse fauna and historical significance.
