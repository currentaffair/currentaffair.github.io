Supreme Court Ruling: Mental Cruelty as Grounds for Divorce

Key Points of the Judgment

The Supreme Court ruled that behavior causing emotional distress to a spouse is considered mental cruelty and a valid ground for divorce.

Case Overview

A husband sought divorce citing his wife’s actions, such as leaving the home and filing criminal complaints, which caused mental cruelty.

The Madras High Court granted the divorce, and the Supreme Court upheld this decision, confirming that sustained emotional torment and loss of trust in marriage can constitute cruelty.

Supreme Court Judgment Highlights

  • Marriage becomes a legal formality when trust and companionship are absent.
  • The husband was directed to pay ₹50 lakh as permanent alimony.
  • The financial independence of the wife does not negate her right to maintenance for dignity and stability post-divorce.
