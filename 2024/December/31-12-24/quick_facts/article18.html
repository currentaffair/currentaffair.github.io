Bald Eagle: America's National Bird

The bald eagle, symbolizing America, has been confirmed as the national bird by President Biden.

Scientific Overview

Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus

Common Name: Bald Eagle

Native Range: Exclusively in North America, from Mexico to Canada

Physical Characteristics

Appearance: Brown body and wings, white head and tail, yellow beak and feet.

Juvenile Appearance: Mostly brown with white markings.

Sexual Dimorphism: Females are larger than males.

Conservation and Recovery

Conservation Status: Delisted from the Endangered Species Act in 2007.

Diet: Primarily fish, small birds, and carrion.

Social Structure: Solitary, monogamous.

Population Decline & Recovery Efforts

Decline Reasons: Hunting, DDT, and habitat loss.

Key Recovery Steps: Banning DDT (1972), Endangered Species Act (1973), captive breeding programs, and habitat protection.

Restoration Methods

Hacking: A method of raising and releasing eagles from artificial nests to restore populations.
