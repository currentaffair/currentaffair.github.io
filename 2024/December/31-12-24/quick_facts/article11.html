Increased Regulatory Action on Nidhi Companies

Overview

In 2024, the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) and Registrars of Companies (RoCs) significantly intensified regulatory actions against Nidhi companies and firms failing to disclose beneficial ownership.

Actions and Penalties

RoCs issued 131 orders, a 72% increase from 2023, with penalties ranging from Rs 10,000 to Rs 30 lakh to ensure financial transparency and curb illicit activities.

Beneficial Ownership

Beneficial Ownership refers to individuals who control or own 25% or more of a company, even if shares are held in someone elseâ€™s name. This must be disclosed under the Companies Act, 2013.

Nidhi Companies

Nidhi companies are non-banking financial entities (NBFC) formed under Section 406 of the Companies Act, 2013, to encourage savings and provide loans exclusively to members. These companies are exempt from needing an RBI license but must comply with strict disclosure norms.

Key Requirements

  • Must include "Nidhi Limited" in their name.
  • Must have at least 200 members and Rs 20 lakh in net owned funds.
