India Internet Shutdowns 2024

Overview

India experienced 60 internet shutdowns in 2024, the lowest in eight years, a decrease from 96 in 2023 and 132 in 2020.

Historical Context

The highest number of shutdowns occurred in 2020, particularly in Jammu & Kashmir (J&K) following Article 370â€™s abrogation.

Recent Trends

  • Reduction observed in J&K and Manipur, suggesting administrative stabilization.
  • Strategic shutdowns in Haryana (Ambala) and Manipur due to unrest.

Government's Stance

  • Government defends shutdowns as necessary for controlling unrest and preventing misuse.
  • Lack of centralized data on shutdowns and their economic impact.

Concerns

  • Digital rights groups criticize shutdowns for violating freedom of expression.
  • Shutdowns imposed without transparency, violating Supreme Court orders.
  • Economic costs impact tourism, education, healthcare, and business sectors.
  • India ranks high globally in internet shutdowns, facing international criticism.

The Way Forward

  • Policy reform needed for a structured, transparent approach to shutdowns.
  • Independent studies on social, economic, and political consequences of shutdowns.
  • Engagement with civil society to balance security needs and digital rights.
