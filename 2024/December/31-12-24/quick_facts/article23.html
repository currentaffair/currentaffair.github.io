Conjugal Visits in Prisons

The Delhi Government is reconsidering a proposal to allow conjugal visits for prisoners.

What are Conjugal Visits?

Conjugal visits are private family visits where inmates spend time, including intimate moments, with their legal spouses within the prison. These visits promote rehabilitation, enhance psychological well-being, and sustain marital ties.

Judicial Landmarks

The Punjab & Haryana High Court in 2014 upheld the right to procreation for prisoners. The Madras High Court in 2018 allowed parole for conjugal relations. In 2023, Justice S.M. Subramaniam urged the Tamil Nadu government to consider conjugal relations within prison precincts.

Conjugal Visits in Other Countries

In some US states, conjugal visits are allowed, though not in federal prisons. In Europe, countries like Spain, France, Sweden, and Denmark permit these visits. Spain offers monthly visits, while Sweden allows up to 9-hour sessions.
