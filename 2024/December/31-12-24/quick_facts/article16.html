ISRO’s SpaDeX Mission: Advancing Space Docking Technology

Mission Overview

ISRO’s Space Docking Experiment (SpaDeX) will launch on December 30, 2024. This mission aims to demonstrate satellite docking capabilities, a critical technology for future space endeavors.

What is SpaDeX?

SpaDeX is a technology demonstrator mission by ISRO to showcase in-space docking. It will test the docking, undocking, and rendezvous of two small spacecraft (SDX01 and SDX02) in low Earth orbit.

Mission Objectives

  • Primary: To demonstrate docking of two small spacecraft (SDX01 and SDX02).
  • Secondary: Test electric power transfer and spacecraft control.

Mission Design

The two satellites will be launched into orbit using PSLV and will approach each other from 20 km to 3 meters. The Bharatiya Docking System (BDS) will be used for docking.

Space Docking

Space docking involves maneuvering spacecraft to connect in orbit, crucial for building modular space infrastructure, crew transfers, and satellite servicing.

Why is Space Docking Crucial for India?

  • Supports modular space stations and lunar/Mars missions.
  • Vital for future crew transfers and emergency evacuations (e.g., Gaganyaan mission).
  • Positions India as a global leader in space docking, enhancing satellite servicing and international collaboration.
