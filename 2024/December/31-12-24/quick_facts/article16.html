Behaviour that Causes Mental Pain to Spouse: Ground for Divorce

The Supreme Court ruled that persistent emotional distress caused by a spouse can be considered mental cruelty, a valid ground for divorce.

Mental Cruelty as Divorce Ground

Ruling: Emotional distress leading to the breakdown of trust is mental cruelty and grounds for divorce.

Definition: Cruelty goes beyond physical harm and includes sustained actions that make cohabitation impossible.

Case Background

A husband filed for divorce citing his wife’s behavior, which included leaving their home and filing criminal complaints. The Madras High Court granted the divorce, and the wife challenged it in the Supreme Court.

Supreme Court Judgment

The Supreme Court upheld the High Court’s decision, stating that loss of trust and sustained emotional torment constitute cruelty. Without mutual trust, marriage becomes a mere legal formality.

Alimony

The husband was ordered to pay ₹50 lakh as permanent alimony. The Court clarified that financial independence of the wife does not negate her right to maintenance, as it ensures dignity and stability post-divorce.
