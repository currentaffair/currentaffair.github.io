India's Internet Shutdowns in 2024

Overview

India experienced 60 internet shutdowns in 2024, the lowest in eight years, a reduction from 96 shutdowns in 2023 and 132 in 2020.

Historical Context

The 2020 peak in shutdowns was largely due to political unrest following the abrogation of Article 370 in Jammu & Kashmir.

Recent Trends

Shutdowns decreased in Jammu & Kashmir and Manipur, suggesting stabilization. Temporary shutdowns were seen in Haryana and Manipur due to political unrest.

Government's Position

The government justifies shutdowns as a measure to control unrest and prevent misuse by anti-social elements, but lacks centralized data on their impact.

Digital Rights and Economic Impact

Digital rights groups criticize the shutdowns for violating freedoms. They have significant economic consequences on tourism, education, healthcare, and businesses.

Global Criticism

India is one of the highest deployers of internet shutdowns globally, facing international criticism. Persistent shutdowns may strain diplomatic relations with nations emphasizing digital rights.

Way Forward

Policy reforms are needed for a more structured approach. The government should commission independent studies and engage with digital rights groups to create balanced policies.
