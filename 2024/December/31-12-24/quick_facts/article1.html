ISRO's Docking Mission On

Overview

ISRO successfully launched the Space Docking Experiment (SpaDeX) mission, marking a significant achievement in India’s space technology.

Mission Details

  • Launched on Monday via PSLV C60 rocket from Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota.
  • Carried two satellites: SDX01 (Chaser) and SDX02 (Target), each weighing 220 kg.
  • Satellites deployed into a 475-km circular orbit at around 10 p.m.

Operational Progress

  • Successful deployment of spacecraft solar panels.
  • Satellites are maneuvering into position with a 20 km distance increase over the next few days.
  • Docking process expected to begin by January 7.

Technological Significance

The mission showcases rendezvous, docking, and undocking technologies crucial for manned moon missions, sample returns, and space station building.

Strategic Goals

  • Enhances India's capability for future space missions, including sending an astronaut to the moon.
  • Aims to create an Indian space station.

ISRO’s Achievement

Solidifies ISRO as a leader in cutting-edge space technology, showcasing India's advancing space capabilities.
