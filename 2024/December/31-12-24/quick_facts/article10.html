Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)

Context

The inclusion of the mumps vaccine in the Universal Immunization Programme (UIP) has been proposed by Tamil Nadu following a rise in mumps cases. Currently, the Measles-Rubella (MR) vaccine is part of the UIP but mumps remains uncovered.

UIP Overview

The Universal Immunization Programme (UIP) is a global public health initiative by the Government of India to provide free vaccines to children and pregnant women. Its goal is to reduce mortality and morbidity through high immunization coverage.

Mission Indradhanush

Launched in 2014, Mission Indradhanush aims to increase immunization coverage by targeting unvaccinated and partially vaccinated children and pregnant women. Over 54.6 million children and 13.2 million pregnant women have been vaccinated through its phases.

Measles, Mumps, and Rubella

Measles

Caused by the measles virus, it is transmitted airborne and can lead to complications like pneumonia and encephalitis.

Mumps

Caused by the mumps virus, mumps can cause swollen salivary glands, orchitis, meningitis, and infertility.

Rubella

Rubella virus can cause congenital rubella syndrome in newborns if a pregnant woman is infected, leading to severe birth defects.
