GPS Interference and Spoofing

Instances of GPS spoofing have increased significantly from 300 to 1,500 flights impacted daily in 2024, as reported by OPSGROUP.

What is GPS Interference and Spoofing?

GPS Interference disrupts the accuracy of GPS signals, affecting navigation systems.
Spoofing is a cyber-attack where fake GPS signals are transmitted, misleading systems to think they are in different locations.

Impacts of Spoofing

Inertial Reference System (IRS)

The IRS ensures navigation without external signals like GPS, using gyroscopes and accelerometers. It provides reliable navigation, even in GPS-rejected environments.

Safety Benefits of IRS

IRS operates safely for up to five hours if GPS fails, ensuring aircraft safety during GPS spoofing or jamming attacks.
