Fighter Jet Generations

Fighter jets are classified into generations, each marking a significant leap in technological capabilities.

First Generation (1943–1955)

Introduced during WWII, these jets were subsonic with basic avionics and armed with machine guns. Examples: Me 262, MiG-15.

Second Generation (1955–1970)

Fighter jets with afterburners, supersonic speeds, and radar-guided missiles. Examples: MiG-21, F-104 Starfighter.

Third Generation (1960s–1970s)

Multi-role aircraft with advanced radar and electronic warfare systems. Examples: F-4 Phantom, MiG-23.

Fourth Generation (1970–2000)

Fly-by-wire systems, advanced avionics, and stealth technology. Examples: F-16, Su-30, Rafale.

Fifth Generation (2000–Present)

Stealth, advanced computer systems, and networked capabilities. Examples: F-22 Raptor, F-35, Su-57.

Sixth Generation (Under Development)

Expected to include hypersonic speeds, AI, and directed energy weapons. Potential examples: Tempest, FCAS.
