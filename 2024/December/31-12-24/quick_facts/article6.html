Tiger Translocation & Conservation in India

Context

Chief Minister Mohan Yadav has directed the translocation of 15 tigers from Madhya Pradesh to Rajasthan, Chhattisgarh, and Odisha as part of conservation efforts.

Key Objectives of Tiger Translocation

  • Population Recovery: Reintroduce tigers in areas where they are extinct.
  • Genetic Diversity: Prevent inbreeding by introducing new genetic material.
  • Conflict Mitigation: Reduce human-wildlife conflicts in areas with high tiger density.
  • Habitat Restoration: Reintroduce apex predators to restore ecological balance.

Role of NTCA (National Tiger Conservation Authority)

The NTCA supervises and coordinates tiger relocation projects, ensuring scientific methods and best practices are followed.

Challenges & Lessons Learned from the Satkosia Project

  • Failure Reasons: Lack of local community involvement, habitat unsuitability, and project management issues.
  • Lessons Learned: Need for comprehensive planning, local community engagement, and advanced monitoring systems.

Benefits of Tiger Relocation

  • Ecological Balance: Restores predator-prey dynamics in underpopulated reserves.
  • Mitigates Human-Tiger Conflicts: Reduces overcrowding in tiger reserves.
  • Regenerates Landscapes: Revives tiger populations in historically extinct areas.
