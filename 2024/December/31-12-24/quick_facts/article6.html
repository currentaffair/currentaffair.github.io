Jammu Region's Rise in Militant Activity

Overview

In 2024, the Jammu region witnessed a significant rise in militant activity across all 10 districts, reflecting a shift in militant tactics and strategies, with security forces facing more casualties than militants.

Key Findings

  • Casualty Statistics: Jammu saw 18 security personnel killed versus 13 militants, with Kashmir seeing 55 militants killed and 10 security personnel dead.
  • Militant Tactics: Militants adapted to the difficult terrain, using forests and hard-to-reach ridges for attacks.
  • Civilian Casualties: 14 civilian deaths in Jammu, including 9 pilgrims killed in Reasi, and 16 deaths in Kashmir, with increased civilian-targeted violence.

Security Measures

  • Increased Security: The National Security Guard (NSG) was deployed in Jammu for the first time to counter rising threats.
  • Crackdown: 168 locals were arrested under the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, and 14 houses linked to militants were attached.

Key Attacks and Incidents

  • Ganderbal Attack: Militants targeted a strategic tunnel project in October 2024, marking a shift to high-profile attacks.
  • Reasi Attack: Militants killed pilgrims in June 2024, highlighting increased violence against civilians.

Implications

The rise in militant activity in Jammu signals a shift in militancy dynamics, with the region now facing a full-fledged insurgency. New operational tactics demand enhanced counter-insurgency measures.
