India’s Obligations towards the Rohingya

The plight of Rohingya refugees in India reflects human rights violations, with violations of constitutional rights as highlighted by a study from The Azadi Project and Refugees International.

International Protections for Rohingya Refugees

Non-refoulement: Prohibits deporting individuals to countries where they face harm. India is bound by international obligations like the ICCPR, though it isn't a signatory to the Refugee Convention.

India's Legal Position

Rohingyas are considered “illegal migrants” under Indian law, and the Citizenship Amendment Act (2019) excludes them from citizenship pathways. The courts have recognized non-refoulement, but deportations are allowed for national security concerns.

Living Conditions and Challenges

Rohingya refugees face harsh living conditions in detention centers like Assam's Matia Camp. NGOs are restricted in their ability to provide aid due to legal restrictions.

Policy Gaps

India lacks a standardized refugee policy, resulting in inconsistent treatment. Geopolitical factors influence the treatment of various refugee groups.
