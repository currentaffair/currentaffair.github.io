GPS Spoofing and Interference

Instances of GPS spoofing have increased dramatically, from 300 flights daily in January to 1,500 by August, causing significant impacts on aviation and navigation.

What is GPS Interference and Spoofing?

GPS Interference disrupts the accuracy of GPS signals used for navigation, while Spoofing involves a cyber-attack where fake GPS signals are transmitted to mislead navigation systems.

Attackers mimic legitimate GPS signals, tricking systems into sensing false positions, speeds, or altitudes.

Impacts of GPS Spoofing

Spoofing impairs navigation accuracy for aircraft and drones, triggering incorrect speed or terrain warnings. It is used in conflict zones to deceive enemies, such as along the India-Pakistan and India-Myanmar borders.

Recent hotspots include Northern Iraq, the Black Sea, Western Russia, and the Baltics.

Inertial Reference System (IRS)

The IRS is a standalone navigation system that operates without GPS signals. It uses gyroscopes to measure angular velocity and accelerometers to track linear acceleration, ensuring accurate navigation even in GPS-jammed environments.

IRS acts as a redundancy, ensuring aircraft safety during spoofing or jamming attacks.
