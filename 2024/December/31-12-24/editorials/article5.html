What Spending Surveys Show and What They Don’t?

Introduction

The Government of India released the results of the household consumption expenditure survey for the period from August 2023 to July 2024. This large-scale survey aims to provide valuable insights into the spending patterns of Indian households, with a focus on changes in consumption expenditure, rural-urban disparities, wage trends, and inequality. This survey follows up on a similar study conducted in 2022-23, providing a comparative basis for evaluating trends in household spending.

About the Survey

The survey, conducted on a sample of 2.61 lakh households, provides comprehensive data on household consumption expenditures across various regions in India. The rationale behind conducting two consecutive surveys in 2022-23 and 2023-24 was to check the robustness of the results and validate the methodology. By comparing two surveys, the government can assess whether the findings reflect true trends or are influenced by methodological variations.

Key Findings of the Survey

Increase in Monthly Per Capita Consumption Expenditure

The survey revealed a notable increase in the average monthly per capita consumption expenditure across both rural and urban areas. In rural areas, the average expenditure rose from Rs 3,773 in 2022-23 to Rs 4,122 in 2023-24. Similarly, urban areas saw an increase from Rs 6,459 to Rs 6,996. After adjusting for inflation, the growth rate in consumption expenditure stands at approximately 3.5%. This indicates a steady rise in household spending, particularly in rural areas, where spending is growing at a faster pace compared to urban centers.

Changes in Food Spending

One notable trend highlighted by the survey is the shift in the share of food expenditure in total household consumption. In rural areas, the percentage of expenditure on food dropped from 52.9% in 2011-12 to 46.38% in 2022-23 and remained relatively stable in 2023-24. In urban areas, the share of food expenditure decreased from 42.62% in 2011-12 to 39.17% in 2022-23. This reduction could be attributed to a combination of high food inflation during the period and the margin of error in survey data.

Rural-Urban Gap

The rural-urban consumption gap continues to narrow, with rural areas experiencing faster growth in expenditure compared to urban regions. The difference in average monthly consumption expenditure between rural and urban areas decreased from 83.9% in 2011-12 to 71.2% in 2022-23, and further to 69.7% in 2023-24. This reflects a positive trend in improving rural economic conditions, although disparities still remain.

Wage Trends and Economic Implications

Rural Wages

Rural wage growth has been modest in recent years. Between 2018 and 2023, nominal wages grew at a rate of 5.2%. However, when adjusted for inflation, real wages declined by 0.4%, indicating a stagnation in purchasing power for rural households. This wage stagnation is particularly concerning as it limits the ability of rural households to fully benefit from the rising consumption expenditure, despite their increased spending.

Insight: The divergence between rising consumption and declining real wages in rural areas suggests the need for policy interventions to address wage growth and inflation impacts on purchasing power.

Inequality and the Gini Coefficient

The Gini coefficient, a measure of income inequality, showed a decline over the past decade, both in rural and urban areas. In rural areas, the Gini coefficient fell from 0.283 in 2011-12 to 0.266 in 2022-23, and further to 0.237 in 2023-24. In urban areas, it declined from 0.363 in 2011-12 to 0.314 in 2022-23, and 0.284 in 2023-24. This suggests a trend towards reduced income inequality, though disparities remain significant between rural and urban populations.

Significance of the Surveys

Household consumption expenditure surveys are critical for understanding shifts in spending patterns, which can have far-reaching implications for economic policy. These surveys provide a basis for estimating poverty levels, constructing the consumer price index, and making other important economic calculations. With dramatic changes in household consumption since the 2011-12 survey, these surveys are increasingly valuable for informing decisions about poverty alleviation, inflation control, and social welfare programs.

Conclusion

The results of the 2023-24 household consumption expenditure survey offer important insights into India’s evolving economic landscape. While there are positive trends, such as a narrowing rural-urban gap and declining inequality, challenges remain. The continued stagnation of rural wages and the impact of inflation on real income highlight the need for targeted economic policies. Moving forward, India must address these issues to ensure that rising consumption is supported by sustained income growth, particularly in rural areas, while working to maintain the overall trend of reduced inequality.
