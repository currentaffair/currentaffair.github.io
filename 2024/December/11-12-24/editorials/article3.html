UNCCD's Drought Atlas

Introduction

The UNCCD's World Drought Atlas was launched at the UNCCD COP16 in Riyadh, highlighting global drought risks and strategies to mitigate them. This publication is vital in understanding the systemic nature of droughts and the need for a more sustainable and proactive approach to land management, especially as droughts become more frequent due to climate change.

Key Findings of the UNCCD's Drought Atlas

Systemic Nature of Drought Risks

Droughts are no longer isolated events but a global systemic risk affecting multiple sectors such as agriculture, energy, and trade. According to the UNCCD, by 2050, 75% of the world’s population could be impacted by drought conditions if current trends continue.

Economic Consequences: The cost of drought-related damages is often underestimated. The UNCCD estimates that droughts result in an annual economic loss of USD 307 billion, which is 2.4 times more than previously thought.

Drought Vulnerability in India

India, with its vast and diverse climate, is particularly susceptible to droughts. Approximately 60% of the agricultural land in India is rain-fed, making it vulnerable to fluctuations in rainfall patterns.

Human Impact: Droughts are not just natural phenomena but are exacerbated by human activities such as deforestation and poor water management, leading to resource degradation.

Combatting Drought: Key Initiatives

Integrated Drought Management Programme: A joint initiative between the Global Water Partnership (GWP) and WMO.

UNCCD’s Drought Resilience Policy: Promotes science-policy collaboration to ensure drought preparedness.

Key Recommendations from the World Drought Atlas

  • Governance: Countries should prioritize the development of national drought plans.
  • Land Use Management: Sustainable agricultural practices should be implemented.
  • Water Supply Management: Investment in water infrastructure is essential.

Conclusion

The UNCCD's Drought Atlas is an essential resource for understanding and managing the systemic risks posed by droughts. It emphasizes the need for coordinated global action to mitigate drought impacts.
