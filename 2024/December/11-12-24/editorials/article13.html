Minilateralism and India's Role in Global Governance

Introduction

Minilateralism is reshaping the global order by fostering smaller, more focused coalitions to address specific regional and global challenges. In contrast to the often slow and ineffective processes of multilateralism, minilateral partnerships are gaining momentum due to their agility and efficiency in tackling urgent issues. India, playing a pivotal role in this transformation, is leveraging minilateral frameworks to advance multipolarity and secure its strategic interests across various sectors, from security to trade and climate action.

What is Minilateralism?

Minilateralism refers to the formation of smaller, more focused coalitions of countries working together on specific regional, global, or issue-based challenges. These coalitions are typically formed by nations with shared interests, allowing for quicker decision-making and targeted outcomes. An example of minilateralism is the Quadrilateral Security Dialogue (Quad), which includes the United States, India, Japan, and Australia, aiming to ensure a free and open Indo-Pacific.

Minilateralism vs Multilateralism

The key difference between minilateralism and multilateralism lies in the number of participants and the scope of issues addressed. While minilateralism typically involves a small group of countries working on specific issues, multilateralism involves broad, global participation on a wide range of concerns. Minilateral frameworks often enable faster decision-making and more efficient responses to crises, unlike multilateral institutions that tend to be bogged down by the need for consensus among a large number of members.

Why the Shift Towards Minilateralism?

The shift towards minilateralism is driven by several global trends:

  • Fragmentation of Global Consensus: Multilateralism struggles to achieve consensus due to diverging national interests, evident in the WTO's inability to finalize the Doha Development Agenda for over two decades.
  • Power Asymmetry and Emerging Geopolitical Rivalries: Larger nations dominate multilateral frameworks, sidelining smaller countries, as seen in China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) reshaping global finance.
  • Efficiency and Speed in Crisis Management: Minilateral frameworks, such as the Quad, have shown agility in responding to crises like the COVID-19 pandemic, while multilateral institutions like the WHO faced delays.
  • Focused and Tailored Approaches: Minilateral coalitions can focus on specific, actionable goals, such as the Australia-UK-US (AUKUS) pact in the Indo-Pacific, addressing security and technological cooperation.

India's Role in the Rise of Minilateralism

India has emerged as a key player in the rise of minilateralism, taking leadership roles in various security, economic, and climate-focused partnerships. Through frameworks like the Quad and the India-UAE Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement, India is building strategic alliances to counter regional threats, enhance trade, and ensure technological advancement.

Challenges Facing India in Minilateralism

While India’s role in minilateralism is crucial, several challenges exist:

  • Balancing Strategic Autonomy with Partnerships: India must manage its historical non-alignment policy while aligning more closely with frameworks like the Quad, which raises concerns about strategic autonomy.
  • Managing Diverging Interests: Diverging priorities among partners, such as the U.S. and India’s cautious approach to China, can complicate decision-making within frameworks like the Quad.
  • Resource and Capacity Constraints: India’s ability to lead multiple minilateral forums is limited by its financial and institutional capacity, affecting its participation in various alliances.

India's Strategy Moving Forward

To navigate the complexities of minilateralism, India must adopt a hybrid diplomacy model, where minilateralism complements multilateralism. By strengthening regional multilateral frameworks and advocating for reforms in global institutions like the UN and WTO, India can ensure a more balanced and effective approach to global governance.

Conclusion

Minilateralism is reshaping global governance by providing more agile solutions to regional challenges through smaller, more focused partnerships. India’s leadership in frameworks like the Quad enhances its global standing and secures its strategic interests. However, the limited scope of these coalitions poses challenges for addressing broader global issues. India must continue to champion multilateral reforms while strengthening its role in minilateral platforms to ensure both regional and global cooperation thrive.
