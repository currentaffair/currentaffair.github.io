One Nation, One Subscription (ONOS) Scheme

Scheme Overview

The ONOS scheme aims to increase access to top research journals for State-affiliated colleges and individual students across India.

Increased Journal Availability

The number of journals available will rise by 62%, covering 95% of published research.

Phased Implementation

ONOS will begin on January 1, 2025, covering all States and government institutions. A review in 2027 will include private universities and colleges.

Financial Implications

The Centre's annual subscription spend will increase from ₹1,000-1,500 crore to ₹6,000 crore between 2025 and 2027.

Article Processing Charges (APC) Fund

A ₹150 crore fund will help researchers cover APCs, ensuring free access to their work.

Implementation via INFLIBNET

INFLIBNET will manage subscriptions across over 6,300 institutions, benefitting around 1.8 crore students, faculty, and researchers.

Accessibility

Students and faculty can register for remote access to research papers, even when off-campus.

Broader Impact

ONOS aims to reduce the knowledge gap and democratize access to high-quality research across India's educational institutions.
