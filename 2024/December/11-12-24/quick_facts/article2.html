PM POSHAN Scheme: Centre's Contribution for 2024-25

Overview

The Centre will bear ₹425.62 crore for inflation-induced food material costs under the PM POSHAN Scheme for 2024-25.

Cost and Impact

The material cost for pulses, vegetables, and oil has increased by 13.7%, with new rates set at ₹6.19 for primary students and ₹9.29 for upper primary students.

Scheme Details

PM POSHAN provides hot cooked meals to 11.7 crore students across 10.24 lakh schools in India, ensuring one hot meal per day to improve children's health and nutrition.

Financial Support

The Centre covers 100% of foodgrain costs and transportation, offering a subsidy of ₹9,000 crore annually. Inflation rates for 2022-23 and 2023-24 were considered for the cost revision.
