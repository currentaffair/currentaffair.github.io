India and Russia Enhanced Cooperation

Key Areas of Collaboration

  • AI: Advancing collaboration in artificial intelligence.
  • Cybersecurity: Strengthening efforts to enhance security measures.
  • Space Exploration: Joint efforts in the exploration of space.
  • Counter-Terrorism: Mutual cooperation to combat terrorism.

INS Tushil Commissioning

INS Tushil, an upgraded Krivak III class frigate, was commissioned in Kaliningrad. Defence Minister Rajnath Singh highlighted the increase in “Made in India” content in naval ships.

Key Facts About INS Tushil

  • Part of Project 1135.6, which includes six Krivak class frigates.
  • Stealth frigate designed for blue water operations.
  • Equipped with advanced air, surface, underwater, and electromagnetic warfare capabilities.
  • Armed with advanced weapons for naval warfare.

Stealth Frigates Deal

India and Russia signed an agreement in 2016 for four stealth frigates, with two built in Russia and two at Goa Shipyard Ltd. The second frigate, Tamal, will be delivered in 2024.

Indian Navy’s Role

The Indian Navy plays a crucial role in maritime security, particularly in regions such as the Gulf of Oman and the Gulf of Aden, and provides humanitarian assistance and disaster relief.
