PM POSHAN: Additional Cost Due to Food Inflation

Key Points

The Indian government will cover an additional ₹425.62 crore under the PM POSHAN scheme to address inflation-induced food material costs for the year 2024-25.

Food Inflation Impact

Inflation in food material costs, such as pulses, vegetables, and oil, increased by 13.7%. The revision will increase costs by ₹6.19 for primary students and ₹9.29 for upper primary students.

Scheme Coverage

The PM POSHAN Scheme provides hot cooked meals to 11.7 crore students in 10.24 lakh government and aided schools across India, supporting health and nutrition.

Financial Details

The government will cover 100% of the foodgrain cost and transportation, amounting to approximately ₹9,000 crore in annual subsidies.
