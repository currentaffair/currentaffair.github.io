Human Rights Day

Why in News? Human Rights Day, observed every year on 10th December, emphasizes the importance of human rights for justice, peace, and equality.

2024 Theme

The theme for 2024, "Our Rights, Our Future, Right Now," highlights the crucial role of human rights in creating a peaceful and sustainable future.

Historical Significance

Human Rights Day was established in 1950 to commemorate the adoption of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) on 10th December, 1948, by the United Nations. This declaration outlines fundamental human rights for all people.

UN Human Rights Council

Founded in 2006, the UN Human Rights Council promotes global human rights protection. The Council's 47 member States, including India, address violations and emergencies. The Council's secretariat, the Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights (OHCHR), is based in Geneva, Switzerland.

Advocacy and Action

Human Rights Day advocates for collective action against hate speech, misinformation, and human rights abuses. It calls for the promotion of equality and non-discrimination worldwide.

Human Rights and India

In India, human rights are enshrined in the Constitution through Fundamental Rights (Part III) and Directive Principles of State Policy (Part IV). The Preamble of India's Constitution reflects the spirit of the UDHR, committing to justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.

The National Human Rights Commission (NHRC), established in 1993, ensures adherence to human rights in India under the Protection of Human Rights Act (PHRA).
