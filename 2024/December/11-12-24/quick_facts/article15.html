Behind the Great Indian Internet Shutdown

Overview

India is known as the “world’s Internet shutdown capital” due to frequent instances of Internet suspension, especially in sensitive regions.

Supreme Court Ruling

The Supreme Court ruling in 2020 (Anuradha Bhasin vs Union of India) declared that the Internet is a fundamental right and any restriction must be temporary, lawful, necessary, and proportionate.

Government Compliance Issues

Despite the ruling, states often fail to comply with guidelines, and shutdown orders are not always promptly published, hindering judicial review. Statutory amendments in 2020 weakened enforcement.

Impact of Internet Shutdowns

In 2020, India lost $2.8 billion due to 129 shutdowns. These shutdowns affect daily life, disrupting education, healthcare, and livelihoods, with significant social and psychological impacts.

Socio-economic Disparities

Mobile data restrictions disproportionately affect lower socio-economic classes, as the majority of mobile internet users rely on data rather than broadband.

Call for Reform

To prevent arbitrary shutdowns, there is a need for better compliance with the Supreme Court's guidelines. Internet shutdowns should be rare and only in genuine public emergencies.
