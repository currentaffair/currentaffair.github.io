Maritime Rescue Coordination Centres

Overview

A recent joint rescue operation between the Indian Coast Guard (ICG) and Pakistan Maritime Security Agency (MSA) successfully saved 12 crew members from the sunken vessel MSV Al Piranpir in the North Arabian Sea. This operation highlighted the critical role of the Maritime Rescue Coordination Centres (MRCCs) in both nations, ensuring smooth communication during the rescue.

What are MRCCs?

MRCCs are units responsible for organizing Search and Rescue (SAR) services at sea. In India, these centres are managed by the ICG within defined Search and Rescue Regions (SRRs). The Indian SRR includes three areas: Mumbai, Chennai, and Port Blair.

International Cooperation & Conventions

India follows international maritime conventions such as the SAR Convention (1979) and UNCLOS (1982), fostering cooperation on SAR operations and promoting maritime safety globally.
