India-Russia Enhanced Cooperation and INS Tushil

Why in News? India and Russia are strengthening ties in sectors like AI, cybersecurity, space exploration, and counter-terrorism. A key highlight is the commissioning of INS Tushil in Kaliningrad.

Key Developments

The two countries are collaborating in critical areas:

  • AI & Cybersecurity: Enhanced focus on mutual growth in these cutting-edge fields.
  • Space Exploration: Joint efforts to advance space technologies.
  • Counter-Terrorism: Strengthened cooperation for regional security.

INS Tushil Commissioning

The INS Tushil, a part of the upgraded Krivak III class frigate series, was commissioned by Defence Minister Rajnath Singh. It highlights India’s growing naval capabilities and collaboration with Russia. This marks the inclusion of "Made in India" content in naval ships.

Naval Collaboration

The INS Tushil is designed for blue water operations, equipped with advanced air, surface, underwater, and electromagnetic warfare capabilities. The ship is armed with modern weaponry for comprehensive naval defense. India’s Navy plays a vital role in securing maritime routes and providing humanitarian aid.

Future Prospects

In 2016, India and Russia agreed on a deal for four stealth frigates. The second ship, Tamal, is expected to be delivered next year, with some frigates built at Goa Shipyard Ltd.
