Behind the Great Indian Internet Shutdown

Why in News? India is known as the "world's Internet shutdown capital" due to frequent Internet suspensions.

Supreme Court Ruling

In the Anuradha Bhasin vs Union of India (2020) case, the Supreme Court recognized the Internet as a fundamental right. It ruled that any Internet restrictions must be temporary, lawful, necessary, proportionate, and subject to judicial review.

Government Compliance Issues

Non-compliance: States often fail to follow the Supreme Court's guidelines, delaying shutdown orders and hindering judicial review.

Statutory Gaps: The 2020 amendments to the Telecom Suspension Rules did not mandate the publication or periodic review of shutdown orders, weakening enforcement.

Impact of Internet Shutdowns

Economic Loss: In 2020, $2.8 billion was lost due to 129 instances of Internet suspension, impacting over 10 million people.

Disruption to Daily Life: Shutdowns affect education, healthcare, livelihoods, and access to information.

Psychological, Social, and Journalistic Harm: Limited access disrupts communication, news dissemination, and emergency responses.

Socio-economic Disparities: Mobile data restrictions disproportionately affect lower socio-economic classes.

Call for Reform

Increased Compliance: Emphasize adherence to the Supreme Court's guidelines to prevent arbitrary shutdowns.

Statutory Support: Advocate for statutory recognition of the Supreme Court's directions to ensure government compliance.

Limitations: Internet shutdowns should be rare and only in clear public emergencies.
