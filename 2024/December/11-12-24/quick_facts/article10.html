Maritime Rescue Coordination Centres

Why in News? Recently, a joint rescue operation by the Indian Coast Guard (ICG) and the Pakistan Maritime Security Agency (MSA) successfully saved 12 crew members from the sunken Indian vessel MSV Al Piranpir in the North Arabian Sea. This operation demonstrated the essential role of Maritime Rescue Coordination Centres (MRCCs) of both nations in ensuring effective communication during rescue efforts.

What are Maritime Rescue Coordination Centres?

MRCCs are specialized units responsible for Search and Rescue (SAR) operations at sea, within a designated Search and Rescue Region (SRR). In India, MRCCs are operated by the Indian Coast Guard (ICG) in three areas: Mumbai, Chennai, and Port Blair.

SAR Cooperation and Related Conventions

India is part of various international conventions, such as the International Convention on Maritime Search and Rescue (SAR) 1979 and United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS) 1982, that enhance maritime SAR cooperation.

About the Indian Coast Guard

The Indian Coast Guard, established in 1978, is responsible for maritime search and rescue, protection of life at sea, and enforcement of maritime laws. It operates under the Ministry of Defence and is headquartered in New Delhi.

ICG Duties Related to SAR

The ICG provides assistance to fishermen in distress, ensures safety at sea, conducts SAR missions, and enforces maritime laws.
