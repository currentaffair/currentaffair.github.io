India-Japan Forum 2024

Why in News? Recently, at the India-Japan Forum in New Delhi, India’s External Affairs Minister emphasized the potential for a strategic semiconductor alliance between India and Japan. This collaboration aims to improve global supply chain resilience by reducing dependence on China.

Semiconductor Collaboration

India and Japan are joining forces with Taiwan to enhance their semiconductor industries and reduce risks in the global supply chain. This aligns with both countries' strategic goals of technological autonomy and countering China's influence.

Quad's Growth

India’s External Affairs Minister highlighted the revival and expansion of the Quad, a strategic alliance involving India, Japan, Australia, and the US. The Quad has evolved into a platform for regular cooperation, enhancing its resilience and fostering intergovernmental partnerships.

About the India-Japan Forum

The India-Japan Forum facilitates discussions and cooperation between leaders of both countries. It is organized by the Ananta Centre and India’s Ministry of External Affairs, focusing on shaping future bilateral relations and strategic partnerships.

Quadrilateral Security Dialogue (Quad)

The Quad is a strategic forum aimed at promoting regional security and economic growth in the Indo-Pacific, strengthening security, countering China’s assertiveness, and improving maritime security. It was revived in 2017, with its first formal summit held in 2021.
