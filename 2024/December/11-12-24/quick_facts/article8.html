PM POSHAN Scheme Update

Why in News? The Centre will bear an additional ₹425.62 crore in 2024-25 due to food inflation costs under the PM POSHAN Scheme, which provides hot cooked meals to millions of students.

Key Highlights

The costs of food materials such as pulses, vegetables, and oil have risen by 13.7%, prompting an increase in the per-student subsidy. The revised rates are ₹6.19 for primary students and ₹9.29 for upper primary students.

The inflation rate for 2022-23 was 6.45%, and for 2023-24 it is 6.74%, which were factored into the revision of these rates. These changes apply across all States and Union Territories, with flexibility for higher contributions from some states.

PM POSHAN Scheme Details

PM POSHAN, formerly known as the Mid-Day Meal Scheme, serves hot cooked meals to 11.7 crore students in government and government-aided schools across India. It aims to improve health and nutrition, ensuring one hot meal per day for children in Balvatika and Classes 1 to 8.
