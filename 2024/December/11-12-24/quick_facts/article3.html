Saurashtra Fossils Say Early Humans Didn’t Stick To Coast As They Migrated

Relevance: GS 3 (Science)

Coastal Migration Theory

The theory suggests early humans migrated along coastlines, utilizing maritime routes. Genetic studies support this, but new evidence from Saurashtra contradicts it.

Evidence from Saurashtra

Palaeolithic sites in Saurashtra, India, show inland settlements, not coastal. Tools made from chert and jasper date back 56,000-48,000 years, supporting inland migration.

Inland Settlement

Relative dating places the artefacts in the Middle Palaeolithic period, with no evidence of Late Palaeolithic coastal tools or marine resources like fish or shellfish.

Landmass Connections

Saurashtra was connected to other regions, providing a land route for early humans, contradicting the coastal theory.

Challenges in Proving Coastal Migration

Debate continues due to the lack of securely dated coastal sites and the submergence of potential coastal sites because of rising sea levels.

Broader Implications

This study challenges traditional migration theories, suggesting inland routes for human dispersal across India.
