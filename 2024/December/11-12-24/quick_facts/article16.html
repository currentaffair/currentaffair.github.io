Human Rights Day

Overview

Human Rights Day is celebrated annually on December 10th, emphasizing the importance of human rights for justice, peace, and equality.

2024 Theme

The theme "Our Rights, Our Future, Right Now" highlights the role of human rights in shaping a sustainable and peaceful future.

Historical Significance

Established in 1950, Human Rights Day commemorates the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR), adopted by the UN on December 10, 1948.

UN Human Rights Council

The UN Human Rights Council (formed in 2006) has 47 member states, including India, and works on global human rights protection through its office in Geneva, Switzerland.

Advocacy and Action

The day calls for collective action to combat hate speech, misinformation, and human rights abuses while promoting equality and non-discrimination.

Human Rights and India

The Indian Constitution enshrines human rights in its Fundamental Rights and Directive Principles of State Policy, reflecting the UDHR's values. The National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) ensures the protection of human rights in India.
