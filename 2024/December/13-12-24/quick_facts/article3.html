Khorlochhu Hydropower Project

Context: Tata Power has begun construction on the Rs 6,900 crore Khorlochhu Hydropower Project in Bhutan, with a 600 MW capacity, expected to be completed by 2029.

About the Project

Collaboration: Druk Green Power Corporation (DGPC) and Tata Power.

Capacity: 600 MW hydroelectric facility.

Equity: DGPC holds 60%, Tata Power 40%. Total project cost: Nu 70 billion.

Timeline: Estimated completion by 2029, aligned with Bhutan's goal of 20 GW hydropower by 2040.

What is a Gravity Dam?

A gravity dam uses the weight of its concrete or stone masonry to hold back water. Each section is stable on its own and resists the water pressure.

Hydropower Projects in India

  • Bhakra Nangal Dam: Sutlej River, multipurpose project for irrigation and power.
  • Tehri Dam: Bhagirathi River, tallest dam in India, part of Tehri Hydropower Complex.
  • Sardar Sarovar Dam: Narmada River, key for irrigation and drinking water in Gujarat.
  • Nathpa Jhakri Dam: Sutlej River, largest hydropower plant in India by capacity.
  • Koyna Dam: Koyna River, hydroelectric power and irrigation in Maharashtra.
  • Rangit III Dam: Rangit River, supports Sikkim's energy needs.
  • Dhauliganga Dam: Dhauliganga River, critical for power generation in Uttarakhand.
  • Indira Sagar Project: Narmada River, largest reservoir in India, provides power, water.
  • Subansiri Lower Project: Subansiri River, largest under-construction project in India.
