Tiger Translocation: Case of Zeenat

Overview

Zeenat, a tiger translocated from Maharashtra’s Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve to Similipal in Odisha, aims to improve genetic diversity. The move is part of an ongoing wildlife conservation effort in India.

Current Movement

Zeenat has traveled into Jharkhand and is now approaching West Bengal. Real-time tracking of her movement is being conducted through radio collars.

Collaborative Efforts

Efforts between the states of Odisha, Jharkhand, and West Bengal are crucial for Zeenat’s successful relocation.

Challenges

  • Preventing further movement into urban or vulnerable areas.
  • Ensuring a natural return or using tranquillization if necessary.
  • Improving inter-state coordination for future tiger translocations.

Significance

This case highlights the complexity of wildlife conservation and the need for interstate cooperation in managing translocated species.
