Kawasaki Disease and Blood Vessels

Context: A stand-up comedian revealed that his son is suffering from Kawasaki Disease (KD), a rare illness affecting children.

What is Kawasaki Disease?

Kawasaki Disease is an acute illness primarily affecting children under 5, characterized by inflammation of blood vessels, with potential severe complications if untreated.

Characteristics

  • Affects children under 5, more common in boys.
  • Most prevalent in Asia, especially Japan, but found worldwide.
  • More frequent in late winter and early spring.

Causes

The exact cause is unknown, but triggers may include viral or bacterial agents, genetic factors, or abnormal reactions to environmental factors.

Symptoms

Fever lasting more than 5 days with at least four of the following symptoms:

  • Conjunctivitis (redness in both eyes)
  • Lymphadenopathy (swollen lymph nodes)
  • Oral changes
  • Swelling and redness in hands and feet
  • Peeling of skin around nails in later stages

Treatment

Treatment typically involves Intravenous Immunoglobulin (IVIG) to reduce inflammation and prevent coronary complications, alongside aspirin.

Blood Vessels

Different types of blood vessels serve various functions in the circulatory system:

  • Arteries: Carry oxygenated blood away from the heart (e.g., aorta, coronary arteries).
  • Veins: Carry deoxygenated blood toward the heart (e.g., vena cava, pulmonary veins).
  • Capillaries: Facilitate gas and nutrient exchange (e.g., lung and muscle capillaries).

Types of Blood Vessels

  • Arteries: Thick, muscular walls, narrow lumen.
  • Arterioles: Smaller, regulate blood flow and pressure.
  • Venules: Thin-walled, collect blood from capillaries.
  • Veins: Larger lumen, contain valves.

Additional Vessels

Lymphatic Vessels: Transport lymph, a fluid containing white blood cells.

Sinusoids: Specialized capillaries in organs like the liver and spleen.
