Female Moths and Acoustic Emissions

Context: Moths use sounds emitted by plants to decide where to lay their eggs, according to the study "Female Moths Incorporate Plant Acoustic Emissions into Their Oviposition Decision-Making Process".

About Moths

Moths belong to the order Lepidoptera and make up the majority of its species. There are about 160,000 species of moths, most of which are nocturnal.

Some moths are diurnal (active during the day), while others are crepuscular (active at dawn or dusk).

Moths have feathery antennae, unlike butterflies, which have thin, clubbed antennae. Moth larvae (caterpillars) make cocoons and eventually emerge as moths.

Predators and Parasites

Moths are preyed upon by nocturnal insectivores like bats, owls, and some birds, as well as other animals like lizards, rodents, and cats. Moth larvae are vulnerable to parasitism by Ichneumonidae.

Significance

Some moth species, particularly caterpillars, are agricultural pests, including corn borers and bollworms. Others, like those in the family Tineidae, damage fabrics made from natural fibers such as wool and silk.
