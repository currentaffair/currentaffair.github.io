Union Cabinet Approves Bills on Simultaneous Polls

Cabinet Approval

The Union Cabinet has approved two Bills to facilitate simultaneous elections for the Lok Sabha and Assemblies, also known as "One Nation, One Election."

Bills Overview

One Bill proposes a constitutional amendment to enable simultaneous elections, while the other aligns the terms of Union Territories with Legislative Assemblies to match those of State Assemblies.

Reactions

Chief Ministers M.K. Stalin of Tamil Nadu and Mamata Banerjee of West Bengal criticized the Bills, calling them attacks on democracy and federalism.

Legislative Process

The Bills will be reviewed by a joint committee of Parliament. Notably, the constitutional amendment Bill does not require ratification by 50% of the States.

Committee Recommendations

A high-level committee suggested simultaneous polls for municipalities and panchayats, but the Cabinet decided against it.
