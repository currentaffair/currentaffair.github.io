Nicobarese Population & Genetic Research

Context: Recent genetic research by CSIR-Centre for Cellular and Molecular Biology (CCMB) reveals that the Nicobarese population shares significant ancestry with Austroasiatic groups from South and Southeast Asia.

About Nicobarese

The Nicobarese are an Austroasiatic-speaking group from the Nicobar Islands, part of India's Andaman and Nicobar Islands Union Territory. The Nicobar Archipelago has 19 islands, of which 12 are inhabited.

Language: The Nicobarese language belongs to the Austroasiatic family, with different dialects across the islands. The Car Nicobar dialect is the most widely understood.

Religion & Society: The majority are Christians, and their society is matriarchal with a chief.

Economy: The Nicobarese follow a horticultural economy, growing crops like coconuts, bananas, mangoes, and other fruits.

History and Population

The Nicobarese settled in the archipelago around 5,000 years ago, with earlier theories suggesting their arrival around 11,700 years ago. They likely coexisted with the Shompen tribe.
