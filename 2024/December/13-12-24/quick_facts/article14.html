Wildlife Species Overview

Layson Albatross

The Layson Albatross (Phoebastria immutabilis) is a pelagic bird primarily found in the North Pacific. It is known for its expert soaring abilities and its breeding sites in the Northwestern Hawaiian Islands. The species is protected under the Migratory Bird Treaty Act, and efforts are ongoing to preserve its habitat.

Markhor

The Markhor (Capra falconeri) is a wild goat with spiral-shaped horns, found across the mountainous regions of Pakistan, India, and Central Asia. It is known for its distinctive corkscrew horns and is classified as Near Threatened by the IUCN. It is a national animal of Pakistan and is protected under various conservation laws.

Indian Star Tortoise

The Indian Star Tortoise (Geochelone elegans) is distinguished by its star-patterned shell. Found in dry, semi-arid regions of India and Sri Lanka, this species faces threats from urbanization and habitat fragmentation. It is listed as Vulnerable by the IUCN and is protected under the Wildlife Protection Act of India.
