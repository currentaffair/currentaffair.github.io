Simultaneous Elections: A Critical Step for Local Democracy

Introduction

Urban Local Governments (ULGs) play a vital role in decentralized governance, providing essential civic services and ensuring the quality of life for citizens. The 74th Constitutional Amendment Act (CAA), introduced in 1992, sought to institutionalize ULGs' role in strengthening local democracy. However, despite over 30 years of implementation, the full potential of the Act remains unrealized.

The current discourse surrounding One Nation One Election (ONOE) presents a unique opportunity to discuss an often-overlooked aspect: the importance of timely and regular elections for ULGs. This is crucial to maintaining democratic governance at the local level, which has historically been sidelined in ONOE debates.

State-Level Challenges in Local Elections

Historical Context and Reports

The idea of simultaneous elections for Lok Sabha, State Assemblies, and ULGs has been discussed in various government reports. The 79th Report of the Parliament Standing Committee on Law and Justice (2015) advocated for simultaneous elections but excluded ULGs from its purview. Similarly, NITI Aayog's 2017 discussion paper and the Law Commission's 2018 report argued that ULG elections should be governed at the state level, making synchronization impractical due to the sheer volume of local bodies.

However, the 2024 High Level Committee (HLC) has shifted the narrative by recommending that ULG elections be synchronized within 100 days of the general elections to the Lok Sabha and State Assemblies, highlighting the importance of including ULGs in this national framework.

Importance of Urban Local Governments

India is home to over 4,800 ULGs, responsible for governing cities that contribute more than 60% to the nation's GDP. These cities are integral to economic growth, social well-being, and cultural development. As urban populations are expected to exceed 50% by 2050, the significance of well-functioning ULGs will only increase.

Regular and timely elections are fundamental for ensuring the effective governance of these local bodies, enabling citizens to hold their leaders accountable and ensuring that cities continue to function as engines of economic growth.

Challenges Faced in Holding Timely Elections

Delays in Elections

Despite a constitutional mandate to hold elections to municipalities every five years, delays have been common in ULG elections across India. The Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) of India's 2024 report reveals that over 60% of ULGs experience delayed elections. This undermines the decentralization principle embedded in the Constitution, leaving cities vulnerable to central and state control, and reducing accountability.

Furthermore, a study by Janaagraha on municipal corporations in Karnataka revealed an average delay of 11 months in council formation after election results. This delay disrupts governance and makes elected representatives powerless, affecting citizens' ability to voice their concerns and participate in development processes.

Disempowerment of State Election Commissions

The disempowerment of State Election Commissions (SECs) is another significant issue. According to the CAG's 2024 report, only four out of the 15 states assessed have adequately empowered their SECs to handle the critical tasks of ward delimitation. These delays are often exacerbated by political interference, legal disputes, and the high stakes involved in delimitation and reservation processes.

Given these challenges, it is necessary for SECs to be independent authorities capable of carrying out these functions without undue external influence. Reforming the SECs will be key to improving the efficiency of ULG elections.

Recommendations for Reform

  • Synchronize ULG elections with general elections to reduce delays and enhance democratic accountability.
  • Empower State Election Commissions to carry out ward delimitation and reservation exercises independently.
  • Ensure that elections are conducted on time to uphold the principle of decentralization and provide effective local governance.
  • Engage in a nationwide dialogue to ensure reforms are implemented with the consensus of all stakeholders.

Conclusion

The Government of India has expressed its intention to implement the High Level Committee's recommendations, including synchronizing elections for local bodies with national elections. This presents an opportunity to address the long-standing delays in ULG elections and ensure that local democracy is fully functional. By reforming the election process and empowering local institutions, India can ensure that its cities continue to drive economic and social progress, while also fostering more accountable and transparent governance.
