CoP 29 and Rising Air Pollution in India

Introduction

The **2024 Lancet Countdown Report on Climate Change and Health** highlights the urgency of reducing fossil fuel dependency. It calls for **health-centric climate finance** to mitigate air pollution exposure by transitioning to cleaner energy sources.

Understanding Air Pollution

Ambient Air Pollution (AAP)

AAP, particularly in urban areas like the NCR, garners significant attention due to its severe impacts, especially during winter.

  • Primary sources: Industrial emissions, vehicular exhaust, and crop residue burning.
  • National Clean Air Programme (NCAP): Focuses on improving urban air quality but lacks focus on rural contributors.

Household Air Pollution (HAP)

HAP disproportionately affects rural areas due to the reliance on solid fuels like biomass for cooking.

  • Pollutants: PM2.5, carbon monoxide, and black carbon.
  • Health impacts include respiratory diseases, stunted child growth, and increased morbidity rates.

Challenges in Transitioning to Clean Energy

Energy Equity

Low-income populations depend on biomass due to economic constraints, making transitions to cleaner fuels like LPG challenging.

Misplaced Climate Priorities

The **combustion of biomass** exacerbates climate change. Meanwhile, LPG use emits fewer pollutants, balancing climate and health goals.

Opportunities for Cleaner Energy Transitions

Strengthening the Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY)

  • Increase subsidies for the poorest communities.
  • Expand awareness campaigns on the health benefits of clean cooking fuels.
  • Invest in infrastructure to ensure affordability and sustained access.

Health-Centric Climate Finance

  • Prioritize clean energy in residential sectors.
  • Use international climate finance to support LPG adoption initiatives.

Health Benefits of LPG Adoption

  • Reduces **long-term health expenditures** by mitigating illnesses linked to air pollution.
  • Improves child growth and lowers mortality rates in adults.
  • Immediate reductions in emissions lead to better respiratory health.

Conclusion

The global reluctance to prioritize transitions from biomass to cleaner fuels like LPG risks perpetuating energy and health inequities in developing countries. Strengthening initiatives like PMUY and realigning NCAP to focus on clean energy can ensure inclusive and equitable outcomes, addressing both climate and public health challenges.
