Committee of Creditors and Insolvency Issues

Context: The RBI Deputy Governor suggests the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India (IBBI) should impose a code of conduct for the Committee of Creditors (CoC) to improve its performance in insolvency proceedings.

What is the Committee of Creditors (CoC)?

The CoC is a group of financial creditors formed during the Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP) under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) of 2016. It makes key decisions about the debtor’s debts.

Formation of the CoC

The CoC is formed after the Interim Resolution Professional (IRP) verifies creditors' claims. It includes financial creditors like banks, bondholders, and homebuyers.

Role and Responsibilities

The CoC approves or rejects resolution plans, decides on restructuring or liquidation, and ensures that resolution terms are followed.

Powers Under the IBC

The CoC can make major decisions about the debtor’s functioning, approve resolution plans, or direct liquidation if no resolution is approved.

Issues with CoC’s Performance

The RBI Deputy Governor highlighted issues such as individual creditor interests overriding collective ones, disagreements on resolution plans, and low meeting attendance.

Recommendations for Improvement

To improve the CoC’s performance, the RBI recommends that IBBI implement a code of conduct to ensure transparency, accountability, and better participation.

Current Statistics on Insolvency Cases

As of September 2024, 8,002 insolvency cases were admitted under the IBC, with 33% resulting in liquidation and 25% still open.
