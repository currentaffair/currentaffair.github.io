Understanding Gravity and Zero Gravity

Context: Sunita Williams, an Indian-origin astronaut, hosted a virtual session for students to explain gravity and its effects.

What is Gravity?

Gravity is the force of attraction between objects with mass, governing the motion of objects on Earth and the universe.

Historical Understanding

Ancient philosophers like Aristotle believed heavier objects fall faster. Brahmagupta described gravity as a force drawing objects toward Earth. Galileo, Newton, and Einstein further developed our understanding, with Newton’s Universal Law of Gravitation and Einstein’s General Theory of Relativity.

Characteristics of Gravity

Gravity is universal, acts on all objects with mass, and is the weakest fundamental force. It is an inherent property of mass and decreases with distance.

Gravity on Earth

The gravitational acceleration on Earth is approximately 9.8 m/s², affected by factors such as altitude and latitude.

Applications of Gravity

Gravity keeps planets in orbit, governs galaxies, and is essential for daily life, from water cycles to engineering designs.

Zero Gravity

Zero gravity, or microgravity, occurs when objects are in free fall, like astronauts aboard the International Space Station. It leads to weightlessness and unique effects, such as spherical flames and plant growth in all directions.
