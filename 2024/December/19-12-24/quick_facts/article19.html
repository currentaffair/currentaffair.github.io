Arabian Leopard Conservation

Arabian Leopard Facts

The Arabian leopard (Panthera pardus nimr) is the smallest leopard subspecies, native to the Arabian Peninsula. It has a golden to tawny fur with rosettes. This critically endangered species faces threats like habitat loss, illegal hunting, and retaliation killings. There are fewer than 200 wild individuals left, and its population is declining.

Other Leopard Species

Several leopard species exist worldwide, including the African leopard, Persian leopard, Indian leopard, and the endangered Amur leopard. These species vary in size, distribution, and status, with the Amur leopard being the most endangered.
