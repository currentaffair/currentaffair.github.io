Constitution Debate and Prime Minister's Resolutions

The Constitution Debate commemorated the 75th anniversary of India's Constitution on November 26, 1949. The Prime Minister proposed 11 resolutions to guide the nation’s future development and governance.

11 Resolutions Proposed by the Prime Minister

  • Citizen Duties: Emphasizing the shared responsibilities of individuals and the government.
  • Inclusive Development: Focusing on regional and community growth aligned with "Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas."
  • Zero Tolerance for Corruption: Rejecting the social acceptance of corruption.
  • Pride in Laws and Traditions: Respecting India's heritage and shedding colonial mindsets.
  • Opposition to Religion-Based Quotas: Commitment to existing reservations but no religion-based quotas.
  • Women-Led Development: Envisioning India as a leader in empowering women.
  • Regional Growth for National Development: Advocating for development through state-level growth.
  • Respect for the Constitution: Ensuring the Constitution is protected from political misuse.
  • Dynastic Politics and Meritocracy: Promoting merit-based governance over nepotism.
  • Pride in Indian Heritage: Fostering pride in India’s cultural legacy.
  • Unity in Diversity: Reaffirming the vision of "Ek Bharat, Shrestha Bharat."
