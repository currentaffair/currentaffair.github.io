Supreme Court Directive on Sacred Groves Management

The Supreme Court has directed the Centre to formulate a comprehensive policy for managing sacred groves across India, emphasizing their religious, cultural, and ecological importance.

Key Details

  • Sacred Groves: Protected patches of trees with religious and cultural significance, contributing to biodiversity conservation.
  • Regions Affected: Predominantly found in Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, and Maharashtra.
  • Supreme Court Directive: Focus on vanishing groves, highlighted in a petition from Rajasthan. The Centre is urged to take proactive measures.
  • Policy Responsibility: Traditionally, wildlife management is a state responsibility, but the court insists on a central role in protecting sacred groves.
  • Comprehensive Survey: Led by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) to identify, protect, and prevent reduction of sacred groves.
  • National Forest Policy (1988): Encourages community involvement in forest improvement, as emphasized by Justice Mehta.
  • Piplantri Village Example: A success story of turning barren land into lush groves through community-driven efforts.
  • Ecological Value: Sacred groves serve as ecological reserves and are crucial in preventing unauthorized land use changes.
