Reimposition of Protected Area Regime in North Eastern States

The Union Home Ministry has reimposed the Protected Area Regime (PAR) in the states of Manipur, Nagaland, and Mizoram due to security concerns. Foreigners now require prior permission and special permits to visit these regions.

Key Details

  • Reimposition of PAR: The Protected Area Regime was reinstated after 14 years, effective from December 2022.
  • Security Concerns: Due to the influx of foreigners from neighboring countries, the government has enforced stricter entry guidelines.
  • Entry Guidelines: Foreigners must obtain Protected Area Permits (PAP) as per the Foreigners (Protected Areas) Order, 1958.
  • Manipur Government Statement: The government has dismissed unverified claims from unauthorized groups, such as the "Kuki-Zo Council."
  • Local Tensions: Regional tensions persist, with a new group warning against using Kuki-dominated roads for inter-community travel.
