Dulcibella camanchaca: A New Deep-Sea Species

Context

Researchers recently identified Dulcibella camanchaca, a new species of amphipod, in the Atacama Trench off the western coast of South America.

What is Dulcibella camanchaca?

Dulcibella camanchaca is a new predatory amphipod species classified under the Eusiridae family. It represents a significant addition to the catalog of deep-sea biodiversity, distinguished by its unique genetic makeup and morphology.

Key Features

  • Size: 4 cm, larger than its closest relatives.
  • Distinctive raptorial appendages (gnathopods) for hunting smaller organisms.
  • Named "camanchaca" meaning "dark" in the Indigenous Andes languages, reflecting its deep-ocean habitat.
  • It preys on other amphibians, playing a vital role in the deep-sea food web.
  • Bright coloration enhances survival in its dark environment.

About The Atacama Trench

The Atacama Trench (Peru–Chile Trench) is located off the western coast of South America, with a depth of 8,065 meters below sea level. It marks the boundary between the Nazca plate and the South American plate.
