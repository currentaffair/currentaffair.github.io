Importance of State-Level Water Policy

Water governance in India is primarily handled at the state level, making state policies crucial for effective management. Each state has unique water management needs that require localized solutions.

Localized Water Management Needs

  • Maharashtra: Focus on regulation.
  • Jharkhand: Prioritizes supply augmentation.

Impact of Climate Change

Climate change exacerbates water-related risks, necessitating adaptive water management strategies that are innovative and forward-thinking.

Shifting Focus in Water Management

  • Conventional Approaches: Projects like Polavaram Dam and river interlinking focus on supply augmentation.
  • Shift to Demand Management: Emphasizing demand-side management for efficiency and sustainability.

Institutional Reforms

To overcome inertia, reforms such as an Independent Water Resources Regulatory Authority are proposed to improve efficiency, address regional imbalances, and manage risks like groundwater depletion and river pollution.

Intergovernmental Coordination

Effective water policy requires seamless coordination across different levels of government to tackle trans-boundary risks and enhance holistic governance.
