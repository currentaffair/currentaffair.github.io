NPA Crisis in India

The NPA crisis in India is a significant challenge for the banking sector, with over 43% of the non-performing assets (NPAs) owed by just 100 companies.

What are NPAs?

Non-Performing Assets (NPAs) are loans where the principal or interest payment has been due for more than 90 days. They are categorized into three types:

  • Substandard: Overdue up to 12 months.
  • Doubtful: Overdue for more than 12 months.
  • Loss: Considered uncollectible.

Scale of the NPA Crisis

The total NPAs in India reached ₹9.33 lakh crore by March 2019. 100 companies account for 43% of this total, with manufacturing, energy, and construction sectors dominating the defaulters.

Current Status

NPAs peaked at ₹10.36 lakh crore in 2018 but dropped to ₹5.71 lakh crore by March 2023. Despite recovery efforts, many businesses face liquidation, affecting the banking system's ability to lend.

Steps to Address the NPA Crisis

Ongoing recovery efforts include bankruptcy proceedings and the establishment of Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs) and the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) 2016. However, the recovery potential is limited due to widespread liquidation.

Recommended Actions

The government should focus on improving transparency, strengthening recovery procedures, and assisting banks in managing distressed assets more efficiently.
