Santa Ana Winds & Wildfires in California

About Santa Ana Winds

Santa Ana winds are dry and warm winds in Southern California that blow from the desert region, including Nevada and parts of Utah. These winds occur when high pressure forms over the Great Basin, causing strong winds to travel over mountains toward the Pacific Ocean, heating and compressing as they go.

Impact of Santa Ana Winds

As the winds move, they lower humidity, making conditions dry. This dryness increases the risk of wildfires, such as the recent blaze in Malibu, where vegetation turned into highly flammable material.

California's Geography and Climate

  • Location: Western United States, bordered by the Pacific Ocean.
  • Land Area: 3rd largest state in the U.S.
  • Climate: Mediterranean along the coast, arid in deserts, alpine in mountains.
  • Major Rivers: Sacramento, San Joaquin, and Colorado Rivers.
  • National Parks: Yosemite, Joshua Tree, Death Valley, and more.
  • Volcanoes: Lassen Peak, Mount Shasta, and others.
