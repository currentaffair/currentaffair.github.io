Gaganyaan: HLVM3 Assembly for Uncrewed Flight

HLVM3 Assembly Progress

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has initiated the assembly of the Human Rated Launch Vehicle Mark-3 (HLVM3) for the Gaganyaan mission’s first uncrewed flight, set to launch early next year.

Launch Campaign Commencement

The launch campaign began on December 18, 2024, starting with the stacking of the S200 motor’s nozzle end segment at the Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), Sriharikota.

Preparation and Integration

  • S200 Motors: Segment assembly, control systems, and avionics integration are ongoing.
  • L110 and C32 Stages: Both are ready at the launch complex.
  • Crew Escape Systems: Components have been delivered to SDSC.

Module Integration

  • Crew Module: Integrated at Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC).
  • Service Module: Prepared at U R Rao Satellite Centre (URSC).
  • Orbital Module: Integration and tests to follow at URSC.

Gaganyaan Project Objective

The Gaganyaan mission aims to demonstrate India's human spaceflight capability, with plans to send a crew of three astronauts to an orbit of 400 km for three days and return them safely to Earth. This mission will be followed by one crewed mission after three uncrewed missions.

Anniversary Significance

The assembly of HLVM3 coincides with the 10th anniversary of the LVM3-X/CARE mission (December 18, 2014).
