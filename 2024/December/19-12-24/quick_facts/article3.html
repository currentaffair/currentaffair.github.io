Konda Reddi Tribe

Context

Gillaram village in Jaladam Valley, home to the Konda Reddis, a Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Group (PVTG), reflects their timeless harmony with nature.

About the Konda Reddi Tribe

The Konda Reddis, or Hill Reddis, are a PVTG in the Indian states of Andhra Pradesh, Odisha, and Tamil Nadu. They live primarily in the Khammam district of Andhra Pradesh. Although they request recognition as tribals in Odisha, they are not listed as such.

The Konda Reddis speak Telugu and are distinct from the Reddy caste.

Religion

Traditional Konda Reddi religion does not believe in a spiritual being within humans. They worship ancestors, hills, and local gods, alongside folk Hinduism, characterized by worship at the community level.

Social Structure

The family structure is patriarchal, and marriage methods include bargaining, love, elopement, service, capture, and exchange. Monogamy is the norm, though polygamy exists.

Political Structure and Economy

The Konda Reddis have a social control system called the "Kula Panchayat" and a hereditary chief, known as 'Pedda Kapu', who is also the village priest. They are shifting cultivators, growing jowar, their staple food, and cultivating commercial crops like cashew, niger, chili, and cotton.

Their unique circular-shaped houses with mud walls and thatched roofs reflect the Bhunga architecture of Gujarat.
