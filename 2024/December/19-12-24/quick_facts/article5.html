Sultanpur National Park and Conservation Efforts

About Sultanpur National Park

Sultanpur National Park, located in Haryana, is a key bird sanctuary recognized as a Ramsar site in 2021. The park hosts over 50,000 migratory birds annually and forms part of the Central Asian Flyway.

Key Features

  • Location: Haryana, India
  • Vegetation: Includes tropical and dry deciduous species such as dhok, khair, neem, and Acacia.
  • Bird Population: Over 50,000 birds from more than 100 species.
  • Resident Birds: Common hoopoe, purple sunbird, and others.
  • Migratory Birds: Siberian crane, greater flamingo, and more.
  • Historical Background: Named after Sultan Singh, a Rajput Chauhan.
  • Conservation Efforts: Includes mound construction, path widening, and tube well digging.

Government Programs

Sultanpur National Park is part of the Union government's Amrit Dharohar initiative aimed at promoting tourism at Ramsar sites.

Nearby Wildlife Areas in Haryana

  • Kalesar National Park: Known for elephants and sambhar.
  • Bhindawas Wildlife Sanctuary: Home to neelgai, jackal, and various birds.
  • Khaparwas Wildlife Sanctuary: Known for hornbills and blue peafowl.
  • Chhilchhila Wildlife Sanctuary: A habitat for blue peafowl and hornbills.
