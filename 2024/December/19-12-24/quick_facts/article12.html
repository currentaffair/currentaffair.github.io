ISRO's CE20 Cryogenic Engine Test

Sea Level Hot Test

ISRO successfully conducted a sea-level hot test of its CE20 cryogenic engine, essential for India’s GSLV Mk III rocket. This test was crucial for validating engine performance under real-world conditions, utilizing a Nozzle Protection System to prevent flow separation and minimize vibration and thermal damage.

Significance of the Test

The CE20 engine powers GSLV Mk III and supports missions like Gaganyaan. The test enhances ISRO’s self-reliance in cryogenic technology, demonstrating the engine’s capacity for 19–22 tonnes of thrust, which enables heavier payloads and interplanetary missions.

Key Specifications

  • Thrust: 200 kN
  • Specific Impulse: 442 seconds (vacuum)
  • Burn Time: 640 seconds
  • Engine Mass: 587 kg

Major Achievements

  • Successfully launched in GSLV Mk III-D1 (2014).
  • Enabled Chandrayaan-2 mission (2019).
  • Supports Gaganyaan, India's first human spaceflight program.

Global Comparison

The CE20 is competitive with global engines such as the RL10 (USA), RD-0120 (Russia), and Vinci (Europe) in terms of thrust and efficiency, using LOX and LH2 propellants for optimal performance.
