Supreme Court Seeks Government Response on PIL for Stricter Measures Against Sex Offenders

Overview

The Supreme Court is considering stricter measures for sex offenders in India. A PIL has been filed calling for chemical castration, a ban on online pornography, and denial of bail for accused in heinous crimes against women.

Petition Details

The petition was filed by the Supreme Court Women Lawyers’ Association, represented by senior advocate Mahalakshmi Pavani. It highlights that 12 years after the Nirbhaya case, women still do not feel safe in India.

Court's Observations

Justice Surya Kant called chemical castration a “radical” suggestion. The court emphasized that the lack of enforcement, not laws, is the reason for the persistence of crimes against women.

Proposals

The petition suggested that the Law Commission of India draft a law that includes:

  • Chemical castration for convicts of sexual offenses against women and children.
  • Permanently castrating those sentenced to life imprisonment or the death penalty.
  • Establishing fast-track courts for serious crimes.
