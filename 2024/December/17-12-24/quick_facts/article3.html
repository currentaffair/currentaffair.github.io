La Niña and Its Global Impact

What is La Niña?

La Niña is a climate phenomenon where ocean temperatures in the equatorial Pacific cool. It impacts global weather, particularly in India, where it affects monsoons and winter seasons.

Monsoon Impact

La Niña leads to normal or above-normal rainfall during India’s monsoon season, benefiting agriculture.

Winter Impact

It brings colder winters, especially in northern India, influencing regional weather patterns.

Global Effects

Globally, La Niña causes droughts in Africa and intensifies hurricanes in the Atlantic.

Current Situation (2024)

There is a 57% chance of La Niña forming this winter, though it is expected to be weak.

Meteorological Insights

Higher wind speeds during La Niña winters may help reduce air pollution. However, a lower Planetary Boundary Layer Height (PBLH) could trap pollutants near the surface.

Future Outlook

If La Niña persists into 2025, it could mitigate summer heatwaves and support strong monsoons, offering a contrast to the disruptive effects of El Niño.
