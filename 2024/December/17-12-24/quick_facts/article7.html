COP16 and the Paris Agreement

The 12-day COP16 meeting under the UNCCD, held in Saudi Arabia, ended without a binding drought response protocol but made progress towards a future global drought regime.

Key Points

  • No Binding Deal: COP16 failed to finalize a drought response protocol, but progress was made towards an agreement for COP17 in 2026.
  • Drought Impact: Droughts cost over $300 billion annually, with 75% of the global population expected to be affected by 2050.
  • Disagreements: Developed countries wanted a non-binding framework, while African countries and indigenous groups pushed for a binding protocol.
  • Financial Pledges: Over $12 billion was pledged to bolster drought resilience, alongside the formation of the Riyadh Global Drought Resilience Partnership.
  • Future Goals: The UNCCD aims to restore 1.5 billion hectares of land by the decade’s end, requiring $2.6 trillion in investments.

Paris Agreement Challenges

  • Emissions and Temperature Rise: Global emissions have risen by 8%, and temperatures have increased by 1.45°C since pre-industrial times.
  • Financial Issues: The Paris Agreement's financial pledges are insufficient, with promises reduced to $300 billion by 2035, far from the required trillions.
  • Developing Countries’ Discontent: Small island nations and developing countries are dissatisfied due to lack of accountability and financial support from developed nations.
  • US Withdrawal: The United States may withdraw again under Donald Trump's leadership, weakening global climate commitments.
