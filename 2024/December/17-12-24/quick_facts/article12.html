Supreme Court on Places of Worship Act 1991

The Supreme Court recently prohibited civil courts from registering lawsuits that challenge the ownership or title to religious sites. It also halted any orders for surveys of disputed religious sites until February 17, 2025. The Places of Worship Act, enacted in 1991, aims to preserve the religious character of places of worship as they stood on August 15, 1947. The Act has been challenged in the courts, especially regarding its constitutional validity.

Core Issues

Petitioners argue that the Act violates judicial review and limits the religious rights of certain communities. The Supreme Court has been tasked with examining whether the Act restricts civil suits and compromises religious freedom in India.

Notable Disputes

The article also discusses several ongoing disputes related to religious sites, such as the Gyanvapi Mosque in Varanasi and the Shahi Idgah Mosque in Mathura, which challenge the law's provisions.
