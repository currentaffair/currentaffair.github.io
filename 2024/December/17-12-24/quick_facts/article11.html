Odisha's International Loan Plan for Agriculture

The Odisha government is planning to secure a Rs 734.86 crore loan from the International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD) to implement the Odisha Empowerment and Livelihood Improvement Program (OPELIP-II). This plan has raised concerns among tribal experts who question the need for an international loan, considering the Union government has already allocated substantial funds for the development of Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs) through the PM-JANMAN Mission.

About IFAD

The International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD) is a UN-specialized agency founded in 1977. Its objective is to improve the livelihoods of rural people by enhancing food security, nutrition, and income generation. IFAD provides grants and low-interest loans to support agricultural and rural development projects globally. Its headquarters is in Rome, Italy, and its primary aim is to empower rural communities through sustainable agricultural practices and innovations.
