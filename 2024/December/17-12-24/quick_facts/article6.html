Urban Local Governance & Election Delays

Context

The 74th Constitutional Amendment Act (CAA) of 1992 established Urban Local Bodies (ULBs), granting them constitutional status. These bodies play a critical role in governance, addressing urban planning, public health, and water supply.

Significance of ULGs

ULGs impact nearly 40% of the population, contributing to over 60% of India's GDP. By 2050, their population share will exceed 50%. Efficient governance in ULGs is essential for economic and social growth.

Reasons for Delayed Elections

Delays in Urban Local Government elections are primarily due to political interference, delays in ward delimitation, and inefficiencies in State Election Commissions (SECs).

Consequences of Delayed Elections

Without democratically elected representatives, ULGs suffer from reduced accountability and increased state government control, eroding public trust.

Steps for Timely Elections

To address delays, SECs need more autonomy and resources. Strict adherence to the constitutional mandate for elections every five years is necessary.

ULG Elections and One Nation One Election (ONOE)

Initially excluded from ONOE discussions, ULG elections face logistical challenges. However, synchronising ULG elections with national elections could reduce administrative burdens and increase voter turnout.

Way Forward

The High-Level Committee (HLC) suggests that local body elections be held within 100 days of Lok Sabha and State Assembly elections, marking progress in electoral reform.
