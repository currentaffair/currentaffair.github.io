Anti-Doping and Energy Conservation Efforts

Context: Athlete Biological Passport (ABP)

The World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) has approved India’s National Dope Testing Laboratory (NDTL) to manage the Athlete Biological Passport (ABP). The ABP monitors an athlete's biological markers, ensuring fair play by detecting doping substances.

Modules of ABP

The ABP program has three modules:

  • Haematological Module – Monitors blood doping markers.
  • Steroidal Module – Detects steroid doping markers.
  • Endocrine Module – Detects human growth hormone (hGH) usage.

Significance of Approval

WADA’s approval strengthens NDTL's global position, making it a key player in anti-doping efforts worldwide.

About WADA

Founded in 1999 by the IOC, WADA coordinates global anti-doping efforts and monitors sports regulations.

National Energy Conservation Efforts

On 14th December 2024, India celebrated National Energy Conservation Day. The event recognized achievements in energy efficiency and highlighted initiatives under the Energy Conservation (Amendment) Act of 2022.

National Energy Conservation Awards (NECA)

The NECA 2024 presented 71 awards, recognizing efforts to reduce energy consumption across sectors. The Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE), established under the Energy Conservation Act, leads these initiatives.

Energy Efficiency Initiatives

BEE promotes energy efficiency through programs like Standards and Labeling (S&L), Perform, Achieve and Trade (PAT), and Demand Side Management (DSM), as per the Energy Conservation Act of 2001.
