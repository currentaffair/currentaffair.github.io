Digital Currencies: Evolving Landscape with CBDC and Stablecoins

Introduction

The evolution of digital currencies has taken a significant turn with the emergence of stablecoins, which offer the benefits of cryptocurrencies while maintaining value stability by being pegged to traditional assets. In India, the landscape of digital finance is being transformed by two parallel developments: the RBI's Central Bank Digital Currency (e-rupee) and rupee-backed stablecoins. According to the Bank for International Settlements, digital currencies can cut transaction costs by up to 50%, making them an attractive option for both businesses and individuals. As India stands at this crucial juncture, the coexistence of e-rupee and rupee-backed stablecoins could potentially create a more inclusive and efficient digital financial ecosystem, provided there's proper regulatory oversight.

What is Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)?

Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) is a digital form of a country's fiat currency, issued and regulated by the central bank. It serves as a secure, seamless, and efficient alternative to cash, reducing costs associated with printing, distribution, and storage, while addressing risks like counterfeiting and theft. CBDCs can enhance financial inclusion, streamline cross-border payments, and support the shift towards a digital economy, with their design tailored to minimize disruptions to the financial system.

Types of CBDC

Retail CBDC: Designed for private sector users, including non-financial consumers and businesses. It acts as an electronic version of cash for retail transactions, with features like real-time payment settlements.

Wholesale CBDC: Primarily for interbank transfers and wholesale financial transactions, this type of CBDC is used for activities like bond settlements and nostro transfers, restricted to select financial institutions.

Key Benefits of CBDC

Enhanced Financial Inclusion

CBDC can provide banking services to unbanked and underbanked populations, reducing reliance on physical bank branches. With mobile-based digital wallets, financial services can be accessed at fingertips. The RBI also proposed offline functionality in CBDC-R for enabling transactions in areas with limited internet connectivity.

Reduced Transaction Costs

CBDCs can lower the domestic cost of printing and transporting physical currency by eliminating intermediaries. The cost of printing and distributing one ₹100 note is estimated to be around ₹15-17 over its four-year life cycle, a figure that CBDCs could help reduce.

Improved Monetary Policy Implementation

CBDCs allow central banks to monitor money flow in real time, enabling precise policy measures like targeted liquidity injections. This could help mitigate issues like hoarding or black-market activities, ensuring better policy transmission.

Cross-Border Payments

CBDCs can modernize and streamline cross-border payment systems, reducing remittance costs. The m-CBDC Bridge Project, involving Thailand, Hong Kong, and the UAE, has already shown how CBDCs can facilitate near-instant settlements across borders.

Key Concerns Associated with CBDC

Cybersecurity and Privacy Risks

CBDCs increase the risk of cyberattacks on digital infrastructure, posing a threat to national financial security. Centralized systems are attractive targets for hackers, as demonstrated by the 2020 SolarWinds cyberattack. The increasing prevalence of online theft only exacerbates these risks.

Impact on Commercial Banking

The introduction of CBDCs could lead to reduced deposits in commercial banks, as people may prefer storing their money directly with the central bank. This could create liquidity shortfalls for banks and raise funding costs, impacting lending and overall economic growth.

Rupee-Backed Stablecoins and Their Role

Rupee-backed stablecoins are digital tokens pegged 1:1 to the Indian rupee (INR), enabling fast, low-cost transactions both domestically and internationally. Unlike volatile cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, these stablecoins aim to maintain price stability while retaining the benefits of digital currencies, such as transparency and security.

Implementation Measures for CBDC in India

Building Robust Digital Infrastructure

Strengthening digital infrastructure, especially in rural areas, is critical for CBDC adoption. Initiatives like BharatNet should be accelerated to improve broadband connectivity and enhance digital literacy, ensuring inclusivity in the CBDC ecosystem.

Cybersecurity Framework

Developing a comprehensive cybersecurity framework is essential to protect CBDC systems from cyberattacks. Implementing advanced encryption and real-time threat detection mechanisms will be key in safeguarding digital transactions.

Conclusion

CBDCs, along with rupee-backed stablecoins, hold immense potential to reshape India's digital finance landscape. While there are challenges to overcome—such as cybersecurity risks and regulatory hurdles—the benefits of enhanced financial inclusion, reduced transaction costs, and efficient cross-border payments cannot be ignored. With careful implementation and robust infrastructure, CBDCs and stablecoins can pave the way for a more inclusive, resilient, and innovative financial ecosystem in India.
