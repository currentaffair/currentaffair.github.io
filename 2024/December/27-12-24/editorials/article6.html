Dr. Manmohan Singh

Introduction

Prime Minister Narendra Modi paid tribute to Dr. Manmohan Singh, the former Prime Minister and key architect of the 1991 economic reforms, following his passing on 26th December 2024.

Who Was Dr. Manmohan Singh?

Early Life

Dr. Manmohan Singh was born on 26th September 1932 in Gah, Punjab (now in Pakistan). His life was shaped by the aftermath of the Partition of India and Pakistan in 1947, which led his family to migrate to India.

He pursued higher education in economics, earning a Bachelor's and Master’s degree from Panjab University and later studying at Cambridge and Oxford, where he received a D Phil in Economics. His doctoral thesis focused on India’s export performance between 1951–1960, laying the foundation for his future contributions to the Indian economy.

Singh held teaching positions at Panjab University and the Delhi School of Economics, shaping future policymakers.

India’s Export Trends and Prospects for Self-Sustained Growth.

Economic Administration

Dr. Manmohan Singh held critical government positions, including Chief Economic Advisor, Secretary of Economic Affairs, Governor of the Reserve Bank of India, and Chairman of the University Grants Commission.

As RBI Governor (1982–1985), Singh pushed for financial stability and policy discipline.

1991 Economic Reforms

As Finance Minister during the 1991 balance of payments crisis, Dr. Manmohan Singh, along with Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao, initiated the LPG reforms (Liberalization, Privatization, and Globalization), also known as the Rao-Manmohan Model. This reform helped stabilize and grow India’s economy by devaluing the rupee to boost exports, dismantling the License Raj, and liberalizing foreign investment policies.

Contributions as Prime Minister (2004–2014)

India’s 14th Prime Minister, Dr. Manmohan Singh, served as the third-longest-serving Prime Minister. Known for balancing coalition politics with effective governance, Singh led India to sustained economic growth during his first term, with the economy growing at 8-9% annually. India emerged as the world’s second-fastest growing major economy in 2007, and he steered the country through the 2008 global financial crisis.

Key Legislation Under Singh's Leadership

Key legislations passed during Dr. Manmohan Singh’s tenure included the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) of 2005, the Right to Information Act (RTI) of 2005, and the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) of 2005. His second term saw the passage of the Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act (RTE) of 2009 and the National Food Security Act (NFSA) of 2013.

Foreign Policy and Global Relations

Dr. Singh played a pivotal role in the India–United States Civil Nuclear Agreement (2008), facilitating civilian nuclear cooperation. He represented India at various international forums, leading delegations to key meetings such as the Commonwealth Heads of Government Meeting in Cyprus (1993) and the World Conference on Human Rights in Vienna (1993).

Awards and Recognition

Dr. Singh received numerous awards, including the Padma Vibhushan (1987), the Jawaharlal Nehru Birth Centenary Award (1995), and multiple Finance Minister of the Year awards from Asia Money and Euro Money. He also won the Adam Smith Prize (1956) and the Wright’s Prize (1955) from the University of Cambridge.

What Lessons Can Be Drawn from Dr. Manmohan Singh’s Leadership?

Dr. Manmohan Singh’s leadership exemplified a balance of academic rigor and policy pragmatism. He was known for his consultative leadership style and commitment to inclusive growth, even while supporting market-oriented reforms. Singh’s ethical leadership, integrity, and commitment to strengthening institutions set him apart as a leader who earned respect across political lines.

Leadership in Adversity: Despite facing political challenges, Singh maintained a calm, focused approach and handled setbacks with dignity, further solidifying his legacy as a respected leader.

Conclusion

Dr. Manmohan Singh’s contributions to India’s economy, foreign policy, and governance will continue to be felt for decades to come. His leadership principles of integrity, inclusivity, and pragmatism have left an indelible mark on Indian politics and its path to economic modernization.
