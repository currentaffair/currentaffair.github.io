One Nation One Election Proposal: Insincerity and Inconsistency

Introduction

The Union Cabinet's approval of the controversial "One Nation, One Election" proposal has sparked debates regarding its feasibility, democratic implications, and practicality. The proposal, stemming from a high-level committee led by former President Ram Nath Kovind, aims to synchronize elections for the Lok Sabha, State Legislative Assemblies, and local bodies. This proposal has raised significant concerns about its impact on federalism, democracy, and the logistics of its implementation.

Concerns Raised

Democratic and Federal Issues: Out of 47 political parties consulted, 32 parties (primarily NDA members) supported the proposal, while 15 parties opposed it, citing it as anti-democratic and anti-federal. Critics argue that the proposal would marginalize regional parties and centralize political dominance, reducing the states' flexibility to dissolve assemblies when needed.

Practical Challenges

Logistical Issues: Conducting simultaneous elections would require extensive preparations, including the setting up of polling stations, training polling staff, and ensuring security forces are mobilized across the country. Mobilizing over 15 million staff for these elections would be an overwhelming task.

Expenses: The costs associated with conducting simultaneous elections are unclear. The committee has failed to provide alternative solutions to reduce expenses or ensure that the process is efficient.

Legal Roadblocks and Amendments

Amendments to Articles 83, 85, 172, and 356 are essential to enable the proposal. The dissolution of assemblies before completing their terms, especially in cases of no-confidence motions or hung assemblies, could create legal and constitutional challenges that need to be addressed.

Criticism of the Report

Disruption of Normal Political Life: Concerns have been raised regarding the potential disruption of normal political life. The proposal has not adequately addressed how it would prevent disruptions in state politics, which is a significant issue.

Cost Analysis: A clear cost-benefit analysis has not been presented, leaving many skeptical about the proposalâ€™s overall effectiveness and efficiency.

Recommendations from the Report

The report suggests amendments to Article 325 of the Indian Constitution to recognize simultaneous elections across all levels of government. It also proposes aligning state election terms to match the national schedule and strengthening the Election Commission's authority to ensure smoother implementation of the proposal.

Conclusion and Way Forward

The proposal for simultaneous elections has raised concerns about its practicality, impact on federalism, and democratic values. Further consensus among stakeholders, backed by legislative clarity and a comprehensive cost analysis, is critical for its successful implementation. Before moving forward, a deeper debate and thorough examination of its implications are required.

The Bill should be referred to a Joint Parliamentary Committee (JPC) for further scrutiny and attempts at consensus-building.
