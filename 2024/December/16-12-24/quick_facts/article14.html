WHO Global Drowning Statistics 2021

Key Statistics

  • Global Deaths: 300,000 deaths (30 per hour).
  • Location: 92% of deaths in low- and middle-income countries.
  • South-East Asia: 83,000 deaths (28% of global burden).

Vulnerable Groups

  • Children under 5: 24% of deaths.
  • Age 5-14: 19% of deaths.
  • Top Causes: 4th leading cause for children aged 1-4, 3rd for ages 5-14.

Progress and Challenges

  • Global Reduction: 38% decline in deaths since 2000.
  • Europe: 68% reduction; South-East Asia: 48% reduction.
  • Underreporting: Natural disasters and water accidents often excluded.

Challenges in Prevention

  • Limited Programs: 33% countries train bystanders in rescue.
  • Legislative Gaps: Existing laws are insufficient.
  • Data Deficiency: Inconsistent data affects prevention efforts.

Implications for India

  • High Burden: Due to frequent flooding and lack of swimming skills.
  • Disproportionate Impact: Poor and marginalized groups are most affected.

Recommendations

  • Policy Initiatives: Strengthen laws and allocate funds for prevention.
  • Aware & Train: Promote swimming and water safety in schools.
  • Infrastructure: Build safe water facilities in vulnerable areas.
  • Data & Research: Improve data collection and monitoring systems.
