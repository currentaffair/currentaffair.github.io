Gene Therapy Treatment for Haemophilia

Overview

Indian scientists developed a one-time gene therapy for Haemophilia A, replacing frequent Factor VIII injections. A trial at Christian Medical College, Vellore, showed patients have been free of bleeding episodes for over a year.

What is Haemophilia A?

Haemophilia A is a genetic disorder due to insufficient Factor VIII, a clotting protein. It’s inherited in an X-linked recessive pattern, predominantly affecting males. India has 40,000 to 100,000 patients, making it the second-largest population globally.

Current Treatments

Treatments are lifelong and costly, with up to ₹2.54 crore over 10 years. Gene therapy is a more cost-effective alternative.

Gene Therapy Approach

The Vellore trial uses a lentivirus vector, replacing defective genes with healthy ones to produce Factor VIII in the liver. This method is considered safer and potentially suitable for children, unlike the FDA-approved Roctavian, which uses adenovirus vectors.

Benefits of Gene Therapy

Gene therapy reduces bleeding episodes, offers long-term relief, and eliminates the need for frequent treatments. It’s a breakthrough in affordable healthcare, especially for India’s large haemophilia population.
