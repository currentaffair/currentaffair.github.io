RBI Consumer Confidence Survey - November 2024

Survey Overview

The Reserve Bank of India's bi-monthly Consumer Confidence Survey (CCS) highlights a drop in consumer confidence regarding the economic situation, employment, income, and spending. However, future expectations show optimism.

Key Findings

  • Current Situation Index (CSI): Dropped from 94.7 in September 2024 to 94 in November 2024, indicating weaker confidence in the current economy.
  • Future Expectations Index (FEI): Increased by 0.5 points, reaching 121.9, showing optimism about the future despite current concerns.
  • Household Inflation: Increased inflation perceptions, with current inflation at 8.4%, and one-year expectations rising to 10.1% due to food and housing costs.

Conclusion

The CCS reveals mixed sentiments, with consumers pessimistic about the present economic conditions but hopeful about the future. Rising inflation concerns reflect increased pressure from food and housing costs.
