Centre Launches Jalvahak Scheme for Cargo Movement

The Indian government has launched the Jalvahak scheme to promote the use of National Waterways 1, 2, and 16 for long-haul cargo transport, aiming to reduce congestion on railways and roadways.

Objective

The scheme seeks to enhance economical, eco-friendly, and efficient cargo movement by utilizing inland waterways, which will help decongest the countryâ€™s transport system.

Incentives

The scheme offers up to 35% reimbursement of operating costs and encourages the use of vessels owned or operated by government entities.

Operational Details

The scheme is valid for three years and features fixed day scheduled sailing services from Kolkata to Patna, Varanasi, and Guwahati via the Indo-Bangladesh Protocol Route. UltraTech Cement has already begun using NW-1 for large-scale gypsum transport.

Significance

This initiative optimizes supply chains, supports major shipping companies, and strengthens the role of waterways in India's logistics network.
