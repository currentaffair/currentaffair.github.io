Disease X

Overview

The December 2024 outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) has led to over 400 deaths, reigniting concerns about Disease X, a hypothetical pathogen that could trigger a global pandemic.

What is Disease X?

Termed by WHO in 2018, Disease X represents an unknown pathogen capable of causing a global pandemic. It highlights the need for better preparedness against emerging diseases.

Risk Factors

Factors such as high biodiversity, weak healthcare systems, global connectivity, zoonotic spillovers, deforestation, antimicrobial resistance, and climate change contribute to the risk of Disease X.

Mitigation and Preparedness

Strengthened surveillance and healthcare systems, as well as global cooperation and frameworks like the Pandemic Treaty and WHO's pathogen list, are essential to address Disease X risks.

About the DRC

The DRC is the second-largest country in Africa and the eleventh-largest globally. It is significantly impacted by emerging diseases like Disease X due to its complex healthcare and environmental challenges.
