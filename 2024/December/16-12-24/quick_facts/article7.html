C. Rajagopalachari - Indian Statesman

Shri C. Rajagopalachari, also known as Rajaji, was an Indian statesman, writer, lawyer, and independence activist, born on 10th December 1878 in Salem, Madras Presidency.

Contributions to India's Freedom Struggle

Rajagopalachari joined the Indian National Congress (INC) and played a key role in the independence movement. He participated in major events such as the Calcutta session (1906), agitations against the Rowlatt Act (1919), Non-Cooperation Movement, Vaikom Satyagraha (1924â€“25), and the Dandi March (1930).

Leadership and Ideology

He opposed the Government of India Act 1919, advocated for the "No-Changers" group, and led negotiations between Gandhi and Jinnah. He also supported dialogue with the British and the Muslim League, which demanded India's partition.
