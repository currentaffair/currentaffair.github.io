Carbon-14 Diamond Battery: A Revolutionary Technology

Scientists at the University of Bristol and the UK Atomic Energy Authority (UKAEA) have developed the carbon-14 diamond battery, capable of powering devices for thousands of years.

Key Features

  • Power Source: Powered by carbon-14 with a half-life of 5,700 years.
  • Energy Conversion: Harnesses energy from the radioactive decay of carbon-14, similar to solar panels.
  • Diamond Encasing: Provides strength, resilience, and safe containment of radiation.
  • Sustainable: Uses carbon-14 extracted from nuclear reactor waste.

Applications

  • Medical Devices: Powers pacemakers, hearing aids, and ocular devices.
  • Space Missions: Ideal for spacecraft and satellites.
  • Remote Locations: Perfect for deep-sea equipment and remote sensors.

Advantages

  • No need for frequent battery replacements.
  • Contains radiation safely within a diamond casing.
  • Reduces environmental hazards by repurposing nuclear waste.
  • Applicable across various industries from medical to aerospace.

Carbon-14 & Diamond Overview

Carbon-14: A radioactive isotope with a half-life of ~5,730 years, discovered in 1940, used in radiocarbon dating and medical applications.

Diamond: A crystalline form of carbon, the hardest known material, used in industrial tools, jewelry, and now battery encasing.
