Fractured Bonds Along the Border

Overview

Tensions between India and Bangladesh are impacting cross-border ties, trade, tourism, and cultural exchanges, especially in West Bengal. Protests, economic blockades, and political shifts have caused disruptions in various sectors.

Impact on Trade and Tourism

  • Decline in trade at the Petrapole-Benapole border due to protests.
  • 90% drop in Bangladeshi tourists, affecting hotel occupancy in Kolkata.
  • Medical tourism has decreased by 60-80% due to visa restrictions.

Political and Cultural Ramifications

  • Protests by organizations like ISKCON have worsened cross-border relations.
  • Burning of goods and cultural actions have intensified hostility.
  • Shared cultural heritage between Bengal and Bangladesh, including Tagore’s songs, is at risk.

Security and Border Dynamics

  • Increased security with Border Security Force deployment at zero line in Murshidabad.
  • Transport disruptions, including suspension of Bandhan and Maitree Express trains since July.

India-Australia CECA

India and Australia are progressing toward completing the Comprehensive Economic Cooperation Agreement (CECA). This free trade agreement aims to eliminate tariffs, liberalize services sectors, and address new areas like agri-tech, critical minerals, and MSMEs.

Trade Growth & Other Initiatives

  • India-Australia trade has grown, with India being Australia's fifth-largest trading partner.
  • India’s CECA initiatives also include agreements with Singapore, Malaysia, and Thailand.
  • Indo-Pacific Economic Framework for Prosperity (IPEF) and Trilateral Supply Chain Resilience Initiative (SCRI) are also in progress.
