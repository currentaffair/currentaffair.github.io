Can We Make Black Holes Reveal Themselves in Echoes of Light?

What are Light Echoes?

Light echoes occur when light passes near a black hole, bending due to its gravity, creating time-delayed "echoes" that reveal the black hole's properties like mass and spin.

New Measurement Technique

Light echoes offer a clearer signal than traditional methods, overcoming interference from hot gases and radiation around black holes to measure their mass and angular momentum more accurately.

Testing the Method

Researchers tested this method using data from the Event Horizon Telescope and simulations, analyzing light delays around the M87 black hole to correlate with its characteristics.

Technological Requirements

Long-baseline interferometry, involving telescopes on Earth and in space, is necessary to detect light echoes.

Implications

This method could confirm Einstein's predictions about black holes and provide new insights into spacetime geometry and the structure of supermassive black holes.
