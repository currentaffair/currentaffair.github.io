Caspian Sea

Overview

The Caspian Sea is the world's largest inland water body, located between Asia and Europe. It is bordered by Russia, Azerbaijan, Iran, Turkmenistan, and Kazakhstan. It is a landlocked body of water, once part of the prehistoric Paratethys Sea.

Geography and Formation

The Caspian Sea was separated from the ocean more than 5 million years ago due to tectonic activity and a drop in sea levels. Despite its name, it is technically a lake, with no direct outlet to the ocean.
