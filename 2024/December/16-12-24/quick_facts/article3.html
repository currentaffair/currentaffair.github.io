India-Australia CECA

India and Australia are working towards concluding the Comprehensive Economic Cooperation Agreement (CECA), with discussions covering key areas such as trade in goods, services, agri-tech, and mobility.

What is CECA?

The CECA is a free trade agreement aimed at eliminating tariffs on goods and liberalizing the services sector to facilitate business. It addresses five core areas: goods, services, digital trade, government procurement, and Rules of Origin/Product Specific Rules.

Negotiation Background

  • Negotiations began in May 2011, paused in 2016, and resumed in 2021.
  • The India-Australia Economic Cooperation and Trade Agreement (ECTA) came into force in 2022, serving as a preliminary trade agreement.
