Gene Therapy Treatment for Haemophilia

Introduction

Indian scientists have developed a groundbreaking gene therapy for Haemophilia A. This innovative therapy provides a one-time treatment, reducing the need for frequent clotting factor injections.

Haemophilia A

Haemophilia A is a genetic disorder caused by a deficiency in Factor VIII, leading to improper blood clotting. India has the second-largest population of haemophilia patients worldwide, with 40,000 to 100,000 affected individuals.

Current Treatments

Traditional treatments involve costly and lifelong Factor VIII injections. Gene therapy offers a more cost-effective alternative by replacing defective genes with healthy ones, eliminating the need for constant infusions.

Gene Therapy Approach

The novel gene therapy uses a lentivirus vector, offering a safer alternative to adenovirus vectors like Roctavian. This method is potentially suitable for children and could be used in resource-constrained settings.
