Andhra Pradesh All Set to Get Skill-Tagged

Overview

Andhra Pradesh is launching India's first statewide skill census in January 2025 to identify skill gaps and strengths across the state. This census aims to enhance employability by mapping the existing skills of the workforce and targeting youth unemployment, particularly in the 15-29 age group.

Key Objectives

  • Classify and organize workforce skills, identifying gaps.
  • Address youth unemployment, especially in the 15-29 age group.
  • Align skills with industry demands to enhance employability.
  • Boost job creation and economic development in Andhra Pradesh.

Implementation Details

  • Encompasses 3.5 crore working-age individuals across 1.8 crore households.
  • Dual census approach covering households and industries, including MSMEs.
  • 50,000 enumerators and 8,000 supervisors conducting the survey in bilingual formats.
  • Post-survey, individuals will receive a Skill ID linked to Aadhaar.

Challenges and Potential Outcomes

  • Apprehensions about sharing personal details and survey purpose.
  • Youth dropout rate and gender gap in labor force participation.
  • Accurate skill data will guide policies for job creation, vocational training, and employment opportunities.
  • Competency mapping for structured workforce skills, promoting economic growth.

Significance

This initiative offers a holistic approach to skill mapping, combining traditional sectors with modern industries like cybersecurity, creating a comprehensive workforce database that will foster economic growth in Andhra Pradesh.
