Carbon Market and Arctic Report Overview

Carbon Market Basics

The carbon market facilitates the buying and selling of carbon credits, each representing 1,000 kg of CO₂. It evolved from the 1990s U.S. cap-and-trade system and incentivizes emission reductions through financial costs.

Key Challenges: Developing nations face barriers in carbon accounting. Corporates prefer voluntary reporting. Misuse of offsets could undermine genuine emission cuts.

2024 Arctic Report Card

The Arctic is warming at an unprecedented rate, making it a carbon source. Thawing permafrost and increasing wildfires are accelerating global warming. Additionally, sea ice decline contributes to rising sea levels.

Implications: The Arctic’s diminished carbon storage capacity emphasizes the urgent need to reduce GHG emissions to mitigate climate risks.

India and Arctic Engagement

India began its Arctic research program in 2007, launched its first scientific expedition to the Arctic Ocean, and established the Himadri research base in 2008. It has held observer status in the Arctic Council since 2013.
