Guruvayur Temple

Context

The Supreme Court issued notice against the Guruvayur temple for skipping the Udayasthamana pooja on Ekadashi, citing crowd management issues.

About Guruvayur Temple

The Guruvayur Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Guruvayurappan, a four-armed form of Lord Vishnu. It is located in Guruvayur, Kerala.

Historical Significance

The temple's origins are debated, with claims it dates back to either the 17th century or 5000 years. Known as the "Dwarka of South," it was destroyed by the Dutch in 1716 and rebuilt in 1747.

Features

  • Main festivals: Krishna Janmashtami, Ekadashi, Vishu, and a 10-day festival in the Kumbham month.
  • Has a pond, Rudratheertham, where Lord Shiva is said to have bathed for thousands of years.
  • Home to the largest population of captive male Asian Elephants.
  • Built in the Kerala style of architecture, including a 70-feet-tall gold-covered flagstaff (Dwajasthamba).

Prominent Devotees

  • Melpathur Narayana Bhattathiri
  • Poonthanam Namboothiri
  • Vilwamangalam Swamiyar
