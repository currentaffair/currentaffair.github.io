Simultaneous Elections: Impractical and Complicated

Context

The concept of "one nation, one election" has sparked debate, with two enabling Bills postponed in the Winter Session of Parliament.

Arguments in Favor

  • Cost Reduction: Simultaneous elections would lower poll expenses.
  • Governance Focus: Reduces campaigning, allowing political parties to focus on governance.

Criticisms

  • Logistical Challenges: Conducting elections for 1.4 billion people is impractical.
  • Undermines Federalism: Risks blending state and national issues, weakening state-level representation.

  • Encourages Horse-Trading: Defections may rise to avoid government collapses, favoring wealthier parties.
  • Diminished Public Participation: Simultaneous elections reduce continuous public engagement and debate.
