About Guruvayur Temple

Guruvayur Temple, located in Guruvayur town, Kerala, is dedicated to Guruvayurappan, a four-armed form of Lord Vishnu. The templeâ€™s origins date back either to the 17th century or 5000 years ago, depending on various accounts.

Key Features

  • The temple is known as Bhuloka Vikuntha, the holy abode of Lord Vishnu on Earth.
  • It is one of the 108 Abhimana Kshethram in the Vaishnavite tradition.
  • It has been rebuilt after being destroyed by the Dutch in 1716, and rebuilt in 1747.
  • Major festivals include the 10-day Kumbham festival, Krishna Janmashtami, Ekadashi, and Vishu.
  • Rudratheertham, a pond where Lord Shiva is said to have bathed for thousands of years, is located at the temple.
  • The temple is home to the largest population of captive male Asian elephants.
  • The architecture is in the Kerala style, featuring a 70-feet-tall flagstaff called Dwajasthamba, fully covered in gold.

Prominent Devotees

  • Melpathur Narayana Bhattathiri
  • Poonthanam Namboothiri
  • Vilwamangalam Swamiyar
