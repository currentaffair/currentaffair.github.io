2024 Arctic Report Card - NOAA Findings

The 2024 Arctic Report Card by the NOAA highlights significant environmental changes in the Arctic, including warming temperatures, thawing permafrost, and the decline in sea ice.

Key Findings

  • Accelerated Warming: The Arctic is warming faster than other regions, with 2024 marking the second-warmest year since 1900.
  • Carbon Source: Permafrost thaw has transformed the Arctic tundra from a carbon sink to a carbon source, releasing COâ‚‚ and methane.
  • Wildfires: Increased frequency and intensity of wildfires are contributing to carbon emissions.
  • Decline in Sea Ice: Reduction in sea ice extent and thickness, further contributing to global warming due to heat absorption by dark ocean surfaces.
  • Rising Sea Levels: Glaciers and the Greenland Ice Sheet continue to contribute to rising sea levels.
  • Impact on Biodiversity: Decline in species like reindeer/caribou, affecting Indigenous communities dependent on them.

Geopolitical and Economic Importance

The Arctic holds significant natural resources, attracting global interest for oil, gas, and minerals. India has been engaged in Arctic research since 2007 and holds observer status in the Arctic Council.
