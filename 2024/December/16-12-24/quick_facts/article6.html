SC Prioritising SLPs Disposal

The Supreme Court (SC) has started prioritising special leave petitions (SLPs) in response to the backlog of over 82,000 cases as of December 2024.

What is a Special Leave Petition (SLP)?

SLP is a discretionary appeal mechanism under Article 136 of the Constitution of India, allowing appeals against judgments from any court or tribunal, excluding Armed Forces Tribunals.

Key Features and Eligibility

SLP is invoked in cases involving substantial legal questions or injustices. Any aggrieved party can file it within 90 days of a High Court judgment or 60 days if a certificate of fitness is denied.

Supreme Court Cases Related to SLPs

Cases such as Laxmi & Co. v. Anand R. Deshpande (1972) and Pritam Singh v. The State (1950) highlight the SC's selective discretion in granting SLPs, ensuring justice rather than merely serving as an appellate body.
