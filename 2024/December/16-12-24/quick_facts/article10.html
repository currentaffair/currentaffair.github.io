The Digital Frontier of Inequality

India’s digital revolution has led to rapid growth in mobile and internet connectivity, but it has also resulted in an increase in tech-facilitated gender-based violence (TFGBV). The country now has over 1.18 billion mobile connections, 700 million internet users, and 600 million smartphones, reflecting tremendous digital growth.

Initiatives & Challenges

The ‘Ab Koi Bahana Nahi’ campaign raises awareness against Gender-Based Violence (GBV) as part of UN Women’s ‘16 Days of Activism.’ The Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana has also helped, with 55.6% of accounts held by women, facilitating rural financial inclusion.

However, India faces challenges like online harassment, cyberstalking, and low digital literacy, especially among rural women. These issues threaten the country’s digital progress and safety.

Measures & Strategies

The legal framework includes the IT Act of 2000 and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2024, aimed at tackling digital crimes. Programs like Digital Shakti promote online safety, while the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal enables anonymous reporting of digital crimes.

To create a safer digital environment, strategies to strengthen laws, promote digital literacy, engage communities, and partner with tech companies for safety tools are essential.
