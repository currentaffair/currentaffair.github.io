Disease X

The outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) in December 2024 has raised concerns about Disease X, causing over 400 deaths.

What is Disease X?

Disease X is a hypothetical term coined by the WHO in 2018 for an unknown pathogen capable of triggering a global pandemic, emphasizing the need for preparedness against emerging diseases.

Origins and Risk Factors

Emerging after the 2014–2016 West African Ebola epidemic, Disease X can originate from viruses, bacteria, parasites, fungi, or prions. It is particularly risky in high biodiversity regions with weak healthcare systems, global connectivity, and factors like zoonotic spillovers, deforestation, and climate change.

Mitigation Strategies

Strengthened surveillance, healthcare systems, and global cooperation are crucial for mitigating risks. Platforms like CEPI and frameworks such as the WHO’s pathogen list, Pandemic Treaty, and Nagoya Protocol are essential for rapid detection and response.

The DRC

The DRC is the second-largest country in Africa and the 11th largest in the world, facing significant challenges related to disease outbreaks.
