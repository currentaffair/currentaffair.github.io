ILO Social Dialogue Report: Key Insights

The International Labour Organisation (ILO) recently released its Social Dialogue Report, highlighting a decline in countries' compliance with freedom of association and collective bargaining rights between 2015 and 2022.

Report Highlights

  • Compliance Decline: A 7% decline in compliance, mainly due to violations of civil liberties and bargaining rights.
  • Recommendation: Governments are urged to respect labour rights and support social dialogue.

What is Peak-Level Social Dialogue (PLSD)?

PLSD refers to collaborative processes involving governments, employers, and workers to address labour, economic, and social issues. These discussions can be:

  • Bipartite: Involving only employers and workers.
  • Tripartite: Including the government for national consultations.

Impact of Social Dialogue

Social dialogue promotes balance between economic development and social progress, aiding in the transition to low-carbon economies and fostering equitable workplaces for both economic growth and social justice.

About the ILO

  • Established: 1919 as part of the League of Nations.
  • Members: 187 member states.
  • Awarded: Nobel Peace Prize in 1969 for its role in promoting international peace and worker justice.
