Human Evolution and Migration

Why in News?

Scientists confirm that Homo sapiens evolved in Africa and migrated globally, though the routes and timing remain debated. Two main migration theories exist: Coastal Dispersion and Inland Dispersion.

Human Evolution

Human evolution traces the emergence of Homo sapiens, evolving from primates, with key stages including Dryopithecus, Ramapithecus, Australopithecus, and various Homo species.

Route of Human Migration

Genetic studies confirm the out-of-Africa migration, but the timing and routes are debated, with two theories proposed:

Coastal Dispersion Theory

This theory suggests humans migrated along coasts, benefiting from warm climates and abundant food. However, archaeological evidence in India contradicts this theory.

Inland Dispersion Model

The inland migration model is supported by research from Gujarat, showing early humans used terrestrial routes. The absence of coastal resources further supports this theory.

Conclusion

Studies reveal that migration was complex, involving both coastal and inland routes, necessitating further research and interpretation.
