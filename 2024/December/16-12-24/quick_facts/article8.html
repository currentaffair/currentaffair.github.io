Caspian Sea

Kazakhstan's state-owned energy company, KazMunayGas, has successfully decontaminated oil waste along the Caspian Sea’s shores, addressing pollution and global warming effects.

Location

The Caspian Sea lies between Asia and Europe, bordered by Russia (northwest), Azerbaijan (west), Iran (south), Turkmenistan (southeast), and Kazakhstan (northeast).

Formation & Characteristics

Once part of the prehistoric Paratethys Sea, tectonic forces and a drop in sea level caused the Caspian to become landlocked over 5 million years ago. It is the world's largest inland water body, though technically a lake without a direct ocean outlet.

Rivers & Resources

The Caspian Sea receives water from three major rivers: Volga, Ural, and Terek. It is rich in oil and natural gas reserves and is famous for producing most of the world’s caviar.
