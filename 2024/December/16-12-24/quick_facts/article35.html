Indian Dominance in High-Paid H-1B Visa Applications

Overview

Recent data shows that Indian professionals make up a large share of high-paying H-1B visa applicants, with smaller U.S. firms sponsoring many of them.

Key Findings

  • Indian Dominance: 78% of H-1B applicants earning over $1M annually are Indian, with over 25% of them being women.
  • Company Sponsorship: 65% of these high-paid Indian applicants are sponsored by smaller U.S. companies, which tend to offer higher salaries than Indian-based firms.
  • H-1B Process: The annual lottery for H-1B visas takes place in April, where employers submit petitions with salary and job details.
  • Data Summary: Out of approximately 100,000 approved petitions (2021-2024), only 123 had salaries above $1M, with 96 of those from India.

Conclusion

The data highlights the dominance of Indian professionals in the high-paying H-1B segment and the role of smaller U.S. companies in sponsoring these applicants.
