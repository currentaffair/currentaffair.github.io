ILO Social Dialogue Report Summary

Report Highlights

The ILO report reveals a 7% decline in countries' compliance with freedom of association and collective bargaining rights between 2015 and 2022. This is due to increased violations of civil liberties and workers' rights. The report calls for governments to strengthen labour rights and provide necessary resources for effective social dialogue.

Peak-Level Social Dialogue (PLSD)

PLSD is a collaborative process between governments, employers, and workers' organizations to address labour, economic, and social issues. It can be bipartite (employers and workers) or tripartite (including government) for broader consultations.

Impact of Social Dialogue

Social dialogue contributes to balanced economic development, equitable workplaces, and social justice. It supports the transition to low-carbon economies and digital innovations while ensuring that all groups benefit from economic growth.

International Labour Organization (ILO)

Founded in 1919, the ILO is a specialized UN agency with 187 member states. It is known for its tripartite structure, including representatives from governments, employers, and workers. The ILO won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1969 for its work in improving worker justice and peace.
