Consumer Confidence Survey: RBI Findings

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) conducted a bi-monthly Consumer Confidence Survey (CCS), which reveals a weakening in consumers' confidence regarding the economy, employment, income, and spending.

Key Findings

  • Current Situation Index (CSI): Dropped by 0.7 points (from 94.7 in September 2024 to 94 in November 2024), indicating slight pessimism in the economic outlook.
  • Future Expectations Index (FEI): Increased by 0.5 points to 121.9, showing optimism about future economic conditions despite current concerns.
  • Inflation Perception: Median perception of current inflation rose to 8.4%. One-year ahead inflation expectation increased to 10.1%, driven by rising food and housing costs.

Survey Significance

The Consumer Confidence Survey (CCS) helps evaluate consumer sentiment towards India's economy, with the results used to compute the Consumer Confidence Index (CCI), offering insights into economic trends.
