Pangolins and Conservation Efforts

About Pangolins

Pangolins are the most trafficked mammals, sought for their meat and scales used in traditional medicine in Asia and Africa. They feed on ants and termites, and have a distinctive armor of scales.

Pangolin Species

Africa: Black-bellied, White-bellied, Giant Ground, and Temminck's Ground pangolins.
Asia: Indian, Chinese, Philippine, and Sunda pangolins.

Conservation Status

The species range from Vulnerable to Critically Endangered. Pangolins are protected under international trade bans and listed in Appendix I of the IUCN Red List.

Recent Efforts

Authorities in Nigeria seized 2.18 tonnes of pangolin scales, marking a significant step in the fight against pangolin trafficking.
