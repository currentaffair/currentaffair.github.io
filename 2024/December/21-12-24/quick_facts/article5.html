Carbon Markets in India

What are Carbon Markets?

Carbon markets allow businesses to offset emissions by purchasing carbon credits from projects that reduce emissions. In India, markets are voluntary or compliance-based.

Carbon Pricing & Mitigating Climate Change

Carbon pricing provides financial incentives for businesses to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, supporting the global climate change mitigation efforts.

Key Principles of Carbon Credit Projects

The principles of additionality and permanence govern carbon credit projects. These ensure that emission reductions are genuine and long-lasting.

Measuring Carbon in India

Technologies like remote sensing, satellite imagery, and drones are improving the accuracy of carbon measurement and emissions tracking in India.

Carbon Markets' Potential in Agriculture

Carbon markets can transform Indian agriculture by rewarding farmers for adopting sustainable practices that reduce emissions, providing both environmental and financial benefits.

Challenges Facing Carbon Farming

Challenges include lack of inclusivity for small farmers, delayed payments, and insufficient training. Addressing these issues is crucial for expanding India's carbon farming sector.

Improving Carbon Farming Projects

To improve, India should focus on inclusivity, fair payments, better training, and collaboration with research institutions for better techniques and yield.
