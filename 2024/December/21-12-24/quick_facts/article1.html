Nanoplastics Causing Antibiotic Resistance

Key Highlights of the Study

Recent studies show that nanoplastics, especially from single-use plastic bottles, contribute to the spread of antibiotic resistance (AR), posing a serious health risk.

Risk to Gut Microbiome

Nanoplastics can transform gut bacteria like Lactobacillus acidophilus into carriers of AR genes, which may then be transferred to harmful bacteria, worsening the AR crisis.

Horizontal Gene Transfer (HGT)

Nanoplastics facilitate the transfer of AR genes between bacteria through Horizontal Gene Transfer (HGT), allowing bacteria to acquire resistance from others.

AR Gene Transfer Mechanism

Two key mechanisms: Direct Transformation, where nanoplastics carry AR genes across bacterial membranes, and OMV-Induced Transfer, where oxidative stress triggers gene transfer via outer membrane vesicles (OMVs).

What Are Nanoplastics?

Nanoplastics are tiny plastic particles (1 nm to 1000 nm), either intentionally produced (primary) or formed from the breakdown of larger plastics (secondary). They can disrupt ecosystems and harm health.

Concerns with Nanoplastics

Nanoplastics cause environmental damage, bioaccumulation, and toxicity, leading to digestive issues, immune dysfunction, and higher infection risks.
