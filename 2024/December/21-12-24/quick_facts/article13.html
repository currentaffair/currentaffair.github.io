Golan Heights Overview

Why in News?

The Golan Heights has become a focal point as Israel plans to double its population in the strategically important region.

Key Facts About Golan Heights

The Golan Heights is a 1,200 km² plateau in southwestern Syria, overlooking the Jordan River Valley. The region is crucial for water supply, and the name "Golan" originates from a biblical city of refuge.

Historical Context

Israel captured the Golan Heights from Syria in the 1967 Six-Day War and annexed it in 1981. The annexation was not recognized internationally, but the US acknowledged Israeli sovereignty in 2019.

Military Significance

The Golan Heights serves as a security buffer zone for Israel and allows surveillance of Syria and Lebanon.

International Position

India voted in favor of a UN resolution demanding Israel’s withdrawal from the Golan Heights in December 2024, stressing the illegality of Israeli settlements.
