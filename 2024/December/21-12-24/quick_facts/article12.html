Baiga Tribe & Jodhaiya Bai

Context

Madhya Pradesh's famous Baiga tribal artist Jodhaiya Bai, a Padma Shri recipient, passed away in Umaria district after a prolonged illness.

About Baiga Tribe

The Baiga tribe is one of India's Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTG), primarily based in Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Bihar, Odisha, West Bengal, Madhya Pradesh, and Uttar Pradesh.

Traditional Procedures

Traditionally semi-nomadic, the Baiga tribe practiced slash-and-burn cultivation ('Bewar') and relied on minor forest produce. Tattoos, made using kajal from Ramtilla seeds, are integral to their culture, with specific designs for different body parts and age groups.

Cultural Practices

The Mahua tree is central to their culture, fermented to make an intoxicating drink. Bamboo is a vital resource in their daily lives, and the Baiga tribe was the first in India to be granted habitat rights, showcasing their deep connection with forests.

Jodhaiya Bai

Jodhaiya Bai played an instrumental role in bringing Baiga tribal art international recognition. She was awarded the Padma Shri in 2023 for her outstanding contributions to art, and her artwork depicting Baiga culture has been exhibited in many countries worldwide.
