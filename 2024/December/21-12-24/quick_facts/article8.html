Shanan Hydel Project Dispute

Context

Recently, Haryana has added a new dimension to the legal dispute over the 110 MW Shanan Hydel Project between Himachal Pradesh and Punjab.

About the Project

Commissioned in 1932, India's first megawatt-capacity hydroelectric project is located in Joginder Nagar, Himachal Pradesh, on the Uhl River. Leased to Punjab in 1925 for 99 years, the lease expires in March 2024, with Himachal Pradesh seeking ownership.

The Dispute

The project was originally leased by Raja Joginder Bahadur of Mandi to Colonel BC Batty in 1925. Following India's independence, the project served undivided Punjab and later Punjab alone after partition.

Legal Issues

In 1966, the project was transferred to Punjab under the Punjab Reorganization Act. Himachal Pradesh claims prior ownership and control, citing the deteriorating condition of the project.

Claims and Actions

Punjab argues its rights based on the 1967 Central Notification, while Himachal seeks the Center's support for transferring control. The Supreme Court and interim measures have been invoked to maintain the status quo.

Future Implications

Both states are expected to resolve the dispute through legal proceedings, with a cooperative approach supported by federal authorities.
