Wroughton's Free-Tailed Bat and Conservation

About Wroughton's Free-Tailed Bat

The Wroughtonâ€™s free-tailed bat is a rare species known for its large size, prominent ears, and two-tone velvety fur. It roosts in caves and other dark, warm environments, often forming medium-sized colonies.

Distribution

Primarily found in the Western Ghats with only one known breeding colony. Other sightings include the Jaintia Hills of Meghalaya and Cambodia.

Conservation Status

Previously classified as Critically Endangered (until 2000), it is now listed as "data deficient" by the IUCN and protected under Schedule I of the Nature Conservation Act (1972). This species is important for insect regulation and plant pollination.

Importance of Delhi's Bats

Delhi is home to 14 bat species, including some that are locally extinct. It also houses the only known roost of Blyth's sparrowhawks in the Delhi NCR region.

About Yamuna Biodiversity Park

The park, located along the Yamuna River in Delhi, serves as a habitat for migratory and resident bird species. It also plays a role in groundwater recharge and the preservation of wild genetic resources of crops.
