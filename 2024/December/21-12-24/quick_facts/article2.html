Ganges River Dolphin Conservation

About the Ganges River Dolphin

The Ganges River Dolphin is an apex predator and ecosystem indicator species, unique to India's freshwater systems. Known as "Susu" for its breathing sound, it has been the national aquatic animal since 2009.

Habitat and Adaptation

Adapted to freshwater environments, these dolphins rely on echolocation for navigation. They are nearly blind, breathing every 5-30 seconds at the water's surface.

Conservation Efforts

Protected by laws such as the IUCN endangered status and the Nature Conservation Act. The Vikramshila Gangetic Dolphin Wildlife Sanctuary in Bihar and Project Dolphin focus on their conservation.

Conservation Status

Listed as endangered by the IUCN and protected under various international agreements.

Other River Dolphin Species

  • Indus River Dolphin: Threatened, found in Pakistan.
  • Amazon River Dolphin: Vulnerable, found in South America.
  • Irrawaddy Dolphin: Endangered, found in Southeast Asia.
  • Tucuxi: Data Deficient, found in South America.
  • Baiji Dolphin: Extinct, once found in China's Yangtze River.
