How India is Doing Better in the War Against TB
Introduction

India has been a global leader in implementing innovative strategies to tackle tuberculosis (TB). Over the years, these measures have brought significant improvements in TB prevention, diagnosis, and treatment, showcasing the nation's commitment to eliminating this disease.

Key Achievements in TB Elimination
  • According to the WHO’s Global TB Report 2024, India achieved a 17.7% decline in TB incidence from 2015 to 2023, double the global average.
  • India diagnosed 25.1 lakh TB patients in 2023, with treatment coverage increasing from 59% in 2015 to 85% in 2023.
100-Day Campaign

On December 7, 2024, the government launched a 100-day campaign targeting 347 high-burden TB districts. This initiative aims to:

  • Identify every TB patient early and ensure timely, quality treatment.
  • Encourage collaboration among elected representatives, health practitioners, civil society, corporations, and communities under the spirit of Jan Bhagidari (participatory governance).
Nikshay Poshan Yojana (NPY)
  • Introduced in April 2018, this nutritional support scheme provides financial aid to TB patients through Direct Benefit Transfer.
  • Since its inception, Rs 3,295 crore has been disbursed to 1.16 crore beneficiaries.
  • The monthly support under NPY was recently doubled from Rs 500 to Rs 1,000 in November 2024.
Pradhan Mantri TB Mukt Bharat Abhiyaan (PMTBMBA)

This initiative addresses the nutritional, vocational, and psychological needs of TB patients, making TB care a national movement:

  • It mobilized 1.75 lakh Nikshay Mitras to distribute 21 lakh food baskets to patients across the country.
  • By engaging communities, the program fostered greater awareness and support for TB elimination efforts.
Introduction of New Drugs and Tests

India has advanced TB treatment through the adoption of newer drugs and diagnostic technologies:

  • New Drugs: Bedaquiline and Delamanid have improved treatment outcomes for drug-resistant TB patients.
  • BPaLM Regimen: A shorter and effective 6-month treatment for drug-resistant TB, compared to conventional 19–20-month regimens.
  • Molecular Diagnostic Machines: India now has 8,293 molecular diagnostic machines in every district, a sharp rise from just a few hundred in 2014–15.
  • Indigenous molecular tests, endorsed by the WHO, were developed under the Make in India initiative.
Conclusion and Way Forward

India’s efforts in TB elimination exemplify its leadership in global healthcare. From pioneering research to community-driven initiatives, the country has made remarkable progress. The intensified 100-day campaign underscores India’s collective commitment to eradicating TB. Moving forward, massive Jan Bhagidari in identification, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention remains crucial to achieving the goal of a TB-free India by 2025.
