School Closures in Delhi Due to Air Pollution: A Critical Analysis

Introduction

The decision to shift schools in Delhi to online mode in November 2024 as part of the Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP), aimed at mitigating the severe air pollution, has raised significant concerns. While GRAP is designed to protect public health during pollution episodes, the temporary closure of schools brings forward crucial debates regarding the scientific, practical, and socio-economic implications of such measures.

Understanding the Air Quality Index (AQI) Problem in Delhi

Health Impact Beyond AQI 400

Air pollution in Delhi begins to harm human health once the AQI exceeds 50, categorized as "good" under Indian norms. However, AQI levels between 51-399, though often normalized by GRAP thresholds, are also harmful, with acute risks to vulnerable populations. This raises concerns about the long-term exposure of children and adults to harmful air, especially as Delhi has seen no "good" air quality days in 2024 and only one such day in 2023.

This constant exposure makes a case for more structural solutions instead of relying on temporary measures like school closures.

Impacts of School Closures

Learning and Development Loss

While online education offers a quick fix, it fails to replicate the dynamic in-person learning experience. Classroom interactions, critical thinking, and engagement are severely impacted, especially for young children under the age of 12, who are particularly vulnerable to screen-based learning.

Social and Emotional Development

Schools are crucial for socializing children, teaching life skills, and providing emotional support. Online classes fail to meet these needs, and prolonged isolation disrupts children's socialization, as evidenced during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Equity and Access Challenges

Digital Divide

A major concern is the digital divide. Many marginalized families lack access to reliable internet or digital devices, which exacerbates educational inequality. Urban-rural disparities further widen this gap, leaving large sections of the population disconnected from online learning opportunities.

Science-Based Alternatives to School Closures

Infrastructure Upgrades

Instead of closing schools, a more sustainable solution is to upgrade infrastructure. Installing and maintaining air purifiers in classrooms and ensuring proper ventilation can mitigate the effects of poor air quality.

Customized Mask Usage

Rather than blanket mask mandates, the government should recommend masks based on individual needs, such as for students with respiratory conditions or in environments without air purifiers.

Allowing hybrid options for concerned parents would ensure flexibility without compromising learning continuity for the majority.

Long-Term Preventive Strategies

Strengthening Healthcare Protocols

Healthcare measures such as annual health check-ups and vaccinations for diseases exacerbated by air pollution, such as influenza and pneumonia, can protect children's health in the long run.

Community Awareness and Investment

Community education on air quality, mask usage, and preventive health measures is critical. Subsidizing the installation of air purifiers and green barriers in schools can help improve indoor air quality and protect children from the adverse effects of pollution.

International Best Practices

Countries like Beijing, South Korea, and Germany have implemented successful strategies for managing air pollution and protecting children in schools. From air quality alerts to the installation of high-efficiency air purifiers and green barriers, these countries show that structural solutions can be effective in maintaining a safe learning environment despite poor air quality.

Conclusion

As Nelson Mandela aptly said, “There can be no keener revelation of a society’s soul than the way in which it treats its children.” While school closures offer a temporary solution to immediate air pollution crises, they are not sustainable in the long term. The real focus should be on investing in infrastructure, enhancing healthcare protocols, and raising awareness to protect the health and well-being of children, while simultaneously addressing the root causes of pollution.
