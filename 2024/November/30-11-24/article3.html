The Constitution at 75: A Gift to Independent India

Introduction

The Indian Constitution, at 75, stands as a remarkable testament to the vision and wisdom of India's founding fathers. Despite the challenges faced by a young nation marked by diversity, the Constitution has not only endured but thrived, adapting to the evolving needs of its people. This article delves into the journey of the Indian Constitution, its drafting, unique features, and its enduring relevance in safeguarding democracy and justice.

The Longevity of the Indian Constitution

Constitutions across the globe have an average lifespan of only 17 years. In comparison, India's Constitution has completed 75 years, a feat unmatched in South Asia, where Sri Lanka, Pakistan, and Nepal have replaced their constitutions multiple times.
