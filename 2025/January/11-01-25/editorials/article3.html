Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue

Introduction

Every year, January 12 is celebrated across India as ‘National Youth Festival’, which coincides with the birth anniversary of Swami Vivekananda. The Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue is a forward-thinking initiative launched with the goal of engaging the youth of India in the nation’s developmental journey. This dialogue aims to tap into the collective energy, creativity, and leadership potential of young minds to contribute towards realizing a Viksit Bharat (Developed India).

In line with Prime Minister Narendra Modi's vision, which emphasizes the central role of youth in India’s growth, this platform enables young leaders to engage in discussions, exchange ideas, and collaborate on solutions to address the country’s challenges. The competition and its stages have been designed to inspire critical thinking and proactive involvement in the nation’s progress.

Purpose: A Dialogue with a Competitive Edge

The Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue is a dynamic, multi-stage initiative created to engage and empower India's youth in its development process. Each stage of the competition tests participants' knowledge, creativity, leadership, and communication skills.

Competition Stages

Stage 1: Viksit Bharat Quiz

The first stage takes place digitally on the My Bharat platform. It focuses on testing participants’ knowledge regarding India’s achievements, challenges, and the vision for its future, touching on aspects like sustainable development, technology, and national policies. Top scorers advance to the second stage.

Stage 2: Essay and Blog Writing

Participants write essays or blogs on themes such as "Tech for Viksit Bharat" and "Empowering Youth for Viksit Bharat", providing their insights and visions for India’s future. Submissions are evaluated for originality.

Stage 3: Viksit Bharat Vision Pitch Deck

Participants at the state level present their innovative development ideas through a compelling pitch deck. The best teams from each state move on to the fourth and final stage.

Stage 4: Viksit Bharat National Championship

The finale is held at the Bharat Mandapam in New Delhi on January 11-12, 2025. In this stage, selected teams present their visionary solutions to the Prime Minister. This is an exclusive opportunity to directly influence national policy and contribute to India’s vision of a Viksit Bharat.

Skills Tested

The competition tests not only knowledge and creativity but also leadership and communication skills. The format challenges participants to think critically and solve real-world problems in innovative ways.

The Features for 2025

Purpose of the National Youth Festival

The National Youth Festival is an annual celebration aimed at promoting national integration and empowering the youth. This year’s theme, "Youth for Sustainable Development," focuses on recognizing the role of young people as key drivers of global change. The festival features workshops, seminars, and cultural activities focused on creating a greener, more inclusive future.

Focus on Technology and Innovation

This year, there is an emphasis on technology and innovation as crucial elements in achieving India’s national goals. Several sessions are dedicated to "Tech for Good" and "Innovation for India’s Development," where young innovators showcase their solutions.

Expanded Virtual Participation

The expanded virtual participation allows youth from remote areas to engage in discussions, workshops, and showcase their ideas online, ensuring broader inclusivity.

Key Features of the 2025 National Youth Festival

Workshops on leadership, innovation, and sustainability, tech showcases addressing health, education, agriculture, and urban development, and direct engagement with leaders from government, business, and academia will empower young participants to shape their communities.

The Role of MY Bharat

MY Bharat, an autonomous body under the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, plays an instrumental role in guiding the direction of the National Youth Festival. It provides youth with the resources, mentorship, and opportunities to actively participate in nation-building activities.

Conclusion

India’s youth is undoubtedly its most precious asset. As the country strives towards its vision of becoming a Viksit Bharat, it is the energy, creativity, and ambition of young people that will power the nation’s transformation. The Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue will not only open new chapters in youth engagement but also shape the future direction of national leadership. Swami Vivekananda’s belief in the limitless potential of youth energy resonates with the vision of a bright future for India, shaped by its young minds.
