India’s Defence and AI: Challenges and Opportunities

Introduction

As the world accelerates its use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) for military purposes, India is working on integrating AI systems within its military ecosystem. The defence budget of ₹6.21 lakh crore ($75 billion) in the past year emphasized upgrading and modernizing India's military capabilities. Investments from tech giants like Microsoft are also contributing to the growth of AI technology within the country. Despite these strides, India faces several challenges in fully capitalizing on AI's potential in defence.

AI's Potential for Military Operations

AI's potential in military operations is vast, ranging from predictive analytics to autonomous decision-making systems. Union Defence Minister Rajnath Singh has highlighted AI’s ability to revolutionize military operations. AI-driven systems can improve defence readiness by streamlining decision-making, offering real-time analysis, and enhancing operational efficiency. However, integrating these technologies remains an ongoing challenge due to infrastructure and funding constraints.

India’s International AI Initiatives

India is also actively participating in international AI initiatives, positioning itself as a key player in military AI. While these international collaborations can facilitate knowledge-sharing, India still faces several internal challenges regarding the implementation of AI technologies within its military infrastructure.

Challenges to AI Adoption

The lack of digitized data to train AI systems, combined with a shortage of funds, remains a significant obstacle. Additionally, setting up data centres necessary for AI systems incurs a high cost. The Indian military’s need to replace legacy systems adds further strain to its budget, making the implementation of AI technologies more difficult.

Policy and Regulatory Challenges

India’s policies regarding AI integration into the military are still fragmented. The National Strategy for Artificial Intelligence outlines the country’s vision for AI but lacks concrete mechanisms for deployment. Similarly, the Responsible AI for All paper emphasizes the importance of accountability but does not provide detailed guidance on military applications. A more comprehensive policy framework is necessary to guide AI’s safe and ethical deployment in military operations.

Efforts to Bridge the Gaps: DAIC and DAIPA

The establishment of the Defence Artificial Intelligence Council (DAIC) and the Defence AI Project Agency (DAIPA) signifies India’s intent to bridge the gaps in AI adoption within its military. These bodies aim to address the technological and policy challenges. However, there have been limited updates on their progress, and greater transparency is needed to ensure effective governance.

The International Picture

Global Focus on AI Integration

Countries like Israel and China have made rapid strides in deploying AI within their military frameworks, positioning themselves as global leaders in military AI. Their technologies are already far ahead, and their quick adoption provides valuable lessons for India to accelerate its own AI initiatives.

India’s Need for a Clear Vision

For India to catch up with the global AI revolution, it needs to adopt a clear and coherent vision for AI in the military. Inconsistent government attitudes and a lack of clarity on the military’s AI roadmap have hindered the rollout of these technologies. As seen with the Global Partnership on Artificial Intelligence (GPAI) in 2023, there is a consensus on AI’s critical importance, but the methods for implementation remain vague.

Concerns Regarding AI’s Dangers

Despite the potential of AI, there are widespread concerns about its dangers. India’s Minister of External Affairs has compared AI to nuclear weapons, underscoring its potential global risks. Prime Minister Modi has also expressed caution, warning of AI’s darker sides. This cautious approach is understandable, but it should not slow down progress in AI adoption for military purposes.

The Specific Problems in India

Historically Siloed Armed Forces

One of India’s greatest challenges is the historically siloed nature of its armed forces. The Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force operate under different doctrines, systems, and communication practices, making joint operations and interoperability difficult. This fragmentation could hinder the efficient integration of AI systems across the forces.

Procurement Challenges for AI Systems

The reliance on public sector units (PSUs) for AI system procurement also poses a challenge. Though there is a misconception that most defence manufacturers in India are public sector entities, private companies and startups produce advanced systems that could contribute to military AI. Encouraging public-private partnerships (PPPs) could lead to faster and more effective AI system deployment.

Need for Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs)

Public-private partnerships have proven successful in other sectors like space technology, and they could play a crucial role in the development of AI systems for the military. These partnerships can foster innovation and help provide advanced, cost-effective systems at scale.

Conclusion

India has made notable progress in integrating AI into its defence ecosystem. However, several impediments remain, including policy gaps, siloed operations, and procurement challenges. A cohesive strategy that emphasizes innovation, transparency, and public-private collaboration will be essential for the successful deployment of AI in India's military. With a clear vision and robust frameworks, India can effectively harness the full potential of AI in defence.
