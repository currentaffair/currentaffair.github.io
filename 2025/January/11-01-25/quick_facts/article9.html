Climate Records of 2024

Key Highlights

  • Global temperatures rose 1.60°C above pre-industrial levels.
  • Global average temperature: 15.10°C (0.72°C above the 1991-2020 average).
  • First calendar year where global temperature exceeded 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.
  • Warmest day on record: 22 July 2024, with 17.16°C global average temperature.

Regional Impacts

  • Europe: Warmest year on record with a 1.47°C increase above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Arctic: Temperatures 1.34°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Oceans: Sea surface temperature reached a record high of 20.87°C.

Climate Indicators (2024)

  • CO₂ levels: 422 ppm, a 2.9 ppm increase from 2023.
  • Methane levels: 1897 ppb, a 3 ppb increase from 2023.
  • Atmospheric water vapour: 5% above the 1991-2020 average.

Key Takeaways

  • Climate crisis is escalating with multiple records broken in 2024.
  • Paris Agreement’s 1.5°C limit is at risk of being permanently breached.
  • Urgent need for evidence-based climate action to limit global warming.
