2024: The First Year to Breach Global Warming Limit

Key Temperature Data

  • 2024’s global mean temperature: 15.1°C, 0.72°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • 1.6°C above the pre-industrial period (1850-1900), breaking long-term records.
  • All years from 2015 to 2024 are among the top 10 warmest on record.

Implications of Breaching 1.5°C

  • Marks a critical point in the climate crisis, with severe consequences predicted.
  • On track to exceed 2°C by 2050, endangering ecosystems and human life.
  • Carbon emissions and El Niño effects are key contributors to rising temperatures.

Sea Surface Temperature (SST) Records

  • Annual average SST: 20.87°C, 0.51°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Warmest SST from January to June 2024; second warmest July to December after 2023.

Experts’ Warnings

  • Every fraction of a degree adds harm to ecosystems and people (Joeri Rogelj).
  • Climate disasters intensify in developing economies (Vaibhav Chaturvedi).
  • Failure of COP29 to deliver finance hampers mitigation efforts.

Urgency for Immediate Action

The data emphasizes the need for global emissions cuts to prevent further warming, as climate talks fail to generate meaningful progress.
