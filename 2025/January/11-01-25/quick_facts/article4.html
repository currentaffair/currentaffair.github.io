Iran’s Fast-Aging Population Fuels Economic Fears

Government Response and Public Campaigns

  • Iran promotes pro-natalist sentiments via billboards encouraging larger families.
  • Economic challenges (inflation, unemployment) prevent many from expanding their families.

Economic Impact of an Aging Population

  • The aging population risks shrinking Iran's labor force and increasing financial pressure on younger generations.
  • Potential labor shortages could slow economic growth, especially in sectors like technology and education.

Declining Population Growth

  • Population growth has slowed from 1.29% in 2011 to 0.6% in 2023.
  • Changing social norms and economic difficulties contribute to the decline in birth rates.

Long-Term Outlook

  • Iran’s population could shrink by more than half in the next 75 years, posing severe economic and social challenges.
  • Policies encouraging higher birth rates and fostering economic innovation are necessary.

Conclusion

Iran’s aging population and declining birth rates pose significant economic challenges. These demographic trends could lead to a shrinking workforce, increased social welfare burdens, and slower economic growth.
