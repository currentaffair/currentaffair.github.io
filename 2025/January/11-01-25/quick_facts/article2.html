2024 Breaches Global Warming Limit

Temperature Records and Data

The year 2024 marked a significant moment in the climate crisis, being the first to surpass the 1.5°C threshold above pre-industrial levels. The mean global temperature reached 15.1°C, which is 0.72°C above the 1991-2020 average and 1.6°C above the 1850-1900 period.

Implications of Breaching 1.5°C

Experts emphasize that this milestone signals a point of no return. If warming trends continue, the global temperature could exceed 2°C by 2050, with severe consequences for ecosystems and human life.

Sea Surface Temperature (SST) Records

2024 also recorded the highest annual average SST at 20.87°C, exacerbating the impacts of global warming. SST trends indicate accelerated warming, with January to June 2024 being the warmest period on record.

Warnings from Experts

Joeri Rogelj warns of increased harm from each fraction of a degree of warming, while Vaibhav Chaturvedi highlights the disproportionate impacts on developing economies and the failure of global climate talks to secure funding for mitigation.

Call for Immediate Action

The data underscores the urgent need to reduce global emissions to mitigate further warming and avoid catastrophic climate consequences.
