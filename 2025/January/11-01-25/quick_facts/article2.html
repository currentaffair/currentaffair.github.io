GDP Growth Slump in India: Economic Challenges and Solutions

Overview

India's GDP growth projection has decreased from 8.2% in 2023-24 to below 6.5-7%, signaling a slowdown in the economy despite a nominal growth rate of 9.7%.

Key Issues

  • Data Discrepancies: Reliance on the volatile Wholesale Price Index (WPI) distorts real GDP calculations.
  • Private Investment Challenges: Despite tax cuts, private corporate investment remains low, hindering economic revival.
  • Sectoral Slowdown: Manufacturing, mining, power, and services show broad economic contraction.

Fiscal and Revenue Challenges

  • Slower Tax Revenue Growth: Tax targets have not been met, affecting public spending plans.
  • Need for Revenue Mobilization: A new tax policy, focusing on wealth and profit taxes, is necessary to boost capital expenditure and welfare spending.

Conclusion

A revised approach to tax policy is needed to address the economic slowdown, support capital expenditure, and ensure public welfare initiatives.
