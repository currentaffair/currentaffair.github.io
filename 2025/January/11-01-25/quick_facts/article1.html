Three Million Sudan Children Facing Acute Malnutrition

Conflict and Humanitarian Crisis

The war between the Sudanese Army and Rapid Support Forces (RSF) has led to a humanitarian crisis, displacing 12 million people and causing tens of thousands of deaths. Infrastructure destruction has hindered access to essential resources like food and medical care.

Severe Malnutrition Among Children

Over 3.2 million children under five face acute malnutrition in 2025, with 772,000 at risk of severe acute malnutrition (SAM), which is life-threatening without urgent treatment.

Famine and Food Insecurity

Famine grips five regions in Sudan due to disrupted agricultural production and trade. Rising food prices and violence hinder humanitarian aid distribution, exacerbating food insecurity.

Health Crisis

The healthcare system has collapsed, with hospitals destroyed and staff fleeing. Preventable diseases like diarrhea and respiratory infections are widespread, increasing child mortality risks.

International Response

Humanitarian efforts face challenges due to security issues and limited access to conflict zones. Political leaders’ failure to reach peace agreements continues to prolong the crisis.

The Future of Sudan’s Children

Malnutrition affects children’s development, education, and future prospects. Without intervention, Sudan’s children may face long-term socioeconomic challenges.
