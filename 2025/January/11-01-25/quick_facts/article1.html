Freshwater Species Extinction Threats

Overview

4,294 species out of 23,496 assessed on the IUCN Red List are threatened, with 89 confirmed and 178 suspected extinctions. Freshwater species, including decapod crustaceans, fishes, and odonates, are at high risk.

Causes of Freshwater Decline

  • Agriculture (pollution, water extraction)
  • Invasive species
  • Dams and water management
  • Climate change (direct and indirect impacts)

Impact on Ecosystem Services

  • Freshwater ecosystems support 10% of all species globally, one-third of vertebrates, and half of all fish species.
  • Provide vital services: nutrient cycling, flood control, climate change mitigation.

Major Threats Identified

  • Pollution
  • Overharvesting
  • Dams and water extraction
  • Climate change

Urgency of Action

There is an urgent need for conservation actions addressing the threats to freshwater species. IUCN's Catherine Sayer stresses the importance of protecting freshwater biodiversity.
