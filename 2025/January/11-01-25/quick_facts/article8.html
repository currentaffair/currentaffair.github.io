Modhweth Festival

Why in News?

The Toda tribe of the Nilgiris Hills celebrated their traditional 'Modhweth' festival to mark the New Year.

What is the Modhweth Festival?

  • Held annually on the last Sunday of December or first Sunday of January at the Moonpo temple.
  • Prayers are offered to the deity, Thenkish Amman, for health, rains, and harvest.
  • Participants perform a dance outside the temple.
  • Toda youth lift a greased boulder (80 kg) to showcase strength and masculinity.
  • Women do not participate in the celebrations as per tradition.

About the Toda Tribe

  • A pastoral tribe of the Nilgiri Hills, classified as a Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Group (PVTG).
  • The Toda language is distinct among Dravidian languages.
  • They follow a pantheon of gods, including Tökisy (goddess) and Ön (god of the underworld).

Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve

  • First biosphere reserve in India, established in 1986, spanning Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Kerala.
  • Home to several tribal groups, including Adiyan, Aranadan, Kader, Kurichian, Kuruman, and Kurumbas.
  • Famous for its biodiversity, including animals like Nilgiri tahr, Nilgiri langur, and elephants.
