Global Climate Change 2024: Record Temperatures and Urgent Action Needed

Global temperatures in 2024 rose to 1.60°C above pre-industrial levels, surpassing the previous record holder, 2023. For the first time, the average global temperature exceeded 1.5°C, marking a significant milestone in climate change.

Regional Impacts and Climate Indicators

  • Europe: Warmest year with a temperature of 10.67°C, 1.47°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Arctic: Temperatures 1.34°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Oceans: Record sea surface temperature of 20.87°C, 0.51°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): Reached 422 ppm, an increase of 2.9 ppm from 2023.
  • Methane (CH₄): Reached 1897 ppb, an increase of 3 ppb from 2023.
  • Atmospheric Water Vapour: Increased by 5%, a record high in 2024.

Human Impact and Climate Stress

44% of the globe faced 'strong' to 'extreme heat stress' in 2024, contributing to widespread disruptions, including unprecedented heatwaves and heavy rainfall events.

Experts’ Statements on Climate Crisis

  • Carlo Buontempo (Copernicus Climate Change Service): "Humanity is in charge of its own destiny, and how we respond to the climate challenge should be evidence-based."
  • Samantha Burgess (ECMWF): "We are now teetering on the edge of passing the 1.5ºC level defined in the Paris Agreement."

Key Takeaways

The climate crisis is accelerating rapidly, with numerous records broken in 2024. Immediate and decisive action is required to prevent a permanent breach of the 1.5ºC limit set by the Paris Agreement.
