Debate Over National Anthem

Why in News?

The Governor of Tamil Nadu left the Legislative Assembly without delivering his customary address due to non-playing of the National Anthem, triggering a dispute over state legislature practices.

National Anthem & National Song

National Anthem: Jana-gana-mana, composed by Rabindranath Tagore in 1911, was adopted in Hindi. It was first sung at the 1911 Calcutta Session of the INC.

National Song: Vande Mataram, written by Bankim Chandra Chatterjee, first sung at the 1896 INC session. Both were adopted by the Constituent Assembly on 24th January 1950.

Protocols and Conventions

  • Presidential Address: National Anthem played as President reaches the dais and as they exit.
  • Governor’s Address: Different states follow different practices; for example, Tamil Nadu plays State Anthem before the Governor’s address and the National Anthem after.

National Anthem in Cinemas

In 2018, the Supreme Court ruled that the playing of the National Anthem in cinemas is optional, not mandatory.

Safeguards to Protect the Honour of the National Anthem

Constitutional Perspective: Section 51 (A) (a) mandates respect for the National Anthem.

PINH Act, 1971: Punishment for disrespecting the anthem, including imprisonment for up to 3 years.

MHA Guidelines: Mandate the full National Anthem during formal functions and military parades.
