GDP Growth Slump

Overview

India's GDP growth forecast for 2024-25 has been reduced to below 6.5%, down from 8.2% in 2023-24. Nominal GDP growth is estimated at 9.7%, lower than the 10.5% projection in the Union Budget.

Data Discrepancies

Experts cite flaws in India's GDP estimation, primarily due to reliance on the volatile Wholesale Price Index (WPI) instead of the Producer Price Index (PPI). This affects accurate GDP monitoring.

Private Investment Issues

Private corporate investment remains low despite tax cuts, failing to drive the anticipated economic revival. Unlike the UPA era, private investments have not significantly contributed to growth.

Sectoral Slowdown

Sectors like manufacturing, mining, power, and services have witnessed a slowdown. Public administration is the only sector expected to grow faster in 2024-25, highlighting the role of public spending.

Fiscal Strain

Tax revenue growth has been sluggish, achieving only 56% of the target by November 2024. This could lead to reduced public spending and exacerbate the economic slowdown.

Recommendations

A revised tax policy focusing on wealth and profit taxes is essential to support capital expenditure (capex) and public welfare initiatives.
