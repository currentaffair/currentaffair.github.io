Polar Vortex

Why in News?

A severe winter storm has affected over 60 million people across 30 states due to the southward expansion of the polar vortex, causing frigid temperatures and severe storms.

What is the Polar Vortex?

The polar vortex is a large area of low-pressure and cold air rotating around the Earth's polar regions. It exists year-round but strengthens in winter and weakens in summer.

Types

Tropospheric Polar Vortex: Located at the lowest atmospheric layer, affecting most weather phenomena.
Stratospheric Polar Vortex: Occurs higher up and is strongest during autumn, disappearing in the summer.

Mechanism of Extreme Cold

The polar vortex prevents cold air from moving south when strong, but a weakened vortex disrupts the jet stream, leading to cold temperatures, storms, and snow.

Global Warming and Polar Vortex

Arctic amplification, where the Arctic warms faster than the rest of the planet, weakens the polar vortex, causing extreme cold events.

Other Geophysical Phenomena

Arctic Oscillation (AO): Influences winter weather. The negative phase causes cold outbreaks.
North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO): Measures pressure differences affecting North American and European weather patterns.
