India GDP Growth Slump

Key Issues

Indiaâ€™s projected GDP growth for 2024-25 is down from 8.2% in 2023-24 to below the earlier forecast of 6.5-7%. The nominal GDP growth is also estimated at 9.7%, lower than the previous forecast of 10.5%.

Data Discrepancies

Experts highlight flaws in GDP estimation, particularly reliance on the volatile Wholesale Price Index (WPI) instead of the Producer Price Index (PPI), which distorts the real GDP calculations.

Private Investment and Sectoral Slowdown

Despite tax cuts, private corporate investment remains sluggish. Key sectors such as manufacturing, mining, and services (including retail, transport, and finance) are seeing slowdowns. Public administration is the only sector projected to grow faster.

Fiscal Strain and Revenue Mobilization

Tax revenue growth has been slower than expected, with only 56% of the target achieved by November 2024. A revised tax policy is needed to address the economic slowdown and support capital expenditure (capex) and welfare spending.

Conclusion

A revised approach to tax policy is crucial for economic recovery, especially to boost capex and public welfare initiatives.
