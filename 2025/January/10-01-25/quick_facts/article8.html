India’s Progress Under NCAP

Progress Overview

India achieved a 26.84% reduction in Particulate Matter (PM) levels from 2019 to 2024. NCAP cities showed a 24.45% improvement with targeted interventions.

Top Performers

  • Varanasi: 76.4% reduction
  • Moradabad: 58% reduction
  • Kanpur: 51.2% reduction
  • Kolkata: 21.5% reduction

Challenges

Delhi and Byrnihat remain among the most polluted cities. Cities like Gurugram, Faridabad, and Ghaziabad require urgent interventions.

Key Contributors

  • Industrial emissions
  • Vehicular pollution
  • Stubble burning

NCAP Goals

Launched in January 2019, the program aims to reduce PM10 and PM2.5 levels by 20% by 2024-25, with a revised target of 40% reduction by 2025-26.
