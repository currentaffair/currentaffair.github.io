Global Temperature Milestone and Climate Change Impact

Key Highlights:

  • Global temperatures rose 1.60°C above pre-industrial levels.
  • Global average temperature: 15.10°C, 0.72°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • First year where global temperature exceeded 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.
  • Warmest day on record: 22 July 2024, with 17.16°C global average temperature.

Regional Impacts

  • Europe: Warmest year on record with an average temperature of 10.67°C.
  • Arctic: Temperatures 1.34°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Oceans: Sea surface temperature reached a record high of 20.87°C.

Climate Indicators (2024)

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) levels reached 422 ppm, an increase of 2.9 ppm from 2023.
  • Methane (CH₄) levels reached 1897 ppb, an increase of 3 ppb from 2023.
  • Atmospheric water vapour increased by 5% above the 1991-2020 average.

Human Impact

  • 44% of the globe experienced 'strong' to 'extreme heat stress', a 5% increase from previous years.
  • Unprecedented heatwaves and heavy rainfall events caused widespread disruptions.

Expert Statements

Carlo Buontempo (Copernicus Climate Change Service) stated, "Humanity is in charge of its own destiny, and how we respond to the climate challenge should be evidence-based."

Samantha Burgess (ECMWF) stated, "We are now teetering on the edge of passing the 1.5ºC level defined in the Paris Agreement."

Key Takeaways

  • Climate crisis is escalating rapidly with multiple records broken in 2024.
  • Urgent need for swift and decisive climate action to limit global warming.
  • Paris Agreement's 1.5ºC limit is at risk of being permanently breached.
