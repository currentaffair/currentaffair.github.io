Freshwater Species at Risk of Extinction

Key Highlights

Decapod crustaceans, fishes, and odonates are at high risk of extinction, with 4,294 species threatened out of 23,496 assessed on the IUCN Red List. There have been 89 confirmed extinctions and 178 suspected extinctions since 1500.

Geographic Hotspots

Threatened species are mainly found in Lake Victoria (Africa), Lake Titicaca (South America), Sri Lanka’s wet zone, and the Western Ghats, India. These regions house unique freshwater biodiversity, including endemic species.

Causes of Decline

The major causes include agriculture (pollution and water extraction), invasive species, dams, water management issues, and climate change. Direct climate impacts involve changes in temperature, severe weather, and flow regimes, while indirect impacts amplify other threats.

Impact on Ecosystem Services

Freshwater ecosystems, though covering less than 1% of Earth’s surface, support 10% of all species globally and provide essential services like nutrient cycling, flood control, and climate change mitigation.

Urgency of Action

The lack of data on freshwater biodiversity is no longer an excuse for inaction. Integrated water management and species-specific conservation efforts are crucial to prevent further biodiversity loss.
