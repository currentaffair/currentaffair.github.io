GDP Growth Slump

India’s projected GDP growth for 2024-25 is expected to fall below the earlier forecast of 6.5-7%, dropping from 8.2% in 2023-24. The nominal GDP growth is estimated at 9.7%, lower than the 10.5% forecast in the Union Budget.

Key Developments

Experts highlight flaws in India’s GDP estimation, such as the reliance on the volatile Wholesale Price Index (WPI) instead of the Producer Price Index (PPI). Private corporate investment remains sluggish despite tax cuts, leading to a slow economic revival. There is a broad sectoral slowdown, with data revealing a decline in sectors like manufacturing, mining, power, and services.

Challenges and Recommendations

The fiscal strain has disrupted budget plans, with only 56% of the tax revenue target met by November 2024. The report recommends reworking the revenue strategy, with a focus on increasing taxes on wealth and profits to boost capital expenditure and welfare spending.
