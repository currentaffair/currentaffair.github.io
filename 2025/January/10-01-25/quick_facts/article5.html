2024 Climate Crisis Highlights

Key Highlights

Global temperatures rose 1.60°C above pre-industrial levels, marking a critical milestone in climate change.

The warmest day on record: 22 July 2024, with a global average temperature of 17.16°C.

Regional Impacts

Europe: Warmest year on record, averaging 10.67°C.

Arctic: Temperatures 1.34°C above the 1991-2020 average.

Oceans: Sea surface temperature reached a record high of 20.87°C.

Climate Indicators

CO₂ levels: 422 ppm, an increase of 2.9 ppm from 2023.

Methane levels: 1897 ppb, an increase of 3 ppb from 2023.

Water vapour: Increased by 5%, setting a new record.

Human Impact

44% of the globe faced ‘strong’ to ‘extreme heat stress’, with heatwaves and heavy rainfall disrupting lives.

Key Takeaways

The climate crisis is intensifying with urgent calls for climate action to limit global warming below the Paris Agreement target of 1.5°C.
