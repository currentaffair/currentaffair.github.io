Relevance

This issue pertains to GS 3 (Economic Development).

Data Discrepancies

Experts cite flaws in India's GDP estimation, including the reliance on the volatile Wholesale Price Index (WPI) over the Producer Price Index (PPI), distorting real GDP calculations.

Private Investment Issues

Despite tax cuts, private corporate investment remains sluggish, failing to spark the expected economic revival. This contrasts with the UPA era, where private investment significantly boosted economic expansion.

Sectoral Slowdown

There is a broad slowdown across sectors such as manufacturing, mining, power, and services. Public administration is the only sector projected to grow faster in 2024-25, underscoring the importance of public spending.

Fiscal Strain

Tax revenue growth is slower than expected, with only 56% of the target achieved by November 2024. This could lead to reduced public spending, worsening the economic slowdown.

Revenue Mobilization Recommendations

A revised revenue strategy is needed, focusing on increasing taxes on wealth and profits to support capital expenditure (capex) and welfare spending.

Conclusion

A new approach to tax policy is critical to address the economic slowdown, particularly to support public welfare and capex initiatives.
