Polar Vortex

Why in News?

A severe winter storm has affected over 60 million people in 30 states, caused by the southward expansion of the polar vortex, resulting in frigid temperatures and storms.

What is the Polar Vortex?

The polar vortex is a large area of cold air rotating around the Earthâ€™s polar regions. It weakens in summer and strengthens in winter, causing extreme cold weather when disrupted.

Types of Polar Vortex

  • Tropospheric Polar Vortex: Located in the lowest layer of the atmosphere.
  • Stratospheric Polar Vortex: Found higher up, it strengthens in autumn and weakens in summer.

Mechanism of Extreme Cold

The vortex keeps the jet stream stable, preventing cold air from moving south. A weakened vortex disrupts the jet stream, allowing Arctic air to flow south, causing extreme weather.

Global Warming and Polar Vortex

Arctic amplification, caused by faster warming in the Arctic, weakens the polar vortex and reduces the temperature gradient between the poles and mid-latitudes.

Other Geophysical Phenomena

  • Arctic Oscillation (AO): Affects winter weather in the Northern Hemisphere, causing cold outbreaks when negative.
  • North Atlantic Oscillation (NAO): Influences weather in North America and Europe, with positive phases bringing warmer conditions and negative phases colder weather.
