Freshwater Species Decline: Urgent Action Needed

Findings show that decapod crustaceans, fishes, and odonates are at high risk of extinction. Out of 23,496 assessed species on the IUCN Red List, 4,294 are threatened, with 89 confirmed extinctions since 1500.

Regional Extinctions

  • United States: 22 species
  • Mexico: 15 species
  • Philippines: 15 species (all endemic to Lake Lanao)

Impact on Ecosystem Services

Freshwater ecosystems support 10% of all species globally, one-third of vertebrates, and half of all fish species. They provide essential ecosystem services like nutrient cycling, flood control, and climate change mitigation.

Major Threats Identified

  • Pollution
  • Overharvesting
  • Dams and water extraction
  • Climate change

Urgency of Action

Conservation efforts must address the specific needs of freshwater species. The IUCN World Conservation Congress in 2025 will set conservation targets aligned with the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework and SDGs by 2030.

Takeaway

There is an urgent need for targeted actions to protect freshwater species. Integrated water management and species-specific conservation efforts are essential to prevent further biodiversity loss.
