2023 Delhi Floods: Inaccurate Flood Forecasting

Issue: Inaccuracy in Flood Forecasting

The Central Water Commission (CWC) issued an inaccurate flood forecast for the 2023 Delhi floods, which was caused by obstructions in the Yamuna river.

CWC Report Findings

  • Forecasting Error: The CWC forecasted steady water levels, but the river rose to 208.66 meters, triggering floods.
  • Obstructions: Issues like silt deposits, tree growth, and muck obstructed river flow between Wazirabad and Okhla.
  • Impairment: Non-opening of the ITO barrage gates worsened the river's ability to carry floodwaters.

Impact of the Flood

Central Delhi, particularly ITO, faced severe flooding due to the inaccurate forecast and rising water levels.

Government Response

The CWC's findings were submitted to the National Green Tribunal (NGT), which raised concerns about the forecast's accuracy.

Way Forward

  • Strengthen flood forecasting systems with real-time monitoring.
  • Regulate and maintain infrastructure like barrages to avoid blockages.
  • Minimize muck and debris disposal near sensitive river areas.
