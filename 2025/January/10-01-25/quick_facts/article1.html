Global Climate Change 2024: Record Temperatures and Urgency for Action

Key Highlights

Global temperatures reached 1.60°C above pre-industrial levels, surpassing 2023's record. The global average temperature hit 15.10°C, with 2024 marking the first calendar year exceeding 1.5°C.

Regional Impacts

Europe recorded its warmest year with temperatures 1.47°C above the 1991-2020 average. Arctic temperatures rose 1.34°C, and sea surface temperatures reached a record high of 20.87°C.

Climate Indicators

CO₂ levels hit 422 ppm, while methane levels increased to 1897 ppb. Atmospheric water vapor also rose 5%, setting a record high in 2024.

Human Impact

44% of the globe experienced 'strong' to 'extreme heat stress,' with unprecedented heatwaves and rainfall disrupting communities.

Statements from Experts

Carlo Buontempo of Copernicus: "Humanity is in charge of its own destiny." Samantha Burgess from ECMWF warns that the 1.5ºC limit in the Paris Agreement is at risk.

Key Takeaways

The climate crisis is accelerating, with records broken in 2024. Urgent climate action is needed to avoid breaching the Paris Agreement's 1.5ºC limit permanently.
