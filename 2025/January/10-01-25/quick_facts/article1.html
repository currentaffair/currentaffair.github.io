India's GDP Growth Slump

Key Highlights

India's GDP growth for 2024-25 is projected at 6.5-7%, a decline from 8.2% in 2023-24.

Nominal GDP growth is estimated at 9.7%, lower than the Union Budget forecast of 10.5%.

Data Discrepancies

Reliance on WPI (Wholesale Price Index) instead of PPI (Producer Price Index) distorts GDP estimates.

Mismatch between GDP by activity and GDP by expenditure complicates economic monitoring.

Private Investment Issues

Private corporate investment remains sluggish despite tax cuts.

Sectoral data shows a slowdown in manufacturing, mining, power, and services.

Public administration is the only sector projected to grow faster in 2024-25.

Fiscal Strain

Tax revenue growth lags with only 56% of the target achieved by November 2024.

Fiscal consolidation could reduce public spending, worsening the slowdown.

Recommendations

Revise tax policies to increase taxes on wealth and profits, boosting capital expenditure and welfare spending.

Conclusion

A revised tax strategy is crucial to address the economic slowdown and support public welfare initiatives.
