Marine Fungi

Overview

Marine fungi, comprising 5% of ocean biomass, are vital to marine ecosystems, thriving in diverse environments. They play crucial roles in decomposition, symbiosis, and produce bioactive compounds.

Types

Marine fungi are categorized into obligate marine fungi (exclusively marine) and facultative marine fungi (evolved from terrestrial environments, can survive in marine habitats).

Ecological Importance

These fungi contribute to nutrient cycling and ecosystem stability, with lichens, a symbiotic relationship between fungi and algae, enhancing marine ecosystems.

Fungi Characteristics

Fungi, eukaryotic organisms and heterotrophs, can function as saprophytes or parasites. They reproduce through spores and can be beneficial in medicine, food, and industry, though they may also cause diseases.
