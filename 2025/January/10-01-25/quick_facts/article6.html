India GDP Growth Slump

Overview

Indiaâ€™s GDP growth projection for 2024-25 is significantly lower than previous forecasts, falling below 6.5-7%, marking a notable economic slowdown.

Key Issues

  • GDP Estimation Flaws: Over-reliance on the Wholesale Price Index (WPI) distorts real GDP calculations.
  • Private Investment Slump: Despite tax cuts, private corporate investment remains low.
  • Sectoral Slowdown: Manufacturing, mining, power, and services sectors show sluggish growth.
  • Fiscal Strain: Tax revenue growth is slower than expected, impacting fiscal consolidation.

Recommendations

A revised tax policy focusing on wealth and profit taxes is necessary to boost capital expenditure and welfare spending to counter the slowdown.

Conclusion

The current economic situation requires a shift in policy to support sustainable growth through enhanced investments and improved public welfare.
