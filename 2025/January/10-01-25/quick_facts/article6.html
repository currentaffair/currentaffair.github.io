Debate Over National Anthem

Why in News? The Governor of Tamil Nadu left the Legislative Assembly without delivering his customary address on the opening day of the 2025 session citing non-playing of the National Anthem before his scheduled address. This has triggered a dispute over the ceremonial practices followed in the state legislature.

National Anthem & National Song

The song Jana-gana-mana (national anthem) was composed by Rabindranath Tagore in 1911. It was first sung at the Indian National Congress session in 1911. The National Song, Vande Mataram, written by Bankim Chandra Chatterjee, was first sung in 1896.

Protocols for Playing the National Anthem

Various states follow different conventions for playing the National Anthem during legislative sessions. Tamil Nadu plays the National Anthem at the end of the Governor's address, a unique practice introduced in 1991.

Safeguards to Protect the Honour of the National Anthem

The PINH Act, 1971, punishes disrespecting the National Anthem with imprisonment. The Ministry of Home Affairs mandates the playing of the full National Anthem during formal functions and investitures.
