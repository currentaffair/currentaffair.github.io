Freshwater Biodiversity at Risk

Key Findings

Decapod crustaceans, fishes, and odonates are at high risk of extinction. Of 23,496 species assessed on the IUCN Red List, 4,294 are threatened. Since 1500, there have been 89 confirmed and 178 suspected extinctions.

Geographic Hotspots

Regions like Lake Victoria, Lake Titicaca, Sri Lanka’s wet zone, and India’s Western Ghats are home to unique freshwater biodiversity, including endemic species.

Causes of Decline

Pollution, invasive species, dams, water extraction, and climate change significantly threaten freshwater species. Direct impacts include altered temperature and flow regimes, while indirect effects amplify other threats.

Impact on Ecosystems

Freshwater ecosystems, covering less than 1% of Earth's surface, support 10% of all known species, one-third of vertebrates, and half of all fish species. They provide crucial services such as nutrient cycling, flood control, and climate mitigation.

Urgency of Action

The report calls for immediate conservation measures, integrated water management, and species-specific efforts. The 2025 IUCN World Conservation Congress aims to align targets with global frameworks to curb biodiversity loss.
