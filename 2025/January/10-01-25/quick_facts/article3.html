India's State Revenue Dependency

Overview

Over the past decade (FY16-FY25), Indian states' revenue dependency on Central transfers has risen, constituting 23-30% of their total revenue. Meanwhile, both own tax and non-tax revenues have seen a decline.

Key Points

  • Decline in Own Revenue: States’ own tax revenue share has remained below 50%, with a growing reliance on SGST.
  • Non-Tax Revenue Decline: Non-tax revenue is expected to fall below 24% in FY25, with the Centre contributing a significant share.
  • Efficiency Issues: States have failed to improve tax collection efficiency, particularly in motor vehicle taxes and stamp duties.
  • Expenditure Pressures: Rising expenditure pressures limit states' ability to adopt counter-cyclical fiscal measures.
  • Redistribution Impact: States’ reliance on the Centre affects their redistributive fiscal policies.

Future Implications

States need to enhance tax collection capabilities and reduce dependency on the Centre for a more sustainable and autonomous fiscal future. Reforming inefficient tax collection systems like stamp duties and motor vehicle taxes could be key.
