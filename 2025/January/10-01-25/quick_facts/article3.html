2024 Global Climate Highlights

In 2024, global temperatures rose 1.60°C above pre-industrial levels, marking the first calendar year to exceed 1.5°C above pre-industrial temperatures. The warmest day on record occurred on July 22, with a global average temperature of 17.16°C.

Regional Impacts

Europe experienced its warmest year on record with a 10.67°C average temperature, while the Arctic saw temperatures 1.34°C above the 1991-2020 average. Oceans reached a record high sea surface temperature of 20.87°C.

Climate Indicators

Carbon dioxide levels reached 422 ppm, methane levels rose to 1897 ppb, and atmospheric water vapor increased by 5% above the 1991-2020 average. These indicators reflect the escalating climate crisis.

Human Impact

44% of the globe experienced 'strong' to 'extreme' heat stress, with heatwaves and heavy rainfall causing widespread disruptions.

Statements from Experts

Experts emphasize the urgent need for action, with Carlo Buontempo from Copernicus stating, "Humanity is in charge of its own destiny." Samantha Burgess from ECMWF warned, "We are now teetering on the edge of passing the 1.5ºC level defined in the Paris Agreement."

Key Takeaways

The climate crisis is escalating rapidly, with 2024 breaking multiple records. Urgent action is needed to prevent global temperatures from permanently exceeding the Paris Agreement's 1.5ºC limit.
