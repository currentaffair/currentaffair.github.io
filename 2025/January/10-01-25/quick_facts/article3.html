India’s Progress Under NCAP

Key Highlights

India achieved a 26.84% reduction in Particulate Matter (PM) levels from 2019 to 2024. NCAP cities saw a 24.45% improvement due to targeted interventions.

Top Performers

Varanasi led with a 76.4% reduction, followed by Moradabad (58%) and Kanpur (51.2%). Kolkata achieved a 21.5% reduction due to stricter regulations and improved transport.

Challenges

Delhi, Byrnihat, and cities like Gurugram and Ghaziabad still need urgent interventions.

Key Contributors

Industrial emissions, vehicular pollution, and stubble burning exacerbate pollution in northern states.

NCAP Overview

Launched in January 2019, NCAP aims to reduce PM10 and PM2.5 levels by 20% by 2024-25. The target has now been revised to 40% reduction by 2025-26.
