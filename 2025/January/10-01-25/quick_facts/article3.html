Genome India Project: A Step Towards Precision Medicine

Key Highlights

  • Database includes 10,000 human genomes from 83 population groups.
  • Aims to map India's genetic diversity for healthcare advancements.

Features of the Database

  • Population Representation: Covers 2% of India’s 4,600 population groups.
  • Genetic Variants: 27 million identified, including 7 million rare variants.
  • Allele Frequency: Highlights unique genetic traits in specific groups.

Applications

  • Disease Research: Insights into genetic causes of diseases.
  • Precision Medicine: Tailored treatments based on genetic data.
  • Drug Development: Improved drug efficacy and reduced adverse reactions.

Challenges

  • Cost and Scalability: Expanding to 1 million genomes requires significant resources.
  • Diversity Gaps: Current dataset represents a small portion of India’s diversity.

Future Prospects

The database aims to expand to 1 million genomes, offering a comprehensive view of India’s genetic landscape and supporting global health research.

Conclusion

The Genome India Project is a groundbreaking initiative poised to revolutionize healthcare in India and beyond. Its focus on genetic diversity will enable precision medicine and biotechnology innovations, addressing both national and global health challenges.
