2024 Climate Change Highlights

Key Highlights

  • Global temperatures rose 1.60°C above pre-industrial levels.
  • Global average temperature: 15.10°C (0.72°C above the 1991-2020 average).
  • Milestone: First calendar year exceeding 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.
  • Warmest day on record: 22 July 2024, 17.16°C global average temperature.

Regional Impacts

  • Europe: Warmest year on record with 10.67°C average.
  • Arctic: 1.34°C above the 1991-2020 average.
  • Oceans: Record high sea surface temperature of 20.87°C.

Climate Indicators (2024)

  • CO₂ levels: Reached 422 ppm, up by 2.9 ppm from 2023.
  • CH₄ levels: Reached 1897 ppb, up by 3 ppb from 2023.
  • Atmospheric Water Vapour: 5% increase above the 1991-2020 average.

Human Impact

44% of the globe experienced ‘strong’ to ‘extreme heat stress’, marking a 5% increase from previous years. Heatwaves and heavy rainfall caused widespread disruptions.

Statements from Experts

“Humanity is in charge of its own destiny, and how we respond to the climate challenge should be evidence-based.” - Carlo Buontempo (Copernicus Climate Change Service)

“We are now teetering on the edge of passing the 1.5ºC level defined in the Paris Agreement.” - Samantha Burgess (ECMWF)

Key Takeaways

The climate crisis is escalating rapidly with multiple records broken in 2024. There is an urgent need for swift climate action to prevent breaching the Paris Agreement’s 1.5ºC limit permanently.
