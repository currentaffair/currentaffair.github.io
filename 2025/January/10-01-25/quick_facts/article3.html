India GDP Growth Slump: Economic Slowdown Insights

India’s projected GDP growth for 2024-25 is significantly lower, dropping to 6.5-7% from 8.2% in 2023-24, with nominal GDP growth at 9.7%. Discrepancies in GDP estimation, reliance on the Wholesale Price Index (WPI), and sluggish private investment are key factors.

Key Issues Impacting India’s Economy

  • GDP Estimation Discrepancies: WPI used instead of Producer Price Index (PPI), leading to distorted real GDP calculations.
  • Private Investment Decline: Despite tax cuts, corporate investment is slow, contradicting Union Budget goals.
  • Sectoral Slowdown: Manufacturing, mining, power, and services sectors are facing a broad slowdown, except for public administration.
  • Fiscal Strain: Tax revenue growth slower than expected, leading to potential budget disruptions.

Recommendations for Economic Recovery

  • Revenue Mobilization: Focus on increasing taxes on wealth and profits to fund capex and welfare initiatives.
  • Revised Tax Policy: A new approach to tax policy is crucial to support economic revival.

Conclusion

A revised tax policy and focus on revenue mobilization are essential to mitigate the slowdown and drive economic growth.
