Avian Influenza Case in Maharashtra

Details of the Case

In December 2024, three tigers and one leopard at a rescue centre in Nagpur died due to avian influenza. The infection is thought to be linked to contaminated chicken fed to the animals.

Transmission Concerns

This marks the first case in India of avian influenza affecting non-avian species, highlighting a growing risk of zoonotic diseases crossing species barriers, as seen in other global cases.

Response Measures

Quarantine protocols were immediately implemented to isolate infected animals and prevent further transmission. Staff members involved in the care and post-mortem of the animals are being screened for infection.

Government Actions

The Ministry of Animal Husbandry has enhanced biosecurity protocols, including closing affected establishments, restricting personnel movement, and thorough disinfection of affected areas.

Broader Implications

This case underscores the importance of maintaining high biosecurity standards at animal care facilities to prevent zoonotic diseases and contamination in animal food supplies.
