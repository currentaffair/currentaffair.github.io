India’s School Enrolments Drop

Introduction

In the 2023-24 period, India saw a drop of 1.22 crore in school enrolments compared to 2018-19, mainly due to improved data accuracy via Aadhaar-linked student IDs.

Enrolment Decline Across School Types

  • Government Schools: Declined by 36 lakh (-2.8%).
  • Government-Aided Schools: Fell by 24 lakh (-8.7%).
  • Private Schools: Dropped by 21 lakh (-2.3%).
  • Other Schools (including Madrasas): Declined by 41 lakh (-44.8%).

Unrecognised Madrasas and Schools

  • Recognised Madrasas: Enrolments dropped by 16%, but schools and teachers increased by 7% and 13%, respectively.
  • Unrecognised Madrasas: Enrolments fell sharply by 87%.
  • Other Unrecognised Schools: Enrolments declined by 56%.

Key Issues

  • Ghost and Duplicate Entries: Aadhaar-based IDs helped improve data accuracy, but may have disproportionately impacted unrecognised schools and Madrasas.
  • Impact on Education Access: The decline may affect marginalised communities relying on unrecognised schools and Madrasas for education.
  • Student Dropout Clarification: The data does not clearly differentiate between real dropouts and improved data collection.

Possible Implications

  • Education Gaps: The decline of unrecognised schools may worsen educational inequality for vulnerable communities.
  • Policy Challenges: Balancing regulation and ensuring access to education remains a concern.
  • Need for Further Analysis: Further investigation is needed into why recognised Madrasas have declining enrolments despite increased infrastructure and teachers.

Recommendations

  • Detailed Study: Investigate the causes of the enrolment drop, especially in unrecognised schools and Madrasas.
  • Support for Transition: Provide resources for unrecognised schools to meet government recognition criteria.
  • Strengthen Outreach: Focus on enrolment drives for marginalised communities.
  • Improved Monitoring: Enhance data accuracy while preventing exclusion of genuine students.
