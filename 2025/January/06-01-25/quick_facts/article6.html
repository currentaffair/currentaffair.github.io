India's Healthcare Crisis: Rising Disease Burden and Costs

Overview

Indians face a growing crisis in non-communicable diseases (NCDs), which accounted for 65% of deaths in 2022, up from 50% in 2010-13. The healthcare system is under strain from rising disease burdens and escalating healthcare costs.

Key Challenges

  • Prevalence of NCDs: 1 in 4 adult men is hypertensive; 1 in 8 adults is diabetic. Rising early-onset cancers, such as breast, lung, and cervical cancer.
  • Financial Burden: Household spending accounts for 50% of health costs, and NCDs are estimated to cost India â‚¹280 lakh crore by 2030.
  • Underinvestment in Preventive Care: High costs for preventive health checks and stagnant tax deductions hinder accessibility.

Policy Gaps and Recommendations

  • Early Diagnosis Infrastructure: Insufficient low-cost screening facilities and lack of AI integration in public health.
  • Preventive Care Initiatives: Expanding Ayushman Health Centres with AI-enabled imaging, and subsidizing routine screenings for individuals aged 40-60.
  • Financial Revisions: Increase Section 80D tax deductions for preventive health checks and fund from healthcare cess or harmful product taxes.

Potential Impact

Early detection could reduce disease burden, save costs, and improve economic productivity by maintaining a healthier workforce.

Conclusion

India must prioritize preventive care to address the dual healthcare challenges of rising diseases and costs. Effective policies and public awareness are key to a healthier and economically resilient future.
