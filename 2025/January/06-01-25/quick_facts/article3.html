Winter Char Dham

Introduction

Uttarakhand has launched the Winter Char Dham circuit to boost year-round tourism, attracting visitors during the winter months.

Char Dham Shrines

The four Char Dham shrines in the Garhwal Himalayas are:

  • Yamunotri
  • Gangotri
  • Kedarnath
  • Badrinath

Winter Location Shift

During winter, the deities are moved to lower altitudes:

  • Kedarnath: Omkareshwar Temple in Ukhimath (Rudraprayag)
  • Badrinath: Pandukeshwar in Chamoli
  • Gangotri: Mukhba in Uttarkashi
  • Yamunotri: Kharsali in Uttarkashi

Char Dham Project

This project aims to improve connectivity to these shrines by upgrading highways.

About Adi Shankaracharya

Adi Shankaracharya, a key figure in Advaita Vedanta, established the Char Dhams across India: Badrinath, Puri, Dwarka, and Rameshwaram.

Advaita Vedanta

Advaita Vedanta is a non-dualistic philosophy, asserting that the ultimate reality (Brahman) is singular and formless, with individual souls (Atman) being identical to it. Liberation (moksha) is achieved through realizing this oneness.
