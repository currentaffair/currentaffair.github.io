Great Nicobar Project Impact

Overview

The ₹72,000-crore project in Great Nicobar includes a transshipment port, airport, cruise terminal, and shipbreaking yard. It poses a threat to the UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and the island's ecological balance.

Ecological Concerns

  • Loss of 8–10 lakh evergreen trees and coral reefs.
  • Threat to nesting sites for Leatherback turtles and Nicobar Megapodes.
  • Denotification of Galathea Bay Wildlife Sanctuary raises concerns over "greenwashing".

Indigenous Communities

The Southern Nicobarese and Shompen communities hold traditional rights over the islands, relying on them for sustenance and spiritual practices.

Social and Cultural Impacts

  • Risk of displacement for the Shompen and Nicobarese.
  • Disruption of livelihoods rebuilt after the 2004 tsunami.

Conservation Colonialism

The unilateral declaration of wildlife sanctuaries without indigenous consultation undermines their governance and stewardship.

Proposed Solutions

  • Empower Indigenous Stewardship: Recognize indigenous governance for sustainable conservation.
  • Ensure Transparent Decision-Making: Obtain free, prior, and informed consent (FPIC) from local communities.
  • Holistic Conservation: Combine ecological and cultural preservation.
  • Reassess Environmental Impact: Explore sustainable alternatives.

Conclusion

Protecting the biodiversity of Great Nicobar and respecting indigenous rights is vital for long-term sustainability.
