Arunachal Pradesh Freedom of Religion Act, 1978

The Arunachal Pradesh government is enforcing the Arunachal Pradesh Freedom of Religion Act, 1978, nearly 46 years after its introduction, in response to concerns about forceful religious conversions in the state.

About the Act

The Act aims to prohibit forceful conversions and safeguard indigenous faiths practiced in Arunachal Pradesh. It recognizes religions like Buddhism, Donyi-Polo, and Vaishnavism, and specifically prohibits conversions under coercive circumstances.

Key Provisions

  • Indigenous Faiths: Recognizes Buddhism, Donyi-Polo, and Vaishnavism as indigenous religions.
  • Prohibition of Forced Conversion: Prohibits coerced conversions with penalties of up to 2 years of imprisonment and fines.
  • Mandatory Reporting: Any religious conversion must be reported to the Deputy Commissioner of the district.

Revival and Support

The revival of the Act was propelled by a Public Interest Litigation (PIL) and the intervention of the Gauhati High Court in 2022. It is supported by groups like the Indigenous Faiths and Cultural Society of Arunachal Pradesh (IFCSAP), who aim to protect local traditions.
