Hydrogel for Cancer Therapy

Introduction

Researchers have developed an injectable hydrogel for localized cancer treatment, providing a controlled release of anti-cancer drugs directly to the tumour site.

How It Works

  • Targeted Drug Delivery: The hydrogel releases drugs to cancer cells while minimizing harm to healthy ones.
  • Biocompatibility: Made from ultra-short peptides, it is biocompatible and biodegradable.
  • Glutathione Sensitivity: The hydrogel responds to elevated glutathione (GSH) levels, abundant in tumour cells.
  • Improved Precision: Ensures localized treatment, improving accuracy and reducing side effects.

Conventional Treatments

Traditional chemotherapy and surgery often harm healthy cells, which this hydrogel aims to avoid by offering a more targeted approach.

What is Hydrogel?

A hydrogel is a water-based, three-dimensional polymer network capable of absorbing and retaining fluids, mimicking living tissues, making it ideal for biomedical uses.
