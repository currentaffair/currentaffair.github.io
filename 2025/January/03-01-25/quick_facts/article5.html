CISF Reduces Suicide Rate by 40%

The Central Industrial Security Force (CISF) has successfully reduced its suicide rate by 40% in 2024, bringing it below the national average for the first time in five years.

Reduction in Suicide Rates

The CISF suicide rate dropped to 9.87 per lakh people in 2024, compared to the national rate of 12.4 per lakh in 2022, marking a significant 40% reduction from the previous year.

Factors Contributing to Stress

CISF personnel face challenges like prolonged family separation, high-pressure work environments, and personal issues. The unique occupational challenges of CAPFs contribute to their vulnerability to stress.

Proactive Measures Taken by CISF

CISF implemented several mental health initiatives, including:

  • Enhanced communication with regular visits and daily briefings.
  • Online grievance portal for real-time monitoring and resolution.
  • 24×7 tele-counselling and in-person counselling services, with over 4,200 personnel availing counselling by September 2024.
  • Collaboration with AIIMS for a mental health study and actionable recommendations at the unit level.

Impact and Significance

The CISF’s proactive efforts show the effectiveness of mental health interventions. These measures may serve as a model for other CAPFs and organizations dealing with high-stress environments.
