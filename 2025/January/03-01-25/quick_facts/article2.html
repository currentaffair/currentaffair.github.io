Brazilian Velvet Ant

A study revealed that the Brazilian velvet ant (Traumatomutilla bifurca) has "ultrablack" body parts, absorbing over 99.5% of visible light, making them nearly invisible. This discovery opens new possibilities in technology.

Key Facts About Brazilian Velvet Ant

  • Taxonomy: Velvet ants are actually wasps from the Mutillidae family.
  • Ultrablack Property: Female velvet ants have ultrablack pigmentation that absorbs nearly all visible light, aiding in camouflage and temperature control.
  • Male vs Female: Only females have ultrablack coloration, while males reflect more light.
  • Role in Ecosystem: Velvet ants serve as pollinators and contribute to ecological balance.
  • Evolutionary Significance: The ultrablack trait is an example of convergent evolution, found in birds-of-paradise and deep-sea fish.
  • Scientific Applications: This ultrablack property inspires advancements in stealth technology and solar panels.

Additional Notes

Ants are eusocial insects with highly organized colonies. They are known for their collective problem-solving abilities and can outperform humans in certain tasks, such as navigating obstacles.
