GEAC Rules Amendments on Conflict of Interest

The Union Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change has amended rules for selecting expert members to the Genetic Engineering Appraisal Committee (GEAC) to address concerns of conflict of interest.

Amendments to GEAC Rules

Expert members are required to disclose any direct or indirect interests that could cause conflicts with their duties. They must recuse themselves from discussions when necessary and provide a detailed affiliation form covering the past 10 years.

Supreme Court’s Role

The Supreme Court's split verdict on GM mustard in July 2023 highlighted the need for addressing conflict of interest in decision-making bodies. The final resolution of GM mustard’s approval is still pending.

Background of Conflict Allegations

In 2013, allegations arose regarding a member of a Technical Expert Committee having ties to Monsanto, which raised questions about the impartiality of GM crop recommendations.

Significance of the Amendments

The amendments aim to enhance transparency and accountability in GEAC decisions, addressing public concerns about bias in approving GM crops and following the SC's directive on conflict of interest issues.

Next Steps

The Centre is expected to formulate a national policy on GM crops as per the SC's 2023 order. The final decision on GM mustard’s release is still under judicial review.
