DILRMP and Digitalisation of Land Records

Introduction

The Digital India Land Records Modernization Programme (DILRMP) has been a significant step in digitizing India's land records, with the aim of improving transparency, reducing disputes, and enhancing access to land data. As of 2024, 98.5% of rural land records have been digitized, marking a major milestone. This article delves into the details of DILRMP, its importance, key initiatives, challenges, and the way forward for achieving a fully digitized and transparent land record system in India.

What is DILRMP?

The National Land Record Modernization Programme (NLRMP), which was initiated in 2008, was revamped and renamed as DILRMP in 2016. This program is fully funded by the Indian government and focuses on modernizing land records to improve transparency, security of ownership, and overall land administration.

The major goals of the program include streamlining land record management, reducing land disputes, and making land records more accessible to the public. The program also integrates technology to bring efficiency and accuracy to land record keeping.

Key Initiatives Under DILRMP

  • Unique Land Parcel Identification Number (ULPIN): A 14-digit alphanumeric code that is assigned to each land parcel, based on its geo-coordinates. This initiative has been implemented in 29 states/UTs and is aimed at streamlining land transactions and resolving disputes.
  • National Generic Document Registration System (NGDRS): This e-registration system provides a uniform process for registering documents across India. It has been adopted by 18 states/UTs, helping facilitate online document entries, payments, and appointment bookings.
  • e-Court Integration: This initiative aims to link land records with e-courts, providing accurate land data to the judiciary, reducing land disputes and enabling quicker case resolution. This has been implemented in 26 states/UTs.
  • Transliteration of Land Records: To make land records accessible to people from different linguistic backgrounds, the DILRMP has begun transliterating documents into 22 languages of India. This initiative has been implemented in 17 states/UTs.
  • Bhoomi Samman: This initiative has recognized 168 districts across 16 states for their successful completion of core components like land record computerization and map digitization.

Why India Needs a Digitized Land Record System

Land is a critical resource for India, especially considering that over 45% of the workforce is employed in agriculture, according to the Economic Survey 2023-24. A transparent and modernized land record system is essential for ensuring fair distribution and use of this vital resource.

Key Benefits of Digitizing Land Records

  • Ensure Equity: Digitization ensures fairness in land distribution and empowers marginalized groups, including women, by securing their land rights.
  • Reduce Litigation: With clear, government-backed titles, disputes over land ownership can be minimized, reducing the burden on the judicial system.
  • Promote Development: A streamlined and transparent land record system facilitates investment, reduces transaction risks, and improves access to credit, all of which are essential for economic growth.
  • Improve Transparency: Outdated land records are a major issue in India. Digitization and integration with databases like Aadhaar will ensure better accuracy and reduce fraud.

Challenges of Land Record Digitization

Despite the progress made, several challenges hinder the full digitization of land records in India:

  • Language and Dialect Barriers: India's linguistic diversity poses challenges in accessing land records, as many rural landowners struggle with digital systems not available in their native languages.
  • Community Shareholdings: In areas with community-based land ownership, such as northeastern states, traditional practices complicate the formalization and digitization of land titles.
  • Lack of Awareness: Many stakeholders are not fully aware of the benefits and procedures of DILRMP, hindering the active participation needed for its success.
  • Quality of Land Records: Outdated and unclear land records, especially those lacking cadastral maps, make it difficult to establish accurate ownership and land boundaries.
  • Complexity of Land Management Systems: Multiple regulatory departments and regulations complicate the digitization process, requiring better coordination.
  • Lack of Resources: The DILRMP faces challenges due to limited resources and infrastructure, which needs to be addressed to ensure full implementation.

The Way Forward

  • Integration of Land Records: Develop a unified platform to link land records with property registration, tax payments, and government subsidies.
  • Regular Record Updates: Use drones and satellite imagery for routine audits and updates to ensure accurate and up-to-date land records.
  • Community Participation: Engage local communities in verifying land boundaries and ownership, ensuring accuracy and reducing disputes.
  • Public Awareness Campaigns: Increase awareness among farmers and landowners about the benefits of the digitization process through local media and community outreach programs.
  • Dispute Resolution Mechanisms: Establish dedicated online platforms for land dispute resolution to improve transparency and efficiency.
  • Policy Framework: Develop comprehensive policies that align technology with local needs and national goals.
  • Public-Private Partnerships (PPP): Leverage collaboration between government agencies and private tech companies to enhance land record management.
  • Research and Development: Invest in R&D to explore emerging technologies, such as blockchain, for secure and transparent land transactions.

Conclusion

The Digital India Land Records Modernization Programme (DILRMP) has made significant strides in digitizing India's land records, promoting transparency, reducing land disputes, and enabling better access to land-related services. However, challenges like language barriers, outdated records, and lack of awareness still remain. Addressing these issues through a coordinated effort will ensure that the digitization initiative is successful, contributing to a more efficient and equitable land management system in India.
