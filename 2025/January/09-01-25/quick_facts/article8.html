Maoist Attacks and Security in 2024

The year 2024 saw a significant reduction in large-scale attacks, such as the 2010 Dantewada attack. However, a deadly attack in Bijapur, resulting in 8 casualties, marked an exception, with fatalities exceeding those of early 2024. Maoist casualties were notably high, with 296 Maoists killed, the third-highest since 2000, while security personnel casualties were lower at 24.

Maoist Influence and Geography

Bastar remains the epicenter of Maoist activity, with nearly 30% of left-wing extremism-related deaths occurring in Bijapur. The region saw a significant decline in Maoist influence due to tribal communities distancing from insurgents, reflected in the 2023 Chhattisgarh elections.

State-Specific Trends

In 2024, Chhattisgarh accounted for 80% of left-wing extremism-related deaths. Other states, including Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Jharkhand, saw a notable reduction in Maoist activity and related fatalities.

Implications and Security Efforts

While large-scale attacks have decreased, Maoists still possess the capacity for high-casualty incidents, particularly in Bastar. Continued security efforts, addressing socio-political grievances, and preventing escalation are crucial moving forward.
