Stampede at Tirupati - Vaikunta Ekadasi Festival

Overview

A stampede at the Vishnu Nivasam ticket counter in Tirupati resulted in the deaths of six people and left over 20 critically injured. The incident occurred as a large crowd gathered for tickets to the Vaikunta Ekadasi festival at the Tirumala temple.

Key Points

  • Casualties: 6 killed, over 20 critically injured in the stampede.
  • Cause: Chaos during the rush for tickets for the Vaikunta Ekadasi festival.
  • Mismanagement: Inadequate crowd control and poor planning by the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD) led to the tragedy.
  • Immediate Response: Injured people were taken to Sri Venkateswara Ramnarain Ruia Hospital.

Government Reaction

  • Chief Minister's Statement: N. Chandrababu Naidu expressed shock and called for strict action against TTD employees responsible for the mismanagement.
  • Concerns Raised: Lack of preparation despite knowing the festival's popularity and inadequate infrastructure to handle the crowd.

Way Forward

  • Improved Crowd Management: Enhanced protocols to manage large crowds during major religious events.
  • Online Ticketing: Implement online systems to avoid physical crowding.
  • Accountability: Inquiry into the incident to hold TTD employees accountable and improve future event planning.
