Draft Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025

Why in News?

The Ministry of Electronics & IT has released the draft Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Rules, 2025 aimed at safeguarding citizens' rights to protect their personal data.

Key Points about the Draft DPDP Rules, 2025

  • About: Operationalizes the DPDP Act, 2023 to protect digital personal data while promoting India’s digital economy.
  • Data Transfer: Allows transfer of personal data outside India with government approval.
  • Citizens' Rights: Rights to demand data erasure, appoint digital nominees, and manage data via Data Fiduciaries.
  • Entities: Data fiduciaries include social media platforms, e-commerce companies, and online gaming platforms.
  • Data Erasure: Retention for up to 3 years, with a 48-hour notice before erasure.
  • Digital-First Approach: Establishment of a "digital by design" Data Protection Board for complaints handling.
  • Graded Responsibilities: Startups and MSMEs have lower compliance burden, while larger platforms have higher obligations.
  • Consent Managers: Companies handling consent storage must be incorporated in India with a net worth of ₹2 crore.
  • DPBI Framework: Setting up the Data Protection Board of India with civil court powers for data breach complaints.

Salient Features of the DPDP Act, 2023

  • Right to Data Protection: Empowers individuals to control their data.
  • Data Processing and Consent: Requires clear, explicit consent for data processing.
  • Data Localization: Sensitive data must be stored within India.
  • Regulatory Authority: Establishes DPBI for compliance and grievance handling.
  • Data Breach Notification: Mandates notification for data breaches.
  • Fines and Penalties: Strict penalties for non-compliance.
