Centre's Share in States' Revenue Surges

The Centre’s share in states’ revenue has significantly increased from 20-24% in previous years to 23-30% from FY16 to FY25. Meanwhile, own tax revenue and non-tax revenue in states have seen a decline.

Key Points

  • The share of Centre’s revenue in states has risen, while states' own revenue has dropped.
  • States' own tax revenue has remained below 50% for over a decade.
  • SGST (State Goods and Services Tax) has grown significantly, but other tax revenues have declined.
  • Non-tax revenue, especially grants from the Centre, constitutes 65-70% of non-tax revenue.

Challenges

  • States have not improved tax collection efficiency, with key areas such as stamp duty and motor vehicle taxes underperforming.
  • States' tax-to-GSDP ratio is declining, with Tamil Nadu's ratio falling over the past decade.

Implications

  • States are facing increasing expenditure pressures but lack the revenue to adopt counter-cyclical fiscal measures.
  • The reliance on the Centre affects the redistributive potential of state fiscal policies.

Conclusion

  • The growing reliance on Central transfers highlights structural issues in state finance management.
  • States need to improve their tax collection efficiency to reduce dependency on the Centre.
