Gang Violence in Haiti

Over 5,600 people were killed in gang violence in Haiti, marking a sharp increase from the previous year. Thousands of individuals were injured or kidnapped, exacerbating the country's ongoing crisis.

Geographical and Social Context

Violent gangs control most of Port-au-Prince, Haiti's capital, while political instability further fuels gang activities.

Notable Incident

A massacre in Cite Soleil orchestrated by the Wharf Jeremie gang in December 2024 resulted in 207 deaths, many victims accused of voodoo practices and burned or dismembered.

Human Rights Violations

The UN has documented numerous abuses, including 315 lynchings of gang members, 281 summary executions by specialized police units, and the involvement of Haitian police in some violations.

Impunity and Corruption

Widespread impunity for human rights abuses and corruption persist, with calls for holding police officers accountable for their involvement.

International Response

Despite a Kenyan-led police support mission, violence continues. The UN stresses the need to restore the rule of law and provide support for international missions.

Urgency for Action

The priority is to restore the rule of law, hold violators accountable, and seek international support to combat impunity and violence.
