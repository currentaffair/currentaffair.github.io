Why is Deciphering the Indus Script Important?

Relevance: GS 1(Culture, Heritage)

Background

The Indus Valley Civilization (IVC) spanned across 2,000 sites in 1.5 million square km, including parts of India, Pakistan, and Afghanistan. It remains one of the earliest urban civilizations, comparable in scale to Egypt and Mesopotamia.

Undeciphered Script

The script of the IVC remains undeciphered, unlike the scripts of other ancient civilizations. This hinders the complete understanding of Harappan culture, history, and governance.

Political Context & Dravidian Hypothesis

On January 5, Tamil Nadu CM M.K. Stalin announced a $1 million prize for deciphering the Indus script. This is linked to debates on the potential Dravidian connection with the IVC.

Possible Cultural Contact

Evidence suggests a potential cultural exchange between the IVC and South India. Similar objects and materials found across these regions hint at trade and cultural contact during the Copper and Iron Ages.

Future Research

Further archaeological, linguistic, and scientific investigations are needed to confirm these links and decipher the script's true meaning.
