Draft Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025

Overview

The Ministry of Electronics & IT released the draft Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025, to safeguard citizens' data and foster India's digital economy.

Key Points

  • Data Transfer: Allows certain personal data to be transferred outside India with government approval.
  • Data Erasure: Data must be erased within three years or upon request, with 48-hour notice to users.
  • Digital-First Approach: Digital mechanisms for consent and grievance redressal.
  • Consent Managers: Consent managers collect and store user consent, with a minimum net worth requirement.

Salient Features of the DPDP Act, 2023

  • Right to Data Protection: Empowers users with rights over their data.
  • Data Processing & Consent: Requires clear, explicit consent for data usage.
  • Data Localization: Sensitive data must stay within India for security.
  • Fines & Penalties: Severe penalties for non-compliance.
