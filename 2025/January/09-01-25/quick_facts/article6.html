State Revenue Issues: Decline in Own Revenue and Increasing Dependency on the Centre

Key Points

  • Declining Revenue: The share of states' own revenue, including taxes and non-taxes, has decreased, while Central transfers and grants have increased.
  • SGST Growth: SGST now constitutes a major portion of states' own tax revenue, up from 15% to 22% between FY18 and FY25.
  • Non-Tax Revenue Decline: Non-tax revenue is expected to fall below 24% by FY25, with grants from the Centre making up the majority of this decline.
  • Inefficiency in Tax Collection: States have struggled with tax collection efficiency, especially in areas like stamp duty, registration fees, and motor vehicle taxes.
  • Implications: The growing reliance on Centre's grants limits states' fiscal autonomy and redistributive potential.

Way Forward

  • Enhance Tax Collection: States should focus on improving tax collection efficiency, especially for stamp duty, registration fees, and SGST.
  • Fiscal Autonomy: A reduction in dependency on Centre's transfers will allow for greater fiscal independence and better handling of economic challenges.
