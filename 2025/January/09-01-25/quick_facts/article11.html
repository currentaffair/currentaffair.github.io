Bail Pleas of Umar Khalid and Sharjeel Imam

Case: Bail pleas of Umar Khalid and Sharjeel Imam in the February 2020 North-East Delhi riots case.

Court: Delhi High Court.

Query: The court questioned if organizing a protest alone could justify invoking the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act (UAPA), an anti-terror law, in the case.

Legal Context

The Delhi Police charged Khalid, Imam, and others with being part of a larger conspiracy behind the communal violence in February 2020, resulting in 53 deaths and over 700 injuries.

Courtâ€™s Observation

The court emphasized that intent is a crucial factor under UAPA. Organizing protests isn't enough unless there is evidence showing an intention to instigate violence.

Evidence and Allegations

The prosecution linked the accused to a WhatsApp group organizing protests, but the court asked for specific evidence showing a transition from peaceful protest to violence.

Implications and Legal Path Forward

The case could influence how UAPA provisions are applied in cases where the line between peaceful protest and terrorism is contested.
