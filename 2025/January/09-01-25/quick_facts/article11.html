Forecast for 2023 Delhi Floods was Inaccurate as Flow of Yamuna was Marred by Silt, Muck

The 2023 Delhi floods were the worst in the city's history, and the forecast for these floods was inaccurate. The Central Water Commission (CWC) submitted a report on January 4, 2024, to the National Green Tribunal (NGT), revealing that the flow of the Yamuna River was obstructed by silt, muck, and other factors, leading to the forecast's inaccuracy.

Key Findings

  • The forecast failed due to obstacles like silt islands, tree growth, and muck disposal.
  • The river's flow was impaired by non-opening of ITO barrage gates and other construction activities.
  • The water level reached an all-time high of 208.66 meters, severely impacting central Delhi, especially areas like ITO.

Concerns & Implications

  • Lack of timely action and poor management led to forecasting misjudgments.
  • The need for better river management, improved infrastructure, and flood forecasting systems was emphasized.

Way Forward

  • Strengthen forecasting systems with accurate data and real-time monitoring.
  • Ensure proper maintenance of infrastructure like barrages and channels.
  • Implement measures to reduce muck disposal near the river.
