Coal Mine Rescue Operation in Assam

Overview

On January 8, 2025, nine coal miners were trapped in a water-filled mine in Dima Hasao district, Assam. The authorities were informed later that day, and rescue operations commenced with deep-diving Navy teams, along with the Army, NDRF, and other agencies. The mine conditions include rising water levels of over 100 feet, severely hindering efforts.

Rescue Operations

Rescue teams have encountered challenges due to the mine's tunnel system and the high water level. Despite these obstacles, the operation continues with specialized diving gear from the Navy and support from various emergency teams.

Investigation

Police have arrested Punish Nunisa under the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, with investigations suggesting that the mine may have been operating illegally.

Historical Context

Mining mishaps are frequent in Northeast India, with several notable incidents such as fires and collapses, including the Ksan disaster in Meghalaya that killed 15 in 2018.

Challenges

The mine's location in a remote jungle area complicates rescue efforts. With high water levels and difficult terrain, rescue teams are facing significant delays.
