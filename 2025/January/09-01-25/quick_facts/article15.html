Reduction in Maoist Attacks and Casualties, 2024

Recent Trends in Maoist Attacks

The number of large-scale attacks, such as the 2010 Dantewada attack, has significantly reduced. However, the recent Bijapur attack (8 killed) stands out with casualties higher than the first week of 2024.

Maoist Casualties

In 2024, 296 Maoists were killed, marking the third-highest number since 2000. In contrast, only 24 security personnel died, the second-lowest in the same period.

Bastar Region: The Epicenter of Maoist Activity

The Bastar region in Chhattisgarh remains the focal point of Maoist activities, with nearly 30% of left-wing extremism-related deaths between 2020-2025 occurring in Bijapur and nearby districts like Narayanpur, Sukma, and Dantewada.

Geographical Contraction of Maoist Influence

The Maoist influence has sharply reduced, especially due to tribal communities distancing themselves from the insurgents. This decline is reflected in the 2023 Chhattisgarh elections, where there was a significant drop in NOTA votes.

State-Specific Trends

In 2024, Chhattisgarh accounted for 80% of the deaths linked to left-wing extremism. Meanwhile, other states such as Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Jharkhand, Maharashtra, Odisha, and West Bengal saw a major decline in Maoist activity and related fatalities.

Implications for Security

While large-scale attacks are declining, Maoists still retain the capacity for high-casualty incidents, particularly in Bastar. Ongoing security efforts and addressing socio-political grievances remain crucial to preventing further escalation.
