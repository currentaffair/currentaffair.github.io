Supreme Court Criticizes Delay in Appointing Information Commissioners

The Supreme Court has criticized the delay by both the Centre and States in appointing Information Commissioners, which hampers the effective functioning of the Right to Information (RTI) Act. The court emphasized the importance of ensuring that these commissions are staffed to uphold citizens’ rights to access information under the RTI.

Key Points

  • Vacancies at Central Information Commission (CIC) leading to a backlog of over 23,000 pending appeals.
  • Several State Information Commissions have been defunct since 2020.
  • Justice Surya Kant questioned the lack of personnel to enforce transparency laws.
  • Despite previous court orders, there has been little progress in diversifying the pool of Information Commissioners.

Legal and Government Response

  • The Centre has been asked to provide a timeline for filling vacancies.
  • The Department of Personnel and Training must outline the timeline for appointments.
  • States with incomplete processes must publish applicants and search committee composition within two weeks.

Implications

  • The court’s intervention is essential to ensure effective RTI functioning.
  • Delays in appointments undermine public faith in transparency and accountability.
  • The ruling stresses the importance of improving the appointment process to prevent politicization.
