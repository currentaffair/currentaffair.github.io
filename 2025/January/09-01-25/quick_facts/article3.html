Indus Valley Civilization and Dravidian Connection

Key Points

  • Indus Valley Civilization (IVC): Spanned 2,000 sites across 1.5 million sq. km. in India, Pakistan, and Afghanistan.
  • Undeciphered Script: The Indus script remains undeciphered, hindering the understanding of Harappan culture.
  • $1 Million Prize: Tamil Nadu CM M.K. Stalin announced a $1 million prize for deciphering the Indus script.
  • Dravidian Hypothesis: Some scholars suggest the Indus script may encode a proto-Dravidian language, based on linguistic evidence.
  • Recent Findings: A study of graffiti marks in Tamil Nadu reveals parallels with the Indus script.
  • Cultural Contact: Material evidence suggests potential cultural exchanges between IVC and South India during the Copper Age and Iron Age.

Future Prospects

  • Further Research: More archaeological and linguistic studies are needed to confirm the cultural links between the IVC and South India.
