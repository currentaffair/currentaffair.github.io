Draft Digital Personal Data Protection Rules, 2025

The Ministry of Electronics & IT has recently released the draft Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Rules, 2025, aimed at safeguarding citizens' personal data.

Key Points of DPDP Rules

The rules operationalize the DPDP Act, 2023, focusing on protecting citizens' digital data while promoting India's digital economy.

Data Rights and Responsibilities

Citizens have rights to demand data erasure, appoint digital nominees, and manage their data through Data Fiduciaries, who include platforms like social media and e-commerce websites.

Data Erasure and Retention

Data can be retained for up to three years after the last interaction. Data Fiduciaries must notify users at least 48 hours before erasure.

Grievance Redressal and Compliance

The DPBI (Data Protection Board of India) will resolve complaints, with graded responsibilities for startups and large data fiduciaries.
