China’s Long Game in Africa

China’s Strategy

China is deepening its influence in Africa through political engagement, establishing schools, and expanding diplomatic ties. In 2022, it launched the Mwalimu Julius Nyerere Leadership School in Tanzania, aimed at training future African leaders in Chinese governance principles.

Target Audience

The first cohort of the school included 120 officials from countries with historical ties to China, such as South Africa, Mozambique, Angola, Namibia, Zimbabwe, and Tanzania.

Promoting Governance Model

The leadership school fits into China’s broader goal of promoting a centralized, party-controlled governance system, similar to China’s political structure, and strengthening ties with ruling African parties.

China’s Broader Influence

China invests in educational exchanges and refurbishing political institutions, such as the Herbert Chitepo School in Zimbabwe, and has political ties across 51 African countries.

Geopolitical and Economic Impact

China's growing influence in Africa is part of its strategy to challenge the Western-led global order. Africa’s 55 countries play a critical role in multilateral forums, which is key to China’s ambitions.

Long-Term Goals

China’s strategy includes nurturing ties with opposition parties and maintaining influence regardless of political regime changes. Its approach combines diplomacy and influence-building rather than relying solely on economic or military power.

Conclusion

China’s political education programs and investments reflect a long-term strategy to embed itself in Africa’s political and governance systems, shaping African politics and global governance.
