6 Killed, 20 Hurt in Tirupati Stampede

Incident Overview

6 people were killed, and over 20 were injured in a stampede at the Vishnu Nivasam ticket counter in Tirupati during the rush for Vaikunta Ekadasi festival tickets on January 12. The festival attracts thousands of devotees for darshan at the Tirumala temple.

Cause and Chaos

The crowd rushed to collect tickets starting from 5 a.m. on Thursday, causing chaos due to inadequate crowd management. Key areas like Vishnu Nivasam and Bairagipatteda saw the worst of the mismanagement, with police struggling to control the situation.

Government Reaction

Andhra Pradesh CM N. Chandrababu Naidu expressed shock, urging strict action against TTD employees responsible for the chaos and ensuring the injured received the best medical care.

Concerns and Way Forward

The incident raised concerns about the lack of proper planning by TTD, despite prior knowledge of the festival's popularity. The way forward includes strengthening crowd management protocols, introducing online ticketing, and conducting an inquiry to fix accountability.
