Key Points of the 2023 Delhi Floods

  • Forecasting Inaccuracy: The Central Water Commission (CWC) faced issues forecasting the flood levels accurately, partly due to obstacles in the Yamuna River.
  • Key Causes: Obstructions such as silt deposits, tree growth, and construction muck impaired river flow, which affected flood predictions.
  • Flood Impact: The river's water level reached 208.66 meters on July 13, 2023, severely affecting central Delhi, especially around ITO.
  • Government Response: The CWC's findings were presented to the National Green Tribunal (NGT), which raised concerns after an article in The Hindu exposed inaccuracies.

Key Findings and Solutions

  • River Flow Management: Non-opening of gates at the ITO barrage and construction debris worsened river flow conditions.
  • Recommendations: Better flood forecasting, improved infrastructure around river channels, and stricter construction regulations are essential.
