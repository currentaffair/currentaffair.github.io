GDP Growth Projections

Overview

India's GDP is projected to grow at 6.4% in FY 2024-25, down from 8.2% in FY 2023-24. The growth is driven by economic recovery, with a 6% growth in the first half of the year and a projected 6.8% in the second half to meet the 6.4% target.

Sector Performance

  • Agriculture: Expected growth of 3.8%, up from 1.4% in FY 2023-24.
  • Public Administration, Defence, and Other Services: Expected growth of 9.1%, up from 7.8% in FY 2023-24.
  • Manufacturing: Expected to slow down with a growth rate of 5.3%, down from 9.9% last year.
  • Mining and Quarrying: Growth forecast at 2.9%, a decrease from 7.1% in the previous year.

Investment Concerns

Gross Fixed Capital Formation (GFCF), an indicator of new investments, is projected to grow by just 6.4%, down from 9% in FY 2023-24.

GDP Value

The real GDP for FY 2024-25 is estimated at ₹184.88 lakh crore, compared to ₹173.82 lakh crore in FY 2023-24.

Budget Implications

The revised growth projection highlights the challenge of reviving economic growth above 7% for the 2025-26 Union Budget.

Projections from RBI and Finance Ministry

The Reserve Bank of India revised its growth forecast to 6.6%, down from 7.2%, while the Finance Ministry lowered its GDP growth estimate to around 6.5%.
