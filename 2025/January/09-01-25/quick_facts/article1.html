Drop in Student Enrolment, 2023-24

Change in Data Collection Methodology

The Ministry of Education (MoE) introduced a new data collection method in 2022-23, including Aadhaar numbers, which corrected overestimated enrolment figures. This led to a decline from 26.3 crore in 2018-19 to 24.8 crore in 2023-24.

Reasons for Decline

The sharp decrease is attributed to the elimination of duplicate enrolments and adjustments for inflated records. However, the MoE has not provided clear explanations for the decline or the reduction in government schools.

Decline in Schools

Between 2017-18 and 2023-24, over 87,000 schools were closed, primarily in government-run institutions. Key states like Jammu and Kashmir, Assam, and Uttar Pradesh saw significant reductions.

Impact on Students

School closures, especially in rural areas, hinder re-admission processes and lead to higher dropout rates. Distance and inconvenience are major reasons for students leaving the education system.

State-Specific Trends

States like Jammu and Kashmir lost 4,509 schools, which may exacerbate accessibility issues and increase dropout rates due to long distances to the nearest schools.

UDISe+ Data Caution

The MoE has warned that the 2022-23 and 2023-24 UDISE+ data is not directly comparable to previous years due to changes in methodology, though dropout and retention rates are still considered valid for analysis.
