Decline in Student Enrolment and School Numbers

Total student enrolment dropped by over 1 crore from 26.3 crore (2018–19) to 24.8 crore in 2023-24, mainly due to changes in data collection methodology and elimination of duplicate enrolments.

Change in Methodology

From 2022-23, the Ministry of Education (MoE) began collecting individual student data, including Aadhaar numbers. This new approach revealed that previous enrolment figures were overestimated by 5-6%.

Reasons for Decline

The significant drop in enrolment is attributed to the correction of inflated enrolment numbers, including the elimination of duplicates.

Decline in Schools

The number of schools under UDISE+ decreased by 87,012 from 2017-18 to 2023-24, with most reductions in government-run schools, particularly in Jammu and Kashmir, Assam, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, and Maharashtra.

Impact of School Closures

School closures, especially in rural areas, make it difficult for students to seek re-admission, contributing to higher dropout rates due to distance and inconvenience.

Effect on Funding

The decline in enrolment raises concerns about the effective utilization of funds, particularly under schemes like Samagra Shiksha, which allocated ₹32,515 crore in 2022-23 despite the decrease in enrolment.

State-Specific Trends

States like Jammu and Kashmir, Assam, and Uttar Pradesh experienced the largest reductions in schools, leading to accessibility issues and higher dropout rates.

UDISe+ Data Caution

The MoE cautioned that the UDISE+ data for 2022-23 and 2023-24 is not directly comparable to previous years due to methodological changes. However, key indicators like dropout and retention rates remain comparable.
