New ISRO Chairman Dr. V. Narayanan

Overview

Dr. V. Narayanan will take over as the new Chairman of ISRO and Secretary of the Department of Space starting January 14, 2025, for a two-year tenure. He will oversee crucial upcoming missions including NVS-02, Gaganyaan, and the NISAR satellite launch.

Dr. Narayanan’s Role

  • Upcoming Missions: Narayanan will lead significant missions such as NVS-02 for India’s navigation system, the Gaganyaan unmanned mission, and the Indo-US NISAR satellite launch in March 2025.
  • Previous Contributions: He will succeed Dr. S. Somanath, who led the Chandrayaan-3, Aditya-L1, XpoSat, and INSAT missions, and initiated the SSLV and RLV-LEX programs.

ISRO's Journey

  • Foundation: ISRO was formed on August 15, 1969, and succeeded INCOSPAR (Indian National Committee for Space Research) founded by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai.
  • Space Commission: Established in 1972, with ISRO coming under its control and the Department of Space (DoS) being created.
  • Headquarters: Located at Antariksh Bhavan in Bangalore.
