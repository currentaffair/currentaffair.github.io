Border Fencing on in West Bengal despite BGB Objections: BSF

Overview

The BSF continues fencing work in Sukdebpur area, West Bengal, despite opposition from the Border Guard Bangladesh (BGB). The BSF asserts that the fencing is being carried out well within Indian territory in the South Bengal Frontier area.

Dispute and Allegations

The BGB has made allegations in the media regarding their control over a 5-km stretch of Indian land, which the BSF strongly denied, calling it baseless. The contested area lies in Ranaghat village, North 24 Parganas district, India.

Political Context

The border fencing issue has been exacerbated by political instability in Bangladesh, particularly following the fall of the Sheikh Hasina regime. This has highlighted the vulnerabilities along the 2,216-km border between India and Bangladesh.

Local Reactions

BJP MLA Swapan Majumdar visited the Angrail area and interacted with BSF officials and local villagers to address concerns about the ongoing border fencing.

Way Forward

To curb illegal activities like smuggling and migration, India should prioritize completing the border fence. Diplomatic efforts are needed to resolve tensions and prevent misunderstandings over territorial disputes.
