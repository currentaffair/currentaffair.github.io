Coal Mine Rescue in Assam

Nine coal miners have been trapped in a water-filled mine in Dima Hasao district, Assam, since 8 a.m. on January 8, 2025. Authorities were informed around 2 p.m. on the same day.

Rescue Efforts

The Navy, along with deep divers, joined the rescue operations on January 9, 2025. Other agencies involved include the Army, Assam Rifles, National Disaster Response Force (NDRF), State Disaster Response Force (SDRF), and local police.

Mine Conditions

The mine is 20 feet in radius and 300 feet in depth. The water level inside has risen to over 100 feet, complicating rescue efforts.

Casualties and Arrests

Three bodies were seen from the surface but have not been recovered. Punish Nunisa was arrested for illegal mining under the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957.

Historical Context

Mining mishaps in Assam are frequent, including incidents in 2024, 2022, and earlier disasters like the 2018 Ksan coal mine collapse in Meghalaya.

Investigation and Government Response

The Chief Minister confirmed that the mine appeared to be illegal. Additional equipment is required due to the mine's tunnel system, and local authorities are investigating its legality.

Challenges in Rescue Operations

The mine is located 30 kilometers from the nearest police station in a remote jungle, with the high water level creating significant challenges for rescue teams.
