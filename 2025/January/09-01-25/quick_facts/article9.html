Climate Change and African Easterly Waves

Why in News?

A study predicts that climate change will increase extreme flooding in the Sahel region, driven by changes in African easterly waves (AEWs).

Key Highlights of the Study

  • Increased AEW Activity: AEWs are expected to intensify over the Sahel-Sahara region by the end of the century.
  • Enhanced Monsoon Flow: Warming reinforces monsoon flow, affecting AEWs formation.
  • Saharan Dust Transport: Strong winds can delay tropical cyclones and affect rainfall.
  • Connection to MCSs: Increased AEW activity may lead to more frequent flooding in the Sahel.

African Easterly Waves (AEWs)

AEWs are weather systems that bring rainstorms and can influence Atlantic hurricanes. They affect the hydroclimate in the Sahel, making their behavior under global warming critical.

The Sahel Region

The Sahel is a semiarid region of Africa, acting as a transition zone between the Sahara Desert and the humid savannas. The region is vital for water sources like the Niger River.
