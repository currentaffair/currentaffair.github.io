Stampede at Tirumala Temple Ticket Counter

Location: Vishnu Nivasam ticket counter, Tirupati

Casualties: 6 people killed, over 20 critically injured

Cause: Stampede during rush to collect tickets for the Vaikunta Ekadasi festival at Tirumala temple on January 12 (Friday)

Context

The Vaikunta Ekadasi festival draws thousands of devotees for darshan at the Tirumala temple. The Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD) had set up around 90 ticket counters in Tirupati, but the overwhelming crowd led to chaos and mismanagement.

Chaos and Mismanagement

The crowd management arrangements were inadequate, with key areas like Vishnu Nivasam, Bairagipatteda, and Ramachandra Pushkarini being overcrowded. The police struggled to control the situation.

Immediate Response

The injured were taken to Sri Venkateswara Ramnarain Ruia Hospital. The authorities have not yet identified the victims.

Government Reaction

Andhra Pradesh CM N. Chandrababu Naidu expressed shock and called for the best medical care for the injured, as well as strict action against TTD employees found guilty of mismanagement.

Concerns Raised

There were concerns about the lack of crowd control measures despite the prior knowledge of the festival's popularity and the inadequate infrastructure and planning by TTD.

Way Forward

The authorities should strengthen crowd management protocols, introduce online ticketing systems, and conduct an inquiry to improve future arrangements.
