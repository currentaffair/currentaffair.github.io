India-ASEAN Relations

Introduction

Indonesia’s participation in India’s Republic Day celebrations reflects the deep-rooted cultural and strategic ties between India and ASEAN. As ASEAN’s largest economy and a key G20 member, Indonesia exemplifies India’s broader engagement with the region, particularly in maritime security and economic cooperation. India-ASEAN relations have evolved from historical solidarity to a comprehensive strategic partnership, marked by shared interests in regional stability and prosperity.

Historical and Cultural Ties

India's influence on Southeast Asia is evident through the spread of Buddhism, Hinduism, and Sanskrit. Ancient trade routes between India and Southeast Asian kingdoms facilitated cultural exchanges, seen in landmarks like Angkor Wat and Ramayana traditions.

Colonial Era and Anti-Colonial Struggles

India's support for Indonesia's independence during colonial times cemented early bonds between the nations. The Bandung Conference of 1955 marked India's engagement with newly independent Asian and African nations, focusing on anti-colonial solidarity.

Post-Independence Period

From the 1950s to the 1980s, India's non-alignment policy and domestic priorities led to limited engagement with ASEAN. However, the region remained significant in India's foreign policy outlook.

Economic and Strategic Engagement

The 1990s and 2000s saw India’s shift toward deeper engagement with Southeast Asia through initiatives like the Look East Policy. India's trade and strategic cooperation with ASEAN grew substantially, particularly after India became a Sectoral Dialogue Partner of ASEAN in 1992.

Look East Policy

Launched after India’s economic liberalization, the Look East Policy aimed to strengthen economic, political, and security ties with Southeast Asia. Over the years, India upgraded its relationship with ASEAN, culminating in the Comprehensive Strategic Partnership (CSP) in 2022.

Key Areas of Cooperation

India and ASEAN share common concerns in the Indo-Pacific region, particularly regarding maritime security and China's growing assertiveness in the South China Sea. India supports ASEAN's centrality in the Indo-Pacific and has increased joint naval exercises and cooperation in areas such as trade, infrastructure, and defense.

Strategic Cooperation and Security

India and ASEAN have enhanced maritime cooperation through joint naval exercises and strategic dialogue. India’s Indo-Pacific Oceans Initiative (IPOI) aligns with ASEAN’s Outlook on the Indo-Pacific (AOIP), promoting regional stability.

Trade and Economic Integration

ASEAN is one of India’s largest trading partners, with trade growing substantially over the last decade. The India-ASEAN Free Trade Agreement (FTA) is being reviewed to address trade imbalances, particularly focusing on supply chain resilience and the digital economy.

Energy Security and Green Transition

Both India and ASEAN emphasize renewable energy cooperation, aiming to reduce reliance on fossil fuels. India’s role in promoting green hydrogen, solar power, and biofuels is central to this collaboration.

Challenges in India-ASEAN Relations

Despite strong cooperation, challenges remain. Trade imbalances, delays in connectivity projects, and differences in defense strategies pose obstacles to deeper integration. ASEAN’s reliance on China for trade and its cautious stance on military engagement with India further complicate relations.

Trade Imbalance and RCEP Withdrawal

India faces a significant trade deficit with ASEAN, which has raised concerns over market access and unfair trade practices. India’s decision to withdraw from the Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP) in 2019 was a response to concerns over China’s dominance in the trade bloc.

Measures to Strengthen India-ASEAN Relations

To improve relations, India and ASEAN can fast-track connectivity projects, strengthen the FTA, and collaborate on emerging technologies. Joint defense initiatives and the promotion of renewable energy projects will also enhance cooperation between the two regions.

Accelerating Connectivity Projects

India should prioritize infrastructure connectivity, including special economic zones (SEZs) and maritime links, to enhance trade and tourism between India and ASEAN countries.

Strengthening the FTA

To address India’s trade deficit, both sides should revise the India-ASEAN Free Trade Agreement, focusing on market access for Indian goods and services, and removing trade barriers.

Conclusion

India-ASEAN relations have evolved into a comprehensive strategic partnership with significant cooperation in economic, security, and cultural domains. While challenges such as trade imbalances and defense priorities persist, both sides are working to deepen engagement. Strengthening connectivity, enhancing trade, and fostering people-to-people exchanges are key to furthering cooperation in the coming years.
