Rakhigarhi Reservoir & Saraswati

Rakhigarhi Site

Rakhigarhi, located in Haryana's Hisar district, is a significant Harappan site, discovered in the 1960s. It is one of the largest cities of the Indus Valley Civilization, dating back to 6500 BCE. The site features planned neighborhoods, mud-brick houses, and artifacts like red ware ceramics, seals, and animal sacrifice pits.

Key Findings

Important findings at Rakhigarhi include seven archaeological hills, cylindrical seals with Harappan characters, terracotta objects, and bone points. The site provides evidence of early Harappan practices and funerary customs, offering insight into the Harappan culture.

Saraswati River

The Saraswati River, mentioned in the Rigveda, was once a major river flowing through Haryana, Rajasthan, North Gujarat, and Pakistan. It had two branches—Western and Eastern—and flowed between 6000 and 4000 BCE, lasting over 2000 years. It is believed to have met the western sea near the Rann of Kutch.

Harappan Civilization Sites

Several important Harappan sites like Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro, Kalibangan, and Lothal have been excavated, showcasing advanced urban planning, trade, and cultural practices. Rakhigarhi adds to the understanding of the Harappan civilization, providing unique insights into its early phases.
