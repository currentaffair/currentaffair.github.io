Pangsau Pass Festival 2025

Overview

The Pangsau Pass International Festival returns to Nampong, Arunachal Pradesh, after a four-year break, bringing together cultures from India and Myanmar.

Pangsau Pass

Located on the Patkai Hills along the India-Myanmar border, Pangsau Pass is named after a nearby Burmese village. It holds historical significance, having been a key route for the Shan tribe’s invasion into Assam in the 13th century and a crucial obstacle for General Stilwell’s road-building effort during WWII. The pass is also known as "Hell Pass" due to the tough terrain and is famous for the "Lake of No Return" visible from its summit.

Pangsau Pass International Festival

Held annually in Nampong, Arunachal Pradesh, the festival promotes cross-trade and cultural exchange, showcasing the diverse cultures of the Northeast tribes and Myanmar. It highlights the traditions, arts, and crafts of both regions.

Festivals in Northeast India

Various festivals are celebrated across Northeast India, including the Hornbill Festival in Nagaland, Bohag Bihu in Assam, and Dree Festival in Arunachal Pradesh, each marking cultural, agricultural, and religious significance.

Important Passes in India

India is home to several important passes like Nathu La in Sikkim, Khardung La in Ladakh, and Bum La in Arunachal Pradesh. These passes serve strategic, military, and trade purposes, connecting India to neighboring regions like Tibet and China.
