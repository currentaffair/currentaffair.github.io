Nahargarh Wildlife Sanctuary

About Nahargarh Wildlife Sanctuary

Established in 1980, the Nahargarh Wildlife Sanctuary is located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. It is named after the Nahargarh Fort and is part of the Ranthambore Tiger Reserve corridor.

Geography and Flora

The sanctuary is spread across approximately 50 square kilometers in the Aravalli Hills. It is home to dry deciduous and thorny forests, with key trees like DHOK, Babool, and Khejri. Various grass and shrub species also thrive here.

Fauna

The sanctuary is home to leopards, hyenas, wild boars, jackals, sambars, peacocks, migratory birds, reptiles, and pollinators, creating a rich biodiversity.

Legal Revisions

The Forest Department has planned to revise the sanctuary's boundaries to address legal issues and enhance its conservation efforts.

Other Wildlife Sanctuaries in Rajasthan

Rajasthan houses numerous wildlife sanctuaries, such as Keoladeo Ghana National Park, Mukundra Hills National Park, and Ranthambore National Park, all contributing to the region's rich biodiversity and conservation efforts.
