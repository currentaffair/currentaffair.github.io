India's Civilian Service Awards

Overview

On Republic Day 2025, India honored individuals and groups for their exceptional contributions across various fields.

Civilian Service Awards

The Bharat Ratna and Padma Awards are Indiaâ€™s highest civilian honors. Bharat Ratna, established in 1954, is awarded for exceptional service in any field. Padma Awards, also established in 1954, consist of Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Shri, recognizing distinguished service in multiple fields. The awards this year included 7 Padma Vibhushan, 19 Padma Bhushan, and 113 Padma Shri, with 23 women among the awardees.

Service Medals

Service Medals are awarded to recognize distinguished service in the Armed Forces, Paramilitary Forces, and Police. The President's Medal for Distinguished Service (PSM) and Medal for Meritorious Service (MSM) were announced this year, with 101 PSMs and 746 MSMs awarded.

Gallantry Awards

Gallantry awards are given for acts of bravery. The highest wartime awards are the Param Vir Chakra, Mahavir Chakra, and Vir Chakra. Peacetime awards include the Ashoka Chakra, Kirti Chakra, and Shaurya Chakra. Police Gallantry Medals are given to recognize bravery in the police force, while Civilian Gallantry Medals like the Jeevan Raksha Padak and National Bravery Awards honor civilians for acts of bravery.
