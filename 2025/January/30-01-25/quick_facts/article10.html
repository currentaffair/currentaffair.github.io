Blue Carbon and Mangroves

What is Blue Carbon?

Blue carbon refers to carbon captured by ocean and coastal ecosystems like mangroves, seabeds, and salt swamps. These ecosystems store more carbon per unit area than terrestrial forests and play a significant role in climate change mitigation.

Causes of Loss

Human activities like aquaculture, agriculture, industrial and urban development, and pollution contribute to the degradation of blue carbon ecosystems.

Economic Benefits

Mangrove restoration brings ecological and economic benefits, including boosting coastal GDP by up to 15%. The OECD states that every $1 invested in blue carbon restoration yields $6 in economic benefits.

Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ)

The CRZ policy governs development in coastal areas. It includes zoning provisions and restrictions on harmful activities like industrial expansion and untreated waste disposal.

Zone Key Features
CRZ-I Ecologically sensitive areas like national parks and mangroves.
CRZ-II Developed coastal areas with infrastructure.
CRZ-III Undisturbed rural coastal areas with limited development.
CRZ-IV Coastal stretches in Andaman & Nicobar Islands and Lakshadweep.
