Mumbai Coastal Road Bridge

What is the Bow-String Arch Bridge?

The bow-string arch bridge is part of the Mumbai Coastal Road Project (MCRP), designed to connect Mumbai's Coastal Road to the Bandra-Worli Sea Link (BWSL). It aims to reduce traffic congestion and offer a direct north-south route along the western coastline.

Bridge Design Features

The bridge spans 827 meters, with 699 meters over the Arabian Sea. It features a 143-meter long, 27-meter wide, and 31-meter high 'Bo Arch String Girder' weighing 2,400 metric tons. Its use of high-tension chords minimizes the number of pillars and environmental impact.

Mumbai Coastal Road Project

The MCRP is a large-scale infrastructure project aimed at constructing a 29.2 km, 8-lane expressway along Mumbai's western coastline. It will connect Marine Drive in the south to Kandivali in the north and include India's first undersea tunnel, significantly reducing travel time.

Project Goals

The MCRP aims to ease traffic congestion and create a sustainable, efficient, and livable urban environment for Mumbai's residents, transforming the city's infrastructure.
