Tibet Earthquake - January 9, 2025

On January 9, 2025, a powerful earthquake struck Tibet, causing at least 126 deaths and 188 injuries. The earthquake, with a magnitude of 6.8 to 7.1, hit Tingri County, Xigaze, near Mount Everest and Nepal. Tremors were also felt in Nepal and parts of India.

Magnitude and Impact

The earthquake, one of the deadliest in China since 2014, caused widespread destruction. 3,609 houses collapsed, and rescue efforts are ongoing in the rugged mountainous terrain. Aftershocks followed the main tremor, complicating the relief work.

Casualties and Damage

126 people were confirmed dead, and 188 others were injured, with many in critical condition. Damage was widespread, particularly in the rural and less-developed areas of Tingri County, making rescue efforts challenging.

International Reactions

The Dalai Lama expressed sadness, and Chinese officials, including President Xi Jinping, have extended condolences and called for urgent rescue operations. Local authorities are providing essential supplies to the affected populations.

Challenges in Recovery

The mountainous terrain and subzero temperatures are complicating rescue efforts. The region has previously experienced significant seismic activity, highlighting its vulnerability to earthquakes.
