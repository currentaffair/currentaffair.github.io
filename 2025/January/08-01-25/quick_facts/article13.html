Supreme Court Criticism on RTI Appointments

The Supreme Court criticized the delay in appointing Information Commissioners, which hampers the effective functioning of the Right to Information (RTI) Act.

Key Points

The Central Information Commission has eight vacant posts, resulting in over 23,000 pending RTI appeals. Several state commissions have been defunct since 2020, and some no longer accept RTI petitions.

Justice Surya Kant questioned the purpose of transparency institutions if there are no personnel to enforce the law. He also expressed concern about the appointment of retired bureaucrats, limiting diversity.

The Supreme Court had issued previous judgments urging the diversification of Information Commissioners, but little progress was made. The Centre has been asked to file an affidavit detailing a timeline for filling vacancies.

The court's directives include clear timelines for appointments, publication of search committee composition, and compliance reports from Chief Secretaries.
