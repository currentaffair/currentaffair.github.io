India's First Organic Fisheries Cluster in Sikkim

India launched its first organic fisheries cluster in Soreng District, Sikkim, under the Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY). This initiative aims to produce eco-friendly, antibiotic, chemical, and pesticide-free organic fish for global markets.

Key Features

The cluster promotes sustainable aquaculture, preventing pollution and preserving aquatic ecosystems. The National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development will fund infrastructure and support Fisheries-based Farmer Producer Organizations (FFPOs).

Sikkim: India's First Organic State

Sikkim, with 75,000 hectares of certified organic land, is India's first fully organic state. PMMSY focuses on enhancing fish production, infrastructure, and marketing while ensuring the welfare of fishers and farmers, with an investment of Rs 20,050 crores.
