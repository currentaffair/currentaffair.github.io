Jammu and Rayagada Railway Division Inauguration

Prime Minister virtually inaugurated India's 70th railway division, Jammu, carved from the Firozpur Division, covering 742.1 km. Additionally, the Cherlapally terminal in Telangana was inaugurated, and the foundation for the Rayagada railway division in Odisha was laid.

Key Developments

The Jammu Railway Division covers 742.1 km, with a new Katra-Reasi railway section and Vande Bharat train services planned to start in January 2025. Indian Railways now comprises 17 zones and 70 divisions.

Future Plans

The PM highlighted ongoing efforts in expanding metro networks, dedicated freight corridors, solar-powered stations, and the development of high-speed rail systems.

About Indian Railways

Indian Railways, established in 1853, is one of the largest railway networks globally. By 2050, India is projected to account for 40% of global rail activity. India has the 3rd largest metro-rail network in the world after China and the US.
