Maoist Activity and Security Updates

Recent trends show a significant reduction in large-scale attacks by Maoists, with a notable exception in Bijapur (8 killed). Despite the drop in fatalities, the region remains a focal point for left-wing extremism.

Key Points

In 2024, Maoist casualties totaled 296, the third-highest since 2000. However, only 24 security personnel died, marking the second-lowest number in recent history. Bastar continues to be the epicenter of Maoist activity in Chhattisgarh, accounting for nearly 30% of related deaths between 2020-2025.

Decline in Maoist Influence

Maoist influence has sharply contracted due to tribal communities distancing themselves from insurgents. The 2023 Chhattisgarh elections reflected this shift, with a notable decline in NOTA votes. Other states, including Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Jharkhand, have seen a decrease in Maoist activity and fatalities.

Implications

While large-scale attacks have declined, the Maoists still retain the capacity for high-casualty incidents, especially in Bastar. Continued security efforts and addressing socio-political grievances are crucial to prevent further escalation.
