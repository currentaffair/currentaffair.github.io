358th Birth Anniversary of Guru Gobind Singh Ji

The Prime Minister recently paid tributes to Guru Gobind Singh Ji, the 10th Sikh Guru, on his 358th birth anniversary, celebrated as Prakash Utsav.

About Guru Gobind Singh Ji

Born on 22nd December 1666 in Patna Sahib, Bihar, he succeeded his father, Guru Teg Bahadur. Guru Gobind Singh Ji is known for establishing the Khalsa in 1699, which was a warrior community dedicated to protecting faith and justice.

He introduced the Five K’s as symbols of Sikh identity: Kangha, Kesh, Kara, Kirpan, and Kachhera.

Guru Gobind Singh Ji's sons, Zorwar Singh and Fateh Singh, were executed for their refusal to convert to Islam. His elder sons, Ajit Singh and Jujhar Singh, sacrificed their lives in the Battle of Chamkaur in 1705. The martyrdom of his sons is observed on 26th December as ‘Veer Bal Diwas’.

Guru Gobind Singh also established the Panj Pyare, an institution where five men volunteered for sacrifice.
