India's GDP Growth Projections - FY 2024-25

India's GDP is expected to grow at 6.4% in FY 2024-25, a decline from 8.2% in FY 2023-24. This marks the lowest growth rate in four years, according to the National Statistics Office.

Economic Recovery

The economy grew by 6% in the first half of the year, with a projected 6.8% growth in the second half to meet the full-year target of 6.4%.

Sector Performance

  • Agriculture: Growth expected at 3.8%, up from 1.4% in FY 2023-24.
  • Public Administration & Defence: Expected to grow by 9.1%, up from 7.8% last year.
  • Manufacturing: Expected growth of 5.3%, down from 9.9% last year.
  • Mining & Quarrying: Forecasted growth at 2.9%, a decrease from 7.1% last year.

Investment Concerns

Gross Fixed Capital Formation (GFCF), a key investment indicator, is projected to grow by 6.4%, down from 9% last year.

GDP Value

The real GDP for FY 2024-25 is estimated at ₹184.88 lakh crore, up from ₹173.82 lakh crore in FY 2023-24.

Budget Implications

Reviving economic growth above 7% will be a major challenge for the 2025-26 Union Budget.

RBI & Finance Ministry Projections

The Reserve Bank of India reduced its growth forecast to 6.6% from 7.2%, while the Finance Ministry revised its range to 6.5%.
