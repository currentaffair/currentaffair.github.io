78th Foundation Day of Bureau of Indian Standards

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) celebrated its 78th Foundation Day on 6th January 2025. BIS is the statutory national standard body of India, established under the BIS Act, 2016, for the development of standardization, marking, and quality certification of goods.

About BIS

Initially set up as the Indian Standards Institution (ISI) on 6th January 1947, BIS functions under the Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food, and Public Distribution.

Functions of BIS

BIS operates various schemes such as Product Certification (ISI mark), Hallmarking of Gold and Silver Jewellery, and the ECO Mark Scheme for environmentally friendly products.

Standards National Action Plan (SNAP) 2022-27

This plan focuses on standardization to address emerging technologies and concerns related to sustainability and climate change. It is key to strengthening the "Quality Culture" in the nation.

Achievements of BIS

94% of Indian standards have been harmonized with international standards (ISO and IEC). Over 44.28 crore articles of gold, jewelry, and artifacts have been hallmarked to date.
