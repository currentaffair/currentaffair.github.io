Deepfakes and Their Impact on Women in Politics

Deepfakes are AI-generated altered images or videos, often created with malicious intent such as producing non-consensual explicit content.

Victims of Deepfakes

  • Women Politicians: Women, especially politicians from countries like the U.S., Italy, the U.K., and Pakistan, are increasingly targeted.
  • High-profile Victims: Notable victims include U.S. Congresswomen, U.K. Deputy Prime Minister Angela Rayner, and Italy’s Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni.

Why Deepfakes are Concerning

  • Reputation Damage: Deepfakes harm the credibility of victims, affecting their careers.
  • Harassment and Blackmail: Deepfakes lead to threats and exploitation.
  • Impact on Democracy: Harassment discourages women from participating in politics, fearing further victimization.

How AI Enables Deepfakes

  • Technological Advancements: Cheap, widely available AI tools make creating deepfakes easier.
  • Apps for Digital Manipulation: Apps allow users to alter images, including creating explicit deepfakes.

Case Studies

  • U.S. Congresswomen: Over 35,000 deepfake instances involving 26 Congresswomen were found on pornographic websites.
  • Italy: Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni sought damages after deepfake videos were shared online.
  • Pakistan: Lawmakers Meena Majeed and Azma Bukhari were targeted with deepfakes in sensitive contexts.

Impact on Women’s Participation in Politics

  • Chilling Effect: The fear of harassment from deepfakes discourages women from entering political careers.

Legal and Regulatory Challenges

  • Global Disparities: Regulations on deepfakes are inconsistent worldwide.
  • U.K. and U.S. Efforts: The U.K. is working on banning sexualized deepfakes, and several U.S. states have criminalized their creation.
  • Pakistan’s Lack of Legislation: Pakistan lacks specific laws to combat sexualized deepfakes.

Future Risks

  • Disparity of Privilege: Women with fewer resources struggle to have deepfakes removed.
  • Systemic Inequalities: High-profile figures may get faster responses for takedown, while everyday women face prolonged harm.

Conclusion

The growing use of AI-generated deepfakes to target women, especially politicians, is a serious threat to privacy, safety, and participation in public life. Global regulations and stronger protections are urgently needed to address this issue.
