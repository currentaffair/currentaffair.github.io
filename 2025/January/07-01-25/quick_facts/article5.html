CBI Exempt from State Consent for Central Employees

The Supreme Court recently ruled that the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) can register cases under Central laws, like the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, against Central government employees in states without state consent.

What is State Consent for CBI?

  • About: Refers to the need for CBI to seek permission from state governments before investigating cases within their jurisdiction.
  • Legal Basis: Section 6 of the Delhi Special Police Establishment (DSPE) Act, 1946, requires state consent unless directed otherwise by the judiciary or central government.
  • Types of Consent: General Consent (blanket permission) and Specific Consent (case-specific permission).

About CBI

  • Establishment: The CBI was established in 1963 following recommendations by the Santhanam Committee.
  • Functioning: Operates under the Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances, and Pensions.
  • Supervision: Oversighted by the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) for Prevention of Corruption Act cases.
  • Director Appointment: Recommended by a committee including the Prime Minister, Leader of Opposition, and Chief Justice of India under the Lokpal Act, 2014.
