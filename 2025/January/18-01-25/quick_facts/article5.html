2024 Climate Anomaly and Global Warming Impact

In 2024, global temperatures were 1.55°C above pre-industrial levels, surpassing the 1.5°C threshold outlined in the Paris Agreement. Water vapour in the atmosphere was 4.9% higher than the 1991–2020 average, marking a significant rise.

Global Warming Feedback Loops

Warmer temperatures increase evaporation, allowing the atmosphere to retain more water vapour, which amplifies global warming. Water vapour, as a potent greenhouse gas, sets the stage for extreme weather events like heavy rainfall and storms.

Extreme Weather Events in 2024

2024 saw catastrophic floods in Sudan, Nigeria, Niger, and other regions, along with the devastation caused by Hurricane Helene. The increased atmospheric moisture directly influenced these extreme weather events.

Oceanic Heat and Moisture

Sea surface temperatures were 0.61°C above the 1981–2020 baseline. High SSTs fueled record ocean heat content, contributing to more atmospheric moisture and worsening extreme weather events.

Geographical Anomalies

Regions such as India, North America, Sahara, Russia, and China recorded unprecedented moisture levels, further intensifying the impact of climate change in these areas.

Scientific Consensus

Experts confirm that greenhouse gases are amplifying atmospheric moisture and warming. Although single-year anomalies do not mark the immediate failure of climate goals, they underline the urgent need for effective mitigation strategies.

Future Risks

Accelerating feedback loops involving warming, moisture, and ocean heat content pose risks of more frequent extreme weather, glacial melting, and sea-level rise. Global policy responses are crucial to mitigate these effects.
