National Turmeric Board

Key Highlights

Why in News? The Ministry of Commerce & Industry launched the National Turmeric Board (NTB) headquartered in Nizamabad, Telangana.

Objective: Promote turmeric cultivation, production, and marketing in India while enhancing farmers' income.

Representation: Includes members from Ministries of AYUSH, Pharmaceuticals, Agriculture, and Commerce, and state representatives.

Key Goals: Focus on farmer welfare, research, product innovation, and increasing global exports.

About Turmeric

Botanical Info: Turmeric, part of the ginger family, contains curcumin known for anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.

Cultivation: India is the largest global producer, growing over 30 varieties across 20+ states.

Export: India accounts for 62% of global turmeric exports, targeting markets in the US, UAE, Bangladesh, and Malaysia.

GI Tags: Unique varieties include Lakadong (Meghalaya), Kandhamal (Odisha), and Erode (Tamil Nadu).

Medical Benefits: Turmeric reduces inflammation, neutralizes free radicals, and supports digestion.
