Spotted Deer Population in Nagarahole Tiger Reserve

Key Highlights

The spotted deer population in Nagarahole Tiger Reserve has reached a density of 28 per square kilometer, reflecting a healthy ecosystem. The reserve spans 847.981 sq. km and houses 142 tigers, ranking third in India.

Biodiversity and Balance

The reserve is home to various species such as tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and elephants, supporting a balanced prey-predator relationship. An increase in deer population indicates a thriving ecosystem.

Contributing Factors

Factors such as abundant food, diverse habitat, and balanced predator-prey dynamics contribute to the growth of the deer population. The reserve's rich biodiversity ensures ecological stability.

Ecological Implications

The increase in spotted deer suggests a healthy environment for apex predators like tigers. It underscores the importance of conserving wildlife corridors and preserving habitats in the Western Ghats.

Tourism and Future Challenges

With a higher density of wildlife, the reserve attracts eco-tourism, supporting conservation efforts. Future challenges include managing herbivore populations and preventing habitat fragmentation.
