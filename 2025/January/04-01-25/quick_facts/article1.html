Inter-State Translocation of Tigers

Why in News?

Madhya Pradesh is moving 15 tigers to Rajasthan, Chhattisgarh, and Odisha for conservation. This includes 12 tigresses and 3 tigers from Bandhavgarh, Pench, and Kanha tiger reserves.

Translocation Details

The tigers will be distributed as follows:

  • Chhattisgarh: 6 tigresses, 2 tigers
  • Rajasthan: 4 tigresses
  • Odisha: 1 tiger, 2 tigresses

Madhya Pradesh's Tiger Population

Madhya Pradesh has the highest number of tigers in India with 785 tigers, according to the NTCA 2022 report. It has nine tiger reserves, including the newly notified Madhav Tiger Reserve.

Purpose of Translocation

Translocation enhances genetic diversity by introducing new tigers to isolated groups, reducing inbreeding, and supporting long-term species survival.
