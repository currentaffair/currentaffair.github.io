Indo-Pak Exchange of Nuclear and Prisoner Lists

Overview

India and Pakistan recently exchanged lists of nuclear installations and shared details of prisoners and fishermen, despite ongoing strained diplomatic ties.

Exchange of Lists

  • Exchange of prisoner and fisherman lists occurs biannually on January 1st and July 1st under the Agreement on Consular Access (2008).
  • Nuclear installation lists are exchanged annually on January 1st under the Agreement on the Prohibition of Attack against Nuclear Installations (1988).

Historical Context

This was the 34th consecutive exchange, first initiated on January 1, 1992. The nuclear installation exchange agreement was signed on December 31, 1988, and became effective on January 27, 1991.

Agreement Details

Both countries exchange nuclear installation details but do not disclose specific information about their installations.

Legal Framework

Article 36 of the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations (1963) mandates that detained foreign nationals must be informed of their right to have their embassy or consulate notified.
