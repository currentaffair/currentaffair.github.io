Sanskrit Inscription Found in POK

Why in News?

A 4th century CE Sanskrit inscription in Brahmi script was recently discovered near Gilgit, in Pakistan-Occupied Kashmir (POK). The inscription mentions "Pushpasingha" installing a Mahesvaralinga for the merit of his guru.

Related Discovery

In 2024, a 10th-century CE Sanskrit and Sharada script inscription was found near Peshawar, mentioning Buddhist Dharini chants, sacred mantras used in Buddhism for protection and blessings.

Importance of Historical Inscriptions

Inscriptions serve as authentic historical sources, providing details on political history, administrative systems, linguistic evolution, and socio-economic structures. They are crucial in reconstructing ancient Indian history.

Notable Inscriptions of Ancient India

Important inscriptions include:

  • Junagarh Inscription (Rudradaman): 2nd-century CE Sanskrit inscription detailing achievements of Rudradaman and the construction of Sudarshana Lake dam.
  • Bhitari Pillar Inscription: Chronicles Skandagupta’s military success.
  • Paharpur Inscription (Buddha Gupta): Highlights land grants during the Gupta period.
  • Mandsaur Inscription: Details Yashodharman’s victory over the Huns.
  • Gwalior Inscription (King Bhoja I): Describes grants to Brahmins under the Gurjara-Pratiharas.
