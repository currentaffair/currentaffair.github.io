India Protests China’s New Counties in Ladakh

Overview

India has lodged a formal protest with China over the creation of two new counties, He’an and Hekang, in the Hotan prefecture that incorporate territory of India’s Ladakh.

India’s Stance

India maintains its position on sovereignty over the area, rejecting China’s illegal occupation. The creation of new counties does not affect India’s stance.

Hydropower Project Concerns

India raised concerns about China’s mega hydropower project on the Yarlung Tsangpo River (Brahmaputra), which flows through India’s Arunachal Pradesh and Assam. The project is expected to be the world’s largest infrastructure project, and India is worried about the lack of official communication from China.

Diplomatic Engagement

The protest occurred during a meeting between National Security Adviser Ajit Doval and Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi in December 2024, aiming to address tensions following the Galwan clashes of June 2020.

India’s Demand

India urged China to ensure transparency in the dam project and consult downstream countries like India before proceeding with the project. India will continue to monitor the situation to protect its national interests.
