Digitisation of Farmlands Gains Pace

The Union government is linking digital IDs (e.g., Aadhaar) to land records for efficient implementation of agricultural policies. This initiative aims to create a comprehensive database of farmers, which will allow better targeting of policies and direct beneficiary transfers.

Scope of the Drive

10 million farmers across 10 states have been covered in the digitisation process, with the goal of enhancing access to government schemes and benefits.

Kisan Pehchaan Patra

A unique digital ID, known as the ‘Kisan Pehchaan Patra,’ includes key details such as land holdings, crops grown, and other relevant agricultural data, facilitating easier access to benefits.

Current Challenges

Approximately 80% of farmers in India are landless or marginal, and it remains uncertain how these farmers will be included in the digitisation drive, as they do not have land records for linkage.

Census Implications

The digitisation effort aligns with the upcoming Census, which is expected to provide more data for the agricultural database.
