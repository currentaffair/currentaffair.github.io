Gaza Truce Approved by Israel Security Cabinet

Israel's Security Cabinet has approved a ceasefire deal with Hamas, pending final approval. The ceasefire may begin as early as Sunday.

Hostage Exchange Agreement

Under the deal, 33 hostages will be released in exchange for hundreds of Palestinian prisoners. A second phase will focus on releasing the remaining hostages, including soldiers.

Mediatorsâ€™ Role

Qatar and the U.S. mediated the agreement, with delays blamed on Hamas, according to Prime Minister Netanyahu.

Political Repercussions

The deal faces opposition from Netanyahu's far-right allies, adding political instability to Israel's government.

Contextual Background

The October 7 attack by Hamas started the 15-month conflict. The death toll on both sides is staggering, with over 46,000 Palestinians killed and significant Israeli casualties.

Humanitarian Impact

Millions of Palestinians have faced displacement and destruction, with severe humanitarian needs. The ceasefire is expected to allow aid to flow into Gaza.

Immediate Implications

The truce will allow for the return of hostages and humanitarian aid, with Israel preparing for the recovery of captives.

Challenges Ahead

The second phase of negotiations will be more difficult, especially with Hamas demanding an Israeli withdrawal in exchange for more hostages.

Long-term Ceasefire Prospects

Hamas seeks a lasting ceasefire and full Israeli withdrawal. However, Israel's stance on dismantling Hamas complicates the path to peace.
