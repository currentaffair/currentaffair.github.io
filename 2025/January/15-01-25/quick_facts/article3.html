Cyclone Dikeledi Hits Mayotte

Details of the Cyclone

Cyclone Dikeledi hit Mayotte, a French Indian Ocean territory located in the Mozambique Channel on January 2025. This storm follows Cyclone Chido, which struck the region in December 2024.

About Mayotte

Mayotte consists of two islands in the Comoros archipelago: the main island, Mayotte (Grande Terre), and the smaller island, Pamandzi (Petite Terre). It is the poorest territory in both France and the European Union.

Historical Context

Mayotte was colonized by France in 1843, and it annexed the entire Comoros archipelago by 1904. Despite a 1974 referendum where 95% of the Comoros voted for separation, Mayotte chose to remain French. The other islands, including Grande Comore, Anjouan, and Moheli, declared independence in 1975.

Impact of Cyclone Chido

Cyclone Chido, the worst storm in 90 years, hit Mayotte in December 2024, causing significant damage to the archipelago.
