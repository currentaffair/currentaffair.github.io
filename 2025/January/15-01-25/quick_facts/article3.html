ISRO's SPaDeX Mission: Satellite Docking Attempt

Launch Details

The PSLV C60 rocket launched two satellites, SDX01 (Chaser) and SDX02 (Target), as part of the SPaDeX mission on December 30, 2024, from Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota.

Trial Update

In the trial attempt, ISRO successfully brought the satellites within 3 meters of each other. Initially, they were 15 meters apart. However, due to unexpected drift between the satellites, the docking attempt was delayed from January 7 to January 8, 2025. ISRO overcame this issue and is proceeding with further analysis before attempting the docking.

Significance of the Mission

The SPaDeX mission is crucial for advancing technologies necessary for future space missions, such as:

  • Sending an Indian astronaut to the moon.
  • Moon sample return missions.
  • Establishing an Indian space station.

Success will position India alongside leading countries like the U.S., Russia, and China in mastering spacecraft docking technology.

Next Steps

After analyzing trial data, ISRO will attempt the docking again. If successful, India will join the ranks of the U.S., Russia, and China in this critical space technology.
