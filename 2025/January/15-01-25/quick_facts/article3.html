Plasticiser Degradation Using Bacterial Enzymes

Why in News?

IIT Roorkee has successfully used the esterase enzyme from the soil bacterium Sulfobacillus acidophilus to break down Diethyl Hexyl Phthalate (DEHP), addressing growing environmental and health concerns posed by plasticisers.

What is Plasticiser Degradation?

Plasticisers like DEHP are harmful pollutants added to plastics to improve flexibility. The esterase enzyme works by breaking down DEHP into modified phthalates and alcohol, which are further broken down into harmless substances like water and carbon dioxide.

Plastic and its Impact

Plastics, widely used for their lightweight, durable nature, do not decompose naturally. The global production of plastic has increased drastically, with India leading in plastic waste generation. Biodegradable and compostable plastics are emerging as alternatives to reduce environmental harm.
