India’s Humanitarian Assistance to Cuba

About India’s Assistance to Cuba

India has sent humanitarian aid to Cuba after the devastation caused by Hurricane Rafael. This aid aligns with India's philosophy of Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam, emphasizing global solidarity.

Cuba

Cuba, located in the Northern Caribbean, is the 9th largest producer of Nickel. Its capital is Havana, a significant economic hub.

Historical Relations

India recognized Cuba’s government after the Cuban revolution of 1959. The two countries have supported each other in international forums such as the UN, NAM, and WTO.

Economic Relations

In FY 2022-23, India's exports to Cuba increased to USD 79.04 million, with imports standing at USD 4.87 million. Exports include pharmaceuticals, medical equipment, and textiles.

India’s Aid to Cuba

India has extended disaster relief after hurricanes in 2008, 2016, and 2017. India has also set up an IT center in Cuba and offered life-saving medicines during the Covid-19 pandemic.

Cultural Relations

India’s culture, including Yoga and Ayurveda, is appreciated in Cuba. Prominent figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Rabindranath Tagore are celebrated. A bust of Tagore was unveiled in Havana in 2007.
