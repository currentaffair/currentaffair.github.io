World Hindi Day 2025

About World Hindi Day

World Hindi Day, celebrated annually on 10th January, marks the first time Hindi was spoken at the United Nations General Assembly in 1949. It also commemorates the inaugural World Hindi Conference in 1975. The day, designated on 10th January 2006, is distinct from Hindi Diwas, which commemorates Hindi's adoption as an official language of India in 1949.

Significance

With over 600 million speakers, Hindi is the third most spoken language globally and the most spoken language in India. The day raises awareness about Hindi's importance as a global language of communication.

World Hindi Day 2025 Theme

The theme for World Hindi Day 2025 is "A Global Voice of Unity and Cultural Pride," focusing on promoting linguistic exchange and fostering cultural pride through Hindi.

Constitutional Status of Hindi

Hindi is recognized as the official language of India under Article 343 of the Constitution and is listed in the 8th Schedule, which includes 22 languages recognized for official use alongside English.
