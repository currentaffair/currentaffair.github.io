Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023

The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) released the draft rules for the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act), 2023. The rules, after a 16-month wait, outline the implementation framework for India’s first comprehensive data privacy law.

Lack of Detail in Draft Rules

Critics, especially civil society, have expressed concerns about the lack of a specialized regulator, insufficient protections against government data access, and excessive delegation of regulatory functions to the government.

Key Shortcomings

Right to Access and Erasure: The DPDP Act grants users the right to access, correct, and erase their data, but the draft rules fail to clarify how users can exercise these rights.

Protecting Children’s Data: The rules lack clarity on verifying parental consent for children under 18, leaving questions on age verification and shared device use unanswered.

Relevance to Indian Firms

Indian companies are increasingly focusing on data localisation and telecom equipment production to comply with the regulations. Firms such as CloudPhotonix and DigiBoxx are capitalizing on this trend to establish domestic data storage solutions.

Data Localisation and Regulation

The DPDP Act allows the possibility of future data localisation in certain sectors. The Reserve Bank of India already mandates that payment data be stored locally, driving demand for domestic data storage solutions.

Challenges and Opportunities

Local production of networking equipment like transceivers is gaining momentum as firms seek to reduce reliance on foreign technology, especially Chinese components. The global optical transceiver market is projected to grow, offering opportunities for indigenous companies to contribute to the telecom network's growth.
