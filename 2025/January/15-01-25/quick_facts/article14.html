Plasticiser Degradation Using Bacterial Enzymes

IIT Roorkee has developed a method to break down the harmful plasticizer Diethyl Hexyl Phthalate (DEHP) using the esterase enzyme from the bacterium Sulfobacillus acidophilus, addressing environmental and health concerns.

How Do Bacterial Enzymes Work?

The esterase enzyme breaks DEHP into modified phthalate and alcohol, which are further degraded into harmless substances like water and carbon dioxide. The method is efficient and sustainable, requiring fewer enzyme replacements.

About Plastic

Plastic is lightweight, durable, and widely used, but most plastics do not decompose naturally and break into microplastics. India leads in plastic waste generation, producing 10.2 million tonnes annually.

Types of Plastics

Plastics can be biodegradable, bioplastics, or compostable. Some plastics degrade more quickly under specific conditions, while others persist for long periods, causing environmental harm.
