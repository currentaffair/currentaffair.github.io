Death Anniversary of Lal Bahadur Shastri

About Lal Bahadur Shastri

Lal Bahadur Shastri, the second Prime Minister of India, passed away on 11th January 1966. Born on 2nd October 1904, he was deeply influenced by Mahatma Gandhi and played a key role in India's freedom struggle. Shastri held various ministerial positions, including Home Minister and Minister of Railways, where he displayed moral accountability by resigning twice after train accidents.

Leadership During 1965 Indo-Pak War

As Prime Minister from 1964-1966, Shastri displayed strong leadership during the 1965 Indo-Pak war, taking decisive military action to defend Kashmir. His famous slogan "Jai Jawan Jai Kisan" emphasized the importance of soldiers and farmers in India's progress.

Tashkent Declaration

In January 1966, Shastri signed the Tashkent Declaration with Pakistan, aiming to restore peace and resolve issues from the 1965 Indo-Pak war. His sudden death shortly after signing the declaration remains a mystery.
