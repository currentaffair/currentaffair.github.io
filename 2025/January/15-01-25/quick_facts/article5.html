India’s Humanitarian Assistance to Cuba

Overview

India has extended humanitarian aid to Cuba in response to the devastating impact of Hurricane Rafael, aligning with its philosophy of Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam and global solidarity.

Cuba: Key Facts

Cuba, located in the Northern Caribbean, is the 9th largest producer of nickel. Its capital, Havana, is a significant economic hub.

Historical Background

India was among the first to recognize the Cuban government post-revolution in 1959. Both nations have supported each other in international forums like the UN and WTO.

Economic Relations

India’s exports to Cuba include pharmaceuticals and medical equipment, while Cuba exports tobacco and pharmaceuticals to India. In FY 2022-23, India’s exports to Cuba increased to USD 79.04 million.

India’s Assistance

India has provided disaster relief after hurricanes and established an IT Centre in Cuba. India has extended five Lines of Credit worth USD 243 million in agriculture, agro-food, and renewable energy sectors.

Cultural Relations

India’s culture is widely appreciated in Cuba. Key figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Rabindranath Tagore are celebrated, and yoga and Ayurveda are popular.
