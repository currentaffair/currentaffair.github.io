Prevalence of Hysterectomies in India

Introduction

The National Family Health Survey (NFHS)-4 data highlights the disparity in hysterectomy rates in India, with higher occurrences among low-income agricultural workers and affluent women, due to differing reasons.

Reasons for High Hysterectomy

Agricultural Workers: Poor hygiene, menstrual taboos, delayed gynecological care, and physical labor contribute to high rates in regions like Beed district, Maharashtra.

Wealthier Women: Better affordability and access to healthcare, sometimes influenced by financial schemes like Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY), lead to unnecessary surgeries.

What is a Hysterectomy?

A hysterectomy is a surgical procedure to remove the uterus, performed for conditions like fibroids, endometriosis, abnormal bleeding, pelvic inflammatory disease, cancer treatment, or severe pelvic pain.

Prevalence in India

NFHS-5 data: 3% of women aged 15-49 in India have undergone a hysterectomy. The highest prevalence is in Andhra Pradesh (9%) and Telangana (8%). The lowest is in Sikkim (0.8%) and Meghalaya (0.7%).

The Southern region has the highest prevalence (4.2%), followed by the Eastern region (3.8%), with the Northeast having the lowest (1.2%).
