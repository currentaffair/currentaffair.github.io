Death Anniversary of Lal Bahadur Shastri

About Lal Bahadur Shastri

On 11th January, we observe the 59th death anniversary of Shri Lal Bahadur Shastri, the second Prime Minister of India. Shastri was born on 2nd October 1904, in Mughalsarai, near Varanasi. He played a pivotal role in India's freedom struggle, influenced by Mahatma Gandhi. Known for his humility and dedication, Shastri held key ministerial roles, including Home Minister and Minister of Railways.

Key Contributions

As the Minister of Railways, Shastri showed moral accountability by resigning twice after major train accidents. His tenure as Prime Minister (1964-1966) saw decisive leadership, especially during the 1965 Indo-Pak war. Shastri’s famous slogan, "Jai Jawan Jai Kisan", emphasized the importance of both soldiers and farmers.

His Sudden Death and the Tashkent Declaration

Shastri’s sudden death in Tashkent on 11th January 1966, shortly after signing the Tashkent Declaration with Pakistan, remains a subject of mystery. The declaration aimed to restore peace after the 1965 Indo-Pak war, promoting mutual understanding between the two nations.
