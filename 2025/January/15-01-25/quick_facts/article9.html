Cyclone Dikeledi Hits Mayotte

About Mayotte

Mayotte, located in the Mozambique Channel of the Indian Ocean, is a French territory comprising two islands: Grande Terre (Mayotte) and Petite Terre (Pamandzi). Despite its affiliation with France and the European Union, it remains the poorest region in both.

Historically, Mayotte was colonized by France in 1843 and became part of the Comoros archipelago. In a 1974 referendum, while the larger Comoros archipelago chose independence, Mayotte voted to remain French and is still governed from Paris.

Impact of Cyclones

Cyclone Dikeledi is the latest to hit Mayotte, following Cyclone Chido in December 2024, which was the worst storm in 90 years. These events highlight the vulnerability of the archipelago to extreme weather conditions, posing significant challenges for its inhabitants.
