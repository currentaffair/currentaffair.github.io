India's Global Influence and the MEA Challenges

India's rising prominence is backed by consistent economic growth, political stability, and a strong foreign policy. The country's G20 presidency, its strategic stance in the Russia-Ukraine conflict, leadership in vaccine diplomacy, and advocacy for the Global South are notable successes.

Challenges Faced by the MEA

The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) is severely understaffed, with only around 850 officers for 193 embassies worldwide, in contrast to the U.S. and U.K. who have thousands of officers. The intake of 32-35 officers annually is insufficient for future needs.

Recommendations for Improvement

The MEA could benefit from lateral hiring, consolidation of fragmented divisions, and improved incentives for domestic postings to address staffing challenges. Additionally, enhancing language training and technological capacity is necessary for modern diplomatic needs.

Technological Adaptation

The MEA must develop expertise in cybersecurity, space policy, and AI to keep pace with emerging global challenges. The establishment of divisions like the Centre for Contemporary China Studies signals efforts to adapt to global changes.
