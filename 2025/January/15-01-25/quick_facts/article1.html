Prevalence of Hysterectomies in India

Overview of Hysterectomies

Hysterectomy rates in India are higher among low-income agricultural workers and affluent women, driven by varying factors like access to healthcare and affordability.

Factors for High Hysterectomy

Agricultural workers face issues like poor hygiene, menstrual taboos, and delayed gynecological care. Wealthier women opt for the procedure due to better healthcare access and affordability.

What is a Hysterectomy?

A hysterectomy is a surgical procedure to remove the uterus, used for conditions like fibroids, endometriosis, abnormal bleeding, pelvic inflammatory disease, and cancer treatment.

Prevalence in India

NFHS-5 data shows 3% of women aged 15-49 have undergone a hysterectomy. The Southern region has the highest prevalence at 4.2%, with Andhra Pradesh at 9%.
