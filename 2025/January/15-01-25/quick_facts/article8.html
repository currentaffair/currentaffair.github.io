Plasticiser Degradation Using Bacterial Enzymes

Key Highlights

IIT Roorkee has successfully used the esterase enzyme from Sulfobacillus acidophilus to degrade Diethyl Hexyl Phthalate (DEHP), addressing environmental concerns related to plasticisers.

About Plasticisers

Plasticisers like DEHP make plastics more flexible and are found in products like baby toys and food containers. However, they are harmful pollutants and carcinogens.

How Bacterial Enzymes Work

The esterase enzyme breaks DEHP into two products, which are then further degraded into harmless substances like water and carbon dioxide.

Plastic Overview

Plastics are durable and cost-effective but do not decompose naturally. The world produced 413.8 million metric tons of plastic in 2023.

Types of Plastics

Biodegradable plastics break down through biological processes, while bioplastics and compostable plastics decompose into harmless substances more quickly.
