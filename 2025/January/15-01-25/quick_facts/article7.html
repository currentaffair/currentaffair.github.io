Death Anniversary of Lal Bahadur Shastri

11th January marked the 59th death anniversary of Shri Lal Bahadur Shastri, the second Prime Minister of India.

About Lal Bahadur Shastri

Shastri, born on 2nd October 1904 in Mughalsarai, was a key figure in India's freedom struggle. Known for his integrity, humility, and dedication to public service, he held key positions including Home Minister, Minister of Railways, and Minister of Commerce and Industry. His legacy includes the famous slogan "Jai Jawan Jai Kisan" and decisive leadership during the 1965 Indo-Pak war.

Shastri died suddenly in Tashkent on 11th January 1966, shortly after signing the Tashkent Declaration, which remains mysterious to this day.

Tashkent Declaration

The Tashkent Declaration, signed in January 1966, aimed to restore peace and resolve issues arising from the 1965 Indo-Pak war, promoting mutual understanding between India and Pakistan.
