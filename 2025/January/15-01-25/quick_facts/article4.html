Indian Fishermen Detained by British Navy

Details of the Incident

15 Indian fishermen were detained by the British Navy near Diego Garcia for allegedly crossing the maritime boundary. This is part of an ongoing issue where Tamil Nadu fishermen face frequent detentions by the Sri Lankan and British navies, affecting their livelihood and safety.

About Diego Garcia

Diego Garcia is the largest island of the Chagos Archipelago in the Indian Ocean. It was once part of Mauritius but was separated in 1965 to create the British Indian Ocean Territory (BIOT), which Britain administers but leases to the US for military purposes since 1966.

Diego Garcia became a fully operational US military base in 1986 and played a key role in the US overseas war on terror after the September 11 attacks in 2001.

Recent Developments

In October 2024, Britain agreed to cede sovereignty of the Chagos Archipelago to Mauritius, but will retain sovereign rights over Diego Garcia.
