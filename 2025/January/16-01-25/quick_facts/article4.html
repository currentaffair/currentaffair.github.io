Discoveries in Biology Using C. Elegans

Nobel Winning Research on C. Elegans

The roundworm Caenorhabditis elegans has been central to many groundbreaking biological discoveries:

  • Victor Ambros & Gary Ruvkun (2024): Discovered microRNAs and their role in gene expression control.
  • Osamu Shimomura, Martin Chalfie, & Roger Tsien (2008): Developed GFP for live-cell imaging.
  • Andrew Fire & Craig Mello (2006): Discovered RNA interference, revolutionizing gene-silencing.
  • Sydney Brenner (2002): Contributed to understanding programmed cell death.

About C. Elegans

C. Elegans is a tiny, transparent nematode that measures only 1mm in length. It is a roundworm, either parasitic or free-living, found in soil or water.
