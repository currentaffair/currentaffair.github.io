Chimpanzee Conservation

NGO Papaye International in Cameroon operates a sanctuary for orphaned chimpanzees in the Douala-Edea National Park.

About Chimpanzees

Chimpanzees (Pan troglodytes ssp.) are one of our nearest living relatives, sharing a common ancestor with humans that lived approximately 7-8 million years ago.

Subspecies

There are four recognized subspecies of chimpanzees: Central, Western, Nigeria-Cameroon, and Eastern chimpanzees.

IUCN Status

Chimpanzees are listed as endangered, with Western chimpanzees classified as Critically Endangered.

Cultural Diversity

Chimpanzees exhibit culturally learned behaviors such as tool use, vocal dialects, and feeding techniques, which vary by subspecies.

Feeding Habits

Chimpanzees have diverse diets, including mangrove leaves and other foods depending on their environment.

Threats and Conservation

Habitat destruction and hunting threaten genetic and cultural diversity, making conservation efforts crucial.

About Cameroon

Cameroon is located in Central Africa, bordered by Nigeria, Chad, the Central African Republic, Congo, Gabon, and Equatorial Guinea. It has diverse geography, including coastal plains, mountains, and rainforests.
