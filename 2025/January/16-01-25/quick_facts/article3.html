India Joins UN Panel on Big Data

Why in News?
India has become a member of the United Nations (UN) Committee of Experts on Big Data and Data Science for Official Statistics (UN-CEBD), highlighting its growing influence in global statistical frameworks.

What is UN-CEBD?

The United Nations Committee of Experts on Big Data and Data Science for Official Statistics (UN-CEBD) was established to use big data and data science to enhance global statistical systems. It was set up in 2014 under the UN Statistical Commission (UNSC).

Key Objectives

  • Monitor and report on Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
  • Address challenges of using non-traditional data sources for official statistics.

India's Role and Initiatives

India's induction into UN-CEBD shows its commitment to utilizing big data for statistical improvements. India's major big data initiatives include the National Data & Analytics Platform (NDAP) and Big Data Management Policy.

What is Big Data?

Big data refers to large, complex datasets that traditional systems cannot process effectively. It aids in better decision-making and service development.

Key Drivers of Big Data

The 6Vs driving big data include volume, velocity, variety, veracity, value, and variability.
