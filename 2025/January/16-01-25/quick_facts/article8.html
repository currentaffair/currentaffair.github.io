Golden Langur

Why in News?

A Golden Langur was killed in an accident on National Highway 117 in Assam, raising concerns over the increasing threats to this endangered species.

Key Facts About Golden Langur

Taxonomy and Discovery: Species Name: Trachypithecus geei. Family: Cercopithecidae. Discovered by E.P. Gee in 1953.

Geographic Range: Found in Assam, India, and Bhutan, inhabiting subtropical forests near river borders.

Physical Characteristics

Color: Golden-orange fur. Coat changes seasonally. Facial Features: Black face, pale beard, and a protective hair whorl.

Behavior and Habitat

Diurnal and arboreal, living in troops of 3 to 15 individuals. Threatened by habitat fragmentation and human activities.

Conservation Status

Listed as Endangered by IUCN, protected under CITES Appendix I, and Wildlife Protection Act 1972.

Conservation Measures

Efforts to create habitat corridors and canopy bridges to enhance genetic diversity and movement between populations.
