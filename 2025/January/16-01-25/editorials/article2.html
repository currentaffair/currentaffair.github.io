India's Genomic Data Set

Introduction

India recently achieved a historic milestone at the Genome India Data Conclave in New Delhi by launching the Indian Genomic Data (IGD) Set as part of the GenomeIndia Project (GIP). This initiative positions India as a global leader in genomics, providing global researchers access to genome samples and promoting self-reliance in genomic data. With frameworks like the Framework for Exchange of Data Protocols (FeED) and the Indian Biological Data Centre (IBDC), India is poised to make significant contributions to the field of genomics.

Key Highlights of the Genome India Data Conclave

The Indian Genomic Data Set, launched during the Genome India Data Conclave, consists of 10,000 whole genome sequencing (WGS) samples. These samples are archived at the IBDC, India's first national repository for life science data. The data is now accessible to researchers worldwide, helping to advance genomics research and personalized medicine. The IBDC Portal facilitates seamless access to this valuable genetic data.

FeED Protocols

The FeED Protocols ensure ethical, transparent, and secure sharing of high-quality genomic data. These protocols are implemented under the Biotech-PRIDE (Promotion of Research and Innovation through Data Exchange) Guidelines, which were introduced to provide a framework for sharing biological data across research groups in India.

GenomeIndia Project

The GenomeIndia Project (GIP), led by the Department of Biotechnology (DBT), aims to create a comprehensive database of India’s genetic diversity. The GIP is pivotal in mapping genetic information for over 4,600 distinct population groups in India.

Biotech-PRIDE Guidelines

Released by the DBT in 2021, the Biotech-PRIDE Guidelines allow for the exchange of biological data across research groups, promoting transparency, integration, and equitable access. Under these guidelines, existing datasets are connected to the IBDC, forming the Bio-Grid, which acts as a National Repository for biological data. This Bio-Grid ensures that data exchange occurs under established safety standards and clear access protocols.

What is the GenomeIndia Project?

The GIP, launched in 2020, aims to map the genetic diversity of India. It plans to sequence and analyze the genomes of individuals from 99 ethnic groups. The goal is to create a baseline genetic map that will aid in the study of health, disease predisposition, and other population-specific traits.

Phase I of GIP

The first phase involves sequencing the genomes of 10,000 individuals. The second phase, which focuses on diseases like cancer and diabetes, aims to identify genes linked to these conditions by comparing diseased genomes with healthy ones.

Significance for India

India’s unmatched genetic diversity, with over 4,600 distinct population groups, presents a unique opportunity to uncover genetic factors linked to rare diseases and mutations, such as MYBPC3, which is associated with early cardiac arrest.

Genome Sequencing

Genome sequencing involves deciphering the order of nucleotide bases in DNA, helping us understand genetic makeup and revealing insights into traits and health risks.

Benefits of Indigenous Genomic Data

One of the major benefits of the Indian Genomic Data is the opportunity to develop personalized medicine. As India’s population is genetically diverse, tailoring healthcare solutions based on genetic makeup will enhance treatment effectiveness and outcomes.

The Bioeconomy of India has grown significantly, and genomic data is expected to fuel further growth, positioning India as a global leader in biotechnology. Moreover, it will reduce dependence on foreign genomic databases.

Key Benefits

  • Personalized medicine for a diverse population
  • Enhanced genetic tools and diagnostic tests
  • Growth of the bioeconomy in India
  • Positioning India as a global leader in biotechnology

Other Initiatives for Biotechnology Development in India

  • BioE3 Policy
  • National Biotechnology Development Strategy 2020-25
  • IndiGen Project

Global Genomic Projects

The Human Genome Project and the Earth BioGenome Project are international initiatives to sequence genomes across species, highlighting the global importance of genomic research.

In conclusion, the Indian Genomic Data Set is a monumental step forward for genomics research. It empowers India to lead globally in genetic innovation, fostering the growth of a bioeconomy, improving healthcare, and contributing to worldwide scientific understanding.
