162nd Birth Anniversary of Swami Vivekananda

Introduction

On National Youth Day (12th January), India commemorates the 162nd birth anniversary of Swami Vivekananda, a great spiritual leader, philosopher, and thinker. This day is marked with various activities, including the participation of the Prime Minister in the Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue 2025, highlighting the importance of youth empowerment in shaping the future of India.

The National Youth Policy 2014 defines youth as individuals between the ages of 15-29, a demographic that constitutes nearly 40% of India's population, underscoring the importance of engaging this vibrant group in national development.

What is the Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue?

About the Dialogue

The Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue is a platform aimed at engaging youth in nation-building, which aligns with the Prime Minister's call to involve 1 lakh youth in politics without political affiliations. The event brings together 3,000 dynamic youth aged 15-29, selected through a merit-based process known as the Viksit Bharat Challenge.

Thematic Focus

Youth leaders at the dialogue present ideas on ten thematic areas critical to India's development, including technology, sustainability, women empowerment, manufacturing, and agriculture.

Key Facts Regarding Swami Vivekananda

Early Life and Enlightenment

Swami Vivekananda was born Narendra Nath Datta on 12th January 1863. His spiritual journey began with his association with Ramakrishna Paramhansa. Vivekananda’s enlightenment came in 1892 when he meditated on a rock in the Indian Ocean off the shores of Kanyakumari, which later became the Vivekananda Rock Memorial.

Contributions

Vivekananda introduced the world to the Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga. His interpretation of Hinduism, known as 'neo-Vedanta,' combined spirituality with material progress. His message emphasized self-realization, compassion, and selfless service, urging individuals to serve humanity as an act of worship to Shiva.

Core Values

Youth: Vivekananda believed in the potential of youth to drive change, emphasizing physical and mental strength.

Ethics: He advocated for a life of purity and good conduct, aligned with one's true divine nature.

Education: Vivekananda stressed the importance of character-building education and self-reliance.

Rationality: He supported modern science and reason while promoting faith.

National Youth Policy 2014

Vision and Goals

The National Youth Policy (NYP) 2014 aims to empower youth by focusing on areas such as education, employment, leadership, health, and social justice. The NYP 2024 draft aligns with the Sustainable Development Goals and seeks to enhance opportunities for youth development.

Key Areas Goals
Education Improve career and life skills.
Leadership Strengthen leadership and volunteering opportunities.
Health Enhance healthcare, especially mental health.
Social Justice Ensure safety and support for marginalized youth.

Conclusion

Swami Vivekananda’s teachings on empowerment, education, and spirituality continue to resonate with today's youth. The Viksit Bharat Young Leaders Dialogue, in conjunction with the National Youth Policy, aims to equip young leaders with the tools and values necessary for contributing to India's sustainable and inclusive future.
