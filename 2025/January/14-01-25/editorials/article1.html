The Impact of Trump's Worldview on Global Geopolitics

Introduction

Dissecting the psychology of the next U.S. President is a popular pastime. Trump's bold, disinhibited speaking style, his outspokenness, his focus on deal-making, and his real estate background that defines his coercive leadership and politics, cast him very differently from his predecessors. He is a polarizing figure, wading constantly into controversy, stubbornly resilient, a master of populist rhetoric, presenting himself as the voice of the "common people" arraigned against an out-of-touch political establishment.

It’s ‘America First’

Trump's approach to the "Rules-Based International Order" is shaped by his "America First" doctrine. He debunks multilateralism in global politics and trashes international agreements that he believes do not favor the United States. For instance, he withdrew the U.S. from the Paris Climate Agreement and is likely to withdraw again. He prefers bilateral agreements over multilateral ones, such as trade agreements like the Trans-Pacific Partnership. Furthermore, he is dismissive of international institutions like the United Nations and NATO, arguing they disproportionately benefit other nations at the expense of the U.S.

Trump’s transactionalism and MAGA policy will have significant global implications, especially regarding trade protectionism and increased economic uncertainty.

Trumpian Worldview

The Trumpian worldview emphasizes strategy revolving around the bully pulpit, strong personal branding, unconventional deal-making, and winner-take-all scenarios. Unlike his predecessors, Trump emphasizes practical outcomes rather than theoretical constructs. His approach is transactional—focused on immediate, tangible results rather than abstract international ideals.

Transactionalism and MAGA Policy

Trump’s MAGA ("Make America Great Again") policy entails a rise in protectionism, escalating tariffs, trade wars, and intensified competition with China. His administration will likely influence global geopolitics, particularly in the Indo-Pacific region. Trump's stance against free trade and skepticism toward global institutions will inspire a wave of nationalism and skepticism toward international law.

Trump's transactional approach and skepticism about global institutions will influence global politics, contributing to increased instability, trade frictions, and power realignments.

American Isolationism and Its Global Consequences

Under Trump’s America First doctrine, U.S. isolationism could encourage nations like China and Russia to expand their influence globally. This may lead to power realignments and disruptions beyond American shores, especially in military and economic spheres. Powers opposed to U.S. dominance might use both economic and military strategies to challenge the current global order.

Ties with India: The Line for New Delhi

The strategic outlook for India-U.S. relations under a second Trump presidency is mixed. India has stressed constructive engagement with the U.S. as a key strategic partner. Relations between the two countries have witnessed steady growth over the last two decades, and this trend is likely to continue under Trump, especially with Prime Minister Modi's warm relationship with him.

Geopolitical Clout and Mutual Interests in Countering China

India’s growing geopolitical clout, alongside a mutual interest in countering China, will strengthen the strategic partnership between India and the U.S. The Indo-Pacific region, with its contested security issues, remains a focal point. India-U.S. defense cooperation will likely continue to grow, especially in critical technologies and business.

Strengthening regional security structures like the Quad will be a critical part of both India’s and the U.S.'s strategic outlook, particularly in the Indo-Pacific region.

Strategic Priorities for India

India must prioritize its defense, border security, and military modernization to maintain its strategic autonomy. The rise of China presents both a security challenge and an economic opportunity, and India’s focus on energy security, digital transformation, and defense partnerships will be crucial in navigating this complex geopolitical landscape.

India's Digital Transformation and Soft Power

India’s future strategy will also focus on digital transformation, space exploration, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity to safeguard its infrastructure from hostile threats. Moreover, India's global soft power—focused on democratic values, pluralism, and humanitarian engagement—will serve as a strategic asset moving forward.

Conclusion

As Donald Trump re-enters the global stage with his second presidency, established patterns of history and geopolitics are unlikely to serve as reliable guides. The positioning system for global order may become increasingly disruptive, polarized, and unstable. As analyst Ian Bremmer suggests, a G-Zero world has emerged, where global politics will follow a new, chaotic playbook.
