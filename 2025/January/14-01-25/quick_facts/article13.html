Sovereign AI

The government is focusing on investments in semiconductors and Artificial Intelligence (AI) to ensure AI sovereignty, aiming for a USD 1 trillion digital economy by 2028.

What is Sovereign AI?

Sovereign AI refers to a nation's ability to develop and control AI using its own resources, including infrastructure, data, workforce, and business networks. This encompasses the development of AI models and fostering local talent.

Growth of AI

In 2018, AI models had 340 million parameters, while today, models like ChatGPT have 1.8 trillion parameters. AI parameters are internal variables adjusted during training to optimize performance.

Key Aspects

  • National Control: Ensures AI systems comply with national laws, regulations, and ethics.
  • Data Sovereignty: Emphasizes control over data to protect privacy and security.
  • AI in Governance: Generative AI reshapes industries, governance, and work dynamics.
  • Ethical Considerations: Setting standards for secure and ethical AI use.
  • Strategic Autonomy: Reduces dependency on foreign AI technologies.
  • Economic Competitiveness: AI drives industrial innovation; without it, India risks falling behind globally.
  • Applications: Sovereign AI benefits critical sectors like defense, healthcare, and transportation.

India's Position

India is advancing its AI infrastructure with major players like Tata Group and Reliance. The government has allocated USD 1.2 billion for AI initiatives, including the IndiaAI mission and AI supercomputing facilities.

Global AI Cooperation

A proposed Global AI Compact advocates for sharing AI resources across nations to ensure equitable access to crucial technologies.
