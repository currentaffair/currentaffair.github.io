ISRO's SPaDeX Mission

Overview

ISRO is attempting to dock two satellites, SDX01 (Chaser) and SDX02 (Target), in space as part of the SPaDeX mission. This mission aims to demonstrate spacecraft rendezvous, docking, and undocking technologies.

Launch Details

The satellites were launched into space on December 30, 2024, from Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota, using the PSLV C60 rocket.

Trial Update

ISRO successfully brought the satellites within 3 meters of each other, after initially separating them by 15 meters. The satellites were later moved back to a safe distance for further analysis.

Setback and Recovery

The docking experiment faced a delay due to an unexpected drift between the satellites, which postponed the docking attempt from January 7 to January 8. However, ISRO overcame the issue and is now proceeding with further analysis.

Significance of the Mission

The SPaDeX mission is crucial for developing technologies needed for future space missions, such as sending an Indian astronaut to the moon, moon sample return missions, and establishing an Indian space station. Successful execution will position India among the leading countries capable of spacecraft docking.

Next Steps

After analyzing trial data, ISRO will attempt the docking again. If successful, India will join the ranks of the U.S., Russia, and China in mastering spacecraft docking technology.
