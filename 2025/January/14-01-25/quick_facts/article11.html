Cryo-Born Baby Corals

Introduction

The world's first cryo-born baby corals have been successfully introduced into the Great Barrier Reef, marking a groundbreaking achievement in coral restoration and conservation. Australian scientists used cutting-edge cryopreservation to fertilize coral eggs with cryopreserved sperm collected from the Great Barrier Reef.

Objectives

This effort aims to introduce heat-tolerant corals to protect reefs from climate change and rising ocean temperatures. The CryoDiversity Bank in Australia holds the worldâ€™s largest collection of frozen coral sperm from 32 species, collected annually since 2011.

Coral Facts

Coral reefs are formed by colonies of polyps that secrete limestone skeletons and rely on symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae) for nutrition. Only hard corals form reefs, while soft corals do not produce the necessary calcium carbonate skeletons.
