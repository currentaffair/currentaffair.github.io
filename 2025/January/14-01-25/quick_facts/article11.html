ISRO's SPaDeX Mission: Satellite Docking

ISRO is attempting to dock two satellites (SDX01 – Chaser, SDX02 – Target) in space as part of the SPaDeX mission, demonstrating spacecraft rendezvous, docking, and undocking technologies.

Relevance & Launch Details

GS 3 (Space)
Launched by PSLV C60 on December 30, 2024, from Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota.

Trial Update

  • The satellites were brought within 3 meters of each other during a trial attempt.
  • The initial separation was 15 meters, successfully reduced to 3 meters before a safe distance was maintained.

Setback and Recovery

  • An unexpected drift between the satellites delayed the docking attempt from January 7 to January 8.
  • ISRO overcame the drift issue and is analyzing data for the next docking attempt.

Significance of the Mission

  • Key to developing future space technologies for missions like sending an Indian astronaut to the moon.
  • Positioning India alongside U.S., Russia, and China in spacecraft docking expertise.

Next Steps

Docking will be attempted after further data analysis, with the potential to join the ranks of countries that have mastered spacecraft docking.
