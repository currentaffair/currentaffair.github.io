Linking Voter IDs with Aadhaar: A Critical Discussion

Background

The Aam Aadmi Party (AAP) and Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) have accused each other of manipulating electoral rolls ahead of Delhi Assembly elections. This has reignited the discussion around linking Voter IDs (EPIC) with Aadhaar numbers for better electoral roll accuracy.

History of the Proposal

In 2015, the Election Commission launched NERPAP to address duplicate entries by linking voter data with Aadhaar. However, a 2015 Supreme Court ruling halted the program, only for it to be revived in 2021 after the Puttaswamy case upholding the Aadhaar Act.

Pros of Linking EPIC with Aadhaar

Linking Aadhaar with EPIC can help eliminate duplicate entries from electoral rolls, improving accuracy. Over 650 million Aadhaar numbers are already uploaded to finalize electoral rolls.

Concerns and Issues

Concerns about errors in the Aadhaar database, privacy risks, and its inability to prove citizenship persist. Additionally, the lack of mandatory linkage for voter registration forms raises questions about its effectiveness.

Way Forward

The focus should be on increasing public confidence, ensuring voter privacy, and updating registration forms to reflect the voluntary nature of Aadhaar linking. Addressing these concerns will ensure the electoral process remains transparent and accurate.

Conclusion

Linking Voter IDs with Aadhaar could significantly improve electoral roll accuracy. However, careful management of privacy concerns and data accuracy, along with public awareness, is necessary for success.
