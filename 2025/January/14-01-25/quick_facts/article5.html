Kerala’s Rising Maternal Mortality Rate

Kerala’s maternal mortality rate (MMR) is currently reported at 19 per one lakh live births but is estimated by the State Health Department to be 29. This increase in MMR is primarily linked to the decline in fertility rates rather than more maternal deaths.

Declining Births

Kerala’s fertility rate has dropped to a historic low of 1.46, far below the replacement level of 2.1. This has led to a reduction in the number of births, from an average of 5-5.5 lakh annually to 3.93 lakh in the latest data.

Impact of Declining Fertility

While maternal health outcomes remain stable, the reduced number of births is causing a rise in MMR. Kerala is experiencing a demographic transition, with fewer children born and an increasing elderly population.

Social and Economic Consequences

Migration of young adults for jobs or education is also contributing to low birth rates. Kerala faces a future with an elderly population surpassing children, creating challenges in caregiving and welfare.

Delayed Childbearing and Increased Risks

Delays in marriage and childbearing could result in higher pregnancy-related risks, especially among older mothers, although evidence is still limited.

Health Department’s Response

The State Health Department is making efforts to address maternal health issues but faces significant challenges due to demographic shifts. The goal of reducing MMR to 20 by 2030 seems increasingly difficult.
