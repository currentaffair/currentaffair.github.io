India's Foreign Policy and MEA Challenges

India's global prominence has grown through steady economic progress, political stability, and independent foreign policies. It has taken a leadership role in the G20 presidency, vaccine diplomacy, and advocating for the Global South, while maintaining strategic autonomy in global conflicts.

Challenges Faced by MEA

The Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) is under-resourced, with significantly fewer officers than countries like the U.S. and the U.K. This gap limits Indiaâ€™s diplomatic reach.

Recommendations for Improvement

To address the staffing shortage, India could benefit from lateral hiring from other government services and restructuring the MEA. Additionally, improving incentives for domestic postings would help retain talent.

Diplomatic Expertise

Language training and specialist roles need attention to ensure India remains competitive in global diplomacy. Areas like cybersecurity and AI require technical expertise that current generalists lack.

Positive Steps and Looking Ahead

Recent initiatives and leadership from Dr. S. Jaishankar show progress in adapting to the global diplomatic environment. As India approaches its 100th year of independence in 2047, the MEA must evolve to keep up with India's expanding global role.
