Cryo-Born Baby Corals: A Breakthrough in Coral Restoration

Australian scientists have introduced the world's first cryo-born baby corals into the Great Barrier Reef, a pioneering achievement in coral restoration.

Innovative Cryopreservation

Scientists fertilized coral eggs using cryopreserved sperm collected from the reef, growing the corals in the National Sea Simulator before moving them to the reef in special ‘coral cradles’.

Heat-Tolerant Corals for Climate Protection

This project aims to develop heat-tolerant corals to safeguard reefs against climate change and rising ocean temperatures.

Coral Preservation

  • The CryoDiversity Bank in Australia holds the world’s largest collection of frozen coral sperm, collected annually since 2011.
  • 32 coral species are stored, aiding in coral preservation and restoration efforts.

Understanding Coral Reefs

  • Corals are invertebrates from the class Anthozoa, phylum Cnidaria.
  • Coral reefs form through colonies of polyps that secrete limestone skeletons.
  • Only hard corals produce the massive skeletons needed to create reefs.
