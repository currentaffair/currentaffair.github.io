ISRO's SPaDeX Mission - Satellite Docking

Relevance: GS 3(Space)

Mission Overview

ISRO is working on its SPaDeX mission, which aims to demonstrate spacecraft rendezvous, docking, and undocking technologies. The mission involves two satellites: SDX01 (Chaser) and SDX02 (Target).

Launch Details

The PSLV C60 rocket successfully launched the two satellites from Satish Dhawan Space Centre on December 30, 2024. The satellites were initially separated by 15 meters before being brought within 3 meters for a trial attempt.

Trial and Setbacks

In a trial attempt, the satellites were successfully brought within 3 meters of each other. However, an unexpected drift between the satellites caused a delay, postponing the docking attempt from January 7 to January 8. ISRO has since addressed the drift issue and is now analyzing the data before the next attempt.

Significance

The SPaDeX mission is crucial for future space missions, including sending an Indian astronaut to the moon, establishing an Indian space station, and more. Successful completion will position India alongside the U.S., Russia, and China in mastering spacecraft docking.

Next Steps

ISRO plans to attempt the docking after reviewing the trial data. If successful, India will join the ranks of the leading countries in spacecraft docking capabilities.
