Z-Morh Tunnel

Overview

The Z-Morh tunnel, inaugurated by the Prime Minister, is designed to improve all-weather connectivity between Ladakh and Kashmir, ensuring safe travel despite weather conditions.

Location

The 6.5 km Z-Morh tunnel is located at 8,650 feet in Ganderbal, Kashmir. It bypasses avalanche-prone areas on the Srinagar-Leh highway (NH-1) and is built under the Thajiwas glacier. It has also been renamed the Sonamarg tunnel.

Timeline

Built at a cost of Rs 2,400 crore, the project began in 2015 under the Border Roads Organisation (BRO). Later, the National Highways and Infrastructure Development Corporation Limited (NHIDCL) took over the development.
