Wildfires in California

Why in News?

California is battling severe wildfires, with authorities using pink fire retardants to contain the flames. These fires have become more frequent, occurring outside the usual season, prompting concerns about their causes and solutions.

Causes and Impacts

Natural Causes: Lightning strikes, climate change, and Santa Ana winds are key factors exacerbating wildfires. These factors lead to dry vegetation, making fires more uncontrollable.

Human Causes: Campfires, sparks from vehicles, and power lines account for a significant portion of wildfires.

Impact

Wildfires cause economic loss, air pollution, land degradation, and loss of biodiversity. The economic impact is severe due to property destruction and health risks from air pollution.

Pink Fire Retardant

Pink fire retardant is used to control wildfires. It contains ammonium phosphate and toxic metals like chromium. While effective in slowing fires, it raises environmental concerns due to toxicity and water contamination risks.
