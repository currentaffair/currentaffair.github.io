Digital Personal Data Protection Act - MeitY Draft Rules

The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) has released the draft rules for the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act), India's first comprehensive data privacy law. These rules are open for public feedback until mid-February.

Key Points

The draft outlines data collection processes like notice and consent, breach notifications, and parental consent for children's data. However, critics have pointed out issues:

  • Lack of Detail: Limited guidance on practical implementation of rights such as data access and erasure.
  • User Rights Issues: Unclear procedures for exercising rights like data erasure and correction.
  • Children's Data: No clear mechanism for verifying parental consent, especially in cases involving children under 18.

Evaluation & Next Steps

Despite the long drafting period, the rules remain vague and incomplete. MeitY must consult experts and address the practical implementation challenges before finalizing the rules.
