Kerala’s Rising MMR

Overview

Kerala’s maternal mortality ratio (MMR) has risen to 19 per one lakh live births, with estimates from the Health Department at 29. This increase is linked to declining fertility rates, not necessarily more maternal deaths.

Declining Birth Rates

Kerala's fertility rate has dropped to a historic low of 1.46, well below the replacement level of 2.1. Births have decreased from 5-5.5 lakh annually to 3.93 lakh.

Impact of Fertility Decline

The decrease in births leads to a higher MMR, even though maternal health remains stable. This demographic shift indicates a future increase in the elderly population, creating social challenges.

Demographic Transition

Since 1987-88, Kerala has experienced consistent fertility decline. Projections suggest the total fertility rate (TFR) will continue to fall to 1.35 by 2030.

Social and Economic Effects

Job migration and delayed childbearing contribute to low birth rates. In the coming decade, the elderly population will outnumber children, leading to caregiving and welfare challenges.

Health Risks from Delayed Childbearing

Rising maternal age could increase pregnancy-related morbidities, though evidence is still limited.

Reporting Concerns

Discrepancies in birth registration and delayed reports make it difficult to understand demographic trends clearly.

State Health Department's Efforts

Despite efforts to address maternal health challenges, achieving an MMR of 20 by 2030 seems increasingly unlikely due to demographic shifts.
