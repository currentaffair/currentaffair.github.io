Harvest Festivals in India

The President of India has greeted citizens on the eve of Lohri, Makar Sankranti, Pongal, and Magh Bihu. These are harvest festivals celebrated in diverse forms across various regions of India.

What are the Harvest Festivals in India?

These festivals mark the end of the harvest season across India. They are celebrated under various names such as Makar Sankranti, Pongal, Magh Bihu, Lohri, and more. They follow the solar cycle, unlike lunar-based festivals, and occur on the 14th of January.

Cultural and Astronomical Significance

The transition of the Sun into the Capricorn zodiac (Makara) is celebrated during these festivals. Rituals include bathing, giving charity, offering Naivedhya (food) to Lord Surya, and breaking fast. Sacred rivers like Ganga and Yamuna are significant in the rituals.

Regional Celebrations

In Tamil Nadu (Pongal), the four-day festival celebrates rice harvest with traditional kolams. In Karnataka, locals share sesame and jaggery, symbolizing harmony. Punjab celebrates Lohri with bonfires, folk songs, and offerings to the fire. In Bihar, Khichdi is celebrated with special dishes, and in Rajasthan and Gujarat, kite flying is a key activity.

Assam (Magh Bihu)

Magh Bihu in Assam marks the annual harvest and the beginning of the Assamese new year.
