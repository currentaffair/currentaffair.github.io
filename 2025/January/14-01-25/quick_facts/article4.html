Data Storage and Telecom Equipment Localisation in India

Overview

Indian companies are increasingly capitalising on policy changes to localise both data storage and telecom equipment production. Firms are focusing on manufacturing networking equipment and setting up data storage solutions within India.

Key Players

CloudPhotonix aims to replace Chinese-made components in telecom networks with locally produced ones. DigiBoxx, a cloud storage platform, offers services with servers and data centres based in India to meet data localisation requirements.

Data Localisation and Regulation

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, allows the possibility of future data localisation in certain sectors. The Reserve Bank of India mandates that payment data of Indians be stored locally, driven by privacy concerns and regulatory compliance.

Optical Transceiver Market Growth

The global optical transceiver market is projected to reach $47.64 billion by 2035. The demand for networking equipment is driving the production of transceivers locally, with firms like CloudPhotonix targeting this market.

Challenges and Opportunities

Local production of telecom equipment is becoming crucial amid geopolitical tensions. Companies like CloudPhotonix are responding to the need for indigenous components for telecom networks and data centres.

Cost and Security Considerations

While local data storage options like DigiBoxx may be more expensive, they are gaining popularity due to regulatory compliance and the increasing importance of data privacy and security.

Industry Support

The Union Secretary for Electronics and Information Technology, S. Krishnan, supports the localisation efforts in the telecom and data storage sectors, highlighting the commercial and national benefits of these moves.
