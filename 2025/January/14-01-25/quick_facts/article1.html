Wildfires in California

Why in News?

Los Angeles is battling wildfires causing fatalities and structural losses. Authorities are deploying pink fire retardants to contain the flames. These wildfires are increasing in frequency and occurring outside the usual wildfire season, prompting concerns about causes, climate change, and possible solutions.

What are the Causes and Impacts of Frequent Wildfires in California?

Natural Causes:

  • Lightning Strikes: Ignites dry vegetation.
  • Climate Change: Rising temperatures exacerbate dry conditions.
  • Santa Ana Winds: Strong winds spread flames rapidly.

Human Intervention:

  • Campfires: Unattended or improperly extinguished fires.
  • Power Lines: Faulty or disturbed lines spark fires.
  • Other Activities: Arson, discarded cigarettes, and equipment malfunctions.

Impact of Wildfires

  • Economic loss from destruction of life and property.
  • Air pollution from small particulate matter and chemicals.
  • Land degradation and loss of biodiversity.

What is Pink Fire Retardant?

Pink fire retardants are chemicals sprayed to control wildfires. They contain ammonium phosphate and are highly visible, helping firefighters. However, they include toxic metals like chromium and cadmium, which pose environmental risks.
