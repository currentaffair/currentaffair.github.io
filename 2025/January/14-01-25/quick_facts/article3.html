Digital Personal Data Protection Act & Voter ID Linking

Digital Personal Data Protection Act

The Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) released the draft rules for the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act) after a 16-month wait. These rules, open for public feedback until mid-February, outline the implementation framework for India's first data privacy law.

The rules have faced criticism from civil society for not addressing privacy concerns effectively. Experts call for further consultations and refinements before the rules are finalized.

Voter ID and Aadhaar Link

The discussion around linking Voter IDs (EPIC) with Aadhaar numbers has resurfaced ahead of the Delhi Assembly elections, with both political parties accusing each other of manipulating electoral rolls. While linking Aadhaar with EPIC can help eliminate duplicates, there are concerns regarding privacy and data accuracy.

Pros and Cons

Linking Aadhaar with EPIC can help remove duplicate entries and improve electoral roll accuracy. However, concerns include the accuracy of Aadhaar data and potential privacy risks associated with accessibility by political parties.

Way Forward

The Electoral Commission has clarified that Aadhaar submission is voluntary, but public confidence must be built to ensure transparent elections. There needs to be a careful approach to balancing data accuracy and voter privacy.
