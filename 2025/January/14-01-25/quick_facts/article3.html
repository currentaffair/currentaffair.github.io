Harvest Festivals

Why in News?

The President of India has greeted citizens on the eve of Lohri, Makar Sankranti, Pongal, and Magh Bihu. These are harvest festivals celebrated across various regions of India.

What are the Harvest Festivals in India?

They mark the end of the harvesting season with names like Makar Sankranti, Pongal, Magh Bihu, Lohri, etc. The festivals are linked to the Sunâ€™s movement, transitioning into the Capricorn zodiac and starting its northward journey (Uttarayan).

The festivals are based on the solar cycle, with a fixed date of 14th January each year.

Cultural Significance

Rituals include bathing, offering food to Lord Surya, giving charity, and breaking fast. Devotees often bathe in sacred rivers like Ganga, Yamuna, and Cauvery.

Regional Celebrations

  • Tamil Nadu (Pongal): A four-day festival marking the rice harvest, celebrated with kolams made from rice powder.
  • Karnataka: Locals share sesame and jaggery symbolizing harmony. Cattle are dressed in colorful costumes for 'Kicchu Haisodu'.
  • Punjab (Lohri): Involves bonfires, folk songs, and offerings like groundnuts and popcorn.
  • Bihar (Khichdi): A dish of rice and lentils is prepared, along with sesame and jaggery ladoos.
  • Rajasthan & Gujarat: Celebrated with kite flying and festivals, including the International Kite Festival in Ahmedabad.
  • Assam (Magh Bihu): Marks the harvest and the beginning of the Assamese new year.
