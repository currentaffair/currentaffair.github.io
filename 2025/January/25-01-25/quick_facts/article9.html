Rare Melanistic Tiger News

Poaching Incident in Similipal

The Odisha Forest Department arrested four poachers from the Lodha tribe for illegally hunting a rare melanistic tiger in Similipal Tiger Reserve (STR).

Melanistic Tiger Facts

Melanism is a genetic condition where animals produce more melanin, leading to darker skin or fur. The melanistic tiger in STR is a sub-adult with a rare genetic trait. It is part of a group of only 20 such tigers worldwide.

Melanistic Tigers in STR

The STR is home to 27 tigers, including 13 adult pseudo-melanistic tigers, which have higher melanin levels, creating a unique black and yellow striped pattern.

Other Tiger Color Variations

Tigers can be orange with black or brown stripes (Royal Bengal Tiger), white due to leucism, or golden from a recessive gene called "wideband," reducing melanin production.

About Similipal Tiger Reserve

Located in the Deccan Peninsula Biogeographic Zone, Similipal is known for its rich vegetation and diverse fauna. It has been a Global Network of Biospheres site since 2009.

Lodha Tribe Background

The Lodha Tribe, a Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Group (PVTG), resides in Odisha. Traditionally hunters and gatherers, they now engage in agriculture and small businesses. They are classified under the Criminal Tribes Act by the British.
