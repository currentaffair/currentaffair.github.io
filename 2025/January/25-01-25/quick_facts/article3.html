Statehood Day for Northeast States

Why in News?

The Prime Minister of India greeted Manipur, Tripura, and Meghalaya on their Statehood Day, which is celebrated on January 21st each year.

Historical Context of Statehood

Manipur

Manipur was an independent princely state before 1947. It merged with India after the Maharaja signed the 'Instrument of Accession'. It became a Union Territory in 1956 and gained full statehood in 1972.

Tripura

Tripura, also a princely state, merged with India in 1949. It became a Union Territory in 1956 and achieved full statehood in 1972.

Meghalaya

Meghalaya's demand for autonomy led to its creation as an autonomous state within Assam in 1969. It became a full state in 1972 after the NEA-(R) Act was enacted.

North-Eastern Areas (Re-organization) Act, 1971

The NEA-(R) Act granted full statehood to Manipur, Tripura, and Meghalaya, and established other North-Eastern states as Union Territories. The Act ensured proper representation in the legislature and the establishment of a common High Court (Gauhati High Court).
