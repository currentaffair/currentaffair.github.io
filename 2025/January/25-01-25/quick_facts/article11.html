Indian Grey Wolf

Introduction

The Bankapur Wolf Sanctuary in Karnataka recently celebrated the birth of eight pups of the Indian grey wolf. This subspecies of the grey wolf, known scientifically as Canis lupus pallipes, is found across Southwest Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

Characteristics

The Indian grey wolf is typically less vocal than other subspecies and lives in smaller packs. It is primarily nocturnal, hunting from dusk to dawn.

Conservation Status

The Indian grey wolf is listed as Endangered by the IUCN, with a population ranging from 2,000 to 3,000 individuals in India. It is also protected under CITES Appendix I and Schedule I of the Wildlife Protection Act of 1972.

Bankapur Wolf Sanctuary

The sanctuary spans 332 hectares, featuring scrub forests, hills, and natural caves suitable for the wolf's habitat. It is the second dedicated wolf sanctuary in India, after Mahuadanr Wolf Sanctuary in Jharkhand.

Flora and Fauna

Bankapur is home to various species, including leopards, peacocks, blackbucks, foxes, and hares.
