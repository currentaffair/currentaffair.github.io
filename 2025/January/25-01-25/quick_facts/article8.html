Halal Certification Controversy

Background

The Solicitor General opposed halal certification for non-meat products like cement, iron bars, and wheat flour in response to a petition in the Supreme Court. The practice is alleged to harm businesses that do not adhere to halal standards and create unfair competition.

Allegations

Companies have been accused of issuing forged halal certificates, exploiting religious sentiments for sales, and causing social animosity by violating public trust.

What is Halal?

Halal, an Arabic word meaning "permissible," refers to food and products that meet Islamic law standards. Halal certification ensures that products are prepared according to these laws and remain unadulterated.

Halal Certification in India

India lacks a national halal certification system but is working on streamlining halal certification for meat products through the i-CAS (Indian Conformity Assessment Scheme). This scheme ensures that meat products are certified for export if produced, processed, and packaged in certified facilities.

Role of Solicitor General

The Solicitor General is the second most senior law officer in the government, ranking just below the Attorney General.
