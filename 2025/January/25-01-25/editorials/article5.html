Syllabus:

Topic: Electoral Rolls, Election Integrity

Introduction

In June 1949, Dr. B.R. Ambedkar emphasized the critical role of the Election Commission of India (ECI) in ensuring the independence of elections and preventing executive interference. On the eve of the ECI's 75th anniversary, it is vital to reflect on the integrity of India's electoral rolls and how recent developments undermine this trust. The ECI's credibility is in question after the Maharashtra state elections, raising concerns about possible manipulation of voter enrollment and the fairness of the election process.

The Case of Maharashtra

Dr. Ambedkar's foresight about electoral roll manipulation by the government has been realized in Maharashtra, where both exclusion and possible mass inclusion of voters have raised serious concerns. The process of enrolling voters in the state election has been questioned for its irregularities.

Voter Enrollment in Maharashtra

The ECI's voter enrollment data for the 2024 Maharashtra state elections shows that 9.7 crore voters were registered, even though the Ministry of Health's estimate for the state's adult population was only 9.54 crore.

This discrepancy indicates that 16 lakh more voters were registered than the total adult population estimate, raising suspicions about the accuracy and legitimacy of the enrollment process.

Discrepancy in Voter Enrollment

Despite the government’s estimate being a projection, registering more than 100% of the adult population of Maharashtra is highly irregular. This was not the case during previous elections, such as the Maharashtra Lok Sabha election held just six months earlier. How did the ECI manage to enroll more voters than the total adult population, and what implications does this have for election fairness?

Mass Enrollment in Just Months

In just six months, 48 lakh new voters were enrolled for the Maharashtra state election, while only 32 lakh new voters had been enrolled between 2019 to 2024. This sharp increase in voter registration over such a short period demands closer scrutiny. What could explain this sudden surge in registrations, and why was it specifically limited to the state elections?

Analysis of the BJP Alliance’s Vote Gain

The BJP-led Mahayuti alliance gained 72 lakh more votes in the state election compared to the Lok Sabha election. While a portion of this increase came from voters switching from the Congress-led Maha Vikas Aghadi alliance, the remaining 48 lakh votes are unaccounted for. Were these additional votes linked to the 48 lakh new voters who registered only for the state election?

Where Did the Additional 48 Lakh Votes Come From?

The ECI officially confirmed that 48 lakh new voters were enrolled for the state election, raising suspicions about the legitimacy of these voters. How did these newly enrolled voters contribute to the surge in support for the BJP alliance, and why were they not seen in the previous elections?

Questions About the Validity of New Voters

Several questions remain unanswered about the 48 lakh new voters. Were these voters verified? How could so many new voters have appeared in such a short period and all appear to support one political alliance? These questions challenge the credibility of the voter registration process in Maharashtra.

ECI’s Response and Lack of Transparency

The ECI's response to these concerns has been inadequate. Despite the serious implications for the election’s integrity, the ECI has not provided transparent explanations or released relevant data. This silence undermines public trust in the institution.

ECI’s Counter-Argument

In defense, the Chief Election Commissioner suggested that opposition parties should have been more vigilant about the issue of ghost voters. However, this defense is insufficient. It is the ECI's constitutional responsibility to ensure a fair election process, not to rely on political parties for election integrity.

Conclusion: The Need for Aadhaar Integration

The issues raised in Maharashtra highlight the urgent need for Aadhaar integration to ensure the integrity of India's electoral rolls. Using biometric verification for voter registration and voting could eliminate ghost voters and de-duplicate the rolls. While the implementation of such a system requires careful planning, it is an essential step toward safeguarding India’s electoral democracy. As Dr. Ambedkar advocated, the independence and credibility of the ECI must be preserved, and the use of Aadhaar could be a significant part of this process.
