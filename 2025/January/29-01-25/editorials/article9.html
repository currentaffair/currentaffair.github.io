76th Republic Day Celebration

Introduction

The 76th Republic Day of India, celebrated on 26th January 2025, focused on the theme 'Swarnim Bharat: Virasat aur Vikas,' emphasizing military strength, development, and cultural diversity. Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto was the chief guest at the event. Republic Day marks the day India’s Constitution came into force in 1950, symbolizing India’s transition into a sovereign democratic republic.

Key Highlights of the 2025 Republic Day Tableaux

Tri-Services Tableau

For the first time, a Tri-Services Tableau was featured at the Republic Day parade, highlighting the coordinated synergy between the Army, Navy, and Air Force. The tableau showcased integrated operations across land, water, and air, under the theme ‘Shashakt aur Surakshit Bharat’ (Strong and Secure India). It displayed indigenous defense technologies such as the Arjun Main Battle Tank, Tejas MKII fighter aircraft, Advanced Light Helicopter, and INS Visakhapatnam destroyer.

DRDO Tableau

The DRDO tableau, themed ‘Raksha Kavach–Multi-layer Protection against Multi-domain Threats’, displayed cutting-edge innovations for national security. It included technologies like the Quick Reaction Surface-to-Air Missile, Medium Power Radar - Arudhra, Drone Detection System, and Indigenous Unmanned Aerial Systems, showcasing India’s focus on indigenously developed defense technologies.

Key Highlights of the 76th Republic Day

Padma Awards

139 Padma Awards were conferred on the 76th Republic Day, including Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Shri. These awards recognize exceptional service in various fields, with the Padma Vibhushan being the highest award, followed by Padma Bhushan and Padma Shri.

Gallantry Awards and Defence Decorations

President conferred Gallantry awards for 93 personnel from the Armed Forces and Central Armed Police Forces. These include Kirti Chakras, Shaurya Chakras, Bar to Sena Medal, and others. Gallantry awards are announced twice a year, on Republic Day and Independence Day, recognizing acts of bravery and devotion to duty.

Other Significant Awards

President conferred 305 defence decorations including Param Vishisht Seva Medals, Uttam Yudh Seva Medals, and Yudh Seva Medals, recognizing distinguished services during war, conflict, or peacetime. Additionally, 49 Jeevan Raksha Padak Awards were conferred, acknowledging civilian bravery in saving lives.

Significance of Republic Day

Foundation of India's Democracy

Republic Day on 26th January commemorates the adoption of the Indian Constitution in 1950, marking India’s transition to a sovereign democratic republic. This day also honors the democratic values enshrined in the Constitution. January 26th was specifically chosen to commemorate the Indian National Congress (INC)'s declaration of Purna Swaraj on this day in 1930.

Purna Swaraj Declaration

The Purna Swaraj declaration, passed by the INC in December 1929, called for complete independence from British rule. From 1930 to 1947, January 26 was celebrated as Independence Day, highlighting India’s fight for sovereignty and self-rule.

Unfurling of the National Flag

On Republic Day, the President of India unfurls the national flag, symbolizing the country's emergence from British colonial rule and commitment to democratic values. Unlike Independence Day, where the Prime Minister hoists the flag, Republic Day’s flag unfurling symbolizes India’s establishment as a republic.

Conclusion

The 76th Republic Day celebrations showcased India’s military strength, cultural diversity, and commitment to development. The country continues to honor its democratic principles and strive towards becoming a global leader, while remembering the sacrifices and struggles that led to its independence and sovereign status.
