NGO Regulation Under FCRA

Why in News?

The Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) warns NGOs using foreign funds without a valid FCRA license will face penalties under the amended FCRA, 2010.

What is FCRA?

The Foreign Contribution Regulation Act (FCRA), enacted in 1976, governs the acceptance and utilization of foreign contributions in India. It ensures these funds are used for legitimate purposes and safeguard national interest.

Amendments in FCRA

  • Amended in 2010 and 2020 to ensure transparency and prevent misuse of foreign contributions.
  • FCRA registration is valid for five years, and NGOs need to apply for renewal six months before expiry.

Key Changes in 2020 Amendments

  • MHA can suspend FCRA registration for up to 360 days if violated.
  • Aadhaar identification is mandatory for office-bearers and key functionaries of NGOs.
  • Public servants are prohibited from receiving foreign contributions.
  • Restriction on administrative expenses: NGOs can now only use 20% of foreign funds for admin purposes (previously 50%).
  • Prohibition on transferring foreign funds to other NGOs or entities.

Designated Bank Account

NGOs must open a designated FCRA account at a branch of the State Bank of India (SBI) in New Delhi.

Prohibited Activities

  • NGOs should not represent fictitious entities or engage in religious conversions.
  • Applicants must not have a history of communal tension, disharmony, or seditious activities.

Prohibited Recipients

The FCRA prohibits foreign funds for candidates, journalists, media, judges, government servants, politicians, and political organizations.
