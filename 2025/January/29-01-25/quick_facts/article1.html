Supreme Court Foundation Day

The Supreme Court of India, established on January 26, 1950, began its operations on January 28, 1950. It was initially located in the old Parliament House and moved to its current building in 1958, inaugurated by President Dr. Rajendra Prasad.

Judicial Structure

The Court was originally established with the Chief Justice of India (CJI) and 7 puisne judges. As of 2024, the Court has expanded to a CJI and 33 judges, appointed by the President and retiring at 65. Eligibility includes being an Indian citizen with experience as a High Court judge or a distinguished jurist.

Lady Justice Statue

In 2024, a new "Lady Justice" statue was unveiled, replacing the original. Unlike the previous statue, this one has open eyes and holds scales along with the Indian Constitution, signifying equality under the law. The sword has been replaced by the Constitution, symbolizing its supremacy in justice.

New Flag and Insignia

The Supreme Court also introduced a new flag and insignia in 2024 to mark its 75th year. The flag features the Ashok Chakra, the SC building, and the Book of Constitution, while the insignia bears the inscription “Yato Dharmastato Jayah” meaning "Where there is Dharma, there is victory."
