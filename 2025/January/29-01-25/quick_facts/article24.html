Declining Budget Allocations

Context

The share of India's Union Budget allocated to key social sectors such as health, rural development, education, and social welfare has been decreasing, posing challenges for essential services.

Key Sectors Affected

  • Health: Dropped from 2.47%-2.22% (FY18-22) to 1.85%-1.75% (FY23-25).
  • Rural Development: Allocation stagnated below 6% in recent years.
  • Higher Education: Reduced from 1.57%-1.37% (FY17-20) to 1.27%-0.88% (FY21-25).
  • School Education: Declined from 2.18%-1.96% to 1.61%-1.23%.
  • Social Welfare: Dropped from 1.89%-1.61% to 1.17%-0.97%.

Scheme-Level Allocation Cuts

  • MGNREGS: Allocation for 2024-25 is ₹86,000 crore, marking a 10-year low.
  • Rural Development Shortfall: ₹4,315 crore shortfall leading to delays in wage disbursements.
  • National Social Assistance Programme: Drastic funding cuts for pensions (old age, widow, disability) from 1.21%-0.36% (FY19-21) to 0.2%.
  • PM-POSHAN Scheme: Allocations for mid-day meals have dropped to 0.26% of the total budget in FY25, the lowest in nine years.

Exceptions to the Trend

  • Ayushman Bharat (PMJAY): Stable or increasing funding for healthcare access for vulnerable populations.
  • PMAY-Rural: Allocations have either increased or remained stable for rural housing.
  • PM Schools for Rising India (PM SHRI): Funding for school education in rural areas remains stable or increasing.

The Need for Action in FY26 Budget

The upcoming FY26 Budget, due for presentation on February 1, must address cuts in social sector funding, focusing on critical schemes like MGNREGS, PM-POSHAN, and social assistance programs.

Key Recommendations

  • Critical Social Schemes at Risk: The continued decline in allocations could undermine vital services for rural development, healthcare, and education.
  • Focus on Inclusivity: The Budget should prioritize the socio-economic inclusion of vulnerable communities, ensuring fair distribution of resources.
  • Sustainability of Key Schemes: Programs like MGNREGS, PM-POSHAN, and social assistance need sufficient funding to maintain their impact.
