India’s 75th Constitution Anniversary

Context

On 26th January 2025, India celebrated the 75th anniversary of enforcing its Constitution, marking a journey of transformation from a colonial state to a sovereign, democratic republic.

Key Features of the Constitution

  • Adopted: November 26, 1949, enforced on January 26, 1950.
  • Dr. B.R. Ambedkar’s Vision: Placing the nation above personal beliefs for nation-building.

Federalism in India

Dr. Ambedkar emphasized that the Union and States are co-equal, with Union powers overriding during emergencies. Federalism, though not explicitly mentioned, is fundamental to India’s governance.

Judicial Support for Federalism

  • S.R. Bommai v/s Union of India (1994): Upheld federalism as a basic structure.
  • Government of NCT of Delhi v/s Union of India (2024): Reinforced federalism principles.

Challenges in Federal Structure

  • Conflicts: Between State Governors and elected governments.
  • Simultaneous Elections: Proposals for one-time national and state elections.
  • Regional Issues: Neglect of languages and demands for linguistic equality.
  • Fiscal Federalism: Concerns about the Finance Commission and GST regime.
  • Delimitation Exercise: May intensify debates over Union-State representation.

Strengthening the Constitutional Philosophy

  • Protect Democracy: Safeguard liberty and equality against emerging threats.
  • Promote Unity: Address caste and socio-economic divisions.
  • Resolve Conflicts: Between the Union and States, ensuring linguistic and fiscal equality.
  • Ethical Leadership: Foster informed, ethical leadership across sectors.
