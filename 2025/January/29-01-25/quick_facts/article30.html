OpenAI Copyright Jurisdiction Dispute

Overview of the Case

The Delhi High Court is hearing a copyright infringement case filed by Asian News International (ANI) against OpenAI for allegedly using publicly available content to train ChatGPT. OpenAI argues that Indian courts do not have jurisdiction over the matter.

OpenAI’s Jurisdiction Argument

  • OpenAI's Defense: OpenAI argues that disputes must be resolved in California courts as per their terms of use, governed by California law.

Industry Bodies' Involvement

  • Federation of Indian Publishers (FIP) & DNPA: These organizations have intervened in the case, supporting ANI's allegations against OpenAI.

Court’s Approach

  • Jurisdiction Issue: Justice Amit Bansal decided to address the jurisdiction question along with other aspects of the case.
  • Notice to OpenAI: The court has issued a notice to OpenAI regarding the interventions by FIP and DNPA, awaiting a response within two weeks.

Legal Assistance for the Court

  • Experts Appointed: The court has appointed Arul George Scaria and Adarsh Ramanujan to assist in outlining the legal issues in the case.

Broader Implications

  • Future of AI: Dr. Scaria highlighted that the outcome of this case could have long-term consequences for AI research and development in India.

Accusations Against OpenAI

  • Copyright Infringement: ANI, FIP, and DNPA accuse OpenAI of unlawfully using publicly available content to train its AI models, arguing this constitutes copyright infringement.
