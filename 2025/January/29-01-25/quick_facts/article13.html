UCC Implementation in Uttarakhand

Introduction

Chief Minister Pushkar Singh Dhami launched the Uniform Civil Code (UCC) in Uttarakhand, excluding Scheduled Tribes and natives who migrated out of the state.

Key Provisions of the UCC

  • Bans Harmful Practices: Halala, iddat, and talaq (under Muslim Personal Law) are prohibited.
  • Womenâ€™s Rights: Ensures equal rights for women in property and inheritance.
  • Online Marriage & Divorce Registration: Marriages, divorces, and live-in relationships must be registered on the government portal.

UCC Portal Features

  • Aadhaar-Based Verification: Mandatory for authentication.
  • AI-Based Translation: Translates content into 22 languages.
  • Integrated Data: Combines data from 13 government departments, including police and courts.

Marriage and Divorce Registration

  • Marriage Registration: Marriages since March 26, 2010, must be registered within 6 months, while those after UCC implementation must be registered within 60 days.
  • Divorce/Annulment Registration: Requires marriage registration, court case details, and final order.

Will Registration

Will registration can be done via:

  • Form filling on the portal
  • Uploading handwritten or typed will
  • Recording and uploading a 3-minute video

Potential Impacts

The UCC rollout is seen as a major policy shift, aiming to standardize marriage, divorce, and inheritance laws across all communities, except those exempted.
