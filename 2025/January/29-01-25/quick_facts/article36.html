Dhanauri Wetland & Species

Location and Features

Dhanauri Wetland is situated in Greater Noida, Gautam Buddha Nagar District, Uttar Pradesh, near Okhla Bird Sanctuary and Surajpur Wetland, within 15 km of the Yamuna River.

The wetland is primarily a bog, classified as an Important Bird Area (IBA) by BirdLife International, home to various species including the vulnerable Sarus Crane (Antigone antigone).

Significant Species

The Sarus Crane is the tallest flying bird in the world, with three subspecies: Indian, Eastern, and Australian. The Indian Sarus Crane is the heaviest and tallest of these subspecies, listed as "vulnerable" on the IUCN Red List.

Other bird species in the area include Teal, Mallard, Northern Plover, Greylag Goose, White-headed Goose, Wattle-necked Stork, Black-necked Stork, Painted Stork, and Merganser.

National Parks and Sanctuaries in Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh is home to several notable wildlife areas:

  • Amangarh Tiger Reserve: Buffer zone for Jim Corbett Tiger Reserve.
  • Dudhwa Tiger Reserve: Known for swamp deer, tigers, and elephants.
  • Pilibhit Tiger Reserve: Habitat for Bengal tigers and elephants.
  • Bakhira Wildlife Sanctuary: A floodplain wetland important for migratory birds.
  • Chandraprabha Wildlife Sanctuary: Located between the Ganges and Son rivers.
  • Okhla Bird Wildlife Sanctuary: Wetland on the Yamuna River, supporting diverse avifauna.

Ramsar Sites in Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh hosts several Ramsar Sites, including Bakhira WLS, Haiderpur Wetland, Nawabganj BS, and Sur Sarovar, which protect vital bird species like the Egyptian Vultures (EN) and Pallas’s Fish Eagle (EN).

Conclusion

Dhanauri Wetland and other key wildlife sanctuaries in Uttar Pradesh play a critical role in preserving unique species, including the Sarus Crane, and are integral to the region’s biodiversity conservation efforts.
