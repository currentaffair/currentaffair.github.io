Thyroid Health Overview

The thyroid gland produces thyroxine, which is essential for the body's normal functioning.

Disorders

Thyroid disorders include hypothyroidism (low hormone levels) and hyperthyroidism (excess hormone levels). Swelling of the thyroid is common but not always malignant.

Key Statistics

40%-50% of people may have undiagnosed thyroid issues. Thyroid cancers are curable in 90% of cases with timely treatment.

Symptoms
  • Gradual weight changes
  • Lethargy, fatigue, and sleepiness
  • Constipation or diarrhoea
  • Menstrual irregularities
  • Persistent palpitations, sweating
  • Chronic cough, difficulty swallowing or breathing
  • Delayed growth in children
Testing and Diagnosis

Thyroid function tests are essential and often included in routine health packages. Imaging and clinical examinations help in early intervention.

Thyroid Disorders in Pregnancy

Thyroid disorders are the second most common disorder in pregnancy after diabetes. Early detection is crucial, as changes may persist post-pregnancy.

Risk Factors

Higher-risk groups include adolescents, pregnant women, men over 40, and women over 50. Stress, anxiety, and depression can also contribute to thyroid issues.

Expert Advice

Regular check-ups and monitoring are critical for early detection and treatment. Even asymptomatic thyroid swellings should not be ignored.
