Unregistered Firm Suit Ruling

Context and Supreme Court Ruling

The Supreme Court ruled that partners of an unregistered firm cannot enforce contractual rights under Section 69(1) of the Indian Partnership Act, 1932. The ruling prohibits suits except in cases of dissolution or rendition of accounts.

Case Details

  • Issue: Whether partners of an unregistered firm can enforce contractual rights under Section 69 of the Indian Partnership Act, 1932.

  • Supreme Court Verdict: The Court dismissed the suit because the partnership was unregistered, affirming that such suits are barred unless related to dissolution or accounts.

About Section 69(1) of the Indian Partnership Act, 1932

  • Section 69(1): Prohibits suits to enforce rights arising out of a partnership agreement unless the firm is registered.
  • Exception: Section 69(3) allows suits for dissolution of the firm or for rendition of accounts, even if the firm is unregistered.
  • Non-commencement of Business: The Court emphasized that non-commencement of business does not affect the applicability of Section 69(1), rejecting the argument of an unstarted business.
