Recognition of Denotified Tribes

The Adi tribe in Siang, Arunachal Pradesh, was officially recognized on January 28, addressing their socio-economic challenges.

Social Empowerment

Denotified tribes often face marginalization. Recognition can empower them socially by enabling access to affirmative action, including reservations in education and employment.

Economic Benefits

Classification provides access to welfare schemes, financial support, and improved infrastructure, contributing to the economic upliftment of the community.

Historical Context

Denotified tribes were once labeled as "criminal tribes" under colonial rule, and their recognition seeks to undo this historical injustice, contributing to broader tribal welfare discourse.
