Fiscal Health Index 2025

Arvind Panagariya, Chairman of the 16th Finance Commission, launched the Fiscal Health Index (FHI) 2025, a framework developed by NITI Aayog to assess the fiscal performance of states.

About the Fiscal Health Index

The FHI evaluates the fiscal health of 18 major states based on five key sub-indices:

  • Quality of Expenditure: Measures government spending efficiency.
  • Revenue Mobilization: Assesses capacity to generate revenues.
  • Fiscal Prudence: Evaluates management of fiscal deficits.
  • Debt Index: Examines debt levels relative to revenue.
  • Debt Sustainability: Determines ability to maintain debt levels.

Data for the index was sourced from the Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) covering the period from 2014-15 to 2021-22.

State Performance in FHI

Odisha topped the index with a score of 67.8, excelling in debt sustainability, revenue mobilization, and capital expenditure.

States as 'Achievers'

States like Odisha, Chhattisgarh, Goa, Jharkhand, and Karnataka were ranked highly due to:

  • Higher Capital Outlay (up to 4% of GSDP).
  • Revenue Surplus through effective non-tax revenue.
  • Low Interest Payments (<7% of revenue).
  • Sustainable Debt Management and Low Fiscal Deficits.
States as 'Front-Runners and Performers'

States like Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Telangana, and Madhya Pradesh showed strong fiscal management, while Tamil Nadu, Bihar, Rajasthan, and Haryana showed moderate fiscal stability.

Aspirational States

States like Punjab, Kerala, West Bengal, and Andhra Pradesh face fiscal challenges, such as high deficits, growing debt, and weak revenue mobilization.

Recommendations for Improvement

States should focus on:

  • Revenue diversification and improving tax compliance.
  • Investments in infrastructure, health, and education.
  • Managing debt with a sustainable framework.
  • Improving reporting mechanisms and following fiscal responsibility standards.
Way Forward

The FHI provides a framework to guide policymakers in improving fiscal performance. By learning from top performers like Odisha and Chhattisgarh, states can adopt strategies to strengthen their financial health and contribute to the vision of Atmanirbhar Bharat and Viksit Bharat @2047.
