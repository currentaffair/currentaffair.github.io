KaWaCHaM Disaster Warning System

Introduction

KaWaCHaM (Kerala Crisis Warning and Risk Management System) is an advanced disaster warning system aimed at enhancing early disaster preparedness and improving public safety in Kerala. It integrates alerts, sirens, and global weather models to help in risk assessment and planning actions during extreme weather events.

Key Features

  • Purpose: Risk assessment, issuing alerts, and planning actions based on threat levels.
  • Project Goal: Installation of 126 sirens on high towers, government buildings, and schools.
  • Siren Features: Three color-coded strobe lights (red, yellow, orange), 8 speakers, and coverage up to 1200 meters.
  • System Integration: Combines all warning systems into a framework for real-time crisis response.
  • Data Collection: Weather data from IMD, INCOIS, CWC, and social media platforms.
  • Warning Coverage: Includes sea attacks, heavy rainfall, strong winds, and extreme heat.
  • Command Center: Operates at the State Emergency Center and Taluk levels for effective response.

National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project

The National Cyclone Risk Mitigation Project, funded by the World Bank, aims to reduce cyclone risks in coastal regions of India. It focuses on improving community response, emergency shelters, and disaster risk management.

  • Objective: To implement structural and non-structural measures to mitigate cyclone effects.
  • Identified States: 13 states and UTs, including Andhra Pradesh, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, and Odisha.
  • Key Components: Improved distribution systems, better access to emergency shelters, and strengthened local response capacity.
