India Joins Eurodrone Program

Context

India has joined the Medium Altitude Long Endurance Remotely Piloted Aircraft System (MALE RPAS) or Eurodrone program, managed by the Organisation for Joint Armament Cooperation (OCCAR) and led by Airbus Defence and Space.

Eurodrone Overview

The Eurodrone program is a strategic initiative to develop drones for defense, surveillance, and reconnaissance applications.

Key Features of Eurodrone

  • Max Payload: 2.3 tonnes
  • Endurance: Up to 40 hours
  • Motor Configuration: Dual-motor
  • Operational Environment: Designed for harsh weather conditions
  • Program Established: 2022
  • Pre-Initiation Review: 2024
  • Expected Service Entry: 2030
  • Designed For: Safe flight in non-segregated airspace
  • Applications: Istar, Maritime Surveillance, Anti-Surface Warfare, Early Warning

Strategic Importance of India's Participation

India's involvement strengthens defense ties with Europe and supports the shared objective of reducing reliance on American and Israeli drones like the Reaper and Heron. India is already part of various other defense projects like the C-295 aircraft production and Rafale m aircraft for naval use.

Organisation for Joint Armament Cooperation (OCCAR)

About OCCAR

OCCAR, established in 1996, is responsible for managing European armament cooperation programs.

Key Details about OCCAR

  • Established: 12 November 1996
  • Legal Status: Achieved in January 2001 after ratification by France, Germany, Italy, and the UK
  • Member States: Belgium, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, United Kingdom
  • Non-Member Participants: EU and NATO members, Australia
  • Key Decision-Making Body: Board of Supervisors (BoS)
  • Current Programs: Airbus A400M, Eurodrone, Boxer, FREMM frigates, MU90 Impact
