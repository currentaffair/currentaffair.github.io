Dinosaurs' Origins in Gondwana

Context

The evolution of dinosaurs, one of Earth’s most fascinating prehistoric lineages, dates back to the late Triassic Period. Researchers suggest that dinosaurs originated in the low-latitude regions of Gondwana, near the equator.

Gondwana's Role in Dinosaur Origins

  • Gondwana, part of the supercontinent Pangaea, included present-day northern South America and northern Africa.
  • These regions are now separated by plate tectonics into the Amazon rainforest and the Sahara desert.

Key Evidence Supporting Gondwana’s Role

  • Early dinosaur fossils, such as Eoraptor and Herrerasaurus from Argentina, Saturnalia from Brazil, and Mbiresaurus raathi from Zimbabwe, show features suggesting millions of years of prior evolution.
  • Researchers point out gaps in the fossil record in the modern Sahara and Amazon, possibly holding earlier dinosaur remains.

The Environment in Early Gondwana

  • Dinosaurs likely emerged in harsh climates with extreme heat, dry deserts, savannah-like habitats, and some forested areas prone to seasonal wildfires.
  • Although previously thought unsuitable for early dinosaur survival, evidence suggests they adapted to these challenging conditions.

Evolutionary Context

  • Dinosaurs evolved from primitive reptiles following the mass extinction at the end of the Permian Period (252 million years ago), caused by extreme volcanism.
  • The sparse fossil record in equatorial Gondwana may be due to unfavorable preservation conditions and limited exploration in regions like the Amazon and Sahara.

Implications

This research reshapes our understanding of dinosaur origins and emphasizes the potential of unexplored fossil-rich regions to fill critical gaps in Earth’s evolutionary history.
