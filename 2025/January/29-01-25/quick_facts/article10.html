Wetland Accredited Cities Recognition

Why in News?

Indore and Udaipur have become the first Indian cities to gain international recognition as accredited wetland cities under the Ramsar Convention. There are currently 85 Indian wetlands protected under the Ramsar Convention, with 172 member countries globally.

What are Wetland Accredited Cities?

This recognition is granted to cities committed to conserving and sustainably managing wetlands, ensuring they provide essential environmental and community services. The cities must meet six international criteria primarily focused on wetland ecosystem conservation.

Global Recognition

China leads with 22 accredited cities, followed by France with 9. There are over 2,400 Ramsar Sites worldwide, covering more than 2.5 million square kilometers.

Accreditation Criteria

  • Conservation of wetland ecosystems
  • Integration of wetland protection into urban planning
  • Balancing urban development with ecological preservation

Udaipur and Indore's Lakes

Udaipur, known as the City of Lakes, includes Lake Pichola, Fatehsagar Lake, and Swaroop Sagar Lake. Indore is home to Lotus Lake, Choral Dam, Pipliyapala Lake, and Sirpur Lake.

Bhopal's Bhoj Wetland

Bhopal’s Bhoj Wetland was nominated for accreditation but did not receive it due to concerns over a proposed road project potentially harming the ecosystem of the Bhoj Wetland.

Key Facts About Bhoj Wetland

The Bhoj Wetland is a Ramsar site designated in 2002, consisting of two interconnected reservoirs: Upper Lake and Lower Lake. Upper Lake borders Van Vihar National Park.

Biodiversity in Bhoj Wetland

  • Avifauna: Coot, Red-Crested Pochard, Sarus Crane, Black-necked Stork, Pallas’s Fish Eagle.
  • Other Fauna: Chital, Wild Boar, Nilgai, and Sambar in Van Vihar National Park.
