Chronic Pulmonary Aspergillosis in Assam

Overview of the Study

A recent study published in the journal PLOS Neglected Tropical Diseases identified chronic pulmonary aspergillosis (CPA) as a life-threatening fungal infection affecting post-TB survivors in Assam's tea plantations. This is the first study of its kind focused on tea workers.

Prevalence of Tuberculosis (TB) in Assam Tea Plantations

TB is a major public health issue in Assam, particularly in the Brahmaputra and Barak valleys. The TB prevalence rate is 217 per 1,00,000 population (2019-2021 National TB Prevalence Survey). Risk factors include:

  • Poverty
  • Malnutrition
  • Kitchen smoke
  • Close contact with TB patients
  • Congested living conditions

Key Findings of the Study

Researchers studied 128 patients from tea garden hospitals in Dibrugarh district. The prevalence of CPA among post-TB patients was 48.9%, highlighting its significance as a post-TB health issue. Key findings include:

  • CPA prevalence: 17.18%
  • Seropositivity in active TB cases: 18.5%
  • Higher CPA prevalence in middle-aged male workers
  • Assam's CPA prevalence: 60 per 1,00,000 (higher than global average of 42)

Public Health Implications

CPA shares clinical features with TB, complicating diagnosis. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial to reduce morbidity and mortality. Recommended measures include:

  • Testing for Aspergillus antibodies in patients with respiratory symptoms post-TB
  • Training healthcare providers to recognize CPA symptoms
  • Educating tea workers on respiratory health, nutrition, and hygiene

Related Health Issues in Assam

A 2019 study on epidemic dropsy in Assam showed nutritional health risks from consuming oils contaminated with Argemone mexicana, further highlighting health concerns in the region.
