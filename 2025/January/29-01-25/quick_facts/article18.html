India-Indonesia Relations

Context

Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto visited India to attend the celebrations of India's 76th Republic Day as the Chief Guest.

Historical and Cultural Ties

  • South Indian-origin objects discovered in Bali suggest early cultural exchanges around 660 BCE.
  • The Ramayana and Mahabharata continue to influence Indonesian culture, seen in performances like Ramayana dances in Java.
  • Indian Mughal architecture influenced Indonesian Islamic architecture, particularly in Sumatra.

Modern Diplomatic Relations

  • India and Indonesia established diplomatic relations in 1949, with a treaty signed in 1951.
  • Both countries worked together to establish Afro-Asian cooperation at the Bandung Conference in 1955.
  • Bilateral relations have flourished since India’s Look East Policy in 1991.

Strategic Partnership and Security

  • Signed a strategic partnership agreement to strengthen cooperation in national security, maritime security, and economic development.
  • Enhanced defence cooperation through joint military exercises like Ex Garuda Shakti and Ex Samudra Shakti.
  • India and Indonesia adopted a "Shared Vision" for maritime cooperation in 2018.

Economic Engagement

  • Indonesia is India’s third-largest export partner in ASEAN, with bilateral trade reaching US$ 29.4 billion in FY24.
  • India and Indonesia aim for US$ 50 billion trade by 2025.
  • India supported Indonesia’s mid-day meal scheme and signed an MoU on Local Currency Settlement Systems in March 2024.

Outcome of Recent Visit

  • Bilateral ties strengthened with commitments to regular meetings, defence collaboration, and enhanced trade.
  • Agreed to share defence expertise, collaborate on maritime safety, and explore new sectors like renewable energy.
  • Signed MoUs on health, traditional medicine, and digital health cooperation.

Steps to Strengthen India-Indonesia Ties

  • Indonesia’s BRICS membership offers opportunities for India-Indonesia collaboration within this strategic group.
  • Both nations align on Indo-Pacific security issues and can work together in ASEAN and other regional forums.

Way Forward

India and Indonesia should focus on further collaboration through platforms like BRICS, the Indo-Pacific Oceans Initiative, and trilateral frameworks with Australia and Japan.
