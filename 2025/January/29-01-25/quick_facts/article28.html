Typhon and Missile Systems

U.S. Military Typhon Deployment

The U.S. military has relocated its Typhon launchers, capable of firing multipurpose missiles, from Laoag airfield in the Philippines to another site on Luzon island.

Typhon Missile System

  • Name: Typhon (Mid-Range Capability - MRC)
  • Type: Surface-to-surface missile system
  • Manufacturer: Lockheed Martin
  • Missile Types: SM-6 (500 km range) and Tomahawk (2,500 km range)
  • SM-6 Capability: Surface-to-air missile with surface target engagement
  • Tomahawk Capability: Ground attack and wider mission capabilities
  • Battery Components: 4 launchers, command posts, support vehicles
  • Purpose: Provides long-range strike capabilities for the US military

India's Missile Defense Systems

Two-Layer Ballistic Missile Defense

  • 1st Layer (Endo-atmosphere): Advanced Air Defense (AAD) captures missiles at 20-40 km altitude
  • 2nd Layer (Exo-atmosphere): Pradyumna Ballistic Missile Interceptor (300-2000 km range) intercepts at ~80 km altitude
  • Higher Altitudes: Agni-V-based interceptors for missiles at altitudes up to 150 km (range of 5000 km)

Multilayer Air Defense Systems

  • S-400 (Russia): Intercepts aircraft, UAVs, ballistic missiles up to 400 km and 30 km altitude
  • Alternatives: THAAD and Patriot systems (US)
  • Iron Dome: Israeli system for short-range threats
  • BARAK-8 SAM: 70-100 km range, joint system developed by India and Israel
  • Akash System: 25 km range, medium-range for engaging multiple targets

Multilayer Air Defense for Delhi

  • Layers: Ballistic Missile Defense, S-400, BARAK-8 SAM, Akash, NASAMS-2

NASAMS-2

  • Components: 12 multi-missile launchers, AMRAAM, radars, advanced sensors

Hypersonic Technology

  • Speed: >Mach 5 (6,174 km/h)
  • Development: DRDO's use of Scramjet technology for Mach 6 speeds
  • Hypersonic Weapons Systems: Hypersonic Controlled Missiles (HCM), Hypersonic Glide Vehicles (HGV)
  • Importance: Can penetrate missile defense systems, designed for speed and survivability
