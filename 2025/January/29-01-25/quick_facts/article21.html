India-China Relations and JNPA Growth

Direct Flights & Visa Issuance

India and China have agreed to resume direct flights and restart visa processes, making travel easier between the two nations.

Mansarovar Pilgrimage Resumes

The Mansarovar Yatra will restart in summer 2025, allowing pilgrims to travel to the sacred site once again.

People-to-People Exchanges

Measures to enhance cultural, media, and think tank exchanges between India and China have been agreed upon.

Hydrological Data Sharing

India and China will resume dialogue on sharing hydrological data related to trans-border rivers, a key issue for India.

75th Anniversary of Diplomatic Relations

India and China will celebrate 75 years of diplomatic relations, with events marking this milestone. Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi stressed ending mutual suspicion.

Economic & Trade Discussions

  • India raised concerns about Chinese trade restrictions on pharmaceuticals and high-tech exports.
  • China addressed issues about India's restrictions on Chinese companies and business visas.

Progress on Bilateral Dialogue

India's Foreign Secretary met with Chinese Vice Foreign Minister to review and enhance bilateral relations, following the Modi-Xi meeting in Kazan.

Resumption of Dialogue Mechanisms

The FS-Vice FM level bilateral dialogue mechanism has resumed after a hiatus due to tensions at the Line of Actual Control (LAC).

Economic Concerns

  • Issues raised include trade barriers and investment clearance requirements.
  • India highlighted the withholding of critical equipment, such as Tunnel Boring Machines (TBMs), from China.

SCO Summit and Future Engagements

India supports China’s chairmanship of the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO) in 2025, with PM Modi expected to attend the SCO Head of State Summit in June 2025.

Jawaharlal Nehru Port Growth

The Jawaharlal Nehru Port Authority (JNPA) is set to become India’s first port to handle 10 million TEUs annually by 2027. In 2024, it handled a record 7.05 million TEUs with a growth rate of 11%.

JNPA Features & Expansion

  • Located in Navi Mumbai, commissioned in 1989.
  • Operates 5 container terminals, including BMCT, NSICT, and GTIPL.
  • Plans include a satellite port at Vadhavan and dry ports at Jalna and Wardha.

India’s Maritime Sector

India’s maritime sector handles 95% of trade by volume and 70% by value, with 12 major ports and over 200 minor and intermediate ports.
