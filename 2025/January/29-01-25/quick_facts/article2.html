Rhodamine B in Food

Rhodamine B, a synthetic dye known for its bright pink color, is banned in food due to its carcinogenic properties.

What is Rhodamine B?

Rhodamine B is commonly used in textiles, paper, and leather for its fluorescent properties. However, its use in consumables, like food, is concerning due to associated health risks.

Health Risks

Rhodamine B has been linked to cancer. Studies indicate it causes DNA damage, leading to mutations that may result in cancerous growths. Long-term exposure in animals has shown tumors in organs like the liver and bladder.

Global Actions

Countries such as the U.S. have banned Rhodamine B in food products, with the FDA recognizing its carcinogenicity. The European Union also restricts its use in cosmetics.

India's Response

In February 2024, Tamil Nadu banned cotton candy after detecting Rhodamine B. Other states like Karnataka, Puducherry, and Himachal Pradesh have also implemented bans on the substance.

Way Forward

Health experts advocate for strengthening India's food safety ecosystem and conducting more research on food dyes like Rhodamine B. There is a need for proactive regulation, better enforcement of food safety laws, and consumer awareness about food additives.
