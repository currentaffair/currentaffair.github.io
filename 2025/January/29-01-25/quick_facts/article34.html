RBI Liquidity Measures Explained

Overview of RBI Measures

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has announced several measures to ease liquidity pressures and ensure financial stability.

Key Measures Announced

  • Government Bond Purchases: RBI will buy 600 billion rupees worth of government bonds in three tranches, injecting liquidity into the system.
  • 56-Day Variable Rate Repo Auction: On February 7, 2025, a 500 billion rupee auction will help banks manage short-term liquidity needs.
  • USD/INR Swap Auction: A $5 billion swap auction on January 31, 2025, to ease foreign exchange liquidity and stabilize the rupee-dollar exchange rate.

Context of Measures

The banking system's liquidity deficit had reached a one-year peak, signaling tight financial conditions. These steps aim to address the liquidity gap and support smooth credit market functioning.

Expected Outcomes

  • Stabilizing Market: The liquidity measures are expected to stabilize the banking system and ensure adequate funding.
  • Currency Stability: The foreign exchange swap auction will help maintain stability in the rupee against major currencies, particularly the US dollar.
