ISRO’s Third Launch Pad

Overview

The Union Cabinet has approved the establishment of the Third Launch Pad (TLP) at the Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), located in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh. This new pad will have the capacity to handle spacecraft weighing up to 30,000 tonnes in low Earth orbit.

Launch Pad Details

The Third Launch Pad is designed to support NGLV, LVM3 vehicles with semi-cryogenic stages and scaled-up NGLV configurations. Currently, ISRO uses two existing launch pads: FLP for PSLV and SSLV, and SLP for GSLV and LVM3.

Future Vision

India's space vision includes a Crewed Lunar Landing by 2040 and the Bharatiya Antariksh Station (BAS) by 2035. These plans require heavier launch vehicles that cannot be accommodated by the current pads.

New Launchport in Tamil Nadu

In 2024, a foundation stone was laid for ISRO’s second rocket launchport in Kulasekarapattinam, Tamil Nadu, aimed at facilitating commercial and small satellite launches.
