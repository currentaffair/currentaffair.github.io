Nigeria as 9th BRICS Partner Country

Brazil, holding the BRICS presidency in 2025, announced Nigeria's admission as a "partner country" of the BRICS bloc. Nigeria becomes the 9th BRICS partner country, joining Belarus, Bolivia, Cuba, Kazakhstan, Malaysia, Thailand, Uganda, and Uzbekistan.

About BRICS

BRICS was formed by Brazil, Russia, India and China in 2009, with South Africa added in 2010 as a counterweight to G7 countries. In 2023, BRICS added Iran, Egypt, Ethiopia, and the UAE, while Saudi Arabia has been invited to join. Indonesia officially joined as the 10th member in January 2025.

Nigeria's Significance

Nigeria has the world's 6th largest population and Africa's largest, in addition to being the 4th largest economy in Africa. It is located on the Western coast of Africa, often called the "Giant of Africa."
