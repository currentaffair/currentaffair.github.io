India's Performance in QS World Future Skills Index 2025

Context: India ranks second globally in the QS World Future Skills Index 2025, which evaluates nations' preparedness to meet the demands of a rapidly evolving global job market.

About QS World Future Skills Index 2025

The QS World Future Skills Index was developed by Quacquarelli Symonds (QS) to assess countries' preparedness for emerging job trends such as Artificial Intelligence (AI), digital transformation, and green technologies.

India's Performance

India ranks 25th overall in the index, excelling particularly in the "Future of Work" category, where it ranks second globally, just behind the United States. The index highlights India's potential in AI, digital industries, and green sectors.

Key Strengths of India

India benefits from a young population, strong economic growth, and a dynamic start-up ecosystem. The country excels in AI, digital, and green industries, positioning it as a leader in future-ready skills.

Challenges India Faces

Despite its strengths, India faces challenges such as a gap between higher education and industry needs, as well as low scores in sustainable practices and innovation.

Challenges in Skill Development

India faces a skilled workforce gap, with a 29-million worker shortage in key sectors. Only a small percentage of graduates meet employability standards, and access to higher education is limited.

Recommendations

Strengthening collaboration between educational institutions and industries, boosting R&D capabilities, and ensuring equitable access to quality education are essential for improving India's future skill readiness.

Way Forward

India's demographic advantage and strong economic foundation place it in a prime position to shape the future of work. Focused efforts on education, innovation, and industry integration will enhance its preparedness for emerging industries.
