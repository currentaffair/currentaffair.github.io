38th National Games and Kalaripayattu

Why in News?

The Indian Kalaripayattu Federation has accused the Indian Olympics Association (IOA) of downgrading Kalaripayattu to the demonstration section, not the competitive section. Demonstration events showcase sports for promotion and are excluded from the official medal tally.

What are Key Points about Kalaripayattu?

About: One of the oldest and most scientific martial arts, originating in Kerala. Established by the warrior sage Parasurama, later introduced to China by Bodhidharma in the 5th century AD.

Philosophy: "Kalari" refers to a gymnasium, and "Payattu" means fight or exercise, emphasizing mind-body coordination and techniques inspired by eight animals.

Types: Northern Kalaripayattu focuses on weapons and linear movements; Southern Kalaripayattu emphasizes multi-directional movements with fewer weapons.

Stages of Training: Maippayattu, Kolthari, Angathari, Verumkai - Body conditioning, wooden weapons, sharp metal weapons, and bare-hand fighting.

Current Context

It was included as a competitive sport in the 37th National Games but relegated to a demonstration sport in the 38th National Games. Kalaripayattu athletes won 22 medals, including 19 gold, in the 2023 National Games.

Key Points About National Games 2025

About: An Olympic-style multi-sport event where athletes from states and Union Territories compete for medals. The 38th edition will be held from 28th January to 14th February 2025 in Uttarakhand.

Competition Structure: 32 sporting disciplines and four demonstration sports, including Kalaripayattu, Yogasana, Mallakhambh, and Rafting.

Theme and Tagline: The mascot is Mouli, inspired by Uttarakhand's state bird, the Monal. The tagline is "Sankalp Se Shikhar Tak" (from resolution to zenith).
