Global Risks Report 2025

Key Insights

The World Economic Forum (WEF) has identified extreme weather events as the most severe long-term risk for the next decade. The findings are based on the Global Risks Perception Survey 2024-2025.

Top Risks

Immediate Risks (Next 2 Years)

  • Water Scarcity
  • Misinformation and Disinformation
  • Violations of Human Rights
  • Pollution (Air, Water, Land)
  • Labor and Talent Shortages

Long-Term Risks (Next 10 Years)

  • Extreme Weather Events
  • Biodiversity Loss
  • Earth System Changes

About WEF

Founded in 1971 by Klaus Schwab, the WEF emphasizes cooperation for long-term global benefits. It publishes key reports, including the Global Risks Report and the Global Gender Gap Index.
