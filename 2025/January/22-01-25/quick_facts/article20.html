Generations of Fighter Jets

Context

Fighter jets are classified in terms of generations, indicating their development period and technological capabilities. Currently, five generations are in active service, with a sixth generation under development.

What does "generation" mean?

The term "aircraft generation" emerged in the 1990s, referring primarily to fighters. Each generation signifies a major leap in technology, and generational shifts occur when new innovations cannot be integrated into existing designs.

Generations of Fighter Jets

First Generation (1943 to 1955)

Early jet fighters with subsonic speeds, basic avionics, and armed with machine guns or cannons. Examples include the Messerschmitt Me 262 and MiG-15.

Second Generation (1955-1970)

Fighter jets with afterburners and swept wings for supersonic speeds. Key features included fire control radars and semi-active radar-guided missiles. Examples include MiG-21 and Lockheed F-104 Starfighter.

Third Generation (1960s to 1970s)

Multi-role aircraft with advanced radar, electronic warfare systems, and enhanced turbofan engines for improved range and maneuverability. Examples: McDonnell Douglas F-4 Phantom, MiG-23.

Fourth Generation (1970-2000)

Introduction of Fly-by-Wire technology, advanced avionics, and stealth capabilities. Notable aircraft include the F-16 Fighting Falcon and Sukhoi Su-30.
