India’s Automotive Market Growth

Market Overview: Only 3.3% of India’s population owns cars, indicating vast growth potential. 36% of consumers are considering electric vehicles (EVs), and 30% are interested in electric two-wheelers.

Comparison with Developed Markets

India’s automotive market has significant room for growth compared to high-penetration markets like the U.S. and Denmark.

Consumer Preferences and Gender Influence

Women’s Influence: Women impact 52% of EV purchase decisions, an increase from 38% for non-EV vehicles.

Tech Preferences: 41% of two-wheeler owners prefer vehicles with in-built navigation systems, indicating a shift towards tech-savvy vehicles.

Barriers to EV Adoption

Charging Infrastructure: 50% of consumers are concerned about charging stations availability.

High Costs: 45% of consumers are dissatisfied with EV purchase costs.

Battery Life and Model Variety: Concerns regarding battery life and the variety of models are significant for 33% and 20% of consumers, respectively.

Solutions to Overcome Challenges

Infrastructure: Expanding and improving the discoverability of charging stations is critical.

Financing: Innovative financing options can reduce acquisition costs.

Local Manufacturing: Scaling up production to lower costs and increase variety is essential.

Impact of High-Income Households

Wealth Growth: By 2035, a 2.7-fold increase in elite households will make higher-priced EVs more affordable, further boosting market growth.

Preference for Pre-Owned Vehicles

First-Time Buyers: 25% of first-time car buyers are interested in pre-owned vehicles.

Market Influences: Digitization and trust in local selling platforms are key drivers for the pre-owned vehicle market.

Future Trends

Car Rentals: Gen Z is 2.7 times more likely to use self-drive rentals, signaling a shift towards on-demand transportation.

Influence on Purchases: 85% of rental users are open to purchasing the car model they rented.
