Climate and Development Challenges in Cabo Verde

Key Highlights

The World Bank's Country Climate and Development Report (CCDR) emphasizes that Cabo Verde requires $140 million annually from 2024-2030 to mitigate climate and developmental challenges.

  • Extreme climate events: heat, floods, droughts, storms, and sea-level rise.
  • Economic dependency on tourism and imported goods (80% of food consumption).
  • Vulnerability exacerbated by public debt and rural-urban migration.

About Cabo Verde

Location: Archipelago off West Africa, near Senegal.

Features: 10 volcanic islands divided into windward and leeward groups.

Climate: Moderate temperatures with extreme aridity.

Population: Mixed European and African descent.

Capital: Praia.

Languages: Portuguese and Cape Verdean Creole.

About Africa

Africa, the "Mother Continent," hosts diverse regions and landmarks, including the Sahara Desert, Nile River, and Mount Kilimanjaro. It is the world's second-largest continent with 54 sovereign nations.
