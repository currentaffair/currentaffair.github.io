SEBI and Information Ratio

Overview

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) mandates mutual funds to disclose the Information Ratio (IR) daily to enhance transparency and guide investors.

About the Information Ratio (IR)

IR measures portfolio performance against a benchmark, factoring in volatility. It evaluates how well an investment compares to indices like Nifty 50.

Formula

IR = (Portfolio Returns â€“ Benchmark Returns) / Tracking Error

Tracking error shows how closely the portfolio aligns with its benchmark. Lower tracking error indicates consistency, while higher values suggest volatility.

Components

  • Portfolio Returns: Investment performance.
  • Benchmark Returns: Performance of a relevant market index.
  • Tracking Error: Standard deviation of portfolio and benchmark return differences.

Significance

A higher IR indicates better fund manager performance, helping investors compare managers with similar strategies and identify consistent outperformers.

SEBI Background

Established in 1988 as a non-statutory body, SEBI became a statutory authority in 1992 to oversee capital and securities markets.
