Global International Migrant Workers Statistics 2022

International Migrant Workers (IMWs) made up 4.7% of the global labor force in 2022, equating to 167.7 million workers. This was a 30 million increase from 2013. The number consists of 155.6 million employed and 12.1 million unemployed but available for work. However, growth slowed to less than 1% annually from 2019 to 2022 due to the pandemic and economic disruptions.

Age and Gender Distribution

Male IMWs accounted for 61.3% of the total employed migrant workforce, while female IMWs were 38.7%. Despite representing a larger proportion of the total migrant population (64.9 million), the gender gap persists due to lower female representation in the migrant workforce. The majority of IMWs are prime-age workers aged 25-54, accounting for 74.9%.

Sectoral Distribution of IMWs

The services sector employed 68.4% of IMWs, with female workers making up 80.7% in this sector. In comparison, male IMWs in the services sector were 60.8%. The industry sector employed 24.3%, while only 7.4% of IMWs worked in agriculture, much lower than the 24.3% of non-migrants in the same sector.

Factors Behind Male Migration

Economic and social factors, such as demand for low-skill labor in construction, manufacturing, and care work, tend to attract more male migrants. Women face barriers such as social restrictions and fewer opportunities in sectors like construction.

Top Host Countries for IMWs

High-income countries hosted 68.4% of global IMWs, especially in services like healthcare, elder care, and education. Upper-middle-income countries hosted 17.4%. North America and Europe were major hosts, while Arab States accounted for 13.3% of the global IMW workforce.

Key Drivers of IMW Growth

The growing demand for workers due to aging populations in high-income countries and the expanding care economy (elder care, healthcare, child care) continue to attract migrant workers. Economic opportunities in high-income nations further contribute to the rise in international migration.
