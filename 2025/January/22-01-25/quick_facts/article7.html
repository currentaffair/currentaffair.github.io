GPS Spoofing and Interference: A Growing Threat

As per a recent OPSGROUP report, instances of GPS spoofing have increased from 300 flights affected daily in January to 1,500 by August.

What is GPS Interference and Spoofing?

GPS Interference: Disrupting the accuracy of GPS signals used for navigation.

Spoofing: A cyber-attack where fake GPS signals are transmitted, misleading navigation systems into sensing false positions, speeds, or altitudes.

Impacts

Spoofing impacts aircraft and drones, triggering incorrect speed or terrain warnings. It's commonly used in conflict zones to deceive enemies. Examples include incidents in India-Pakistan, India-Myanmar, and the Azerbaijan Airlines crash.

Affected Regions

New hotspots in 2024 include Northern Iraq (Baghdad), the Black Sea, Western Russia, North and South Korea, and the India-Pakistan border.

Inertial Reference System (IRS)

An IRS is a navigation system that uses gyroscopes and accelerometers to maintain accuracy without external signals like GPS, operating independently for up to five hours in GPS-rejected environments.
