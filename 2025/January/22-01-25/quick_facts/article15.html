Mullaperiyar Dam Safety Dispute

Key Points of Inquiry

Court’s Question: The Court is deliberating which committee should oversee the dam’s safety—the Supervisory or Statutory Committee.

Justice Surya Kant’s Concern: The Court emphasized avoiding dual oversight and overlapping roles in managing the dam’s safety.

Next Hearing: Scheduled for February 19, where both states will present their arguments.

Dam Safety Act of 2021

National Committee Formation: The Act establishes a National Committee chaired by the Central Water Commission (CWC), with representatives from both the Centre and States.

Functions: The Act focuses on creating policies and guidelines for dam safety, setting national standards for maintenance and safety.

Positions of the States

Tamil Nadu’s Argument: Tamil Nadu urges cooperation and claims Kerala is obstructing resolution.

Kerala’s Argument: Kerala contends that Tamil Nadu has failed to maintain the dam properly.

Key Legal Debate

Supervisory vs. Statutory Committee: The Court is examining which committee is better suited to ensure the dam’s safety, while addressing potential overlapping concerns and risks.

Safety Risks: The dam’s age and structural concerns make effective governance essential.

Impact of the Decision

Streamlined Oversight: The Court’s decision could clarify authority and improve safety governance.

Potential for Cooperation: The ruling may foster cooperation between Tamil Nadu and Kerala.

Concluding Observations

Specialized Oversight: This case highlights the importance of effective oversight for critical infrastructure like dams.

Dam Safety Act’s Role: The Act provides a national framework, but judicial intervention is crucial in this specific case.
