TRAI's Efforts to Combat Spam

Introduction

The Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) has been proactive in addressing Unsolicited Commercial Communications (UCC), commonly known as spam. Key initiatives include the Do-Not-Disturb (DND) registry, mobile applications for user preferences, and strict enforcement under the TCCCPR, 2018.

Blockchain Technology in Fighting Spam

In 2024, TRAI mandated blockchain use to enhance transparency and accountability in commercial messaging. Blockchain ensures an immutable and tamper-proof record of sender IDs and messages, preventing fraudulent activities and enhancing traceability.

Effectiveness and Challenges

While DND measures reduce spam from legitimate businesses, fraudsters have adapted with tactics like disposable numbers and VoIP calls, complicating enforcement.

Government and Telecom Measures

  • Sanchar Saathi Portal: Tracks and cancels numbers linked to fraud.
  • Telecom Security Operation Centre: Real-time monitoring of suspicious internet traffic.
  • AI by Telecom Companies: Identifies suspicious calls, aiding users in recognizing potential threats.
  • International Call Labeling: Helps distinguish legitimate and fraudulent calls.
