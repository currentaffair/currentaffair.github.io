Eruption of Mount Ibu

Overview

Indonesia’s Mount Ibu erupted 1,079 times in January 2025, with significant ash columns rising between 0.3 km to 4 km. It is a stratovolcano located on the island of Halmahera in Indonesia’s North Maluku province.

Mount Ibu and Stratovolcanoes

A stratovolcano, or composite volcano, has a steep, conical profile formed by layers of lava, ash, and volcanic debris. These volcanoes are typically found at convergent plate boundaries and subduction zones. Examples include Mount Vesuvius (Italy), Mount Fuji (Japan), and Mt Krakatoa (Indonesia).

Other Volcanic Activity

Other Indonesian volcanoes such as Mount Lewotobi Laki-Laki and Mount Ruang erupted multiple times, prompting mass evacuations. Indonesia, located along the Pacific Ring of Fire, experiences frequent volcanic and seismic activity, making it vulnerable to eruptions.
