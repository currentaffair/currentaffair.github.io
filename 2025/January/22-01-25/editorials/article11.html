Revitalizing the RTI Act: A Step Towards Transparent Governance
Introduction

The RTI Act is a cornerstone of India’s democratic framework, ensuring transparency, accountability, and citizen participation in governance. The Supreme Court of India recently highlighted the critical need to revive the Right to Information (RTI) Act, which has been weakened over the years.

Background of the RTI Act

Enactment and Objective

The RTI Act, enacted in 2005, aimed to promote transparency and accountability in governance. It empowered citizens to access information about government actions, fostering an informed and participative democracy. The Act mandates timely responses to queries, penalties for delays, and the establishment of Information Commissions to oversee its implementation.

Significance

RTI is a powerful tool for ensuring good governance, exposing corruption, and holding public authorities accountable. It mandates the timely provision of information, ensuring that citizens can effectively participate in democratic processes.

Important Point: RTI has been essential for increasing transparency in India’s public sector.
Supreme Court’s Observations

Vacancies and Pendency

The Supreme Court noted that eight posts of Information Commissioners are vacant in the Central Information Commission (CIC), with over 23,000 appeals pending. Several state commissions are either defunct or have stopped accepting petitions.

Directive to Governments

The Court ordered the Centre to file an affidavit with specific timelines for completing the selection process. It emphasized the need for immediate appointments in the CIC to address the backlog and improve the RTI process.

Criticism of Government Apathy

The Court criticized delays in appointing key officials and questioned the utility of the RTI institution if key posts remain unfilled.

Key Fact: The backlog in appeals highlights the systemic issues affecting RTI’s implementation.
Issues Undermining the RTI Act

Institutional Weaknesses

Vacancies and Backlogs

Persistent delays in filling positions of Information Commissioners at both central and state levels have crippled the RTI framework. Pending appeals and complaints undermine the Act’s promise of timely information.

Non-Compliance and Harassment

Many government departments fail to respond to RTI queries or delay responses. RTI activists often face threats, attacks, and even murder, deterring citizens from seeking information.

Erosion of Autonomy

Amendments to the Act have reduced the autonomy and independence of the Central Information Commission, undermining its effectiveness. Appointments continue to favor retired bureaucrats, sidelining professionals from other walks of life.

The Importance of RTI in Democracy

Transparency and Accountability

RTI ensures that governance processes are open and accountable, empowering citizens to question and challenge public authorities.

Fighting Corruption

Access to information helps expose corruption and inefficiencies, enabling corrective action and greater transparency in government activities.

Strengthening Democracy

The Act upholds the principle of participatory democracy, ensuring that governance is not opaque or arbitrary.

International Best Practices in Ensuring Transparency

United States: Freedom of Information Act (FOIA, 1966)

The FOIA mandates agencies to respond to requests within 20 working days and includes mechanisms for judicial review if information is delayed or denied.

Brazil: Access to Information Law (2011)

Brazil imposes strict deadlines for providing information, ranging from 5 to 20 days, and non-compliance results in penalties for officials, including fines and disciplinary action.

Norway and Sweden

Norway ensures diverse representation in bodies overseeing access to information, including professionals from academia, civil society, and the private sector. Sweden promotes awareness of citizens’ rights to access information through schools, media, and public campaigns.

Key Takeaways for India

India should adopt best practices from other nations, such as establishing truly independent Information Commissions and mandating proactive publication of essential government data. Introducing strict deadlines and launching awareness campaigns will help strengthen the RTI framework.

Way Forward

Filling Vacancies

Expedite the appointment process for Information Commissioners at the central and state levels and ensure a transparent, merit-based selection process.

Improving Efficiency

Digitize RTI processes to reduce backlogs and enhance accessibility. Introduce robust mechanisms for tracking and penalizing delays in responses.

Protecting Activists

Enact a Whistleblower Protection Act to safeguard RTI activists from threats and harm.

Restoring Autonomy

Reinstate the independence of the CIC and state commissions by reversing amendments that dilute their autonomy.

Conclusion: The Supreme Court’s intervention is a welcome step towards revitalizing the RTI Act. However, sustained efforts are needed to restore its original intent and strengthen its implementation. Transparent governance is a democratic necessity for ensuring India’s vibrant and participatory democracy.
