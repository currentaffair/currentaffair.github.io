Eurasian Otters in Kashmir

Recent Sighting

The Eurasian otters were spotted in Gurez Valley (Kashmir), marking the first live documentation in 25 years. They were observed feasting on fish in the Kishanganga River, which originates from Krishansar Lake in the Ganderbal district of J&K.

About Eurasian Otters

  • Family: Mustelidae (mammalian family)
  • Habitat: Both marine and freshwater environments
  • Local Name in J&K: Vodur
  • Activity: Crepuscular (active around dawn and dusk)

Habitat and Diet

Found in the Himalayas, Northeast India, and Western Ghats. They are carnivorous, feeding on:

  • Fish
  • Crustaceans
  • Amphibians
  • Occasionally reptiles, birds, eggs, insects, and worms

Conservation Status

  • IUCN: Near Threatened
  • Wildlife Protection Act, 1972: Schedule I
  • CITES: Appendix I

Other Otter Species in India

  • Smooth-coated Otter: Found throughout India
  • Small-clawed Otter: Found in the Himalayas and Southern India
