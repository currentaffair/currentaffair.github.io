Nuclear Energy Mission

Context

The Union Finance Minister announced the ₹20,000 crore Nuclear Energy Mission in the Union Budget 2025 to develop indigenous Small Modular Reactors (SMRs). At least five reactors are expected to be operational by 2033.

Budget Allocations

The Department of Atomic Energy has been allocated ₹24,049 crore for 2025-26, slightly lower than the ₹24,450 crore expected by March 2024 and significantly less than ₹25,882 crore in 2023-24.

About Small Modular Reactors (SMRs)

SMRs are advanced nuclear reactors with smaller capacity, offering flexibility, lower costs, and enhanced safety. They are key to India’s goal of achieving 100 GW of nuclear energy by 2047.

BSR vs BSMR

Bharat Small Reactors (BSR) are modified versions of existing Pressurised Heavy Water Reactors (PHWRs), while Bharat Small Modular Reactors (BSMR) are new technologies in the development phase.

Private Sector Role

The government plans to partner with the private sector for setting up BSRs, R&D of BSMRs, and new nuclear technologies. This is a shift from the traditional government-only model.

Development and Operation

BSRs will be built and operated by the Nuclear Power Corporation of India Ltd. (NPCIL), with private sector financing.

Long-Term Goals

India aims to expand nuclear energy capacity from 7 GW to 100 GW by 2047. To achieve this, amendments to the Atomic Energy Act and Civil Liability for Nuclear Damage Act are planned to encourage private sector involvement.
