Ambergris: Whale's Treasure

What is Ambergris?

Ambergris, also known as whale vomit, is a rare and valuable substance produced by sperm whales. Despite its name, it forms in the digestive tract from undigested squid and cuttlefish remains. Over time, exposure to sunlight and seawater hardens its exterior.

Why is Ambergris Valuable?

Ambergris is highly sought after in the perfume industry due to its ability to make fragrances last longer. Its rarity and unique properties can drive prices up to $40,000 per kilogram.

Legal Status

Ambergris is legal in countries like the UK, New Zealand, and France. However, in India, its sale is banned under the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972 to protect endangered sperm whales.

About Sperm Whales

The sperm whale is the largest toothed whale, using a waxy substance called spermaceti to focus sound for deep-sea hunting. These whales are vulnerable due to threats like fishing and pollution.

Conservation Status

  • IUCN Red List: Vulnerable
  • CITES: Appendix I
  • India: Schedule II under Wildlife Protection Act, 1972
  • CMS: Appendix I
