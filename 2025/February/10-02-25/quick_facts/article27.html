Cheetah Reintroduction in India

Project Overview

Project Cheetah aims to reintroduce cheetahs to India after their extinction in 1952. Kuno National Park hosted the first reintroduction in 2022 and now houses 26 cheetahs.

Preparations at Gandhi Sagar

Quarantine Bomas (QBs) are equipped with mud mounds, bamboo roofs, water saucers, and electric fencing to mimic natural habitats. Dual-entry gates and 360-degree surveillance ensure staff safety and prevent escapes.

Health Monitoring

Cheetahs will be quarantined for at least one month before release into a 64 sq km enclosure. Continuous observation will mitigate health risks from new diseases.

Historical & Geographical Significance

The sanctuary's savanna-like habitat mirrors African environments. Rock art at Chaturbhuj Nala suggests cheetahs' historical presence in the region.

Local Displacement & Concerns

Karanpura village (280 families) was relocated, facing resistance and compensation issues. Nearby villages, including Gurjar, Banjara, and Bhil communities, suffer from water scarcity as fencing blocks access to the Chambal river, affecting dairy farming and reducing milk production.

Challenges & Future Implications

  • Human-Wildlife Conflict: Balancing local displacement with conservation goals.
  • Ecological Balance: Maintaining prey populations without harming biodiversity.
  • Sustainable Development: Addressing local grievances while ensuring conservation success.
