Digital Payments Index

Context

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) released the Digital Payments Index (DPI), a tool to measure the growth of digital payments in India.

About the DPI

The DPI was launched in January 2021 with March 2018 as the base year, set at 100. As of September 2024, the index stands at 465.33, up from 445.5 in March 2024, indicating rapid digital payment adoption.

Growth Drivers

The rise in the DPI score is due to enhanced payment infrastructure, improved performance, and expanded digital payment systems across India.

Parameters of RBI-DPI

  • Payment Enablers (25%): Internet penetration, mobile connectivity, and regulatory support.
  • Demand-Side Infrastructure (10%): Availability of digital payment apps and user-friendly interfaces.
  • Supply-Side Infrastructure (15%): POS terminals and payment gateways.
  • Payment Performance (45%): Transaction volume, value, and frequency.
  • Consumer Centricity (5%): User-friendliness and accessibility of payment systems.

Challenges

Despite growth, challenges remain:

  • Limited digital infrastructure in rural areas.
  • Rising fraud and data breaches.
  • Lack of awareness among certain demographics.
  • Incompatibility between payment platforms.

Way Forward

  • Enhance internet and mobile connectivity in rural regions.
  • Implement advanced cybersecurity measures and educate users on safe practices.
  • Launch awareness campaigns and training programs.
  • Develop unified standards for seamless transactions across platforms.
