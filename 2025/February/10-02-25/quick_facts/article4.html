Pradhan Mantri Krishi Yojana

About the Scheme

The Pradhan Mantri Dhan Dhanya Krishi Yojana is a transformative agricultural program launched in the Union Budget 2025-26. It focuses on improving agricultural productivity and sustainability in 100 underdeveloped districts across India.

The scheme targets 1.7 crore farmers, particularly those with small and marginal landholdings of less than 2 hectares.

Main Objectives

  • Enhance agricultural productivity with advanced farming techniques and modern equipment.
  • Promote crop diversification for pulses, oilseeds, and high-value crops.
  • Improve irrigation facilities, including solar-powered and micro-irrigation systems.
  • Augment post-harvest storage to minimize crop wastage.
  • Facilitate credit access for adopting modern agricultural practices.

Implementation Strategy

The scheme will be executed through the convergence of existing agricultural schemes, in collaboration with state governments. Interventions will be tailored to the specific needs of each district, leveraging local resources for effective implementation.

Expected Outcomes

  • Increased income levels for farmers due to enhanced productivity and market access.
  • Sustainable agricultural practices leading to environmental benefits.
  • Reduced migration by creating employment opportunities in rural areas.
  • A strengthened rural economy supported by better infrastructure and financial access.

Background Inspiration

This scheme is inspired by the Aspirational Districts Programme launched in 2018, which focused on transforming underdeveloped districts through quick and effective development strategies.
