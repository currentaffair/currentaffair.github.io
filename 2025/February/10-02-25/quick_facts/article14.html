Ethanol Blending in Petrol

Context

The Union Minister of Road Transport and Highways announced that India will achieve 20% ethanol blending in petrol within the next two months.

What is Ethanol Blending?

Ethanol blending involves mixing ethanol with petrol to reduce fossil fuel reliance and emissions. Ethanol, derived from plant materials, boosts octane levels, improving engine performance and combustion efficiency.

India’s Ethanol Blending Target

India aims for 20% ethanol blending, achieving this a year earlier than planned. This requires 1,100 crore litres of fuel ethanol annually.

Economic Impact

Producing 100 crore litres of ethanol saves Rs 6,000 crore in oil imports. India's oil import bill is approximately ₹10.5 lakh crore.

Sources of Ethanol Production

  • Sugar and Molasses: Contribute around 400 crore litres.
  • FCI Rice: Expected to produce 110 crore litres.
  • Maize: Contributes nearly 400 crore litres.
  • Broken Rice: Supplementary source for ethanol production.

Key Details

India’s ethanol distillery capacity stands at 1,600 crore litres, bolstered by government incentives. The price of FCI rice has been reduced to ₹22.5 per kg to support ethanol production.

Maize's Role in Ethanol Production

Maize has become a significant ethanol feedstock with 42 million tonnes produced in 2024-25. Major maize-producing states include Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Rajasthan, Bihar, and Uttar Pradesh.

Addressing Concerns

The byproduct of ethanol production, Distiller’s Dried Grains with Solubles (DDGS), is used as poultry feed, ensuring traditional uses of maize are maintained.

Way Forward

Balancing ethanol production with food security is crucial. Continuous monitoring and policy adjustments are necessary to ensure sustainability.
