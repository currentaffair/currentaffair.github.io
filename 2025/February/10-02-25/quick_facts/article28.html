USAID Global Aid Freeze

Recent Developments

The US President Donald Trump has imposed a 90-day freeze on foreign aid, halting US Agency for International Development (USAID) programs worldwide.

Additionally, the US announced it will not attend the 2025 G20 Foreign Ministers’ meeting in Johannesburg, South Africa.

About USAID

Role: Primary US agency for global humanitarian and development aid.

Funding: Allocated USD 44.2 billion in 2024, representing 0.4% of the total US federal budget but accounting for 42% of all humanitarian aid tracked by the United Nations.

Key Focus: Healthcare, food aid, disaster relief, and policy advocacy worldwide.

Top Aid Recipients: Ukraine, Ethiopia, Jordan, Somalia, and Afghanistan.

USAID and India

India's association with USAID began in 1951 with the India Emergency Food Aid Act, evolving from food aid to infrastructure, capacity building, and economic reforms.

USAID has supported education, immunization, polio eradication, and HIV/TB prevention. Over the last decade, India received around USD 1.5 billion, accounting for 0.2% to 0.4% of USAID’s total global funding.

Implications for India

Global Role: India positions itself as a bridge between the Global North and South, leveraging its G20 status.

Geopolitical Impact: Reduced US influence in G20 could allow China and Russia to expand their roles, potentially weakening India’s position.

Healthcare Concerns: While direct financial aid to India has decreased, USAID contributions exceeded USD 50 million in 2024. Permanent cuts could impact vaccination programs, infectious disease control, and medical infrastructure.

India may need to redirect domestic funds to sustain projects in health, environment, and governance.
