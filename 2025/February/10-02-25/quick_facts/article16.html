North Sea Landforms Discovery

Context

New images from the North Sea reveal never-before-seen landforms carved by a colossal ice sheet 1 million years ago, now buried under a thick layer of mud.

About the North Sea

The North Sea is a shallow, enclosed sea in Northwestern Europe, considered a marginal sea of the Atlantic Ocean. It connects to the Atlantic via the Norwegian Sea (north) and English Channel (south), and to the Baltic Sea via the Kattegat and Skagerrak straits.

Bordering Countries: United Kingdom, Norway, Denmark, Germany, the Netherlands, Belgium, and France.

Area: Approximately 570,000 sq.km. It hosts two of the world’s largest ports: Hamburg and Rotterdam.

The North Sea is also one of the world’s most important fishing grounds.
