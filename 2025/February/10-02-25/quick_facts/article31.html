Daya and Vishwamitri Rivers

Pollution in Daya River

Activists in Bhubaneswar demand urgent action to clean the polluted Daya River, highlighting health risks and environmental degradation.

About Daya River

State: Odisha

Origin: From the Kusabhadra River, flowing through Khurda and Puri districts before emptying into Chilika Lake, India's largest coastal lagoon.

Length: Approximately 37 km

Tributaries: Malaguni River

Biodiversity: Supports wetland biodiversity, providing habitat for fish and migratory birds near Chilika Lake.

Historical Significance: Linked to the Kalinga War (261 BCE), where Emperor Ashoka's transformation to Buddhism followed the battle's bloodshed, which reportedly turned the river red.

Vishwamitri River Survey

A survey in Vadodara's Vishwamitri River aims to estimate the mugger crocodile population, addressing conservation efforts and human-wildlife interactions.

About Vishwamitri River

Location: Vadodara, Gujarat

Origin: From Pavagadh Hill, flowing through Vadodara city before merging into the Dhadhar River, which drains into the Arabian Sea.

Length: Approximately 80 km

Unique Feature: Known for its large population of mugger crocodiles (Crocodylus palustris), making it a unique urban ecosystem.
