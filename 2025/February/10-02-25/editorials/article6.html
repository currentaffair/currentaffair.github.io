Opportunities

Technological breakthroughs, like the reduction in solar module prices, can significantly accelerate renewable energy adoption in India.

Fiscal Consolidation and Spending

Current Fiscal Performance

Nominal GDP growth for FY2024 is at 9.7%, below the budgeted 10.5%. However, fiscal targets are likely to be met or surpassed due to lower-than-expected spending.

Recommendations for FY2025

  • Adopt a calibrated approach to fiscal consolidation.
  • Stagger the fiscal deficit glide path to support sustained economic growth.

Infrastructure and Project Execution

Challenges

Approximately 40% of central sector projects (costing over Rs 150 crore) face cost and time overruns, highlighting inefficiencies in project management.

Recommendations

  • Focus on shovel-ready projects to ensure timely execution.
  • Enhance coordination with state governments to streamline project execution.
  • Improve efficiency in project management to reduce delays and cost overruns.

Conclusion

India’s growth story is one of resilience and potential. However, navigating global threats requires strategic policy interventions, sustained reforms, and a commitment to innovation and sustainability. By addressing regulatory hurdles, investing in infrastructure, and fostering private sector participation, India can continue to lead as the world’s fastest-growing large economy.
