India's R&D Budget Growth

Introduction

The Union Budget 2025-26 has introduced significant allocations aimed at advancing India's scientific research and development (R&D) capabilities. Key highlights include:

  • ₹20,000 crore allocated for the development of small modular reactors, targeting five reactors by 2033.
  • Introduction of the Research, Development, and Innovation Fund under the Department of Science and Technology (DST).
  • The DST budget allocation has surged to ₹28,000 crore, which is over three times last year’s allocation and seven times the actual expenditure of 2023-24.

Despite these impressive increases, concerns remain about the effective absorption and utilization of these funds.

Structural Challenges in India’s R&D Ecosystem

India's R&D landscape is characterized by several structural challenges that hinder its innovation potential.

Low Private Sector Participation

The private sector contributes only 36% to India’s total R&D expenditure, reflecting limited engagement compared to global standards. In contrast:

  • Overall R&D spending in India was just 0.64% of GDP in 2020, marking the lowest level since 1995.
  • Business enterprises account for approximately 40% of government R&D spending.
  • Public sector R&D units allocate only 0.30% of their sales turnover to research, while the private sector allocates 1.46% (2020-21, DST estimates).

Core Issues Affecting R&D Growth

Despite India’s strengths in technology-driven industries like fuel, metallurgy, pharmaceuticals, textiles, IT, and biotechnology, core R&D returns remain insufficient due to:

  • Lack of supporting infrastructure for emerging technologies like quantum computing and artificial intelligence.
  • Absence of semiconductor fabs and chipsets critical for hardware innovation.
  • Shortage of a skilled engineering workforce to drive research initiatives.
  • Lack of a robust, private sector-driven innovation ecosystem.

Strategic R&D Transformation

To overcome these challenges, India needs a comprehensive strategy that addresses both structural and operational aspects of the R&D ecosystem.

Clear Roadmap for Fund Allocation

Establish transparent mechanisms for private entities to access R&D funding and ensure accountability in fund disbursal.

Incentivizing Private Investment

Introduce tax benefits, grants, and subsidies to attract corporate R&D participation. For instance:

Incentive Expected Impact
Tax Deductions for R&D Spending Encourage businesses to allocate more resources to research.
Grants for Startups Boost innovation among small and medium enterprises.

Building Foundational Infrastructure

  • Develop semiconductor fabrication units and advanced chip manufacturing facilities.
  • Expand AI and quantum computing research hubs to foster cutting-edge innovation.
  • Establish world-class engineering and research institutions to nurture talent.

Ensuring Long-Term Industry Engagement

Foster strong academia-industry partnerships and encourage startup participation in R&D innovation. Additionally, create regulatory frameworks that facilitate smoother technology transfers.

Public Accountability

The government must quantify expected outcomes from increased funding and track its impact on national innovation metrics.

Conclusion

The Union Budget 2025-26 represents a transformative step for India's R&D ecosystem, but its success hinges on effective fund utilization and private sector participation. By addressing structural challenges and fostering a collaborative innovation environment, India can position itself as a global leader in scientific research and technological development.
