Way Forward

  • Fiscal Reforms: Strengthen the
    Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management (FRBM) Act, 2003
    to prevent reckless fiscal spending and ensure sustainable social welfare.
  • Defining Welfare and Freebies: Establish clear policy guidelines to differentiate essential welfare from electoral freebies, considering factors like social utility, long-term impact, and fiscal sustainability.
  • Strengthening Institutional Mechanisms: Enhance the role of financial regulators to monitor public spending, track off-budget borrowings, and manage hidden subsidies, such as the underpricing of electricity.
  • Balancing Welfare and Fiscal Prudence: Prioritize investments in education, healthcare, and job creation to ensure economic stability, while ensuring subsidies and social schemes focus on capacity-building rather than fostering dependency.

Conclusion

The balance between electoral freebies and genuine welfare measures is crucial for sustainable development. While freebies can offer immediate relief and support socio-economic inclusion, unchecked distribution can strain fiscal resources and hinder long-term growth. A judicious approach, focusing on targeted welfare, fiscal responsibility, and transparent governance, is essential to ensure that welfare schemes contribute to holistic development without compromising economic stability.
