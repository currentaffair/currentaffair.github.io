International Best Practices in Pension Systems

Sweden

Sweden guarantees a minimum pension regardless of market performance, combining state contributions with individual savings to ensure security.

United Kingdom (UK)

The National Employment Savings Trust (NEST) provides universal pension coverage through automatic enrollment of eligible workers.

Netherlands

Strong regulatory oversight of pension funds in the Netherlands ensures

sustainable payouts and financial security
.

Singapore

The Central Provident Fund (CPF) offers tax incentives for voluntary savings, reducing state pension burdens while encouraging personal retirement savings.

Way Forward

Introduction of a Minimum Guaranteed Pension

Ensuring a stable income for retirees, especially during economic crises, can reduce dependence on market-driven returns.

Inclusion of Informal Sector Workers

Broadening UPS coverage to include informal workers would enhance financial security for a larger population.

Flexible Tenure Requirements

Restructuring the 25-year service rule to progressively reward employees based on years served would accommodate late entrants and career interruptions.

Establishing a Strong Regulatory Framework

Creating an independent Pension Regulatory Authority would oversee fund management and ensure transparency.

Enhancing Financial Awareness and Advisory Support

Financial literacy programs are crucial to help employees make informed pension decisions.
