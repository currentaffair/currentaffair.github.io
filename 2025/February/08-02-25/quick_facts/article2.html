Sahitya Akademi Award 2024

Overview

The Sahitya Akademi Awards 2024 recognized 22 winners, with Bengali and Urdu awards pending, bringing the total to 24.

About the Award

The Sahitya Akademi Awards honor exceptional contributions in novels, poetry, essays, and plays. It is the second-highest literary honor in India, following the Jnanpith Award.

Established in 1954, the Akademi operates as an autonomous body under the Ministry of Culture.

Eligibility Criteria

Awards are presented for works in the 22 languages listed in the Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, along with English and Rajasthani, totaling 24 languages. Translations of Indian literary works are also eligible.

The author must be an Indian citizen.

Other Sahitya Akademi Awards

  • Bal Sahitya Puraskar - For children's literature.
  • Yuva Puraskar - For authors under 35 years of age.

Additional Initiatives

  • Gramalok: Supports writers from remote areas.
  • Dalit Chetna: Promotes Dalit writers.
