Asteroid 2024 YR4 Impact Risk

Discovery and Potential Risk

NASA identified Asteroid 2024 YR4 with a 1% chance of impacting Earth by 2032. Detected in December 2024, it passed 800,000 km from Earth and will reappear in 2028.

It is rated Level 3 on the Torino Scale, indicating potential localized destruction. If it impacts, it could release 8-10 megatons of energy, far exceeding the 2013 Chelyabinsk meteor.

Understanding Asteroids

Asteroids are rocky remnants from the solar system's formation about 4.6 billion years ago. They primarily orbit in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter.

Types of Asteroids

  • Main Asteroid Belt: Between Mars and Jupiter.
  • Trojans: Share orbits with planets at Lagrangian points.
  • Near-Earth Asteroids (NEAs): Have orbits that bring them close to Earth.

Collision Frequency

Small asteroids burn up in Earth's atmosphere, while larger ones occasionally reach the surface but rarely cause major damage. Global-scale impacts occur approximately once every 260 million years, like the Chicxulub event that led to the extinction of dinosaurs.

Planetary Defense Initiatives

Agencies like NASA are developing planetary defense mechanisms:

  • DART Mission (2022): Successfully altered an asteroid's trajectory.
  • ESA’s Hera Mission: A follow-up to DART for detailed study.
  • NETRA Project: India’s initiative for space object tracking.
