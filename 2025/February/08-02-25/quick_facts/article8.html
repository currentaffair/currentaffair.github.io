Arhar Dal Import Policy

Policy Extension

The central government has extended the duty-free import period for arhar (tur) dal until March 31, 2026 (DGFT notification, January 20, 2025).

Farmers' Concerns

  • Farmers in Maharashtra and Karnataka are selling produce below the Minimum Support Price (MSP) due to cheaper imports.
  • Market price has dropped to ₹7000 per quintal, compared to ₹12,000 per quintal last year.
  • Fear of declining profitability may discourage future cultivation.

Economic and Policy Impact

  • Self-Sufficiency at Risk: Heavy reliance on imports threatens domestic pulse production.
  • Experts warn of reduced production due to falling MSP realization, increasing long-term dependency.

Trader & Industry Concerns

  • Dal mill associations demand reversal of the decision and an increase in MSP to ₹9000 per quintal.
  • Traders hesitant to purchase local produce, awaiting cheaper imports.

Import Trends

  • India imports pulses from Myanmar, Australia, Africa, and Canada.
  • Arhar and urad primarily come from Africa and Myanmar.
  • Chana, peas, and masoor come from Australia, Russia, and Canada.

Wider Implications

  • Impact on Other Pulses: Prices of chana and masoor have fallen below MSP.
  • Contradiction with Government Goals: Despite advocating self-sufficiency, reliance on imports continues.

Conclusion

Short-Term Benefit: Stable consumer prices due to increased supply.

Long-Term Risk: Disincentivizes domestic farmers, leading to greater import dependency and loss of agricultural income.
