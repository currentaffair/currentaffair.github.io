PM-JAY's Impact on Cancer Treatment

Key Findings

36% improvement in timely cancer treatment overall.

90% improvement for AB PM-JAY beneficiaries since 2018.

Delays reduced in reproductive, genitourinary, breast, and blood cancers.

Demographic Insights

Better access for younger patients (< 30 years: 77%).

Higher education levels (70.2%) linked to faster treatment.

Insurance coverage (69%) improved access; higher-income groups faced fewer delays.

Challenges

Delays in radiotherapy, chemotherapy, and surgery reduce survival rates.

India has only 779 radiotherapy machines, far below WHO recommendations.

High out-of-pocket expenses due to exclusions in AB PM-JAY.

Public health spending remains below 2% of GDP.

Cancer in India

Cancer is characterized by uncontrolled cell proliferation harming healthy tissues.

Cancer cases projected to rise from 14.6 lakh in 2022 to 15.7 lakh in 2025.

Breast cancer is the most prevalent, making up 13.6% of cases.

Globally, 20 million new cases and 9.7 million deaths in 2022.
