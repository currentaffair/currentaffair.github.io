Pinaka Ammunition Procurement

Contract Details

The Defence Ministry signed contracts worth â‚¹10,147 crore for advanced ammunition for the Pinaka Multiple Rocket Launch System (MRLS). Agreements were made with Economic Explosives Limited (EEL) and Munitions India Limited (MIL).

Types of Ammunition Procured

  • Area Denial Munition (ADM) Type-1: Designed to disperse sub-munitions over large areas, targeting mechanized forces, vehicles, and personnel.
  • High Explosive Pre-Fragmented (HEPF)-Mk-1 (enhanced) rockets: Enhanced lethality for the Pinaka MRLS.

Strategic Significance

The procurement enhances the range and firepower of the Pinaka MRLS. The upgraded Pinaka will become the cornerstone of India's long-range rocket artillery.

Technological Advancements

  • DRDO successfully tested guided Pinaka rockets with a range of 75 km in November.
  • Efforts are ongoing to extend the range to 120 km and further to 300 km.
  • A Pinaka battery (214-mm MRLS) can launch 72 rockets within 44 seconds.

Software Upgrade

A separate contract was signed with Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL) for Shakti software upgrades, likely improving fire control and operational efficiency.

Implications

  • Boosts India's indigenous defence manufacturing under Atmanirbhar Bharat.
  • Strengthens artillery capabilities against enemy forces along contested borders.
  • Enhances India's deterrence posture with improved precision-strike capabilities.
  • Supports self-reliance in defence production by involving domestic firms.

This procurement marks a significant step in modernizing India's artillery with enhanced range, precision, and operational flexibility.
