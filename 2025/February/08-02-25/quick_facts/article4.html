Beggar-thy-Neighbour Policy

Overview

The United States imposed tariffs on imports from China, Canada, and Mexico under the Beggar-thy-neighbour policy.

What is Beggar-thy-Neighbour Policy?

A protectionist strategy involving measures like trade barriers, currency devaluation, and subsidies to benefit a nation's economy at the expense of others.

Origin: Coined by Adam Smith in "The Wealth of Nations" (1776), criticizing mercantilist policies and advocating for free trade.

Supporters' Arguments

  • Boosts the domestic economy by protecting industries and jobs.
  • Currency devaluation lowers export costs, raises import prices, and may lead to a trade surplus.

Critics' Arguments

  • Triggers retaliatory tariffs, reducing global trade and investment (e.g., the Great Depression, 1929-39).
  • Benefits domestic producers but harms consumers through higher prices due to reduced competition.

Alternative Perspective

Some economists argue for unilateral free trade and avoiding retaliation to foster long-term economic growth.
